Expected timelines

How long does repatriation from Sweden take?

Timelines depend on circumstances. Here is an honest guide to what to expect.

Best case
5 days
Natural death, documentation issued without delay, no post-mortem required.
Typical case
7-12 days
Standard repatriation from Sweden. Most families experience this timeline.
Complex case
18-28 days
Post-mortem, inquest, criminal investigation, or logistically difficult location.

Factors that can extend the timeline

  • Swedish Police Authority (Polisen) investigates all unnatural or suspicious deaths
  • RÃƒÂ¤ttsmedicinalverket (National Board of Forensic Medicine) conducts post-mortems for unnatural deaths; Stockholm has good capacity but regional offices may have backlogs
  • Deaths in remote northern Sweden (Lapland, Abisko, Sarek) require body transfer to the nearest city with forensic capacity before documentation begins
  • Swedish documentation is in Swedish; certified translations required for UK authorities
