Each guide covers the documents required to export ashes, airline hand-luggage rules, costs, and what to do once you are back in the UK.

UK import rules

The UK does not restrict the import of human ashes. There is no customs declaration required. The documentation requirements are on the departure side, and vary by country.

General guidance

What applies to all countries

Cremation certificate

Always required. Issued by the crematorium where the cremation took place. Keep it with the ashes at all times during travel. Most airlines will ask to see it at check-in.

Airline confirmation

Contact your airline at least 48 hours before travel. Rules vary. Metal urns cannot be X-rayed and may be refused as hand luggage. Use a wooden or biodegradable travel urn for the journey.

On arrival in the UK

No import declaration is required. Walk through with your ashes and cremation certificate. You will not be stopped at customs for ashes, but having the certificate avoids any confusion.
