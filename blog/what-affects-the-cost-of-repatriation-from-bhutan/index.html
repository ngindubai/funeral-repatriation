Bhutan Repatriation Costs: The Paro Airport Problem

Limited cargo capacity and a tour-operator system both shape the case here. Contact us any hour for a clear answer.

Bhutan controls tourism tightly: every foreign visitor travels on an approved tour with a licensed guide. That single fact shapes almost everything about how a repatriation case unfolds there, for better and for worse.

The tour operator is already involved

Because every visitor is on a registered tour, the operator typically knows about a death within hours, and has an existing working relationship with local authorities. Notifying the tour operator alongside the FCDO as soon as possible is one of the few things in this process that genuinely speeds things up rather than adding a layer.

Paro Airport’s real constraint

Paro International Airport has one of the most demanding approaches in commercial aviation, and only a small number of certified pilots are qualified to fly it. That limits the airport to two carriers, Drukair and Bhutan Airlines, both routing via Delhi, Kolkata, Bangkok, or Kathmandu. Cargo space isn’t something you can simply book on short notice; it needs advance coordination, and that coordination is where much of the variation in cost and timing comes from.

Certification, not just registration

Death registration goes through the Civil Registration and Census Authority. For any death that isn’t from natural causes, the Royal Bhutan Police must investigate and clear the case first, and that investigation stage is usually the biggest source of delay in a Bhutan case. Documentation is primarily in Dzongkha, so certified English translation is needed throughout.

No local cremation for foreign nationals

There’s no commercial cremation facility in Bhutan equipped to handle a foreign national’s remains. Full body repatriation to the UK is the only route, in every single case, whatever the family might otherwise have preferred.

What we can offer

Once we know whether the Royal Bhutan Police are involved and roughly when cargo space might be available, we can give you a realistic timeline and cost picture rather than an optimistic one.
