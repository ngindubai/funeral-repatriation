SEO Audit and Improvement Report

Repatriate Service (repatriationfuneral.com) · 2 July 2026 · Prepared against the SEO and Search Brief dated 29 June 2026

What this is. A review of the live site against the six stories in the 29 June brief, grounded in the actual templates and content in this repository. Every item below says what to change, why it helps, and the trade-off if there is one. A companion file, SEO-BUILD-PLAN-2026-07-02.md, turns this into an ordered set of build steps. That plan is written for Sonnet to execute, and it marks the few steps that need Opus.

Headline. The site is already in good shape for the two things the brief says matter most: answering the query directly (every one of the 2,467 route pages carries a direct-answer block and FAQ data) and being readable by AI crawlers (the robots file explicitly welcomes the AI search bots). The work now is quality and trust, not volume: tighten the structured data so it is honest and matches the page, add a real author identity for a life-and-death (YMYL) topic, lift the answer block to the top of every layout, and thicken the thinnest generated prose so it is not exposed to a spam update.

What is already right (leave it alone)

  • Direct answers everywhere. All 2,467 routes carry direct_answer_intro, direct_answer_points, faqs and a step timeline. This is exactly the answer-first structure Story 2 says AI Overviews pull from.
  • AI crawlers are welcomed. robots.txt explicitly allows OAI-SearchBot, ChatGPT-User, PerplexityBot, Claude-SearchBot, Google-Extended and GPTBot. Nothing to do here.
  • Schema coverage. Route pages carry Breadcrumb, Service and FAQ schema. Blog posts and country hubs carry Article schema. Home carries Organization.
  • Layout rotation is balanced. The A to E variants are spread evenly (roughly 490 each), so no run of identical pages.
  • Titles and descriptions are mostly in range. Only 3 route title fields exceed 60 characters and only 33 descriptions exceed 155.

Findings and improvements

P1Sonnet

F1. Move the direct-answer block to the top of every layout

What

Variants A and C already place the answer block first. Variants B, D and E do not: B and D lead with the helpline strip, then the overview paragraph, then the answer. E leads with the overview. Roughly 1,478 pages push the direct answer below other content.

Why

Story 2 is explicit: pages cited in AI Overviews answer the query directly and completely near the top, and the same brief-answer structure wins featured snippets. Making the answer block the first section after the hero on all five variants gives every page its best shot at citation.

Trade-off. CLAUDE.md mandates layout rotation so no two consecutive pages look identical. Moving the answer block up narrows that variety a little. Mitigation: keep the answer block first on all variants, but leave the ordering of the sections beneath it varied per variant. Variety is preserved where it matters (the body), consistency is added where it pays (the answer).

P1SonnetOpus for new answer text

F2. Fix the FAQ schema on country hubs so it matches the page

What

On the 239 country hubs, the visible FAQ answers are real and specific (timeline, documents, ashes). But the FAQ JSON-LD writes the same boilerplate sentence as the answer to every question: "For specific guidance on this question, contact our specialist team..." The structured data does not match what the reader sees.

Why

Google's structured-data policy requires the marked-up answer to be the answer shown on the page. A mismatch across 239 pages is a content-mismatch risk and can trigger a manual action, which on a YMYL site is a real cost. Right now the markup is also low value because it repeats one non-answer.

Fix

Make the schema answers mirror the visible answers (reuse the same template logic for both). For the questions beyond the first four, either write real answers or drop those questions from the markup.

Trade-off. Google narrowed FAQ rich results to government and health sites in 2023, so the visible SERP gain is small. The reason to do this is risk removal and honesty of the markup, not a rich-result win. Writing genuine answers for the trailing questions needs Opus (they must be factually correct for a YMYL topic).

P1Opus

F3. Give the site a real author identity (E-E-A-T)

What

No route page names an author in schema. Blog posts list the author as the Organization, not a person. Country hubs use a generic "Senior Repatriation Consultant". The four personas defined in CLAUDE.md (James Whitfield, Dr Amara Osei, Claire Sutton, Thomas Anand) are never wired into any byline or Person schema.

Why

Story 2 lists entity authority as a thing that does matter, and this is a death, legal and consular topic where Google weighs expertise and trust heavily. A consistent, named, credentialed author with a real bio page is one of the stronger trust signals available.

Trade-off, stated plainly. The personas are not verifiable real people. Attaching invented credentials to YMYL content is itself a trust risk, and Story 3 warns about inauthentic authority signals. So this is a decision for Gareth, not an automatic change. Two honest options: (a) stand the personas up properly with genuine author bio pages that describe real experience, keep them consistent everywhere, and link the Person schema to those pages (a sameAs / url), which makes them real entities rather than empty names; or (b) keep the Organization as the author and instead invest the trust signal in the existing "Reviewed by the editorial team" credit plus the methodology page. Option (a) is stronger if the bios are truthful. It should not be done by inventing qualifications. This step is Opus because it is a judgement call plus careful authoring.

P1Opus

F4. Thicken and grammar-fix the thinnest route prose

What

The overview text on routes has a median of 48 words, and 695 pages sit under 40 words. Some of it reads as machine-joined fragments, for example: "The Local authorities takes jurisdiction when the death is: violent, suspicious, or unexplained deaths." The prose lives in front-matter fields, so the fix is in the generators and a backfill, not hand-editing 2,467 files.

Why

Story 1 names the exact vulnerability: the spam update targets thin auto-generated pages that lack original information. Every other page element is strong, so the weakest link is this short, sometimes ungrammatical prose. Fixing it lifts quality and reduces spam-update exposure at the same time.

Trade-off. This is the largest single job and touches many pages, so it should run in batches through the existing QA gate. It must stay inside the YMYL rules: no invented facts, every claim source-able, sentence rhythm varied, banned words avoided. That is why it is Opus, not Sonnet. Do the 695 shortest first for the biggest risk reduction per hour.

P2Sonnet

F5. Remove the duplicate Organization schema on route pages

What

Route pages emit Organization schema twice: once site-wide from baseof.html (the fuller version, with opening hours) and again, thinner, inside routes/single.html.

Why

One clean Organization block per page is correct. Remove the thinner duplicate in the route template and keep the site-wide one.

Trade-off. None. Pure clean-up.
P2Sonnet

F6. Add og:image and a Twitter card

What

No page carries an og:image or any twitter:card tag. Add a default share image site-wide and twitter:card = summary_large_image in baseof.html.

Why

Story 4 notes traffic is fragmenting to social and chat surfaces. A share image controls how the page looks when a grieving family sends a link to a relative, and some AI and social surfaces read these tags.

Trade-off. Needs one image asset chosen with care: calm and brand-appropriate, never a distressing photo. That single choice is the only non-mechanical part; the template edit is Sonnet.
P2Sonnet

F7. Link country hubs down to their route pages

What

Routes link up to their country hub, but hubs do not link down to any route pages. With 2,467 routes, most are reached only through the sitemap and sideways route-to-route links.

Why

Story 5 points at silo structure as a foundation, and Story 6 flags that the indexing report is lagging, so crawl paths matter more than usual. A "Routes from {country}" block on each hub deepens the silo and gives crawlers a clean path into the route matrix.

Trade-off. Adds link volume to each hub. Keep it scoped, for example the main corridors plus a link to the routes index, rather than listing every destination.
P2Sonnet

F8. Shorten the rendered title tag

What

The title field is fine, but the template appends | Repatriate Service. So the actual tag reads, for example, "Afghanistan to UK Repatriation: Guidance for Families | Repatriate Service" at about 73 characters. The brand suffix is past the point Google truncates.

Why

Story 4 ties title and meta quality to the traffic-to-ranking conversion gap. Dropping or shortening the suffix on route and hub pages keeps the useful words inside the visible part of the SERP.

Trade-off. Slightly less brand reinforcement in the tag, but it truncates anyway, so little is lost. Consider dropping the suffix on routes and hubs while keeping it on the home and top pages.

What

Trade-off. None. Small tidy-up.
P3no code change

F10. Keep llms.txt, but do not invest further in it

What

The site ships llms.txt and llms-full.txt. Story 2 states plainly that Google does not read these for AI ranking, and that AI-specific schema and content chunking are not needed.

Why keep them

They still help retrieval by ChatGPT, Perplexity and Claude, and they cost nothing to keep. The point is simply not to spend more time on llms-specific or AI-specific markup tactics that Google has said it ignores.

Trade-off. None. This is a "stop here" note, not a task.
P3governance note

F11. Do not buy brand mentions or use PBNs

What

Story 3 covers vendors selling paid brand placements on low-quality domains to influence AI citations. Google has flagged this as a violation and it does not work on Google's current AI Overview system.

Action

Record it as a do-not in MEMORY.md so no future run is tempted. The correct path is the organic rank plus direct-answer structure this audit already pushes.

Trade-off. None. Guard-rail only.
P3Opus for the fact loop

F12. Spec a quarterly fact-refresh pass

What

Stories 4 and 5 both point at agent-driven content refresh. Regulatory facts on this site go stale: FCDO phone numbers, embassy operating status (the Afghanistan page already notes the Kabul closure), documentation timings.

Action

Write a short spec for a quarterly pass that re-checks the dated facts against named sources and flags changes. Do not automate the edits blind; a person or Opus should confirm each factual change because it is YMYL.

Trade-off. Ongoing effort, but it protects accuracy and freshness, both of which feed the trust signals in Story 2.

Flagged separately: a content-rule conflict, not an SEO fix

Decision neededSonnet to action if approved

F13. Country hubs show prices, which conflicts with the stated no-prices rule

What

The 239 country hubs render price ranges in several places: the hero "Typical cost" stat, the answer-brief intro and stat, a cost-total banner, a full cost section, two of the FAQ answers, and the ashes cost cards. CLAUDE.md content rule 9 says: "No prices. Never state costs or price ranges, not even 'from X'. Direct to the contact form."

Why flagged, not fixed

Following the rule that says to flag uncertainty rather than guess: this may be a deliberate exception for hubs, or it may be drift from the rule. Route pages hold to the no-prices rule; the hubs do not. This needs Gareth's call before any change, because removing the cost content changes the page substantially.

Trade-off either way. Keeping prices risks a family being quoted a number that does not fit their case, and it breaks a stated site rule. Removing prices loses a genuinely useful signal that some searchers want. If removed, replace with a qualitative "what drives the cost" explanation and a route to the contact form, so the section still answers the question without stating a figure.
