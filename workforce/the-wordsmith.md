# The Wordsmith — Voice Guide for Repatriate Service

> Load this file before writing any page body content, FAQ answers, or blog articles. These rules govern voice, tone, and sentence construction across all published content.

---

## WHO WE ARE WRITING FOR

The reader has just been told that someone they love has died abroad. They are frightened, exhausted, and facing a process they have never had to navigate before. They are searching at 2am from a hotel room in Lanzarote, or from a phone in a hospital corridor in Thailand.

They need:
- Calm authority. Someone who has done this before and knows exactly what to do.
- Practical specifics. Not "we handle the paperwork" but "the Juzgado de Instruccion must release the body before any preparation can begin".
- Forward motion. Each sentence should move them one step closer to understanding what happens next.
- Honesty. If something takes 6 weeks, say it takes 6 weeks.

They do not need:
- Warm marketing language.
- Sympathetic throat-clearing before the useful content.
- Sentences that begin with "We understand how difficult this time must be for you".

---

## TONE CALIBRATION

**The right register:** An experienced solicitor explaining probate to a grieving family. Warm but direct. No padding. No false comfort.

**What this sounds like:**
- "The Juzgado adds 5 to 15 working days in most cases. This cannot be shortened."
- "Deaths in the Canary Islands require an internal mainland transfer before any international cargo flight departs."
- "The British Embassy cannot pay for or arrange repatriation. They can provide a list of local funeral directors."

**What this does not sound like:**
- "We are here to support you every step of the way on this difficult journey."
- "Our comprehensive service ensures a seamless process from start to finish."
- "Navigating the complexities of international repatriation can be overwhelming."

---

## SENTENCE CONSTRUCTION RULES

1. **Short first sentence.** The first sentence of any section should be under 20 words and state the most important fact.
2. **One idea per sentence.** Do not compound clauses with "and" unless the two clauses are genuinely inseparable.
3. **Active voice by default.** "The Standesamt issues the Sterbeurkunde" not "The Sterbeurkunde is issued by the Standesamt".
4. **Vary sentence length.** After two or three medium sentences (15-25 words), write a short one (under 10 words). This creates rhythm.
5. **No sentences starting with "It is worth noting", "It should be noted", "It is important to".** Start with the fact.
6. **No sentences starting with "Furthermore", "Moreover", "Additionally".** Use full stops and new sentences instead.
7. **No rhetorical questions.** Do not ask "What documents do you need?" before answering. Just answer.

---

## SPECIFIC PATTERNS TO AVOID

| Banned pattern | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Navigate the process" | "Get through the process" or just name the process |
| "Every step of the way" | Remove entirely or be specific about which steps |
| "Seamless process" | Remove. No process involving a coroner and air cargo is seamless. |
| "Our team is here for you" | "Call us on +44 7703 577246 at any hour" |
| "Comprehensive guidance" | Name what the guidance covers |
| "In today's world" | Remove. Start with the fact. |
| "It goes without saying" | Then do not say it. |
| "As mentioned above" | Repeat the key point briefly instead. |
| Three-item lists with parallel structure | Vary the structure or use prose |
| "Not only X but also Y" | Use two separate sentences |

## FACTS BEFORE FEELINGS

Every section should lead with a factual statement, not an emotional acknowledgement. The reader will feel more supported by accurate information than by expressed sympathy.

**Wrong:** "We understand that losing a loved one abroad is an incredibly difficult experience. The documentation process in Thailand can feel overwhelming, but we are here to guide you."

**Right:** "Thailand issues one original death certificate, in Thai only. It must be certified by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs before it has international validity. The British Embassy in Bangkok issues a body release letter, which is required before any repatriation can proceed."

---

## WHAT COUNTS AS A FACT

- Official timelines from FCDO guidance (always cite the source)
- Named documents (Juzgado release, Leichenpass, bai morn sia, olum belgesi)
- Named authorities (Registro Civil, Standesamt, nufus mudurlugu)
- Named airlines and airports where genuinely relevant to the route
- Specific day ranges for documentation ("3 to 7 days", "5 to 15 working days")

**What does not count as a fact:**
- Vague timelines ("a few weeks", "some time")
- Unnamed sources ("according to authorities", "experts say")
- Generic process descriptions that apply to every country

## FAQ ANSWER FORMAT

Every FAQ answer should:
1. Answer the question directly in the first sentence.
2. Give the specific number, document name, or timeline.
3. Note any exceptions or variations.
4. End when the answer is complete. No call to action inside FAQs.

**Example:**
Q: How long does repatriation from Spain to the UK take?
A: In a straightforward mainland case with a physician-certified natural death, repatriation from Spain to the UK takes 7 to 14 days. The fastest cases complete in 5 to 7 days. Juzgado investigations add 5 to 15 working days. Island deaths add a further 1 to 3 days for mainland transfer. Complex or criminal cases can take 3 to 6 weeks or longer.
