Cremation does not take place in Libya. Libya is a predominantly Sunni Muslim country, and cremation is not part of local burial tradition. There are no cremation facilities in the country. All repatriations from Libya involve body transfer, not ashes.

If a British national has died in Libya and you are looking for information on bringing them home, please read our [repatriation from Libya](/repatriation-from-libya/) guide, which covers the full process including the extreme complexity involved in conflict-zone repatriations.

FCDO advises against all travel to Libya. There is no resident British Embassy. All Libya cases require specialist coordination. Call us immediately for guidance.
