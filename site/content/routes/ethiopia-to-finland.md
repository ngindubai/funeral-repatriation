overview_body: "Repatriation from Ethiopia begins at the local level: contacting the emergency services on 911, then registering the death with the Kebele administration (local government) and obtaining a certified local death certificate, which usually takes 7-14 days. A death that is violent, suspicious or unexplained falls under the jurisdiction of the Police and Prosecutor, which may require a post-mortem first. With the paperwork complete, the body is embalmed, sealed in a zinc-lined coffin, and cleared for export before being flown to Finland as air cargo. A funeral director in Finland then takes custody. The full process most often takes 2-4 weeks."
