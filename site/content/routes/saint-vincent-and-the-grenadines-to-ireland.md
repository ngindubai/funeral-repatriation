    answer: "Deaths on any of the Grenadine islands require internal transfer to Saint Vincent before formal registration and international cargo can begin. The specific transfer method depends on the island: Bequia has a ferry service to Kingstown; Mustique has its own airstrip; more remote islands may require coordination of boat transport. Your repatriation coordinator needs to know the specific island immediately."
  - question: "Does the Irish Embassy in Barbados help with this route?"
    answer: "Yes. The High Commission in Bridgetown covers Saint Vincent and the Grenadines and can register the death with Irish authorities, advise on local funeral directors, and provide documentation guidance.   - question: "What documents are required for repatriation from Saint Vincent and the Grenadines?"
    answer: "The core documents are: the Registry of Civil Status death certificate, post-mortem report where required, embalming certificate, freedom from infection certificate, and export permit. English is the official language, so translation is not required for Ireland purposes."
  - question: "Can we bring ashes home from Saint Vincent and the Grenadines instead of the full body?"
    answer: "Yes. Cremation is available in Saint Vincent. Bringing ashes home requires the death certificate and cremation certificate, along with urn transport documentation. This is generally a less complex option. Your repatriation specialist can advise on the specific requirements."
  - question: "Which airport is used for cargo from Saint Vincent to Ireland?"
    answer: "Argyle International Airport on Saint Vincent is the main departure point. Cargo connections to Ireland route via Grantley Adams International Airport in Barbados or V.C. Bird International Airport in Antigua. Your repatriation specialist manages all cargo arrangements."
  - question: "What happens when the body arrives in Ireland?"
