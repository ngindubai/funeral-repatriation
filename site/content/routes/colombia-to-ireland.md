  - question: "What is the RNEC and how does it issue the death certificate?"
    answer: "The Registro Nacional del Estado Civil (RNEC) is Colombia's civil registry authority. After a doctor certifies the death, the next of kin or funeral director registers it with the local RNEC office. The official death certificate (acta de defunción) is issued in Spanish and requires certified English translation for use in Ireland."
  - question: "What is the Instituto Nacional de Medicina Legal and when does it get involved?"
    answer: "The Instituto Nacional de Medicina Legal y Ciencias Forenses is Colombia's national forensic science institute. It is involved in deaths requiring forensic investigation, including sudden, violent, or suspicious deaths. If the Fiscalía orders a post-mortem, the Instituto Nacional carries it out. Their involvement adds time to the repatriation process."
  - question: "Can I bring ashes home from Colombia to Ireland instead of repatriating the body?"

  - question: "What happens when the body arrives in Ireland?"
