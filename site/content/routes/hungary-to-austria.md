overview_body: "When someone dies in Hungary, the death must be reported to the local emergency services on 112 and registered with the Anyakönyvi hivatal (Civil Registry Office). Obtaining the local death certificate typically takes 3-7 days, and it is this document that lets the rest of the process begin. If the death is treated as violent, suspicious or unexplained, the Prosecutor's Office takes jurisdiction, and a post-mortem may be needed before release. After the documentation is in order, the body is embalmed and placed in a zinc-lined coffin for international transport, then sent to Austria by air cargo. From death to arrival in Austria, most cases take 1-2 weeks."
