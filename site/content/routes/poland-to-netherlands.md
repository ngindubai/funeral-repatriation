overview_heading: "What happens after a death in Poland"
overview_body: "When someone dies in Poland, the death must be reported to the local emergency services on 112 and registered with the Urząd Stanu Cywilnego (Civil Registry Office) within 3 days. Obtaining the local death certificate typically takes 3-7 days, and it is this document that lets the rest of the process begin. If the death is treated as violent, suspicious or unexplained, the Prokuratura (Prosecutor's Office) takes jurisdiction, and a post-mortem may be needed before release. After the documentation is in order, the body is embalmed and placed in a zinc-lined coffin for international transport, then sent to Netherlands by air cargo. From death to arrival in Netherlands, most cases take 1-2 weeks."
