Bangladesh has one of the largest UK diaspora populations of any country we handle cases for, and that history shows up directly in how manageable a case here tends to be, cost included.

## Why local experience genuinely lowers the risk of extra cost

The Dhaka to UK repatriation route is one of the most frequently used in South Asia, and local funeral directors reflect that. Their familiarity with UK documentation requirements means fewer avoidable errors, and every avoidable error, a missing certification, a document sent back for correction, has a cost attached to fixing it. This depth of experience is a genuine, if indirect, cost advantage that newer or less-travelled routes simply don't have.

## What a straightforward Dhaka case involves

Deaths are registered at the local Union Parishad in rural areas or the City Corporation in urban ones, with a certified death certificate issued under the Births and Deaths Registration Act 2004. For a natural death in Dhaka with clear circumstances, this moves efficiently. For an unnatural death, Bangladesh Police investigate, and the magistrate court may order a post-mortem at Dhaka Medical College Hospital's Forensic Department, with the Directorate General of Health Services issuing the export permit once complete. As in most countries, the investigation stage is where time, and cost, accumulates.

## Distance from Dhaka, and the monsoon

Chittagong, Sylhet, and Rajshahi all have divisional hospitals, but forensic capacity between them varies, and complex cases sometimes need transfer to Dhaka Medical College Hospital regardless of where the death occurred. Sylhet deserves a specific mention: it has a particularly large population with UK family connections, so deaths there are a relatively frequent consular matter, though transfer requirements are the same as for any regional case. The monsoon season, June to October, adds a genuine seasonal variable. Sylhet Division floods easily during this period, and a rural death during heavy monsoon conditions can see transfer logistics delayed simply by road access, not by any administrative failure.

## The direct flight keeps the final leg simple

Biman Bangladesh Airlines operates a direct service from Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport to London Heathrow, which avoids the connecting-flight cost that affects many other Asian origins. Combined with the strong local expertise described above, this is why a Bangladesh case, even a complex one, tends to be more predictable in cost than its regional neighbours.
