Connecting flights add a second layer of rules. Your departure airline may allow ashes in cabin, but transit airports and onward carriers can still apply different checks.

## Core checks before travel

1. Confirm rules with every airline on the ticket.
2. Carry cremation certificate in hand luggage.
3. Use an X-ray friendly container.
4. Check transit country restrictions, not only departure and arrival rules.

## Why families get blocked

- Assuming one airline policy applies to all segments
- Using a container that cannot be screened
- Missing original cremation documents during transfer checks

A simple pre-flight checklist with each carrier avoids most same-day surprises.
