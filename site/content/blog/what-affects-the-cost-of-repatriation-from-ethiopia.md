Ask what a repatriation from Ethiopia will cost and the honest answer is: it depends almost entirely on where in the country the death happened.

## Addis Ababa versus everywhere else

Ethiopia's forensic and administrative capacity sits in the capital. A death in Addis Ababa itself can move through the Federal Police, the Ministry of Health, and the Civil Registry without an extra transfer step. A death in Lalibela's rock-hewn churches, the Simien Mountains, or the Omo Valley is a different picture entirely. The body has to reach Addis Ababa first. Charter aircraft from a regional airstrip is the fastest option and the most expensive. A scheduled Ethiopian Airlines domestic flight is slower and more complex to arrange. Road transfer is slowest of all. Whichever option applies, it's an added cost layered on top of the standard process, not a substitute for it.

## A narrow pool of qualified translators

Every document from an Ethiopian death is in Amharic, written in the Ge'ez script. That's not something a general translation agency can usually turn around quickly. A specialist Amharic-English legal translator is a small, specific pool of people, and getting one lined up early, alongside everything else, avoids it becoming the one thing still outstanding once the rest of the case is ready.

## When the Federal Police get involved

Anything other than a straightforward natural death brings in the Federal Police, and a forensic post-mortem at St. Paul's Hospital Millennium Medical College in Addis Ababa may follow. That's an additional 7 to 21 days, and it can only start once the body has already reached the capital. Regional forensic capacity elsewhere in the country is very limited, so this isn't a step that can be shortcut by choosing a different facility.

## No cremation route

Ethiopia has no cremation facilities, so there's no lower-cost alternative to bringing the body home in full. That's worth knowing early, since some families ask about it as a way to simplify the process, and here it simply isn't available.
