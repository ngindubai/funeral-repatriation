A death in Benin brings together three things that don't often appear together: French civil law documentation, non-resident UK consular support based in a different country, and, in the north, a genuine security situation. None of this needs to feel overwhelming if it's understood from the start.

## Accra, not Cotonou

There's no British diplomat living in Benin. The non-resident British High Commission in Accra, Ghana, covers Benin's consular needs, and it can be reached on +233 302 213 250. Start with the FCDO's 24-hour line, +44 (0)20 7008 5000, and let them coordinate with Accra on the specifics; it's a more direct route than trying to work out the Accra contact details alone in the middle of a difficult day.

## The French civil law framework

Death registration goes through the Tribunal de Premiere Instance in the relevant commune, producing the Acte de deces. Before remains can leave the country, the Parquet, the public prosecutor's office, has to confirm no criminal investigation is pending. Only then does the Ministere de l'Interieur issue the export permit. Each of these steps has its own document, and having the full chain, death certificate, Parquet clearance, embalming certificate, freedom from infection certificate, export permit, and UK Coroner permission where relevant, ready before booking a flight home avoids a lot of back-and-forth later.

## The north is a different picture

Cotonou and the south are moderate risk, with reasonably functional infrastructure and a well-established documentation process. Alibori and Atacora, in the north near the Burkina Faso border, are not. FCDO advises against all travel within 5km of that border, given the extension of Sahel-linked jihadist activity into northern Benin since 2022, and a death there can require military or police coordination just to arrange access.

## Cremation means going home first

There's no cremation facility anywhere in Benin. A family that wants cremation rather than burial needs the body repatriated to the UK first, with cremation happening only once UK releases are complete. It's a detail worth having early rather than discovering partway through.
