Malta is one of the more manageable places for a British family to face this. Flights to Heathrow or Manchester take three to four hours, the legal process is clear, and the British High Commission in Valletta has handled cases like this many times before. The one thing that genuinely changes a Malta case is whether the duty magistrate orders a post-mortem.

## The magistrate's decision is the hinge point

A Maltese doctor certifies the death first. Any sudden or unnatural death is then referred to the duty magistrate, who decides whether a post-mortem is required before the body can be released. Without one, a case typically completes in eight to twelve days. With one, it can extend to twenty-one days or longer. There's no way to predict the magistrate's decision in advance, so it's worth building both possibilities into your expectations from the outset.

## Public holidays matter more than you'd think

Government offices in Malta close on public holidays and at weekends, the same as anywhere, but Malta observes a relatively high number of them across the year. If a death occurs mid-week close to a Maltese national holiday, it's genuinely worth factoring that pause into your planning, since documentation simply can't move while offices are shut.

## The paperwork itself

The Certifikat tal-Mewt, the Maltese death certificate, is the core legal document, and it's available in English translation, which removes a step many other countries require. The British High Commission can certify it for UK registration purposes directly.

## Getting home

Malta Luqa International Airport has year-round direct routes to London Heathrow and Manchester, a flight time of three to four hours. Once the magistrate's decision is known and documentation is in hand, this is one of the more straightforward final legs in this service's caseload.

## Choosing who handles it

Not every local funeral director in Malta has documented international repatriation experience. The British High Commission maintains a list of recommended providers, and it's worth confirming a firm's track record with cases leaving Malta specifically, not just funerals held locally.
