Andorra sits in the Pyrenees between France and Spain, small enough to have no airport and no armed forces of its own, yet with a distinct legal and civil system built on Catalan administration. British visitors come mainly for skiing, hiking, and duty-free shopping, and questions about repatriation from Andorra tend to centre on one fact: there's no airport to fly from.

## The road transfer is the defining logistics step

Every repatriation from Andorra starts the same way: a road transfer out of the country, because there is nowhere to fly from directly. Barcelona El Prat, roughly three hours south by road, offers multiple direct daily flights to the UK and is the choice for most cases. Toulouse-Blagnac, a similar distance via the northern route, has fewer direct UK connections but remains a viable alternative depending on where the case originates and which route suits the specific logistics.

Once at either airport, the process from there matches a standard European air cargo repatriation.

## Seven parishes, one civil system

Andorra doesn't run civil registration through a single national office. Each of the seven parish Comuns handles registration for its own territory, and the death is recorded at the Comu covering the parish where it occurred. Documentation comes out in Catalan and needs certified English translation before it has any standing with UK authorities, the same requirement that applies across most of Andorra's neighbouring countries.

Where the death wasn't natural, the Cos de Policia d'Andorra, the national police force, takes over and must clear the body for release before any preparation can begin. A natural death, by contrast, moves through the Comu without that step.

## No cremation, but a workable alternative

Andorra has no cremation facilities at all. This isn't unusual for a country of its size, and it isn't a barrier: bodies needing cremation transfer first to Barcelona, or to Toulouse or Perpignan in France, where cremation takes place before the ashes travel on to the UK. It adds a stage to the process, but it's routine and well understood by funeral directors on both sides of the border.

## What this adds up to on timing

Between five and fourteen days for most cases. Andorra sits toward the straightforward end of this site's complexity scale: no armed conflict, no significant FCDO advisory, no unusual documentation regime beyond the standard Catalan-to-English translation step. The road transfer adds logistics coordination, not delay.
