Turkmenistan is a genuinely unusual case: one of the world's most closed and tightly governed states, yet with a resident British Embassy in Ashgabat providing direct consular support. That combination shapes almost everything about how a repatriation runs here.

## The governance context, and why it matters

Turkmenistan has been under single-party rule since 1991. President Serdar Berdimuhamedow took over in 2022 from his father, who governed from 1985. Foreign nationals face strict movement controls, internet access is heavily restricted, and the FCDO advises caution in any dealings with local authorities. None of this stops a repatriation case going forward, but it does mean bureaucratic timing can shift in ways that are hard to predict from outside the country. Specialist local contacts matter here more than almost anywhere else in the region.

## Who to call

The FCDO Emergency Travel Line, 020 7008 5000, available around the clock, should be your first call. The British Embassy in Ashgabat, +993 12 363 462, is resident and handles consular support directly, a real advantage compared with several neighbouring countries.

## Documentation, Soviet-style

Death registration follows the Soviet-model Civil Registry Office, known as ZAGS, which issues the death certificate. The Ministry of Internal Affairs then issues the export permit, once an embalming certificate and a freedom-from-infection certificate from the Ministry of Health are in place. Every document comes out in Turkmen, often alongside Russian, and needs certified English translation before UK authorities will process it.

## Getting a case out of the country

Ashgabat International Airport is the only international gateway. Turkish Airlines connects to Istanbul with onward UK links and is the route most repatriation cargo uses. Flydubai, via Dubai, is the alternative, and both offer reasonably reliable cargo connections once documentation clears.

## Remote and restricted areas

The border region near Afghanistan is under severe access restriction and requires special permits to enter. The Karakum Desert interior has very limited infrastructure of any kind. A death in either location means specialist ground logistics before the standard process can even begin, and genuinely can add significant time.

## Cremation

Cremation isn't widely available in Turkmenistan. Repatriation of the body to the UK is the standard route for any family who wants it.
