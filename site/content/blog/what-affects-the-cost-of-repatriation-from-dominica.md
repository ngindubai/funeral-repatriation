Not the Dominican Republic. Dominica is a different, much smaller island nation, and mixing the two up costs families real time when they're searching in a hurry.

## English throughout, which helps

Dominica is a Commonwealth nation, and its legal system runs on English common law. The Registrar General's Office issues the death certificate in English, and the Coroner's Court steps in only for non-natural deaths. There's no translation stage to build into the timeline, which is a genuine advantage over several other Caribbean corridors.

## The terrain is the real variable

Known as the Nature Isle for its rainforest, hot springs, and dramatic mountains, Dominica's interior isn't easy to move through. If the death happened away from Roseau or the main coastal settlements, getting a body to Douglas-Charles Airport on the north-east coast can mean narrow mountain roads and a genuinely slow ground transfer. This is the single biggest factor separating a straightforward Dominica case from a difficult one, more so than any document.

## No cremation option

Cremation facilities don't exist in Dominica. Whatever the family ultimately wants, full body repatriation to the UK is the only route out of the country. It's worth knowing early, so the UK-end plans are built around the right assumption from day one.

## Two islands stand between Dominica and the UK

Douglas-Charles Airport's short runway rules out larger aircraft, so every route to the UK goes via Antigua or Barbados first. Caribbean Airlines and LIAT cover the regional leg.
