Tonga is spread across more than 170 islands, and which one a death happens on decides a good deal about how the case unfolds. Most British visitors stick to Tongatapu, home to the capital Nuku'alofa, or the Vava'u group in the north. Deaths anywhere else need to reach Tongatapu before the standard process can start at all.

## Who to call, in order

First the Australian High Commission in Nuku'alofa, which handles consular emergencies for British nationals here under a standing agreement with the UK. Then the FCDO Emergency Travel Line in London, 020 7008 5000, available around the clock. Then a specialist repatriation firm with Pacific experience.

## Moving a body between islands

Only Tongatapu has full post-mortem capacity at Vaiola Hospital. From Vava'u, a charter flight of around an hour, or the ferry, which runs 12 to 20 hours, brings a body across to Tongatapu. The Ha'apai group sits in between, quieter and less visited than Vava'u, with similar transfer arrangements. The Niuas, the northernmost islands, are the hardest case: there's no regular scheduled service to Tongatapu, and arranging a charter can take days rather than hours.

## The whale season factor

Vava'u draws British tourists specifically for humpback whale swimming between July and October, and drowning incidents and boat accidents do occur in that window. Vaiola Hospital has no hyperbaric decompression facility, so a diving death requiring that treatment turns into a medical evacuation before anything resembling repatriation begins.

## Natural disaster risk

The January 2022 eruption of the Hunga Tonga-Hunga Ha'apai submarine volcano was one of the most powerful volcanic events on record, and it triggered a tsunami that knocked out communications and inter-island transport across the group for weeks. Tonga still carries meaningful seismic and volcanic risk. If a death happens during any major natural event, the FCDO Emergency Travel Line is the right first call, since local infrastructure may simply not be reachable for a period.

## Getting home

Documentation moves in English throughout: a death certificate from the Registrar General, police closure where required, a post-mortem from Vaiola Hospital, a Ministry of Health export permit, and an embalming certificate. From Fua'amotu International Airport, cargo connects via Auckland on Air New Zealand or Nadi on Fiji Airways. There's no direct UK route from Tonga.
