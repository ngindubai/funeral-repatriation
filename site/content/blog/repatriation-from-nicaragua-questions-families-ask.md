Nicaragua tends to surprise families in one specific way: the paperwork itself isn't the hard part. President Daniel Ortega has held power since 2007, and since a violent 2018 crackdown on protests, his government has kept a tight grip on civil society. The FCDO advises against non-essential travel. None of that changes how a death certificate gets issued in Managua. It changes what can happen around it.

## The administrative process works

Death registration runs through the Registro del Estado Civil de las Personas, under the Consejo Supremo Electoral, and the Ministerio del Interior issues the export permit once certification and health documentation are complete. In the capital, this is genuinely well-established. It's a process Nicaraguan officials handle routinely, and it doesn't grind to a halt just because the wider political situation is tense.

## Where the risk actually sits

The real complication is how Nicaraguan authorities treat foreign nationals more broadly. Interactions can be unpredictable in ways that have nothing to do with the paperwork itself, and the risk is higher for anyone with any perceived connection to political activity. This is exactly why a specialist firm with current, working in-country contacts matters here more than in most Central American corridors: knowing how things actually move on the ground counts for a lot.

## Getting home

Augusto C. Sandino International Airport in Managua connects via Miami on American Airlines, via Panama City Tocumen on Copa Airlines, or via San José in neighbouring Costa Rica. All three offer workable onward UK connections, and which one makes sense usually comes down to timing rather than anything else.

## Remote regions

The Caribbean Coast Autonomous Regions in the east, RACN and RACCS, have limited roads, and a death in a jungle or coastal community there needs specialist ground logistics arranged before the official process can begin. The Río Coco border area with Honduras carries its own elevated security risk and needs the same careful handling.

Cremation services are limited across Nicaragua, so repatriating the body remains the standard route home for UK nationals.
