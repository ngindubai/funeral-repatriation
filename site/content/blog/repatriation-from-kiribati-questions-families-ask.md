Some repatriation cases are complicated because of politics or paperwork. Kiribati's complexity comes almost entirely from geography. This is a nation of 33 atolls and reef islands with a land area of just 811 square kilometres, spread across roughly 3.5 million square kilometres of Pacific Ocean, straddling both the equator and the International Date Line. The capital, South Tarawa, sits in the Gilbert Islands group, and the population, around 119,000, is concentrated there.

## Three groups, three completely different cases

**Gilbert Islands.** South Tarawa and most of the population live here. Bonriki International Airport connects to Suva and Nadi via Fiji Airways, and this is by far the most straightforward starting point for a Kiribati case.

**Phoenix Islands.** One of the most isolated inhabited island groups anywhere in the world. Only Kanton Island has permanent residents, and air links back to South Tarawa are extremely infrequent. A death here can add weeks before anything else even starts moving.

**Line Islands.** Kiritimati, or Christmas Island, sits more than 3,500 kilometres east of South Tarawa. There's a United Airlines connection via Honolulu, which in practice can make routing through the USA faster than going back through the capital first.

## What has to happen

The Kiribati Registration of Births, Deaths and Marriages issues the death certificate. Non-natural deaths bring in the Kiribati Police Service for clearance. From there, embalming, a freedom-from-infection certificate, and an export permit follow the usual sequence, with consular death registration handled by the British High Commission in Suva.

## Getting home

Gilbert Islands cases route via Fiji, then on through Sydney, Singapore, or Hong Kong. Kiritimati may go via Honolulu instead. Phoenix Islands cases have to reach South Tarawa first before following the Gilbert Islands routing from there. Cremation isn't available anywhere in Kiribati, so every case means bringing the body home in full.

None of this reflects political risk. The FCDO carries no significant travel advisory against Kiribati. It's simply one of the most geographically extreme corridors this service handles.
