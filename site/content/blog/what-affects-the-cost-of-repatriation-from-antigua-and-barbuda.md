Mount St John's Medical Centre in St John's handles the forensic side of most Antigua and Barbuda cases, and it does so with a permanent consultant pathologist, which is more capacity than many islands this size can offer. That's the baseline. What actually moves the final cost up or down from there depends on a small number of specific factors.

## Which island the death happened on

Antigua and Barbuda is legally one country, but for repatriation purposes it behaves as two very different places. A death in St John's or anywhere else on Antigua can move through Mount St John's and the local registry in the ordinary way. A death on Barbuda cannot. The island has had no functioning hospital since Hurricane Irma devastated it in September 2017, and while the health centre there can offer limited first aid, anything serious requires a fifteen-minute air evacuation to Antigua. That evacuation happens before any documentation starts, and it's an added cost that a family from Antigua's mainland would simply never see.

## The consular route runs through Barbados

There is no resident British diplomatic post in Antigua and Barbuda itself. An Honorary British Consul operates from St John's for day-to-day matters, but formal accreditation sits with the British High Commission in Bridgetown, Barbados. This isn't a cost driver in the way a translation requirement would be, but it does mean an extra layer of coordination between two islands rather than one straightforward embassy relationship, and that coordination takes staff time.

## Hurricane season is a real cost variable, not a formality

June to November is hurricane season, and Antigua sits squarely in the active belt of the Atlantic. A storm doesn't need to make direct landfall to cause a problem. Grounded aircraft, a closed port, or a cargo backlog after a nearby storm can all add days to a case that would otherwise have moved quickly. If a hurricane develops while your case is in progress, contact the FCDO Emergency Travel Line without waiting to see how it plays out.

## What works in this case's favour

Set against those factors, Antigua and Barbuda actually has real cost advantages most Caribbean origins don't. British Airways and Virgin Atlantic both operate direct services between V.C. Bird International Airport and London Gatwick, so cargo goes straight through with no inter-island transit and no third-country connection. All documentation, right down to the coroner's paperwork for an unnatural death, is produced in English. Skip the translator, skip the transit hub, and a case that starts on Antigua itself is genuinely one of the more manageable Eastern Caribbean origins.
