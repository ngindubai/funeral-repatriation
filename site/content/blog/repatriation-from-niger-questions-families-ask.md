Niger's political situation changed sharply in July 2023, when the Presidential Guard removed President Mohamed Bazoum in a coup. The military junta that has governed since, the Conseil National pour la Sauvegarde de la Patrie, controls the country's institutions directly. For anyone facing a death in Niger now, that reality shapes the whole process.

## No resident Embassy, no independent civilian pathway

The British Embassy in Abuja, Nigeria, holds non-resident accreditation for Niger and provides every consular service the case will need. Call the FCDO's 24-hour line, 020 7008 5000, first. FCDO advises against all but essential travel to the entire country, so travelling out to handle things in person isn't something to plan around.

On paper, the civil registration process still runs: death certificates come from civil status offices, police clearance from the Police Nationale du Niger. In practice, the CNSP controls every civilian institution involved, and any complication that arises reflects a decision the British Embassy in Abuja has limited power to push back on.

## Who this actually affects

UK deaths in Niger are rare. When they happen, it's mostly among aid workers and NGO staff who've stayed on despite the coup, journalists, and dual UK-Nigerien nationals visiting family. Independent UK tourism, including to the historic Agadez trans-Saharan trade route, has effectively stopped since 2020.

## Where access simply isn't possible right now

FCDO advises against all travel to the Tillabéri region near the Mali and Burkina Faso borders, where Sahel armed groups operate; to Diffa, in the Lake Chad basin, where Boko Haram and ISWAP activity continues; to the area north of Tahoua city; and to anywhere within 100 kilometres of the Libyan and Algerian borders. In these areas, body recovery by ordinary civilian operators isn't currently achievable. A specialist crisis firm with genuine Sahel network contacts may be able to assess what's possible, but families should be prepared for the possibility that repatriation from an active conflict zone simply isn't achievable at present.

## Getting home

Diori Hamani International Airport in Niamey now routes primarily via Addis Ababa on Ethiopian Airlines, the most reliable current pathway, with Casablanca on Royal Air Maroc as an alternative. Air France's status remains variable since the coup and should be checked before any plan is built around it.
