Antigua is among the UK's best-connected Caribbean destinations, and that connectivity shows up directly in how repatriation works. British Airways and Virgin Atlantic both fly direct from London Gatwick, which means cargo routing without the inter-island transit that most Eastern Caribbean repatriations require.

## The flight connection that changes everything

Direct services to Gatwick mean a body can be consigned straight from V.C. Bird International Airport without first routing through a hub island, a step that adds real time and cost elsewhere in the Caribbean. Combined with English-language documentation under Antigua and Barbuda's Commonwealth legal system, this is one of the more logistically straightforward Caribbean corridors on this site: 14 to 20 days for most cases, with the fastest straightforward cases on Antigua itself completing in 12 to 18 days.

## No resident High Commission, but a clear path to one

There's no resident British diplomatic post on Antigua. An Honorary British Consul in St John's is the first point of contact, and the British High Commission in Bridgetown, Barbados, holds formal accreditation for the country. The FCDO Emergency Travel Line, +44 (0)20 7008 5000, operates 24 hours and is worth calling directly rather than waiting to reach the honorary consul first.

## Why Barbuda is a genuinely different case

Barbuda sits about 30 miles north of Antigua, and Hurricane Irma left it without a functioning hospital in September 2017. That hasn't changed since. Any serious medical emergency on Barbuda now means an air evacuation to Antigua, roughly 15 minutes by flight. By the time a death occurs, the medical urgency has usually already passed, but the evacuation step still happens first, and it adds time to the process compared with a death on Antigua itself. Families should expect this stage even where it feels, understandably, like an unnecessary delay after the fact.

## Where post-mortems and inquests happen

Mount St John's Medical Centre in St John's is the national hospital and handles forensic post-mortems, with a permanent consultant pathologist, giving it more capacity than many comparably sized Caribbean nations. Any unnatural death triggers a mandatory coroner's inquest under standard Eastern Caribbean practice.

## Seasonal risk worth planning around

Hurricane season runs June to November, and Antigua sits within the active belt. A significant storm can ground flights and close the port for days, disrupting logistics regardless of how straightforward the underlying case is. Contact the FCDO Emergency Travel Line immediately if a death occurs during an active storm system.
