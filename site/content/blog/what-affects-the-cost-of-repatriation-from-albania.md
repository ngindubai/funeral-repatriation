Albania has become a genuinely popular destination for British visitors looking for an affordable alternative to the Croatian or Greek coast, and most repatriations from here are manageable rather than exceptional. That doesn't mean every case costs the same. A handful of specific factors decide where any individual case lands.

## Natural death versus a Prosecutor's case

A natural death with documentation ready to go is the fastest and most straightforward scenario, typically 10 to 14 days. Where the Prokuroria, the Prosecutor's Office, orders a post-mortem for an unnatural or sudden death, the Institut i Mjekesise Ligjore in Tirana carries out the examination, and that adds 7 to 14 days on its own. More time on the ground generally means more cost, particularly where transport and storage are involved for that extra period.

## Location outside Tirana

Albania's road infrastructure varies considerably once you leave the capital. Forensic capacity outside Tirana is limited, so a death in a coastal resort area or an inland town typically requires transfer to Tirana before any examination can even begin. That transfer is an additional, unavoidable line item, and it's one reason a death on the Albanian Riviera or near Gjirokaster can cost more than an equivalent case in Tirana itself.

## The laissez-passer and embalming

Embalming is mandatory under the Council of Europe's Strasbourg Agreement, to which Albania is a signatory. The laissez-passer, the document permitting international transfer, is issued by the Ministry of Interior or the local prefecture and takes 3 to 7 days once everything else is in order. These are standard, necessary steps, not optional extras, and every firm operating in Albania has to build them into its costs.

## Getting home

Tirana International Nene Tereza Airport has direct flights to London Luton and other UK airports with Ryanair and Wizz Air, but frequency is lower than on major Western European routes. Lower frequency can mean a longer wait for the next available cargo slot, which occasionally affects timing more than cost directly, though the two are rarely entirely separate.

All Albanian documentation is in Albanian and needs certified translation, a standard cost across every case regardless of how straightforward the circumstances were.
