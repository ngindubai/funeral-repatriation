Moldova itself is one of the more manageable Eastern European repatriations. It has a resident British Embassy, a functioning civil registration system, and a direct flight to London Luton. Then there's Transnistria, and everything changes.

## Why Transnistria is different

Transnistria sits on Moldova's eastern border with Ukraine. It has run its own government since 1992, backed by Russia, and no country the UK recognises treats it as anything other than part of Moldova on paper. In practice, Transnistrian authorities issue their own documents and don't answer to Chisinau. A death there can't be processed through the normal Moldovan civil registration route. The British Embassy's reach effectively stops at the administrative line. If this describes your situation, ring the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 straight away rather than trying to work through standard Moldovan paperwork channels; they won't apply.

## What happens in the rest of the country

Outside Transnistria, the process is straightforward. The attending physician certifies the cause of death, and the Civil Status Agency (Agenția Servicii Publice) issues the certificate. For a natural death in a hospital or clinic, this moves fast, often within a week.

Where the death wasn't natural, the State Medical Examiner Service becomes involved. Police secure the scene first. The Medical Examiner then certifies cause of death, police confirm there's no criminal case pending, and only then can the Civil Status Agency issue its certificate and the Ministry of Health its export permit. Routine cases at this stage usually take five to ten days on top of the standard timeline.

## Getting the paperwork translated

Everything official comes out in Romanian, sometimes with Russian mixed in depending on the region. UK authorities need certified English translation of the death certificate, the medical or forensic certificate, the police clearance, and the export permit before they'll accept the file.

## The route home

Chisinau International Airport connects directly to London Luton on Wizz Air, which is about as simple as European routing gets. Where that doesn't suit the schedule, Bucharest, Vienna, and Istanbul all offer onward connections.

For a standard Moldova case, a specialist firm with Eastern European experience will handle this smoothly. For a Transnistria case, ask specifically about that experience before you commit to anyone.
