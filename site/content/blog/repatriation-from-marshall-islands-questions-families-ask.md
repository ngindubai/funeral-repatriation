The Republic of the Marshall Islands has a land area of just 181 square kilometres, smaller than the Isle of Wight, spread across an ocean territory of roughly 1.9 million square kilometres. Twenty-nine atolls and five isolated islands, one international gateway. That mismatch between land and ocean is the entire story of a Marshall Islands repatriation.

## Everything funnels through Majuro

Amata Kabua International Airport in Majuro, the capital, is the only practical international gateway. A death anywhere else, on one of the outer atolls, means boat or chartered light aircraft transfer to Majuro first. Depending on weather and available transport, that alone can take anywhere from several hours to multiple days. A specialist firm has to establish the exact location of death before giving any time estimate at all.

## The legal and consular picture

Death registration goes through the national civil registration authority, in English throughout, a legacy of the US trust territory period alongside the country's current Compact of Free Association with the United States. Non-natural deaths bring in the RMI Police Department for investigation and clearance. There's no resident British Embassy; the British Embassy in Manila, the Philippines, handles consular matters on a non-resident basis.

## Kwajalein is a different process entirely

Kwajalein Atoll houses the Ronald Reagan Ballistic Missile Defense Test Site, a major US Army installation with restricted access. A death there follows US military coordination protocols, not the standard RMI civil process, and a specialist firm needs to know from the outset if this is where a case originates.

## No cremation, and the route home

Cremation isn't available anywhere in the Marshall Islands, so full body repatriation is the only option. From Majuro, Honolulu is the main international connection with United Airlines, then onward US connections toward the UK. Guam is served by United's infrequent Island Hopper service. No route runs direct from the Pacific to the UK, and most cases take twenty-one to fifty-six days.
