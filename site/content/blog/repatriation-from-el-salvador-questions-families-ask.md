El Salvador has changed enormously over the past few years. Government security measures introduced from 2022 transformed a homicide rate that once made headlines, and tourism, surfing on the Pacific coast, coffee country, colonial Suchitoto, has grown steadily since. British deaths still happen here, though, and the repatriation process is more layered than the country's compact size might suggest.

## Two offices, one authority

There's no resident British Embassy in El Salvador. What exists instead is an Honorary Consul in San Salvador, who handles the immediate practical steps: registering the death, liaising with the family, witnessing documents. Real authority, the formal registration and repatriation sign-off that actually matters, rests with the British Embassy in Guatemala City, 350 kilometres away.

That distance has consequences. Case speed depends partly on how the Guatemala City office is managing its wider workload, and a specialist who already has a working relationship with both offices genuinely helps here.

## When the IML gets involved

Any death classed as suspicious, violent, or unexplained triggers investigation by the Instituto de Medicina Legal, working under the Fiscalia General de la Republica, the attorney general's office. The IML in San Salvador is efficient by regional standards. Even so, busy periods can push case backlogs out by 5 to 10 days, and the body simply cannot move until the forensic release certificate is issued and the Fiscalia confirms no criminal proceedings are pending.

## Why the water matters

Surf tourism is one of the country's genuine growth stories, and it brings its own risk profile. El Zonte, known to surfers as Bitcoin Beach, and Punta Roca see strong Pacific rip currents. A drowning here, even one that looks straightforwardly accidental, can still draw IML scrutiny before foul play is formally ruled out. Volcano hiking around Santa Ana and Izalco is a further, smaller source of incidents, and road accidents on the CA-1 Pan-American Highway remain the most consistent single cause of death among visitors.

## Documentation and translation

Everything is issued in Spanish: the Partida de Defuncion from the RNPN civil registry, the IML's forensic release, any FGR clearance where criminal proceedings were considered, embalming certification, and the Ministry of Health export permit. All of it needs certified English translation before UK use.

## Getting home

No direct flights exist between El Salvador and the UK. Every case routes through a US hub, most often Miami, Houston, or Dallas/Fort Worth, before the onward leg.

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first, 020 7008 5000, available at any hour, then the Honorary Consul in San Salvador.
