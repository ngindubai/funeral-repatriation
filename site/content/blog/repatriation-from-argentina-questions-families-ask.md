Argentina draws more British visitors than almost anywhere else in South America, and Buenos Aires, Patagonia, and the Mendoza wine region each bring a different kind of repatriation case. The single biggest variable isn't geography. It's whether a judge needs to get involved.

## The judicial release requirement

For a natural death with a clear medical cause, Argentina's process is manageable: registration at the Registro Civil, an Acta de Defuncion, and a route to the UK via Madrid or Sao Paulo since no direct flights exist. Add unnatural, sudden, or suspicious circumstances, though, and the Poder Judicial takes over. A judge must order the release of the body before an undertaker can even begin embalming. Buenos Aires courts tend to move at a reasonable pace. Provincial courts, especially in Patagonia, do not always share that pace.

## Why Patagonia is its own category

Fitz Roy, Torres del Paine's Argentine approach, and the wider Patagonian trekking region attract serious hikers, and serious hikers occasionally have serious accidents. A death in Santa Cruz or Tierra del Fuego means transfer to Buenos Aires before international air cargo can be arranged, and that transfer alone can take one to three days depending on where in the region it happened. Layer a provincial judicial timeline on top and the total can extend well beyond what a Buenos Aires case would take.

## The Cuerpo Medico Forense

Where a post-mortem is required, it's carried out by the Cuerpo Medico Forense, the national forensic medical corps. This body works alongside the judge handling the case rather than independently of it, which is part of why the judicial order and the forensic finding tend to move together rather than in a fixed sequence.

## Getting the British Embassy involved early

The British Embassy in Buenos Aires should be notified on the day of death. Address: Dr. Luis Agote 2412, C1425EOF Buenos Aires. The FCDO 24-hour emergency line is +44 (0)20 7008 5000, and it's worth calling this number directly rather than waiting to reach the Embassy through other channels first.
