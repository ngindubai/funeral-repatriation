Azerbaijan is unusual among its neighbours in the South Caucasus and Central Asia for one specific reason: Baku has a genuine direct cargo route to London. That single fact changes the cost picture considerably, right up until a case involves the Nagorno-Karabakh region, where it stops applying entirely.

## The direct flight advantage

Azerbaijan Airlines flies direct from Baku Heydar Aliyev International Airport to London Heathrow, and it's the only direct connection from the Caucasus or Central Asia to the UK that handles cargo at all. Every neighbouring country routes through Istanbul or Dubai instead, adding a transit leg and a second handling agent to the booking. Baku doesn't need that, and it's a real, structural cost advantage baked into the geography rather than something we can negotiate case by case.

## Documentation moves through several hands

A standard Azerbaijan case works through a defined chain: a death certificate from the State Registry of Civil Acts, police investigation closure where the death was unnatural, a post-mortem report from the Republican Centre of Forensic Medical Expertise in Baku if required, a Ministry of Health export permit, certified English translation of everything, and an embalming certificate. Azerbaijani has used a Latin script since 1991, and UK-accredited translators for it are more available than for Armenian or Georgian, so this particular cost is manageable rather than the dominant factor it can be elsewhere in the region.

## Distance from Baku still adds cost

The Greater Caucasus mountain range crosses northern Azerbaijan, and the Sheki, Guba, and Qax regions draw British visitors for nature and cultural tourism. A death in any of these areas needs evacuation and a road transfer to Baku of four to eight hours depending on conditions before the documentation chain above can even start. This is a straightforward distance cost, comparable to a regional case in most countries.

## The Nagorno-Karabakh exception

Everything above assumes a standard civilian case. The Nagorno-Karabakh region and the areas around it are different in kind, not just degree. The FCDO advises against all travel there, and a death in or near the region involves Azerbaijani military or security authorities rather than the civilian channels described above. There is no standard timeline here, and consequently no standard cost estimate we can give without knowing the specifics. Notify the British Embassy Baku immediately if this applies to your case; they have protocols that go beyond ordinary consular processing.
