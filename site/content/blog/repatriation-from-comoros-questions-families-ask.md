Ask where in Comoros the death happened before anything else. It's the single question that decides how the rest of a case unfolds.

## Three islands, one gateway

Grande Comore, Anjouan, and Moheli each run their own local administration under the federal government of the Union of the Comoros, but only Grande Comore, home to the capital Moroni, has an airport capable of handling international cargo. A death on Anjouan or Moheli means an inter-island transfer first, typically by small charter aircraft or boat, before the wider process can even begin. It's rarely more than a day or two of delay, but it's a step families aren't always told to expect.

## A faith that shapes the timeline

Comoros is almost entirely Sunni Muslim, and the pressure for a quick local burial is real and well understood in the community. That doesn't mean repatriation isn't possible. It means the window for a decisive first contact, both with local authorities and with a specialist firm, matters more here than in most corridors. Every early hour genuinely counts.

## Registering the death

Comoros combines French civil law with Islamic personal status law. A physician certifies the cause of death, and the civil registrar then issues the acte de deces, in French and Arabic, through the etat civil of whichever island the death occurred on. Where the death wasn't natural, the Gendarmerie Nationale has to attend first and issue its own clearance before the registrar can act.

## Reaching help

There is no resident British diplomatic post in Comoros. All consular support, including death registration for UK purposes, comes from the British High Commission in Port Louis, Mauritius, a well-established non-resident arrangement given the size of the country. Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line on 020 7008 5000, available 24 hours, or the High Commission directly on +230 202 9400.

## Once the paperwork is in order

The Ministry of Health issues the export permit once civil registration and any Gendarmerie clearance are complete, and a licensed funeral director handles embalming. Every French and Arabic document needs certified English translation.

From Prince Said Ibrahim International Airport in Moroni, three routes connect onward to London: Air Austral via Reunion, Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa, and Kenya Airways via Nairobi. All of them involve at least two connections, so cargo coordination needs planning well ahead.
