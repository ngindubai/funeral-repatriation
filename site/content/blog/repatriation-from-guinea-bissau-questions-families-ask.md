Guinea-Bissau's repatriation process runs on Portuguese civil law, a legacy of colonial administration, and on a UK diplomatic arrangement that puts the nearest support two countries away.

## Support from Dakar, not Bissau

No UK diplomat is resident in Bissau. The non-resident British Embassy in Dakar, Senegal, handles consular matters, on +221 33 823 7392, and FCDO advises contacting them directly for any assistance while in Guinea-Bissau. The FCDO Emergency Travel Line, 020 7008 5000, is available at any hour from anywhere.

## Portuguese civil law, step by step

Death registration goes through the Conservatoria do Registo Civil in the relevant district, issuing the certidao de obito. Export requires a permit from the Ministerio da Administracao Territorial. In Bissau itself, this runs reasonably predictably. In rural areas or border regions, reaching the nearest district registry office can mean real travel time, which the timeline has to absorb.

## What makes this a high complexity case

Guinea-Bissau has seen multiple coups and attempted coups since its 1974 independence, and government structures can shift without warning. Outside the capital, civil registries, mortuaries, and health authorities have limited capacity, so a process that takes days in Bissau can take considerably longer in a provincial area. The country is also a significant transit point for cocaine trafficking from South America into Europe, which shapes law enforcement priorities and occasionally affects border and airport operations. FCDO flags heightened vigilance near the Casamance border with Senegal and the border with Guinea to the south-east.

## Religious timing

Around 45 percent of the population is Muslim, and where the deceased is Muslim, families often feel pressure for a swift burial under Islamic timelines. Telling your repatriation coordinator this immediately lets documentation be prioritised with that in mind, rather than discovered as a constraint partway through.

## Getting home

Osvaldo Vieira International Airport in Bissau has limited international cargo frequency. Most cases route via Air Senegal to Dakar, connecting onward through Europe, or via TAP Air Portugal's direct Bissau to Lisbon service, connecting to the UK from there. Cremation isn't available in Guinea-Bissau, so any case requiring it means a full repatriation followed by cremation in the UK.
