Djibouti sits at the southern gateway to the Red Sea, small in size but strategically significant enough to host both French and American military bases. For UK repatriation purposes, it's a workable process shaped by two facts that surprise most families: no resident British Embassy, and every document produced in French and Arabic.

## Consular support, routed through Ethiopia

There is no British Embassy in Djibouti itself. All consular matters, including death registration for UK purposes, are handled remotely by the British Embassy in Addis Ababa, which holds non-resident accreditation for the country. Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first, on 020 7008 5000, available at any hour, or the Addis Ababa Embassy directly on +251 11 617 0100. Managing a case with a specialist firm that has French-speaking East African contacts makes a real difference here, since coordination happens across a border rather than within one office.

## Who dies in Djibouti, and why it matters

The pattern of British deaths in Djibouti tends to involve merchant seafarers, military personnel connected to allied forces, aid workers, and business travellers, a different profile from the tourism-driven pattern seen in most repatriation corridors. That shapes what documentation looks like from the start, since many cases begin with an employer or a ship's agent rather than a hotel or a travel companion.

## Civil registration, French colonial roots

Djibouti's death registration runs through the etat civil, a system inherited from French colonial administration. The attending physician issues the medical certificate, and the registrar produces the acte de deces in French and Arabic. For non-natural deaths, the Tribunal de Premiere Instance has to authorise release, following attendance by the Police Nationale and review by an examining magistrate, typically adding 10 to 21 days for a straightforward accident case.

## The permit, and what it costs in time

Once civil registration is complete and any Tribunal clearance secured, the Ministry of Health issues the export permit, usually within 3 to 5 working days, a comparatively quick step once everything ahead of it is settled.

## Three ways out

Djibouti-Ambouli International Airport connects onward via Ethiopian Airlines to Addis Ababa, the primary and most dependable route for UK cargo, or via Dubai with flydubai or Air Arabia, linking to Emirates or British Airways. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is a further alternative. Whichever route is used, every French and Arabic document has to carry certified English translation before it means anything on the UK end.
