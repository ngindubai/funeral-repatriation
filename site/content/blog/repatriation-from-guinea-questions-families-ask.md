Guinea sits on West Africa's Atlantic coast, bordered by six countries, and has been under military rule since September 2021. For a UK family, three facts shape the whole process: no resident British Embassy, French civil law throughout, and Air France still flying to Conakry despite the political change.

## Consular coverage from Freetown

There's no British Embassy in Guinea. All consular support for British nationals comes from the British High Commission in Freetown, Sierra Leone, on +232 22 232 961, which registers the death for UK purposes and advises on local documentation channels. Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first, 020 7008 5000, available around the clock. FCDO currently advises against all but essential travel to Guinea.

## Registration under French civil law

Guinea's civil registration runs through the etat civil. The attending physician certifies cause of death in French, and the civil registrar issues the acte de deces. Non-natural deaths bring in the Gendarmerie Nationale, who must attend and issue clearance, and where an examining magistrate's review is needed, the Tribunal de Premiere Instance as well. For a straightforward accident in Conakry, this stage runs 7 to 14 days.

## The final steps before release

Once civil registration and Gendarmerie clearance are both in place, the Ministry of Health issues the export permit, and a licensed funeral director embalms and prepares the body. Every document needs certified English translation before UK authorities can act on it.

## One region carries more risk

The N'Zerekore area, Guinea's forested southeastern region, sees periodic inter-communal violence, and access can tighten quickly after an incident. If a case falls in this area, confirming current security conditions comes before any discussion of timeline.

## Getting home from Conakry

Air France maintains scheduled service between Conakry-Gbessia International Airport and Paris CDG, still the most dependable route for UK cargo despite everything else that's changed politically. Royal Air Maroc via Casablanca, Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa, and Brussels Airlines are the backup options.
