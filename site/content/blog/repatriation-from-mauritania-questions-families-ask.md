In Mauritania, almost entirely Sunni Muslim, community expectation strongly favours burial within twenty-four hours of death. That single cultural fact matters more than almost anything else if a family in the UK wants their loved one repatriated rather than buried locally, because the window to communicate that intention through local contacts is genuinely narrow.

## Say it early, and say it clearly

If repatriation is the family's wish, that intent needs to reach local contacts, whoever is present at the death, hospital staff, hotel management, or a tour operator, immediately and unambiguously. Once that's established, a specialist firm can begin coordinating with Mauritanian authorities before the customary burial window closes.

## A legal system split between two traditions

Mauritania runs French civil law for most formal matters alongside Islamic personal status law under the Maliki school. Death registration goes through the état civil, with the attending physician certifying cause of death and the civil registrar issuing the certificate. Documentation is primarily in Arabic, with French also used in formal administrative settings, and both need certified English translation before the case reaches the UK.

## No Embassy in Nouakchott

There's no resident British Embassy in Mauritania. The British Embassy in Rabat, Morocco, provides non-resident consular coverage, registering the death for UK purposes and guiding the family through local Mauritanian administrative channels. For a non-natural death, the Police Nationale must attend and the Parquet, the public prosecutor's office, issues judicial clearance before the Ministry of Health can grant the export permit.

## The Saharan interior is out of reach

FCDO advises against all travel to Mauritania's Saharan interior, where the risk of kidnap by Al-Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb and affiliated groups is real. Body recovery from these areas isn't achievable by standard means. Cases in Nouakchott and the accessible coastal and southern areas don't carry this constraint.

## The route home

Nouakchott-Oumtounsy International Airport connects to Casablanca with Royal Air Maroc, the most reliable UK-bound cargo pathway, with Turkish Airlines via Istanbul as an alternative. Most cases in Nouakchott and accessible areas complete in fourteen to forty-two days.
