Two countries share the name Congo, sit on opposite banks of the same river, and are frequently mixed up by people who've never needed to tell them apart before. For a family arranging repatriation, getting this right from the first phone call matters.

## Not the country you might be picturing

The Republic of Congo, often written as Congo-Brazzaville to keep it distinct, is a separate nation from the Democratic Republic of Congo, whose capital is Kinshasa. They border each other along the Congo River, and their governments, legal systems, and repatriation procedures have nothing in common beyond the name. If a specialist starts talking about Kinshasa when a death happened in Brazzaville, that's actually correct: UK consular coverage for the Republic of Congo comes from the British Embassy in Kinshasa, reachable on +243 81 556 0200, as a non-resident function. The FCDO Emergency Travel Line, 020 7008 5000, should be the first call in any case.

## A working system in two cities

Brazzaville and Pointe-Noire both have functioning civil registration. Death registration goes through the Tribunal de Grande Instance in the relevant district, and the Parquet, the local prosecutor's office, must issue clearance before remains can be released. The Ministere de l'Interieur then issues the export permit. Outside these two cities, particularly in the northern Likouala and Sangha departments, documentation infrastructure is thin, and obtaining certificates can mean real travel to a provincial centre.

## Where the FCDO draws a line

The Pool department, south of Brazzaville, carries an against-all-but-essential-travel advisory, tied to a history of militia activity most recently associated with the Ninja group, with occasional incidents continuing in recent years. A death there requires specialist coordination, sometimes involving police or military liaison. The northern jungle provinces present a different challenge entirely: minimal road access, meaning light aircraft is often the only realistic way to reach a case at all, with significant lead time built in as a result.

## Two airports, one destination

Brazzaville's Maya-Maya Airport offers a direct Air France service to Paris CDG, plus Turkish Airlines via Istanbul and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa. Pointe-Noire, the country's Atlantic coast oil hub, also has a direct Air France route to Paris. Which airport a case uses depends on where the death occurred, though Brazzaville generally serves as the primary logistics hub.

Cremation isn't available anywhere in the country, so any family wanting cremation rather than burial needs to plan for full repatriation to the UK from the outset.
