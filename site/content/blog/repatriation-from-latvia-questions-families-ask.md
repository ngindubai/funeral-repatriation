Not every case is a difficult one, and Latvia is a good example of that. It's a Baltic Sea nation, an EU, NATO, and Schengen member bordering Estonia, Lithuania, Russia, and Belarus, with Riga as its capital and commercial centre. A growing number of British nationals live and work there, and tourism to Riga's Old Town is well established. For a family facing a death abroad, Latvia is genuinely one of the more manageable places for it to happen.

## Why this one moves faster

A resident British Embassy in Riga means direct consular support from the start, no waiting on a non-resident office elsewhere. Death registration goes through the PMLP, the Office of Citizenship and Migration Affairs, which issues the Mirsanas apliecība, the death certificate, in Latvian. That certificate needs certified translation into English before it means anything to a UK Coroner, and your repatriation firm handles that through a sworn translator.

Where the death wasn't natural, the State Police investigate, and if criminal proceedings look possible, the Prokuratūra has to give clearance too. That's usually the only step that pushes the timeline out beyond the shorter end of the range.

## The flight home

Riga International Airport has frequent direct flights to London Gatwick, Stansted, and Luton, operated by Ryanair, Wizz Air, and airBaltic, a flight of roughly three hours. That direct connection is a real advantage over corridors where cargo has to transit through a third country first.

## If cremation is the family's preference

Cremation is available locally, with crematoria operating in Riga and Daugavpils. Ashes come home in a sealed urn if that's the route chosen instead of a full repatriation.

## Worth knowing

The FCDO does flag awareness of the security context near Latvia's borders with Russia and Belarus, though this rarely affects a repatriation case directly. Latvia's process is still one that benefits from specialist coordination, particularly around the Prokuratūra step, even if it's a considerably shorter journey than many of the corridors this service covers.
