Gabon's repatriation process runs on French civil law, and since August 2023 it has also run under a military transition government. Neither fact should alarm a family working with the right specialist, but both shape what happens next.

## Where consular support comes from

There's no British High Commission in Gabon itself. Coverage is non-resident, from the British High Commission in Yaounde, Cameroon, on +237 222 220 796. For urgent help at any hour, call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line on 020 7008 5000.

## The political backdrop

General Brice Clotaire Oligui Nguema's Comite de Transition et de Restauration des Institutions took power on 30 August 2023, removing President Ali Bongo Ondimba after more than a decade in office. Courts and the civil registry have kept working since. What varies is speed: administrative processing at government ministries can slow during periods of political uncertainty, and no firm timeline for a return to civilian government has been confirmed.

## Legal clearance, step by step

Death registration takes place at the Tribunal de Premiere Instance in the relevant district. The Parquet du Tribunal must grant clearance before remains can leave the country. Only then does the Ministere de l'Interieur issue the export permit itself. In Libreville, this chain is well-established. Provincial cases require travel to a regional administrative centre first, which adds time neither the family nor the coordinator controls.

## Gabon's geography works against speed

Equatorial forest covers roughly 85 percent of Gabon's land mass. Outside Libreville, Port-Gentil, and Franceville, road infrastructure thins out fast. A death at a logging camp, an oil facility, or deep inside a national park can mean light aircraft or river transport before any paperwork starts. Cremation isn't available anywhere in the country, so a Gabon case always means a full-body repatriation.

## Getting the body to the UK

Air France flies direct from Libreville's Leon M'ba International Airport to Paris CDG, the most dependable route for UK-bound cargo. Royal Air Maroc via Casablanca and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa are the alternatives when Air France scheduling doesn't fit.
