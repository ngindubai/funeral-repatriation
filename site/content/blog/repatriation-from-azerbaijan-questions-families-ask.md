Baku's restored old town and its position at the meeting point of the South Caucasus and the Caspian Sea have made it a genuine draw for British business travellers and tourists. On the logistics side, Azerbaijan has one advantage its neighbours don't.

## The direct flight that actually matters

Azerbaijan Airlines flies Baku Heydar Aliyev International Airport to London Heathrow directly, and it carries cargo. Nowhere else in the Caucasus or Central Asia offers that combination; everywhere else routes through Istanbul or Dubai first. This single fact is why Azerbaijan sits toward the more manageable end of repatriations from the wider region, even though the underlying documentation requirements are just as thorough as anywhere else.

## The paper trail, step by step

Six things need to happen before remains can travel: a death certificate from the State Registry of Civil Acts, police closure of any investigation into an unnatural death, a post-mortem report from the Republican Centre of Forensic Medical Expertise in Baku, a Ministry of Health export permit, certified English translations across the board, and an embalming certificate. Azerbaijani has used the Latin alphabet since 1991, replacing Cyrillic, and that makes certified translators genuinely easier to find than for Armenian or Georgian documentation.

## Mountains add time, not complexity

British visitors are drawn to the Sheki, Guba, and Qax regions in the Greater Caucasus for nature and cultural tourism, and deaths there require a road transfer of 4 to 8 hours to Baku before anything else can start. It isn't that the process changes; it's that everything is delayed by however long that transfer takes, on top of whatever road conditions are like at the time.

## Nagorno-Karabakh is a different category entirely

FCDO travel advice is clear: don't go there. A death in or near the Nagorno-Karabakh region draws in Azerbaijani military or security authorities alongside the civilian process, and none of the standard timelines given above should be assumed to apply. Call the British Embassy Baku straight away; they run separate protocols for these cases that go beyond ordinary consular handling.
