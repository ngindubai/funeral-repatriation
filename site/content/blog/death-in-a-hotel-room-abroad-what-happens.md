Someone dies alone in a hotel room. No doctor was present. No one else was in the room. Whatever the actual cause turns out to be, most countries will not simply take a family's word for it, and neither will most hotels move fast to explain what happens next.

## Why this isn't treated like an ordinary death

A death with no witnesses and no medical attendance sits differently in almost every country's system than one that happened in a hospital bed under a doctor's care. Local police attend. A doctor, sometimes a forensic examiner, confirms there's nothing else at play before any certificate is issued. It's worth saying plainly: this is routine, not suspicion of wrongdoing. Authorities can't certify a cause they haven't confirmed, and an empty room gives them nothing to go on except the examination itself.

## The hotel's side of it

Hotel staff usually discover the death, or are told by another guest. Management will call the police, and separately, will start thinking about the room. Housekeeping wants it back in service. That's a hotel's ordinary business logic, and it can move faster than a grieving family expects.

Ask, clearly and in writing, that nothing is removed, packed, or disposed of until you or a repatriation coordinator has had the chance to review an itemised inventory. Most hotels will hold the room, or at minimum box the contents properly, once a family member or consular officer has made that request directly to the general manager.

## What the hotel's insurance does and does not cover

A hotel's own insurance protects the hotel: its building, its liability if negligence is found. It does not typically extend to a guest's medical bills or the cost of bringing them home. That cover, where it exists, is far more likely to sit with the deceased's own travel insurance policy. Locate it before assuming the hotel will pick up any part of the bill.

## First calls to make

Before travelling out, if there's time, call the FCDO on +44 (0)20 7008 5000. Consular staff can tell you which police station holds the case and which mortuary the body has been taken to, so you aren't arriving with no fixed point of contact. Once there, the hotel manager and the named police officer handling the file are the two people worth speaking to directly.
