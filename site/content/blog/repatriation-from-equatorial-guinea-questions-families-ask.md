Equatorial Guinea is the only Spanish-speaking country in sub-Saharan Africa, a fact that surprises most people the first time they hear it. Geography adds a second layer of complexity: the country is split between Bioko Island, where the capital Malabo sits, and Rio Muni, a separate mainland territory wedged between Cameroon and Gabon. Every international repatriation exits through Malabo, whatever else is true about a case.

## No resident Embassy

The United Kingdom has no Embassy in Equatorial Guinea. Consular cover comes from the British High Commission in Yaounde, Cameroon, handling the country remotely. The FCDO Emergency Travel Line, 020 7008 5000, is the right first call at any hour; the High Commission itself can be reached on +237 222 220 796.

## Island, mainland, and why it matters

Malabo, on Bioko Island, is where every international flight leaves from. A death there moves through the Registro Civil and on to the Ministerio del Interior for the export permit without an extra transfer step. A death in Rio Muni is different. The mainland has limited road infrastructure in places, and the body has to reach Malabo, by internal flight or overland where the roads allow, before the export process can even begin. This single geographic fact shapes the timeline of almost every mainland case.

## Spanish documentation, and one more thing to know

Every official document, the certificado de defuncion, the embalming certificate, the export permit itself, is issued in Spanish and needs certified English translation. Beyond the language, families should understand that Equatorial Guinea has been governed by President Teodoro Obiang Nguema Mbasogo since 1979, one of the longest-serving governments anywhere in the world. Ministry processing times can be genuinely unpredictable as a result, which is exactly why a specialist firm with current, working relationships in-country matters more here than in most corridors.

## No cremation option

There are no cremation facilities in Equatorial Guinea. Full body repatriation to the UK is the only route home, whatever a family's usual preference might be.

## Getting home

Air France operates a direct Malabo to Paris CDG service, the most reliable option for onward UK connection. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is a workable alternative for cases where the Paris routing doesn't fit.
