Solomon Islands is a nation of roughly 900 islands in the south-western Pacific, east of Papua New Guinea, and its history as a British protectorate until 1978 still shapes how manageable a case here can be. English common law applies throughout, and that alone removes one of the biggest sources of delay families run into elsewhere in the Pacific: translation.

## What actually helps

Death registration goes through the Registrar of Births, Deaths and Marriages, and everything comes out in English from the start. Non-natural deaths bring in the Royal Solomon Islands Police Force for investigation and clearance, but there's no language barrier slowing that process down either. It's a genuinely more straightforward legal environment than most of the Pacific nations this site covers.

## The geography still matters

Guadalcanal, home to the capital Honiara, is where the process actually happens. Everywhere else is a different story. The country's roughly 28,400 square kilometres of land is scattered across hundreds of islands, and provinces like Malaita, Western Province, Choiseul, Isabel, Makira-Ulawa, and Temotu depend on small airports or boats, sometimes both. A death in one of these areas means getting the body to Honiara first, which can take several days on its own before any of the formal documentation process starts.

## The document chain

A death certificate from the Registrar, police clearance where relevant, an embalming certificate from a licensed local mortuary, a freedom from infection certificate from the Ministry of Health, and an export permit. Because it's all in English, this chain tends to move at a reasonable pace once the body has reached Honiara.

## The route home

Honiara International Airport connects via Brisbane or Port Moresby on Solomon Airlines, Fiji Airways, or Air Niugini for onward UK travel. Cremation isn't an option here, so full repatriation is standard for every case.

FCDO advises increased caution in parts of Honiara linked to a history of inter-community tensions, though the situation has settled since the unrest of 2021; check current guidance before travel. The Australian High Commission in Honiara handles consular matters for British nationals in the absence of a resident UK post.
