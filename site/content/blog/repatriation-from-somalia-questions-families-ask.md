Somalia presents a genuinely different challenge to almost anywhere else on this site. Civil registration is split across three separate administrations, each with its own capacity and its own rules, and the British Embassy operates under restrictions that limit what standard consular assistance looks like. The first, most important question in any Somalia case isn't about documents. It's about location.

## Three tracks, three processes

The Federal Government of Somalia controls Mogadishu and some other urban centres; civil registration there runs through FGS civil status offices, with Somalia Police Force involvement for non-natural deaths and a Ministry of Health export permit. Turkish Airlines' Mogadishu to Istanbul service is the main route to the UK from here.

Puntland, in the northeast, operates semi-autonomously with its own civil registration process; Bosaso Airport connects onward via Addis Ababa.

Somaliland, in the northwest, has run its own police, courts, and civil registration since 1991, and is generally the more stable and functional of the three. Hargeisa Egal International Airport connects via Addis Ababa on Ethiopian Airlines, and timelines here tend to run shorter than the other two tracks.

## Where civilian repatriation isn't possible

Al-Shabaab controls large rural areas across south and central Somalia, including most of Lower Shabelle, parts of Middle and Lower Jubba, and the Bay and Bakool regions. Cases originating in these areas are not achievable by civilian means. This isn't a matter of delay; it's a hard limit, and the British Embassy is the right first point of contact for whatever specialist channels, if any, might exist.

## Pressure toward immediate local burial

Somalia is almost entirely Sunni Muslim, and local custom favours rapid burial. Families need to make clear, quickly, through the Embassy and through any local contacts, that repatriation is the intention. Waiting too long to say so can create pressure for burial that's very hard to reverse afterward.

## Who to call

FCDO advises against all travel to Somalia. Call the Emergency Travel Line, 020 7008 5000, at any hour; the Embassy in Mogadishu will confirm current consular capacity for the specific location involved. A firm with existing Embassy contacts and genuine East African experience is a real advantage in navigating which of the three tracks applies.
