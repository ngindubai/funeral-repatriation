Iran is one of the most demanding corridors this service handles, and not for one reason alone. The civil documentation process is unfamiliar to most UK families. Islamic burial custom puts the clock under pressure from the first hour. And underneath both of those sits something most people never anticipate: what British-Iranian dual nationality actually means once someone has died there.

## The distinction that changes everything

Iran does not recognise dual nationality. Full stop. If the person who died held both British and Iranian citizenship, Iranian authorities treat them solely as an Iranian national. The British Embassy cannot demand consular access as a right in that scenario, and Iranian officials may decline embassy involvement in documentation or in releasing the remains altogether.

This isn't a technicality buried in small print. It has genuinely limited what British Embassy Tehran could do for families in real cases. What matters instead is a firm with active, trusted relationships inside Iran, built before your case ever landed on their desk.

## Registration, clearance, and the paperwork behind it

The State Organisation for Civil Registration, known locally as SABTE AHVAL, handles death registration and issues the certificate in Farsi. Where the cause of death isn't natural, the Medical Legal Organisation, Pezeshki Qanuni, has to sign off first, and that step can stretch the timeline considerably if the cause is unclear or disputed. Only once the Ministry of Interior has issued an export permit can the case move forward. Every document needs certified English translation.

## Islamic burial and the pressure it creates

Iran is roughly 98% Muslim, mostly Shia, and burial within 24 hours is a strongly held custom. Scholars do permit delay where the family lives abroad, but that permission depends on the family communicating their intention clearly and immediately. There isn't room to wait a few days before deciding what to do.

## Sanctions add a layer families rarely expect

UK and international sanctions tied to Iran's nuclear programme complicate how money moves to Iranian entities. This is exactly the kind of detail an experienced firm handles as a matter of course, and exactly the kind of thing a family should never attempt through a direct payment on their own.
