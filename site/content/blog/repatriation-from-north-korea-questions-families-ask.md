This is, without question, one of the rarest and hardest situations a UK family can face. North Korea is the world's most closed state, foreign nationals can't move without state permission and assigned minders, and the UK has no embassy there at all. If this is your situation, please call the FCDO straight away on 020 7008 5000. There genuinely isn't a more useful first step.

## Why there's no UK Embassy to call

The UK closed its options for a resident presence in Pyongyang long ago, and consular support for British nationals now comes through the Swedish Embassy, which handles UK matters by prior arrangement with North Korean authorities. The British Embassy in Seoul, reachable on +82 2 3210 5500, coordinates the wider relationship from outside the country. Neither can operate the way an embassy normally would.

## Who tends to be affected

Foreign nationals in North Korea are almost always diplomatic staff, accredited journalists on supervised trips, tourists on officially sanctioned group tours, or business contacts under close state supervision. All of them travel with assigned minders at all times, which means any death becomes known to North Korean authorities immediately, and everything that follows happens under their control.

## What documentation even means here

The Ministry of People's Security oversees civil registration, and there's no independent way to verify anything issued. The Swedish Embassy facilitates whatever communication North Korean authorities permit. Export of remains requires state authorisation, and no specialist repatriation firm operates independently inside the country. This isn't a case that moves through the kind of channels a firm can normally rely on.

## If it becomes possible

Where a repatriation does go ahead, the only viable exit point is Pyongyang Sunan International Airport, with Air Koryo's route to Beijing the sole option, and onward UK connections from there. Timelines are impossible to predict with any confidence: cases that have happened before have taken anywhere from a few weeks to several months.

We won't pretend this is a process we can accelerate. What we can do is stay in contact, keep you informed of anything that changes, and make sure everything is ready to move the moment it's possible.
