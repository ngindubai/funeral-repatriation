Cremation facilities exist in Luanda, though capacity is limited and standards vary. Families requesting cremation in Angola rather than body repatriation should ensure the funeral director used is licensed and that the cremation produces ashes suitable for certified documentation.

The process requires: a Certidao de Obito (death certificate) from the civil registry, SIC forensic clearance if the death was non-natural, a cremation certificate from the licensed facility, Ministry of Health export clearance, and certified English translations of all Portuguese documents.

Ashes are then transported to the UK as cargo or accompanied baggage under IATA Packing Instruction 650.
