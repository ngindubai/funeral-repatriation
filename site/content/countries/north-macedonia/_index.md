North Macedonia is a compact, affordable Balkan country with a resident British Embassy in Skopje, functioning civil institutions, and a competent forensic medicine service. By the standards of the wider region, it is a relatively manageable repatriation destination.

The main variables are the Cyrillic script documentation, the location of death (Skopje versus Ohrid Lake resort area), and whether a forensic investigation is required.

## The consular process

The British Embassy in Skopje handles all consular registration. When a British national dies in North Macedonia, the Embassy registers the death and can issue a formal notification for UK probate and administrative purposes.

The Embassy is also the first point of contact for family members in the UK who need advice. The consular team in Skopje is experienced with repatriation cases, particularly from the Ohrid summer tourist season.

## Institute of Forensic Medicine: how it works

All non-natural deaths — accidents, suspected crimes, unexplained causes — go through the Institute of Forensic Medicine at Ss. Cyril and Methodius University in Skopje. The Institute also handles post-mortem examinations for natural deaths where the cause is unclear.

The Institute operates professionally by regional standards. Once the Ministry of Interior confirms no criminal proceedings are outstanding, the body can be released to a licensed funeral director for preparation.

Ministry of Interior clearance is required before the funeral director can proceed. In straightforward accident cases this typically takes 3 to 5 days. In more complex investigations it can take 10 to 14 days.

## Ohrid: the summer tourist factor

Ohrid Lake is one of the oldest lakes in Europe and a UNESCO World Heritage site. TUI and specialist Balkan tour operators send significant numbers of UK tourists there each summer. The resort town of Ohrid itself has developed hotels and summer activities.

Ohrid Airport (OHD) handles seasonal charter flights, some of which serve UK regional airports directly. A death during the summer season at Ohrid:
- Is registered through the nearest municipal civil registry in the Ohrid area
- May involve cargo coordination via Ohrid Airport with charter operators
- Is approximately the same cost and timeline as a Skopje case — the local infrastructure handles this seasonally

Outside summer, access to Ohrid Airport is limited and routing goes via Skopje.

## Documentation and the Cyrillic script issue

North Macedonian official documents use the Macedonian language in Cyrillic script. The UK authorities will not accept Cyrillic documents without a certified English translation. Western North Macedonia — particularly the Tetovo and Gostivar areas — also uses Albanian, and documents in those municipalities may be produced in both Macedonian and Albanian.

Core document set:

| Document | Issuer |
|---|---|
| Izvod od maticen (death certificate) | Municipal register office |
| Forensic certificate | Institute of Forensic Medicine |
| Ministry of Interior clearance | MoI (for non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming and preservation certificate | Licensed funeral director |
| Ministry of Health export licence | Ministry of Health |
| British Embassy consular registration | British Embassy Skopje |

## Mountain hiking: Mavrovo and Sar Mountain incidents

North Macedonia has several serious hiking ranges. Mavrovo National Park, Sar Mountain, and Pelister Mountain all attract trekkers. Deaths in these areas — falls, medical events, hypothermia — require mountain rescue team involvement before the repatriation process begins. Mountain Rescue North Macedonia (GSSM) operates nationally.

Remote mountain retrieval can add 1 to 3 days before the body reaches Skopje. Factor this into timeline expectations.

## What to do when someone dies in North Macedonia

1. Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: 020 7008 5000 (24 hours)
2. Contact the British Embassy Skopje: +389 2 3299 299
3. Notify the insurer immediately
4. Do not authorise body movement before Ministry of Interior clearance in non-natural deaths
5. Contact a UK repatriation specialist
