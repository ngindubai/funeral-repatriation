Japan is a rapidly growing British tourism destination. Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, and increasingly Hokkaido and the Japan Alps attract significant numbers of UK visitors each year. The British Embassy is in Tokyo, with a Consulate-General in Osaka.

Repatriation from Japan presents specific challenges that differ from European destinations. Japan has a 99.9% cremation rate (Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare, 2022). The entire Japanese funeral industry is oriented towards cremation within 24 to 72 hours of death. Full-body repatriation for international families is an unusual request, and immediate, clear instruction is essential.

If your family member had any religious or personal objection to cremation, that instruction must be communicated in writing to the hospital and the funeral director before any preparation begins. Once cremation occurs, it cannot be reversed. The British Embassy in Tokyo can assist with this communication.

All official documents in Japan are in Japanese. The death certificate, the 死亡診断書 (Shi-bou Shindan-sho), must be officially translated into English before it is valid for UK legal purposes. Translation adds time and cost to every case.

The Japanese administrative system is methodical. Police notification for sudden or unnatural deaths is automatic. A gyousei kansatsu (administrative inspection) may be ordered by the medical examiner. This is equivalent to a coroner's investigation and must run its course before the body can be released.

Contact the British Embassy Tokyo at +81 3 5211 1100 immediately. The Embassy maintains a list of funeral directors in Tokyo and Osaka with documented experience in Western repatriation. Outside these two cities, options are significantly more limited.
