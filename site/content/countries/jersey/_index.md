Jersey is the largest of the Channel Islands, a Crown dependency lying approximately 14 miles off the coast of Normandy. It is not part of the United Kingdom but owes allegiance to the British Crown. The States of Jersey govern the island, and Jersey's legal system draws on centuries of Norman customary law alongside more recent legislation.

For UK families, this means that transferring a loved one from Jersey to mainland UK is not a straightforward domestic process. Jersey legal steps must be completed before transfer can take place.

## Legal Framework

Death registration in Jersey is handled by the Jersey Registry. The Jersey Coroner has jurisdiction over non-natural deaths, including accidents and unexplained deaths. Jersey law is not English law, and documentation issued on the island reflects this distinct framework.

FCDO consular services do not apply. Families should engage a local Jersey funeral director alongside a mainland UK receiving funeral director.

## The Repatriation Route

Jersey Airport (JER) operates direct scheduled services to:

- London Heathrow (LHR), Gatwick (LGW), City (LCY), Stansted (STN) — approximately 1 hour
- Bristol (BRS), Manchester (MAN), Birmingham (BHX), Edinburgh (EDI), Exeter (EXT)

Air transfer of human remains on these routes is routine. Most specialist repatriation firms have established working relationships with Jersey funeral directors.

## Required Documents

| Document | Issuing Authority |
|---|---|
| Jersey death certificate | Jersey Registry |
| Jersey Coroner's order (non-natural deaths) | Jersey Coroner |
| Funeral director documentation | Licensed Jersey funeral director |
| UK receiving funeral director confirmation | UK mainland funeral director |

## Cremation

Jersey Crematorium is located at Mont à l'Abbé, St Helier. Families may choose cremation on the island, returning the ashes to mainland UK, rather than transferring the body. Both options are available and routinely managed.

## The Portuguese Community

Jersey has a substantial Portuguese community, predominantly from Madeira. Roman Catholic funeral traditions are well-established on the island and local funeral directors are experienced in meeting these requirements.
