## Repatriation from Rwanda

Rwanda is a Commonwealth member, admitted unusually in 2009 despite never having been a British colony. The British representative office is the High Commission in Kigali. There are no direct flights to the UK; RwandAir routes cargo via Nairobi (Kenya Airways connections) or Doha (Qatar Airways connections).

Rwanda's primary draw for British tourists is gorilla trekking in Volcanoes National Park. This is the main context in which repatriation matters arise. Deaths in the park require Rwanda Development Board involvement before the formal investigation process begins.

### Gorilla trekking and national park deaths

Deaths in Volcanoes National Park require the Rwanda Development Board (RDB), the national park authority, to be involved and to certify what occurred. This step precedes the Rwanda Investigation Bureau process and adds one to three days. The main causes of death during gorilla trekking are cardiac events from exertion, altitude, and medical incidents on the trek; Volcanoes National Park sits at elevations between 2,400 and 4,500 metres.

Deaths at Lake Kivu or in Nyungwe Forest National Park involve similar national park authority certification requirements before the RIB investigates.

### Official process

Deaths are registered through Rwanda's Civil Registration and Vital Statistics (CRVS) system. For unnatural deaths, the Rwanda Investigation Bureau (RIB) investigates. Post-mortems are conducted at the University Teaching Hospital of Kigali (CHUK). The Rwanda Biomedical Centre and Ministry of Health manage the export permit process.

### Mortuary capacity

Specialist mortuary and embalming capacity in Kigali is improving but remains more limited than in Nairobi. Confirm current local agent capacity at the time of need.
