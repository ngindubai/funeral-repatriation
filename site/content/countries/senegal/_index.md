Senegal has become one of West Africa's most visited destinations for British travellers. Dakar's vibrant arts scene, the beaches of the Petite Côte (Saly), the historic island of Goree, and ecotourism in the Casamance region all attract UK visitors. Repatriation from Senegal requires specialist experience with West Africa; it is not a route for generalist funeral directors.

## Legal framework

Senegal is not a signatory to the Strasbourg Agreement. Repatriation is governed by Senegalese domestic law and bilateral consular conventions. Documentation follows the French administrative tradition, reflecting Senegal's colonial history. All official documents are in French. Source: Senegalese Civil Status Act (Code de l'Etat Civil).

## Death registration

Deaths are registered at the local Etat Civil (Civil Registry) in the commune where the death occurred. The Acte de Deces is the official death certificate. For deaths in Dakar, registration is relatively efficient. For deaths in regional areas (Saint-Louis, Ziguinchor, Tambacounda), the process moves more slowly and physical access to civil registry offices may require travel.

## British Embassy Dakar

The British Embassy in Dakar must be notified as soon as possible. Address: 20 Rue du Docteur Guillet, Dakar. Phone: +221 33 823 7392. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 1908 516666.

## Gendarmerie and post-mortem

For violent, sudden, or suspicious deaths, the Gendarmerie or Police Nationale investigate. In Dakar, the Institut Medico-Legal (IML) conducts forensic post-mortems. Outside Dakar, forensic capacity is very limited; the body typically needs to be transferred to Dakar for examination. The Parquet (Prosecutor's Office) orders the post-mortem and authorises the body's release.

## Laissez-passer

The laissez-passer for international transfer is issued by the Ministry of Interior or regional authority. Required documents: Acte de Deces, Parquet release, embalming certificate, Certificat de Non-Contagion (freedom from contagious disease). Allow 5 to 10 days for the laissez-passer once all documents are submitted.

## Routing to the UK

Blaise Diagne International Airport (DSS), located 45 kilometres outside Dakar, handles international cargo. No direct UK flights operate from Senegal. Standard routing is via Paris Charles de Gaulle (Air France) or Casablanca (Royal Air Maroc) to London Heathrow.
