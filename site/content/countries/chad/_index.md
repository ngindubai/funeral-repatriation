Chad is a landlocked Central African country bordering Libya, Sudan, the Central African Republic, Cameroon, Nigeria, and Niger. It has been governed by the Transitional Military Council since the death of President Idriss Déby in April 2021, succeeded by his son Mahamat Idriss Déby.

There is no resident British Embassy. The British Embassy in Yaoundé, Cameroon provides non-resident coverage. French and Arabic are both official languages.

## Who to contact first

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: **020 7008 5000** (available 24 hours). The British Embassy Yaoundé, Cameroon provides non-resident consular coverage for Chad: +237 222 220 545.

FCDO advises against all travel to northern Chad, eastern border regions, and southern border areas, and all but essential travel to the rest including N'Djamena.

## British Embassy Yaoundé: non-resident coverage

All consular services for British nationals in Chad come from the British Embassy in Yaoundé. The Embassy registers the death for UK purposes and advises on current administrative conditions in N'Djamena.

## Civil registration

Chad operates French civil law. Death registration runs through the état civil (civil status office). Documentation is in French. The attending physician certifies cause of death. The civil registrar issues the acte de décès (death certificate).

For non-natural deaths, the Police Nationale Tchadienne must attend. The Tribunal correctionnel issues judicial clearance where a magistrate review is required. Under transitional military authority, these steps can be slower and less predictable than under a civilian government.

## Ministry of Public Health export permit

Once civil registration and police clearance are complete, the Ministry of Public Health issues the export permit. A licensed funeral director prepares and embalms the body. All documents require certified English translation.

## Routing from N'Djamena

Hassan Djamous N'Djamena International Airport (NDJ):
- Ethiopian Airlines to Addis Ababa (ADD) — most reliable UK cargo route
- Air France direct N'Djamena-Paris CDG — connecting London
- Royal Air Maroc to Casablanca (CMN)

Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is the most consistent option for UK-bound cargo.

## Conflict zones: not achievable

Northern Chad (Tibesti, Borkou) — FCDO against all travel. Eastern Chad (border with Sudan) — Sudan conflict spillover, significant armed group activity, large displaced population. Southern border regions (CAR border). Lake Chad region (Boko Haram/ISWAP active). Cases in all these areas are not achievable by standard civilian means.
