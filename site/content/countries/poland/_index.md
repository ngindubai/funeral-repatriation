Poland and the UK have deep community ties. Hundreds of thousands of Polish nationals live in Britain, and deaths in Poland involving UK-based families are a common repatriation request. The process is well-established on both sides. That does not mean it is without steps, but those steps are predictable.

## Legal framework

Poland is a member of the European Union and a signatory to the Council of Europe Agreement on the Transfer of Corpses (Strasbourg, 1973 — ETS No. 080). This agreement sets minimum standards for documentation, embalming, and transit requirements. Polish domestic law also applies, and the sequence of steps must be followed correctly.

## Death registration with the USC

Deaths are registered at the Urzad Stanu Cywilnego (Civil Registry Office) in the district where the death occurred. An Akt Zgonu (death certificate) is issued. This is the primary document for all subsequent steps.

For unnatural deaths, the police and the District Prosecutor's Office are notified immediately. The Prosecutor's Office must authorise the release of the body after any necessary forensic examination.

## British Embassy Warsaw

The British Embassy in Warsaw must be notified. Address: ul. Kawalerii 12, 00-468 Warsaw. Phone: +48 22 311 0000. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 1908 516666. Consular staff will issue UK documentation and advise on the Polish process.

## Prosecutor's post-mortem

When the District Prosecutor orders a post-mortem, it is conducted by the Institute of Forensic Medicine. Polish forensic medicine infrastructure is well-developed. Warsaw and the major regional cities (Krakow, Gdansk, Wroclaw) have capable institutes with typical turnaround of 5 to 10 days.

## Embalming and the laissez-passer

Embalming is mandatory under the Strasbourg Agreement. The laissez-passer is issued by the Voivode (regional governor's office). Once the Akt Zgonu, embalming certificate, freedom from contagious disease certificate, and laissez-passer are all in order, the body can be booked as air cargo.

## Flights to the UK

Poland has one of the most extensive UK flight networks in Europe. Warsaw Chopin (WAW), Krakow John Paul II (KRK), Gdansk Lech Walesa (GDN), Wroclaw Copernicus (WRO), and Katowice Pyrzowice (KTW) all have direct routes to UK airports. LOT Polish Airlines, Ryanair, Wizz Air, and British Airways all serve Polish routes.
