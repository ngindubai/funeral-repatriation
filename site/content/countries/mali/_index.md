Mali has been under military rule since the coup of May 2021, when Colonel Assimi Goïta took power. The Transition government controls all civil administration — including the Ministry of Health, civil registrars, and the Police Nationale. For UK families managing a bereavement here, this means a slow, unpredictable process with no resident British consular support.

The country is predominantly Muslim. Islamic burial tradition favours rapid local interment, so specialist involvement from the first hours is critical if repatriation is the goal.

## Who to contact first

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: **020 7008 5000** (available 24 hours). The British Embassy Dakar, Senegal provides non-resident consular coverage for Mali: +221 33 823 7392.

Do not travel to Mali. FCDO advises against all but essential travel to most of the country, and against all travel to the north.

## British Embassy Dakar: non-resident coverage

All consular services for British nationals in Mali — including death registration, next-of-kin notification, and liaison with Malian authorities — are handled remotely from the British Embassy in Dakar. This adds a coordination layer but is the standard arrangement for Mali.

## Civil registration and documentation

Mali operates French civil law. Death registration runs through the état civil (civil status office). The attending physician issues the cause of death certificate in French. The civil registrar issues the acte de décès (death certificate) in French.

For non-natural deaths — accident, violence, sudden unexplained death — the Parquet (public prosecutor's office) must issue judicial clearance before the death certificate is released. This step has no guaranteed timeline under junta administration.

## Ministry of Health export permit

Once civil registration and Parquet clearance are in hand, the Ministry of Health issues the export permit for human remains. A licensed funeral director embalms and prepares the body. All documents require certified English translation before UK authorities can act on them.

## Routing from Bamako

Bamako-Sénou International Airport (BKO):
- Ethiopian Airlines to Addis Ababa (ADD) — best UK-bound cargo option
- Royal Air Maroc to Casablanca (CMN) — connecting to London
- Air Côte d'Ivoire to Abidjan (ABJ) — secondary option

Air France suspended its Bamako service in 2023. All UK cargo now routes via third countries.

## Conflict zones: not achievable

FCDO advises against all travel to northern Mali — the regions of Kidal, Gao, and Timbuktu — where JNIM and ISWAP-affiliated armed groups control territory. Civilian body recovery from these areas is not achievable. Cases in border areas with Burkina Faso and Niger are also severely constrained.
