## Repatriation from Latvia to the UK

Latvia is a Northern European EU, NATO, and Schengen member state on the Baltic Sea, bordering Estonia, Lithuania, Russia, and Belarus. The capital is Riga, a significant commercial hub and cultural centre. Latvia joined the EU in 2004 and adopted the euro in 2014.

A growing number of UK nationals live and work in Latvia, and tourism — particularly to Riga's historic Old Town — is established.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours) or the British Embassy Riga directly at +371 6777 4700.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration in Latvia is handled through the **PMLP** (Pilsonības un migrācijas lietu pārvalde — Office of Citizenship and Migration Affairs). The official death certificate (Miršanas apliecība) is issued in Latvian. Non-natural deaths require investigation and clearance by the **State Police** and, where criminal proceedings are possible, the **Prokuratūra** (Prosecutor's Office) before the body can be released.

### Document Translation

The Latvian death certificate requires certified translation into English for the UK Coroner. Your specialist repatriation firm will arrange this through an accredited sworn translator.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate (Miršanas apliecība) | PMLP |
| Certified English translation | Sworn translator |
| State Police / Prokuratūra clearance | Latvian authorities (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed Latvian funeral director |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Health authority |
| Approved sealed coffin documentation | Funeral director |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, Riga |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

**Riga International Airport (RIX)** has frequent direct flights to London Gatwick, Stansted, and Luton operated by Ryanair, Wizz Air, and airBaltic. Flight time is approximately 3 hours.

### Cremation

Cremation is available in Latvia. Crematoria operate in Riga and Daugavpils. If cremation is chosen, ashes are returned to the UK in a sealed urn.
