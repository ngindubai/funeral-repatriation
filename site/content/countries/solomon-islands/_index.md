## Repatriation from Solomon Islands to the UK

Solomon Islands is a sovereign Pacific nation of approximately 900 islands in the south-western Pacific Ocean, east of Papua New Guinea. The capital, Honiara, is on Guadalcanal island. The islands have strong British colonial heritage — the country was a British protectorate until 1978 — and English common law applies throughout.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British High Commission does not maintain a resident presence in Solomon Islands; the **Australian High Commission in Honiara** provides consular assistance for British nationals.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is handled by the **Registrar of Births, Deaths and Marriages**. Non-natural deaths require **Royal Solomon Islands Police Force** investigation and clearance. All documentation is in English — no translation is required for UK purposes.

### Outer Island Deaths

Solomon Islands covers approximately 28,400 km² of land across hundreds of islands. The main provinces — Malaita, Western Province, Choiseul, Isabel, Makira-Ulawa, Temotu — are each served by small airports or boat only. A death on an outer island requires transfer to Honiara before international repatriation, which can take days depending on location and transport availability.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Registrar of Births, Deaths and Marriages |
| Police clearance | Royal Solomon Islands Police Force (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Relevant authority |
| Consular death registration | Australian HC Honiara (acting for UK) |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

**Honiara International Airport (HIR)** is served by Solomon Airlines, Fiji Airways, and Air Niugini. Connections to the UK go via Brisbane (BNE) or Port Moresby (POM).

### Cremation

Cremation is not available in Solomon Islands. Full body repatriation to the UK is required.

### Security Awareness

FCDO advises increased caution in parts of Honiara, particularly areas with a history of inter-community tensions. Confirm the current FCDO travel advice before and during any visit: [FCDO Solomon Islands travel advice](https://www.gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice/solomon-islands).
