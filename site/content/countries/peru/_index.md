Peru draws British travellers primarily to three destinations: Machu Picchu and the Sacred Valley around Cusco, the Amazon basin lodges, and Lima as a gateway city. Each of these locations creates different repatriation challenges, and the complexity scales directly with how far from Lima the death occurred.

## Altitude deaths: what families need to know

Altitude sickness, cardiac events triggered by exertion at high altitude, and accidents on Inca Trail treks are among the most common causes of sudden death among British visitors to Peru. Cusco (3,400 metres above sea level) and Machu Picchu (2,430 metres) are the highest-traffic areas.

Any sudden or unexplained death requires a post-mortem by the Instituto de Medicina Legal (IML). The IML office in Cusco handles most altitude death cases from the Sacred Valley area. Bodies cannot be moved across Peru's international borders until the IML examination is complete and the State Prosecutor has authorised release. Source: Peruvian Ministry of Justice (2023).

## British Embassy Lima

The British Embassy in Lima is the primary consular contact. Address: Torre Parque Mar, Piso 22, Avenida Jose Larco 1301, Miraflores, Lima 18. Phone: +51 1 617 3000. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 1908 516666.

An Honorary Consul operates in Cusco and can assist with initial steps for deaths in that region. The Embassy coordinates remotely for all deaths outside Lima.

## Transfer to Lima

Most international cargo flights depart from Jorge Chavez International Airport (LIM) in Lima. For deaths in Cusco or the Andean region, the body must be transferred to Lima after documentation is complete, either by domestic flight or ground transport. Domestic flights from Cusco to Lima run multiple times daily. This adds 2 to 5 days to the overall process after documentation is finalised.

## Routing to the UK

There are no direct flights from Peru to the UK. Iberia operates Lima to Madrid (MAD) with connections to UK airports. Some repatriations route via Miami or Bogota. Human remains travel as air freight in a zinc-lined sealed coffin. Transit requirements at each connecting airport apply.
