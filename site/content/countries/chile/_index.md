## Repatriation from Chile: What Families Need to Know

Chile is a medium-complexity South American repatriation. The Servicio Medico Legal is a well-established national forensic service and the documentation process in Santiago is reasonably efficient. But Chile has no Strasbourg Convention, no nonstop UK flights, and a geography that creates some of the most logistically demanding remote death scenarios anywhere British tourists travel.

Patagonia draws thousands of British trekkers annually. Torres del Paine, the Carretera Austral, and Atacama expeditions represent a consistent pattern of deaths at serious distance from any urban administration centre.

### The Servicio Medico Legal

The Servicio Medico Legal (SML) is Chile's national forensic medicine service, operating under the Ministry of Justice. The SML must carry out a post-mortem for all deaths that are unnatural, sudden, or unexplained. SML offices are located in Santiago, Valparaiso, Concepcion, La Serena, Antofagasta, and other regional capitals. There is no SML capacity in Patagonia.

This means that a death in Torres del Paine must first be transported to Punta Arenas for initial examination, and may then transfer to Santiago for full SML analysis. That sequence — even before export documentation begins — can take several days.

### The Documentation Chain

1. Certificado de Defuncion (death certificate) from the Registro Civil.
2. SML forensic report (for unnatural or unexplained deaths).
3. Police investigation closure.
4. Seremi de Salud export permit (Secretaria Regional Ministerial de Salud — the regional ministry of health authority).
5. Certified English translations of all Spanish documents.
6. Embalming certificate.

The Seremi de Salud is a regional body. The relevant Seremi is the one covering the region where the death was registered.

### Remote Deaths in Patagonia and the Atacama

Deaths at Torres del Paine National Park fall under the jurisdiction of Ultima Esperanza province (regional capital: Puerto Natales / Punta Arenas). CONAF (Chile's national parks service) and the Carabineros de Chile manage scene response for trekking accidents. Carabinieri hand the body to the local SML jurisdiction.

Deaths in the Atacama (San Pedro de Atacama area) fall under Antofagasta region, with SML capacity in Antofagasta city.

Both require transfer from the death scene to the regional capital before documentation can begin.

### Routing

There are no nonstop flights from Chile to the UK. All routes involve at least one connection. LATAM Airlines via Madrid (connecting with Iberia for the final London leg) is the most reliable and most commonly used cargo route for human remains. Avianca via Bogota is an alternative. All cargo pre-booking should be confirmed before giving families a departure estimate.

Santiago Arturo Merino Benitez Airport (SCL) is the only practical cargo departure point.
