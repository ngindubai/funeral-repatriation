Croatia is among the fastest-growing British tourist destinations. Dubrovnik, Split, Hvar, and the Dalmatian coast attract hundreds of thousands of UK visitors each year. The British Embassy operates from Zagreb, with an Honorary Consulate in Split.

Repatriation from Croatia uses the continental European civil law framework. A physician certifies the death. Unnatural deaths are referred to the state attorney, who decides whether a judicial investigation is required. Croatia's system is well-organised and documentation is processed efficiently in normal circumstances.

The defining geographical factor is Croatia's island network. Over a thousand islands dot the Dalmatian coast. Deaths on inhabited islands, most commonly Hvar, Korcula, Brac, and Vis, require a ferry transfer to Split or Dubrovnik before any repatriation documentation or preparation can begin. This adds time and cost.

Zagreb Airport is the primary cargo hub, but Split and Dubrovnik airports both handle human remains freight and are the preferred origin points for Dalmatian coast deaths. Direct and one-stop services to London Gatwick and other UK airports are available year-round.

Contact the British Embassy Zagreb at +385 1 600 9100. Split's Honorary Consulate can provide initial assistance for coastal cases. The Embassy maintains a list of Croatian funeral directors experienced with UK repatriation.
