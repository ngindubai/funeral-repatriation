Argentina is one of South America's most visited countries by British tourists. Buenos Aires attracts city travellers; Patagonia draws hikers and trekkers to the Fitz Roy massif and Torres del Paine border area; Mendoza attracts wine and skiing visitors. Repatriation from Argentina is classified as high complexity primarily because of the judicial release requirement for unnatural deaths and the absence of direct UK flights.

## Legal framework

Argentina is not a signatory to the Strasbourg Agreement. Repatriation is governed by Argentine federal and provincial law. The judiciary, forensic medical corps, and health authorities are all involved for unnatural deaths. Source: Argentine Civil Registry Act (Ley 26.413); Argentine Code of Criminal Procedure.

## Death registration

Deaths are registered at the Registro Civil (Civil Registry) in the jurisdiction where the death occurred. The Acta de Defuncion is the legally recognised death certificate for international purposes. For hospital deaths, registration is handled by the hospital. For unnatural deaths, the Poder Judicial (judiciary) takes jurisdiction and a judge orders the investigation.

## Judicial release and Cuerpo Medico Forense

For violent, sudden, or suspicious deaths, a judge orders a forensic post-mortem conducted by the Cuerpo Medico Forense (Forensic Medical Corps). The judge must formally order the release of the body before embalming can proceed. In Buenos Aires, judicial processes move faster than in the provinces. For deaths in Patagonia (Santa Cruz, Tierra del Fuego provinces), allow for provincial judicial timelines.

## Transfer to Buenos Aires

Deaths in Patagonia, Mendoza, or the north require transfer to Buenos Aires for international air cargo at Ministro Pistarini Airport (EZE). Domestic Aerolíneas Argentinas flights connect regional cities. For remote Patagonian locations, small aircraft or road transport to the nearest domestic airport is necessary.

## Routing to the UK

No direct flights operate from Argentina to the UK. Standard routing: Buenos Aires EZE via Madrid (Iberia) to London Heathrow. Săo Paulo connections are an alternative. Human remains travel as air cargo. Allow for cargo booking timelines on multi-leg routes.
