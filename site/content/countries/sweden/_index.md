## Repatriation from Sweden: What Families Need to Know

Sweden is a low-complexity European repatriation. The country is an EU member, a Strasbourg Convention signatory, has direct UK flights from three airports, and operates an efficient state forensic medicine authority (Rńttsmedicinalverket) with offices across the country. English is spoken at all official levels.

The Strasbourg Convention on the Transfer of Mortal Remains (1973) reduces some documentation requirements between signatory states. Both Sweden and the UK are signatories. ### Who Manages an Unnatural Death

The Swedish Police Authority (Polisen) investigates all unnatural or suspicious deaths. Sweden has no separate criminal investigation service equivalent to Kripos; the police handle everything. For unnatural deaths, Polisen issues a clearance confirming no investigation is outstanding before the body can be released.
