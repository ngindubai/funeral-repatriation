## Repatriation from Norway: What Families Need to Know

Norway is one of the simpler European repatriations. Direct UK flights operate from Oslo Gardermoen, Bergen, and Trondheim. English is spoken at all official levels. The Norwegian Police Service (Politiet) and Kripos are efficient by international standards. Documentation, while in Norwegian, follows a well-established pattern and translations are straightforward.

That said, Norway's landscape creates specific risks for British tourists that shape a significant portion of repatriation cases. Fjord hiking, off-piste skiing, glacier walking, sea kayaking, and cycling in mountainous terrain all carry fatality risk. Remote deaths require body recovery before documentation can begin, and in winter, that recovery is not always fast.

### Who Manages an Unnatural Death

Kripos (Kriminalitetsbekjempelse og politiets spesialtjenester — the National Criminal Investigation Service) leads investigations into all unnatural or suspicious deaths in Norway. The local police district coordinates with Kripos and issues the Politiattest (police clearance) once investigation is complete.

For deaths clearly natural in cause — confirmed by a physician in a hospital or clinic — the family doctor or hospital issues the Dřdsattest (death certificate) directly and police involvement is minimal.

### The Documentation Chain

Once cause of death is established:

1. The Dřdsattest (death certificate) is issued by the attending physician.
2. For unnatural deaths, the local police district issues the Politiattest confirming no investigation is outstanding.
3. If a post-mortem is required, the Rettsmedisinsk institutt (Institute of Forensic Medicine) at Oslo University Hospital carries it out. Regional cases may go to the nearest forensic medicine faculty.
4. The local police district then issues the Ledsagerdokument — the international transport permit authorising the body to leave Norway.

### Routing

Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) is the main international cargo hub. Direct flights operate to London Heathrow, Gatwick, and Manchester with SAS and British Airways. Bergen (BGO) and Trondheim (TRD) also have direct UK connections for cases originating in western or central Norway.

### Svalbard

Svalbard is a Norwegian archipelago in the Arctic Ocean. Deaths there are governed by the Svalbard Treaty and administered by the Governor of Svalbard (Sysselmannen) in Longyearbyen. The Sysselmannen's office handles initial death procedures before the case transfers to the Norwegian mainland system. British tourists visit Svalbard for Arctic wildlife, Northern Lights expeditions, and glacier trekking — all activities with genuine risk factors.

Families dealing with a Svalbard death should expect the process to take 10 to 15 days longer than an equivalent mainland case.
