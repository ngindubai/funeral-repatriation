Guernsey is a Crown dependency in the English Channel, approximately 30 miles off the Normandy coast. The Bailiwick of Guernsey encompasses the main island plus three inhabited dependencies — Alderney, Sark and Herm — and several uninhabited islets. Each has its own character and, in the case of Sark and Alderney, its own local governance.

Guernsey is not part of the United Kingdom. Its legal system is based on Norman customary law with significant English law influence. The Greffe is the civil registration office handling death registration. HM Coroner of Guernsey handles non-natural deaths.

## Legal Framework

Death registration is via the Greffe in St Peter Port. For deaths on Alderney, the registration process feeds through to Guernsey. Sark and Herm deaths also route through Guernsey administration. HM Coroner of Guernsey has jurisdiction over non-natural deaths throughout the bailiwick.

FCDO consular services do not apply. Families should engage a local Guernsey funeral director alongside a mainland UK receiving funeral director.

## The Outer Islands

**Alderney** has its own airport (ACI) with scheduled services to Guernsey (approximately 10 minutes) and Southampton. Deaths on Alderney can sometimes route directly via Southampton without transfer to Guernsey, though the Guernsey Coroner's involvement is still required.

**Sark** has no airport and no motor vehicles. Transport is by horse-drawn carriage, tractor, or bicycle. A death on Sark involves land transport to the harbour, a ferry to Guernsey (approximately 1 hour), and then standard Guernsey procedures. This adds at least a day to the timeline.

**Herm** is accessible only by ferry from Guernsey (approximately 20 minutes). Deaths on Herm require a similar sea transfer before Guernsey procedures can begin.

## The Repatriation Route

Guernsey Airport (GCI) operates direct scheduled services to:

- London Gatwick (LGW), London City (LCY) — approximately 1 hour
- Bristol (BRS), Manchester (MAN), Birmingham (BHX), Edinburgh (EDI), Southampton (SOU)

## Required Documents

| Document | Issuing Authority |
|---|---|
| Guernsey death certificate | Greffe, St Peter Port |
| HM Coroner of Guernsey order (non-natural deaths) | HM Coroner of Guernsey |
| Funeral director documentation | Licensed Guernsey funeral director |
| UK receiving funeral director confirmation | UK mainland funeral director |

## Cremation

Guernsey Crematorium is at Foulon Cemetery, St Peter Port. Cremation on the island before return of ashes to mainland UK is available and frequently chosen.
