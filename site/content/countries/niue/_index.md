## Repatriation from Niue to the UK

Niue is a self-governing state in free association with New Zealand — a single raised coral island of 261 km² in the South Pacific, approximately 2,400 km north-east of New Zealand. The population is approximately 1,600, making it one of the world's smallest self-governing states by population. The capital is Alofi.

Niue is known for its pristine diving and snorkelling, and attracts visitors primarily from New Zealand and Australia. UK nationals visit in very small numbers.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British High Commission Wellington (+64 4 924 2888) handles consular matters for Niue on a non-resident basis.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is handled by the **Niue Registrar of Births, Deaths and Marriages**. Non-natural deaths require **Niue Police** investigation and clearance. Documentation is in English throughout.

### Air Access

**Niue International Airport (IUE)** at Alofi is served exclusively by **Air New Zealand**, operating approximately twice-weekly services to Auckland. Cargo capacity on these flights is limited. This is the only international routing option — there is no alternative carrier.

Your specialist firm must confirm available cargo space on the next suitable flight before providing a timeline.

### Mortuary Infrastructure

Niue's limited population means local medical and mortuary infrastructure is minimal. Complex medical cases are routinely transferred to New Zealand rather than managed locally. A specialist firm must establish what embalming capacity is available on the island.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Niue Registrar of Births, Deaths and Marriages |
| Police clearance | Niue Police (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary (limited capacity) |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Health authority |
| Export permit | Relevant authority |
| New Zealand transit documentation | New Zealand authorities (via Auckland) |
| Consular death registration | BHC Wellington |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

All repatriations route via **Auckland International Airport (AKL)** on Air New Zealand, then connecting to UK airports. New Zealand transit documentation for human remains applies.

### Cremation

Cremation is not available in Niue. Families may choose to have cremation carried out in Auckland before onward repatriation to the UK — this adds steps and time. Discuss options with your specialist firm.
