## Repatriation from Vatican City to the UK

Vatican City — formally the Holy See — is the world's smallest internationally recognised sovereign state: 0.44 km² entirely enclosed within Rome, Italy. It is the seat of the Roman Catholic Church and the residence of the Pope. The permanent population is a few hundred; several thousand people work there.

Deaths of non-Vatican citizens within Vatican City territory are extremely rare. They do occur — primarily involving visiting pilgrims, tourists, or journalists — but each case is specialist. Most families and many funeral directors will never encounter one.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The **British Embassy to the Holy See** in Rome (+39 06 4220 2431) handles consular matters for Vatican City. This is a separate embassy from the British Embassy to Italy — contact the correct mission.

### The Legal Framework

Civil registration within Vatican City is managed by the **Governorate of Vatican City State** (Governatorato dello Stato della Città del Vaticano). Death certificates are issued in Italian. Non-natural deaths are investigated by the **Gendarmerie Corps of Vatican City State**, the Vatican's police force.

Italian transit law applies when the body leaves Vatican City territory and enters Italian jurisdiction for transport to Rome Fiumicino.

### The Church Dimension

If the deceased was clergy, a member of a religious order, or a Vatican employee, the relevant congregation or dicastery within the Holy See may have its own administrative requirements alongside the Governorate's civil processes. Your specialist firm must establish this early.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Governatorato dello Stato della Città del Vaticano |
| Certified English translation | Sworn translator |
| Gendarmerie clearance | Gendarmerie Corps of Vatican City State (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed Rome-area funeral director |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Health authority |
| Italian transit / export documentation | Italian authorities (Questura) |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy to the Holy See |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

Vatican City has no airport. **Rome Fiumicino Leonardo da Vinci International Airport (FCO)** is the departure point, approximately 30 minutes by road. Direct flights to London Heathrow, Gatwick, and Stansted are frequent.

### Cremation

Cremation is not performed within Vatican City. Given the Roman Catholic Church's historical preference for burial, discuss all options with the British Embassy and your specialist firm.
