Guyana is an English-speaking Commonwealth country on the northeast coast of South America. The British High Commission in Georgetown is resident, official documentation is in English, and the legal system follows English common law. These factors make Guyana significantly more manageable than most South American repatriation destinations from a documentation standpoint.

The challenge is geography. Two-thirds of Guyana is dense Amazonian jungle, accessible only by small aircraft or river. The country has extensive gold and diamond mining operations in the interior, an eco-tourism industry, and a growing offshore oil and gas sector. Deaths occur in all these contexts.

## The consular process

The British High Commission Georgetown is the primary consular contact. The High Commission registers the death and issues the notification needed for UK purposes. For families in the UK, the FCDO Emergency Travel Line is 020 7008 5000 (24 hours). British High Commission Georgetown: +592 226 5881.

## Death registration

Death registration goes through the General Register Office. The attending physician certifies cause of death. The General Register Office issues the Guyanese death certificate.

For non-natural deaths, a post-mortem examination at Georgetown Public Hospital is required. A Magistrate's inquest may also be convened in cases of sudden or unexplained death.

## The interior geography challenge

Georgetown and the coastal strip — where around 90% of Guyana's population lives — have functioning medical, police, and funeral services. Cases here follow a relatively predictable path.

The interior is different. Access to mining camps in the Mazaruni, Essequibo, and Potaro regions requires:
- Charter light aircraft from Ogle Airport (Georgetown) or regional airstrips
- River boat travel on the Essequibo, Demerara, or Berbice rivers
- In some cases, both

Body preservation in jungle heat is an urgent concern. Recovery must begin as quickly as possible. Specialist firms with Guyanese interior contacts can coordinate charter aircraft and local recovery teams.

Common interior death contexts:
- **Gold and diamond mining**: Industrial accidents, drownings, falls, and medical emergencies at remote camps
- **Eco-tourism**: Kaieteur Falls, Iwokrama Rainforest, Rupununi savanna — mainly cardiac events, accidents
- **Offshore oil and gas**: Floating production platforms and support vessels in Atlantic waters — maritime recovery procedures

## Georgetown: the coastal picture

In Georgetown itself, deaths typically involve road traffic accidents, medical emergencies, and occasional violent incidents. Georgetown Public Hospital has pathology services. Funeral directors in Georgetown have international repatriation experience given the large Guyanese-UK diaspora.

## Routing from Georgetown

Cheddi Jagan International Airport (GEO) at Timehri, 40km south of Georgetown, handles international flights. There are no direct UK services:

| Route | Notes |
|---|---|
| Georgetown → Miami (MIA) → London | American Airlines, Caribbean Airlines |
| Georgetown → New York JFK → London | Caribbean Airlines |
| Georgetown → Trinidad (POS) → London | Caribbean Airlines London Gatwick service |
| Georgetown → Barbados (BGI) → London | Via LIAT/Caribbean connections |

Cargo follows these passenger routes. The Miami or Trinidad connections are most commonly used for UK-bound cargo.

## Document checklist

All documents are in English — no translation required.

| Document | Issuer |
|---|---|
| Guyanese death certificate | General Register Office |
| Medical certificate of cause of death | Attending physician |
| Post-mortem report | Georgetown Public Hospital pathologist |
| Magistrate's inquest certificate | Magistrate's Court (where applicable) |
| Police report | Guyana Police Force (violent/suspicious deaths) |
| Export permit | Ministry of Public Health |
| Embalming and preparation certificate | Licensed funeral director |

## What to do first

FCDO: 020 7008 5000. British High Commission Georgetown: +592 226 5881.

For Georgetown cases, a specialist international firm with Caribbean/South American experience can manage the case. For interior jungle cases, a firm with confirmed Guyana interior contacts is needed — body recovery logistics must be the first operational step.
