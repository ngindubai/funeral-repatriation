## Repatriation from Taiwan: What Families Need to Know

Taiwan is a medium-complexity repatriation with a unique diplomatic dimension that affects every aspect of the administrative process. The documentation process is otherwise well-structured: there is a national forensic institute, a clear death registration system, and a direct flight to London. But the absence of formal diplomatic relations between the UK and Taiwan means the British Office Taipei operates differently from a standard British Embassy, and families must understand this from the outset.

EVA Air's direct London Heathrow to Taipei Taoyuan route is a genuine logistical advantage once documentation is in place.

### The British Office Taipei

The UK does not recognise Taiwan as an independent state under the One China policy. The UK maintains its formal diplomatic recognition of the People's Republic of China. In practice, the British Office Taipei (BOT) provides a full range of consular services to British nationals in Taiwan, including emergency assistance when a British national dies. But it operates through a trust arrangement, not as a formal government embassy.

When a death occurs, call the British Office Taipei emergency line immediately. Ask explicitly about their current procedures for repatriation documentation. Procedures at the BOT can differ from those at a standard embassy and may change.

### Death Registration in Taiwan

Deaths are registered with the local household registration office (huji dengji suo) — the administrative office for the area where the deceased was registered as resident, or for visitors, the area where death occurred.

The death certificate (sijwang zhengmingshu) is issued by this office and is the foundational document in the chain.

### The Institute of Forensic Medicine

For sudden, unexplained, or unnatural deaths, the Institute of Forensic Medicine (IFM) under the Ministry of Justice conducts the post-mortem. The main IFM facility is in Taipei. Post-mortem reports are issued in Traditional Chinese.

### The Documentation Chain

1. Sijwang zhengmingshu (death certificate) from the household registration office.
2. Police investigation closure (for unnatural deaths).
3. IFM post-mortem report if required.
4. Ministry of Health and Welfare export permit.
5. Certified English translations of all Traditional Chinese documents.
6. Embalming certificate.

### Translation

Traditional Chinese documentation requires certified translation. The specific translator must be familiar with Taiwanese administrative and legal Chinese, which differs in some terminology from Mainland Mandarin. Engage a translator accredited by the British Office Taipei or recognised by UK courts.

### Routing

Taipei Taoyuan International Airport (TPE) is the main international hub. EVA Air operates nonstop flights to London Heathrow. China Airlines also operates this route. Both carriers have established human remains cargo procedures. Pre-book cargo capacity before confirming dates.
