Belarus presents serious operational challenges for UK repatriation. The British Embassy in Minsk continues to operate but with significantly reduced staff following the diplomatic deterioration since 2020. Direct UK-Belarus flights were suspended in 2021 following the forced diversion of Ryanair flight FR4978. FCDO advises against all but essential travel.

The British national population in Belarus is very small — primarily business visitors, some family visitors within the Belarusian diaspora, and a limited number of aid and NGO workers. Cases are rare but do occur.

## The consular position

The British Embassy Minsk is technically open but operates at reduced capacity. The Embassy registers the death of British nationals and issues notifications for UK purposes.

For families in the UK, the FCDO Emergency Travel Line is 020 7008 5000 (24 hours). The British Embassy Minsk: +375 17 229 82 00.

## Death registration

Death registration goes through the Civil Registry Department (equivalent to ZAGS, Аддзел загсаў). The attending physician certifies cause of death. The Civil Registry issues the death certificate.

For non-natural deaths, the Investigative Committee of Belarus is involved before the death certificate can be issued.

## Investigative Committee

All violent, sudden, or unexplained deaths in Belarus are investigated by the Investigative Committee. The Committee must issue clearance confirming no prosecution is pending before the body can be released.

In straightforward accident cases this process adds 10 to 20 days. In cases with any complication — dual nationality, connection to political activity, or suspicious circumstances — the timeline becomes unpredictable.

## Ministry of Foreign Affairs attestation

All Belarusian official documents for international use require attestation by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. This is the Belarusian equivalent of an apostille (Belarus left the Hague Apostille Convention in contested circumstances — this adds a separate attestation requirement rather than the standard apostille process).

MFA attestation typically takes 10 to 15 working days. It cannot be expedited. This is consistently the single largest delay in Belarusian repatriations.

## Routing since 2021

Direct UK-Belarus flights are suspended. The standard routing:

| Route | Notes |
|---|---|
| Minsk (MSQ) → Warsaw (WAW) → London | LOT Polish Airlines — most reliable route |
| Minsk → Vilnius (VNO) → London | Via Vilnius connections |
| Minsk → Istanbul (IST) → London | Turkish Airlines via Istanbul |

Note: The Belarus-Poland and Belarus-Lithuania borders are subject to tension and crossing procedures. Specialist firms will use known cargo routes.

## Political risk for families

Belarusian authorities have detained foreign nationals in politically sensitive situations since 2020. Families travelling to Belarus to assist with a case face genuine personal risk. FCDO advises against this. All coordination should be handled remotely through specialist firms and FCDO channels.

## What to do first

FCDO: 020 7008 5000. British Embassy Minsk: +375 17 229 82 00. Do not travel to Belarus. Engage a specialist firm with confirmed Eastern European authority networks before taking any other steps.
