Queenstown Airport (ZQN) is the starting point for most repatriations originating in the southern South Island. It is a small international airport by New Zealand standards, but it handles direct international flights to Sydney and Melbourne, which are the primary connection points for remains travelling onward to the UK.

## Why the airport matters first here

For most New Zealand cities, the airport question is secondary. For Queenstown it is the first practical issue families face. ZQN has limited cargo capacity on its international routes. Remains departing from Queenstown will typically be transferred to Christchurch (CHC, approximately 4 hours by road or 45 minutes by air) or to Auckland (AKL, approximately 90 minutes by air) for the international leg. The local funeral director arranges this domestic transfer.

The choice of hub affects the total timeline. Christchurch routes via Singapore, Dubai, or occasionally Los Angeles. Auckland has more frequent international cargo departures and marginally faster booking availability.

## New Zealand Coroner's Court and Queenstown deaths

Deaths in the Queenstown-Lakes district fall under the jurisdiction of the New Zealand Coroner's Court, operating under the Coroners Act 2006. The local coroner's court area for the South Island is administered through Invercargill and Christchurch.

Queenstown has one of the highest rates of coronial referrals in New Zealand for overseas visitors. Adventure activities — bungee jumping, jet boating, white-water rafting, skydiving, paragliding, heli-skiing, and backcountry skiing — are all subject to Worksafe NZ investigation when a fatality occurs. The coroner cannot release the deceased until Worksafe has completed any required investigation.

This is not a quick process. A skiing fatality at Remarkables or Coronet Peak may involve: ski patrol recovery, police attendance, Worksafe NZ investigation, coronial referral, and pathology. Allow 4 to 8 weeks as a minimum working assumption for adventure activity deaths.

Deaths from natural causes or medical emergencies without any connection to an activity are handled more quickly. If an attending physician can certify the cause and no investigation is triggered, the Department of Internal Affairs Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) will process the death certificate in 10 to 20 working days.

Source: New Zealand Ministry of Justice, *Coroners Court*, 2024; Worksafe New Zealand, *Fatal incident reporting*, 2024.

## British consular contacts

The British High Commission in Wellington covers the South Island. For Queenstown, consular assistance is available via Wellington (44 Hill Street) or the Auckland office (151 Queen Street). For urgent assistance at any hour: FCDO 24-hour line +44 (0)20 7008 5000.

## Typical timeline from Queenstown

Natural causes, no coronial involvement: 14 to 21 days including domestic transfer to hub airport. Adventure activity death with Worksafe investigation and coronial review: 6 to 12 weeks. Complex cases requiring inquest: allow 12 to 20 weeks.

## Key local considerations

Queenstown funeral homes are familiar with international visitor deaths but should be asked specifically about their UK repatriation experience. Not all firms in a tourist town have the documentation knowledge for UK-specific requirements, including the zinc-lined coffin standard and the Freedom from Infection certificate.

Winter sports deaths are most common July to September. Summer adventure activity deaths peak December to February.
