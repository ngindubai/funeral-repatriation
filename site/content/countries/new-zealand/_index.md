New Zealand is one of the most popular destinations for British working holiday travellers on the OE (overseas experience), as well as a significant retirement and long-stay destination. The British High Commission in Wellington and a consulate in Auckland provide consular assistance to UK families.

The repatriation process in New Zealand is governed by the Coroners Act 2006. Unlike many countries, New Zealand requires the Coroner to be notified of any sudden, unexplained, or violent death. The Coroner has legal authority over the body until formally releasing it. This is the most common cause of delays for UK families.

New Zealand's English-speaking administration and familiar legal framework make the process more navigable than many destinations, but long-haul freight from Auckland to London Heathrow is a significant cost. Connections route through Singapore, Dubai, or Hong Kong. Christchurch and Queenstown cases require domestic transfer to Auckland first.

The New Zealand death certificate is issued in English. All documents are in English, which removes the translation burden that adds time and cost in non-English-speaking countries.

Travel insurance is essential. A significant proportion of young British nationals travelling on working holiday visas do so without repatriation cover. The FCDO lists New Zealand as a country where comprehensive insurance, including full repatriation, is a priority recommendation.
