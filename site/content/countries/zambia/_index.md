## Repatriation from Zambia

Zambia is a Commonwealth country and the British representative is the High Commission in Lusaka. There are no direct UK flights from Lusaka; cargo routing goes via Johannesburg (South African Airways or British Airways) or Nairobi (Kenya Airways), each adding at least one transit day.

Zambia's main tourist draw is Victoria Falls, approached from Livingstone on the Zambian side. White-water rafting, bungee jumping, and micro-light flights over the Falls are popular activities. The Copperbelt, South Luangwa National Park, and Lower Zambezi are secondary areas with UK visitor activity.

### Victoria Falls deaths (Zambian side)

Deaths at Victoria Falls or in the surrounding national park on the Zambian side require involvement from the Zambia Wildlife Authority (ZWA) alongside the Zambia Police Service. The ZWA must certify what occurred before the police investigation formally begins. Activity deaths (white-water rafting on the Zambezi rapids, bungee accidents) are the most common type.

Note: the Zimbabwean side of Victoria Falls is administered by Zimbabwe. If the death occurred on the Zimbabwean side, Zimbabwean procedures apply.

### Official process

Deaths are registered at the Registrar General's Office under the Births and Deaths Registration Act. For unnatural deaths, the Zambia Police Service investigates. Post-mortems are conducted at the University Teaching Hospital (UTH) in Lusaka. The Director of Public Prosecutions must authorise both the post-mortem and release of the body.

The Ministry of Health issues the export permit once all documentation is complete.
