Bonaire is a Caribbean island approximately 80 kilometres north of the Venezuelan coast. Since 2010, it has been a special municipality (bijzondere gemeente) of the Netherlands — one of three BES islands alongside Sint Eustatius and Saba. Dutch law applies directly to Bonaire, making it legally part of the Netherlands itself rather than a separate Caribbean country.

UK families dealing with a death on Bonaire should call the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (available 24 hours). UK consular assistance is provided by the British Embassy in The Hague, with the Honorary Consul in Willemstad, Curaçao providing closer support.

## Legal Framework

Dutch law governs Bonaire entirely. Death registration is handled by the Civil Registry (Burgerlijke Stand) on Bonaire. Non-natural deaths require involvement of the Officier van Justitie (Dutch Public Prosecutor/Coroner equivalent) before the body can be released. The Court of First Instance for Bonaire, Sint Eustatius and Saba (BES Islands) handles more complex coronial matters.

A specialist firm familiar with Dutch Caribbean legal procedures is essential. The 2010 dissolution of the Netherlands Antilles changed the legal framework; procedures that applied before that date no longer govern Bonaire cases.

## The Repatriation Route

The standard route is:

1. Flamingo Airport (BON) to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) — approximately 9 hours, KLM direct, several times weekly
2. Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) to London Heathrow (LHR) — approximately 1 hour 15 minutes via KLM, British Airways or easyJet

Alternatively, connections via Aruba (AUA, approximately 30 minutes) or Curaçao (CUR, approximately 20 minutes) provide additional routing options.

## Required Documents

| Document | Issuing Authority |
|---|---|
| Dutch death certificate | Civil Registry (Burgerlijke Stand), Bonaire |
| Officier van Justitie release | Dutch Public Prosecutor (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed embalmer |
| Dutch export permit (human remains) | Dutch authorities |
| Airworthiness documentation | KLM/cargo operator |
| UK receiving funeral director confirmation | UK funeral director |

## Cremation

There is no cremation facility on Bonaire. Families wishing for cremation must arrange transfer to Curaçao, where a crematorium is available, or proceed with the full repatriation to the Netherlands or UK and arrange cremation there.

## Community Considerations

Bonaire has a predominantly Roman Catholic and Protestant Christian community. Papiamentu is the main spoken language, alongside Dutch and English. Local funeral traditions reflect the Caribbean setting; a specialist firm with experience of Dutch Caribbean cases will understand the cultural context.
