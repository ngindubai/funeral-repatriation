Akrotiri and Dhekelia are two British Sovereign Base Areas (SBAs) on the island of Cyprus. Unlike British Overseas Territories, the SBAs are not external territories — they are sovereign UK territory retained when Cyprus gained independence in 1960. The Sovereign Base Area Administration (SBAA) governs both areas from its headquarters at Episkopi Cantonment.

The population includes British military personnel and their dependants, UK civilian contractors, and local Cypriot civilian workers and residents.

## Who to Contact First

**Military families**: The MOD casualty notification chain is the primary route. Contact the relevant unit welfare officer or the MOD casualty team. The FCDO's 020 7008 5000 emergency line is not the correct first contact for service personnel deaths.

**Civilian families**: Contact the SBAA Administration at Episkopi. The SBA Police handles non-natural deaths, and HM Coroner for the Sovereign Base Areas handles coronial inquiries. For UK consular guidance on the broader process, the FCDO line (020 7008 5000, available 24 hours) can provide orientation, but the SBAA is the primary authority.

## Legal Jurisdiction

The SBAs operate under the Sovereign Base Areas Administration Act 1960 and subsequent Orders in Council. The legal system is a hybrid framework: English law provides the foundation but with Cypriot elements. HM Coroner for the Sovereign Base Areas has jurisdiction over non-natural deaths within the SBAs. Deaths that occur outside SBA boundaries but nearby fall under Cyprus Republic jurisdiction.

## The Repatriation Route

Most civilian repatriation uses:

1. Larnaca International Airport (LCA) — approximately 45 minutes from the Western SBA (Akrotiri), approximately 30 minutes from Dhekelia. Direct scheduled services to London Heathrow, Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester and other UK airports with Ryanair, Jet2, TUI and British Airways. Flight time approximately 4.5 hours.
2. Alternatively, Paphos Airport (PFO) — approximately 30 minutes from Akrotiri.

Military repatriations may use RAF Akrotiri (AKT), a military airfield, which has direct links to RAF Brize Norton and other military bases in the UK.

## Required Documents

| Document | Issuing Authority |
|---|---|
| SBAA death certificate | SBAA Administration, Episkopi |
| SBA Coroner's order | HM Coroner for the Sovereign Base Areas (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed embalmer |
| Export permit | SBAA Administration |
| Airworthiness documentation | Airline/cargo operator |
| UK receiving funeral director confirmation | UK funeral director |

## Cremation

There is no cremation facility within the SBAs. The nearest option is in the Republic of Cyprus. Families wishing for cremation must arrange transfer to Cyprus or repatriation to the UK where cremation can take place.
