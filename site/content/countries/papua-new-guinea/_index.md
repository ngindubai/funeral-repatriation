## Repatriation from Papua New Guinea: What Families Need to Know

Papua New Guinea is one of the most challenging repatriations a UK family can face. This is not a country where amateur coordination will work. The geography is extreme, the administrative system is under-resourced, security risks are real, and some deaths become entangled in traditional community processes that operate entirely outside the formal legal system.

Engage a specialist UK repatriation company the moment you receive notification of death. Notify the British High Commission Port Moresby at the same time.

### British High Commission Port Moresby

PNG has a resident British High Commission, which is an advantage not available in Vanuatu, Samoa, or Tonga. The British High Commission Port Moresby is the first official contact for British national deaths in PNG. They also have formal accreditation to Vanuatu, Solomon Islands, and Nauru.

### Geography: The Core Challenge

PNG is the eastern half of the island of New Guinea plus over 600 smaller islands. Road connectivity between provinces is minimal: many Highlands provinces have no sealed road connection to the coast at all. Travel between Port Moresby and Mount Hagen, the main Highlands hub, requires a flight. Travel between Port Moresby and remote Highlands provinces (Enga, Hela, Southern Highlands, Gulf) requires small charter aircraft to unprepared airstrips.

Deaths in the Highlands require charter aircraft evacuation to Port Moresby or Lae (the second city, on the north coast) before documentation can begin. This phase typically takes 3 to 7 days. Bad weather — PNG experiences intense rainfall in the Highlands year-round — can delay charter flights by days.

### The Kokoda Track

The Kokoda Track in the Owen Stanley Range is a major pilgrimage destination for British trekkers, veterans, and history enthusiasts. Deaths on or near the Track — from altitude sickness, cardiac events, or injury — require helicopter or small aircraft evacuation. Medical facilities along the 96-km Track are minimal. The nearest hospital of any capacity is Port Moresby General, roughly 50 km from the southern Kokoda end.

### Urban Security

Port Moresby and Lae have high rates of violent crime. The FCDO advises against travel to informal settlements in both cities. Deaths in criminal incidents involve the Royal Papua New Guinea Constabulary, whose forensic investigation resources are limited. The British High Commission is the essential intermediary in these cases.

### Sorcery and Tribal Complexity

PNG has over 800 distinct language groups with deep customary law traditions. Deaths that are perceived within a community as involving sorcery — or that occur in the context of tribal conflict — can become subject to community processes that delay or prevent formal investigation. These are not hypothetical edge cases; they are documented and recurring in PNG. The British High Commission Port Moresby must be informed immediately in any such case.

### Documentation and Cargo Routing

English is the official language of government documentation alongside Tok Pisin. This simplifies the documentation process compared to non-English Pacific destinations. The documentation chain follows standard Commonwealth practice.

All cargo connections operate via Brisbane (Qantas) from Jacksons International Airport (POM). There are no direct UK cargo connections.
