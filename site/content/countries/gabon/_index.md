## Repatriation from Gabon to the UK

Repatriating a loved one from Gabon requires navigating French civil law documentation, coordinating with the non-resident British High Commission in Yaoundé, and planning around the logistical constraints of a country where most of the land mass is equatorial forest with limited road access.

For urgent consular assistance, contact the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (24 hours). The British High Commission Yaoundé can be reached on +237 222 220 796.

### Political Context

On 30 August 2023, the Gabonese military seized power in a coup, removing President Ali Bongo Ondimba who had governed since 2009. The **Comité de Transition et de Restauration des Institutions (CTRI)** now governs, led by General Brice Clotaire Oligui Nguema. Civil administration, including the courts and civil registry, continues to function. The CTRI has stated its intention to return to civilian government, though no firm timeline is confirmed as of May 2026. Administrative unpredictability remains elevated relative to pre-coup norms, and processing times at government ministries can fluctuate.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration in Gabon takes place at the **Tribunal de Première Instance** in the relevant district. Parquet clearance is required before remains can be exported, and the **Ministère de l'Intérieur** issues the formal export permit.

In Libreville, the process follows a relatively well-established path. In remote provinces, obtaining documentation requires travel to provincial administrative centres, adding time and cost.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Acte de décès (death certificate) | Tribunal de Première Instance |
| Parquet clearance for export | Parquet du Tribunal |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Ministère de l'Intérieur |
| Consular death registration | British High Commission, Yaoundé |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing and Transport

Libreville's Leon M'ba International Airport (LBV) is the primary gateway. Standard routing options:

- **Via Paris**: Air France operates direct Libreville to Paris CDG, the most reliable repatriation route.
- **Via Casablanca**: Royal Air Maroc serves Libreville with connections through Mohammed V Airport.
- **Via Addis Ababa**: Ethiopian Airlines serves Libreville with connections to London Heathrow.

### Geographic and Logistical Challenges

Gabon's interior is approximately 85% equatorial forest. Outside Libreville, Port-Gentil, and Franceville, road infrastructure is sparse. Deaths in logging camps, oil facilities, or remote national park areas require specialist ground logistics — often involving light aircraft or boat — before the official documentation process can begin.

Gabon is rated **high complexity** primarily because of:

- The transition government's potential impact on administrative processing
- Remote forest provinces with no road access
- Non-resident UK diplomatic coverage from Yaoundé

### Religion and Cremation

Gabon is predominantly Christian (c.75%). Cremation facilities are not available in the country. Families requiring cremation must repatriate the body to the UK in full.
