## Repatriation from Botswana: What Families Need to Know

Botswana is one of Africa's premier high-end safari destinations. British visitors come primarily for the Okavango Delta — one of the world's largest inland deltas, designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2014 — and for Chobe National Park. The British High Commission in Gaborone handles consular cases. English is the official business language and administrative documentation is in English throughout.

The central challenge is logistics: Botswana is landlocked, the Okavango Delta has no road access, and all international cargo must route via Johannesburg.

### The British High Commission

The British High Commission in Gaborone handles consular cases for British nationals across Botswana. Staff have experience with safari-destination deaths and the logistics of remote bush camp evacuations.

Contact the High Commission emergency line immediately. They will provide current guidance on documentation requirements and advise on the Ministry of Health permit process.

### Forensic Process

The Department of Forensic Pathology at Princess Marina Hospital in Gaborone is the primary forensic post-mortem facility. For deaths in the south of the country, bodies are transferred to Gaborone.

For deaths in the north — Maun (Okavango gateway), Kasane (Chobe), Francistown — hospital capacity is more limited. Maun Hospital and Kasane Hospital have basic post-mortem capability. Complex cases transfer to Gaborone.

### Okavango Delta: Remote Death Logistics

The Okavango Delta is accessed exclusively by small aircraft, mokoro (dugout canoe), or motorboat. There are no roads into the delta concessions. Deaths in private bush camps, on mobile safaris, or in the Moremi Game Reserve require:

1. Charter aircraft or helicopter evacuation to Maun Airport;
2. Post-mortem at Maun Hospital or transfer to Gaborone;
3. Documentation in Gaborone;
4. Export routing via Gaborone Airport to Johannesburg.

Evacuation from the delta takes 1 to 4 hours depending on location and aircraft availability. Then add ground transfer or further air transfer to Gaborone. The total pre-documentation delay from a remote delta death can be 24 to 96 hours.

### Documentation Chain

1. Death certificate from the Registrar General.
2. Police investigation report (for unnatural deaths).
3. Post-mortem report from PMH Gaborone if required.
4. Ministry of Health export permit.
5. Embalming certificate.

All documentation is in English.

### Routing

Sir Seretse Khama International Airport (GBE) in Gaborone is the main cargo hub. Botswana is landlocked. All international flights route via Johannesburg OR Tambo International Airport (JNB), from which British Airways and South African Airways operate London Heathrow services. Nairobi (NBO) via Kenya Airways is an alternative.

Pre-book cargo capacity at Johannesburg before confirming departure dates.
