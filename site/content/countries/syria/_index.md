## Repatriation from Syria to the UK

Syria is in a period of profound political transition following the fall of Bashar al-Assad's government in December 2024 after a rapid advance by Hayat Tahrir al-Sham (HTS) and allied factions. Civil administration is being reconstituted across the country. The UK Embassy in Damascus has been closed since 2012. Repatriation from Syria is among the most complex cases any specialist firm can face.

**Call the FCDO immediately: 020 7008 5000 (24 hours).** The British Embassy in Beirut, Lebanon (+961 1 960 800) provides what consular assistance is currently possible, subject to current capability.

### The Political Transition Context

When Assad's government fell in December 2024, it ended 53 years of Assad family rule. The transition government, led by HTS and allied factions, controls Damascus and most of western Syria. The Kurdish Syrian Democratic Forces (SDF) continue to administer north-east Syria. Former regime stronghold areas along the Alawite coastal region have different local dynamics.

This transition directly affects repatriation: civil registration offices that operated under the Assad government are being absorbed into new administrative structures. In some areas, this process is ongoing and documentation procedures are unclear. A specialist repatriation firm with current Syria contacts is the only realistic source of accurate, up-to-date guidance.

### Civil Documentation: A Variable Picture

Syria's civil registration infrastructure has been severely disrupted by over a decade of conflict and, now, political transition. The situation varies sharply by location:

- **Damascus and main urban centres**: Civil administration reconstitution most advanced. Documentation processes are developing.
- **Aleppo, Homs, Deir ez-Zor (former conflict zones)**: Infrastructure severely damaged. Documentation capacity limited.
- **North-east Syria (SDF-controlled)**: Separate administrative track. Different authority structures.
- **Remote or rural areas**: Documentation may be effectively unavailable.

### Routing and Transport

Damascus Airport (DAM) resumed limited commercial operations following the December 2024 transition. Turkish Airlines and some Middle Eastern carriers have cautiously begun resuming services. However, schedule reliability is low and cargo acceptance for human remains is not confirmed as routine.

Most current repatriation routing uses **overland transfer to Beirut, Lebanon**, then air cargo from Beirut International Airport via Middle East hubs to the UK. This route requires safe passage through Syrian territory — which cannot be guaranteed in all areas.

### Cremation

Cremation is not practised in Syria and no facilities exist. All remains must be repatriated in full.

### Realistic Expectations

Syria is the most challenging repatriation scenario in the Middle East as of May 2026. Families must understand that repatriation may not be immediately achievable and that any firm claiming guaranteed outcomes is not being honest. The FCDO will give direct guidance on what is currently possible in specific locations.
