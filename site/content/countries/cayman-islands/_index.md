## Repatriation from the Cayman Islands to the UK

The Cayman Islands is a British Overseas Territory in the western Caribbean Sea, comprising Grand Cayman, Cayman Brac, and Little Cayman. The capital, George Town, is one of the world's leading offshore financial centres. The islands are a popular destination for UK holidaymakers and divers.

As a British Overseas Territory, the Crown's interests are represented by the Governor. Consular functions for UK nationals are handled via the FCO in London. Call the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours) to register a death and obtain consular guidance.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is managed by the **General Registry** in George Town, under Cayman Islands Government. English common law applies. Non-natural deaths require investigation and clearance by the **Royal Cayman Islands Police Service (RCIPS)**.

This is a British Overseas Territory — there is no requirement for consular visit from a foreign embassy, and all documentation is in English.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | General Registry, George Town |
| RCIPS clearance | Royal Cayman Islands Police Service (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local funeral director |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Cayman Islands Health Authority |
| Export permit | Cayman Islands Government |
| UK Coroner notification | FCO / HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

**Owen Roberts International Airport (GCM)** on Grand Cayman has direct **Cayman Airways** and **British Airways** service to London Gatwick (LGW). Flight time is approximately 10 hours.

Deaths on **Cayman Brac** are handled locally via Gerrard Smith Airport (CYB), but the body must transfer to Grand Cayman for international routing.

### Cremation

Cremation is available in the Cayman Islands. Ashes may be returned to the UK in a sealed urn.
