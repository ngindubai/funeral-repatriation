Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP) is a logistics asset for repatriation: British Airways operates a direct MSP-LHR service, giving Minneapolis one of the most direct cargo routes to London of any Midwest US city. Families should confirm with the funeral director that the BA MSP-LHR cargo capacity is available at the time of the case — the route operates several times weekly and is well-established.

## What the British Consulate does — and does not do

The British Consulate General Chicago (625 N. Michigan Avenue, Suite 2200, Chicago IL 60611) covers Minnesota along with Illinois, Iowa, Wisconsin, Michigan, Indiana, and the broader upper Midwest.

**The Consulate can:** Register the death in UK consular records. Advise on Minnesota documentation for UK authorities. Provide a funeral home list.

**The Consulate cannot:** Repatriate the body. Pay any costs. Instruct Minnesota state authorities.

## What Minnesota law requires

**Under Minnesota Statutes Chapter 390 (Coroners and Medical Examiners)**, Hennepin County (which contains Minneapolis) operates a Medical Examiner system. The Hennepin County Medical Examiner (HCME, 530 Chicago Avenue, Minneapolis MN 55415) investigates sudden, violent, or unexplained deaths in Minneapolis and Hennepin County.

Minnesota death certificates are issued by the Minnesota Department of Health, Office of Vital Records, through the licensed funeral home.

## The documentation chain

**1. Minnesota Death Certificate.** Issued by Minnesota DOH via the funeral home. Certified copies required.

**2. HCME release** (where applicable). Authorises body release on case completion.

**3. Minnesota burial/transit permit.** Arranged by the licensed Minnesota funeral home.

**4. Embalming certificate.**

**5. IATA airline cargo documentation.**

Source: Minnesota Statutes Chapter 390; Hennepin County Medical Examiner, 2024; FCDO USA travel advice, 2024.

## Airport and cargo routing

Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP) has British Airways MSP-LHR direct service. The MSP cargo terminal handles human remains. For deaths in the broader Twin Cities area — Saint Paul (Ramsey County), the western suburbs (Anoka, Washington, Dakota, Scott Counties) — the county coroner or ME has jurisdiction, but MSP remains the departure point for the international cargo leg regardless.

## Timeline from Minneapolis

- Hospital-certified natural death: 7 to 14 days
- HCME investigation: 14 to 21 days
- Extended investigation: 4 to 8 weeks

## Key local considerations

Minnesota has the highest concentration of Scandinavian-American heritage in the United States, which creates a specific demographic category: deaths among British nationals visiting extended family of Norwegian, Swedish, or Danish origin. Minneapolis also has a significant Somali and East African diaspora, and deaths among British nationals of East African origin visiting relatives in Minneapolis are documented cases.

Winter in Minneapolis is severe — temperatures regularly drop below -20°C. Hypothermia-related deaths among visitors unfamiliar with extreme cold are a medical examiner category.
