New York City is the single largest origin point for UK repatriations from the United States. The British-American connection runs deep in New York: British nationals on business trips, British tourists, British-American dual nationals, and the long-established British expat community in Manhattan and the outer boroughs all contribute to a steady stream of cases. JFK International Airport is one of the most established cargo corridors to London in the world, with multiple daily direct services.

New York City has a world-class forensic infrastructure but a complex multi-agency death investigation system that families — and inexperienced funeral directors — can find difficult to navigate.

## What the British Consulate does

The British Consulate General New York (845 Third Avenue, Manhattan, NY 10022) is the primary UK consular post for New York State.

**The Consulate can:** Register the death in UK consular records. Advise on New York documentation requirements. Provide a local funeral home referral list.

**The Consulate cannot:** Repatriate the body. Pay any costs. Instruct OCME or New York state authorities.

## What New York law requires

New York City operates the **Office of Chief Medical Examiner (OCME)** — one of the largest and most sophisticated forensic pathology operations in the world. The OCME investigates all sudden, unexpected, violent, suspicious, and unattended deaths in New York City (all five boroughs). Jurisdiction is clear and well-defined: OCME investigates these categories of death; deaths in hospitals with a certified treating physician are handled by the attending.

For deaths at Bellevue, NYU Langone, Mount Sinai, Presbyterian, or other major Manhattan hospitals with a known cause, the treating physician certifies the death and the hospital issues the death certificate through the New York City Department of Health (DOHMH).

New York City death certificates are issued by the DOHMH Division of Vital Records (125 Worth Street, Manhattan). Certified copies are required.

## The documentation chain

**1. Death certificate.** Issued by NYC DOHMH Vital Records, either through the hospital (physician-certified) or through OCME (medical examiner-certified).

**2. OCME release** (where applicable). The OCME case number and release authorisation are required before the licensed New York funeral director can take custody.

**3. New York burial/transit permit.** Issued by the NYC DOHMH Vital Records office and arranged by the licensed funeral home.

**4. Embalming certificate.**

**5. IATA cargo documentation.**

Source: New York City Administrative Code Title 17; New York State Public Health Law; NYC OCME operating procedures, 2024.

## Airport and cargo routing

John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK, Queens) is the primary international cargo airport for New York City repatriations. British Airways operates JFK-LHR direct (approximately 6.5 hours) with established cargo capacity. Virgin Atlantic also operates JFK-LHR. Both carriers have experience with human remains cargo from JFK.

Newark Liberty International (EWR, New Jersey) is an alternative cargo point — United Airlines operates EWR-LHR. The licensed New York funeral director coordinates the cargo booking based on available flights and capacity.

## Timeline from New York

- Hospital-certified natural death: 5 to 10 days
- OCME investigation, clear cause established quickly: 7 to 14 days
- OCME extended investigation: 21 to 35 days
- Homicide or prolonged criminal case: indefinite

New York is one of the faster origins for UK repatriation in the United States, because of the OCME's capacity and JFK's direct UK cargo connectivity.

## Key local considerations

New York City deaths involving British nationals span a wide range: executives visiting for business; older tourists on a long-awaited trip; second-generation British-Americans visiting family; British nationals who have lived in New York for decades. For long-term residents, the administrative complexity increases significantly — New York estate law, potential US tax exposure, and New York probate proceedings may all need to be addressed in parallel with repatriation. Instruct a New York attorney as well as a UK solicitor at an early stage for long-term resident cases.
