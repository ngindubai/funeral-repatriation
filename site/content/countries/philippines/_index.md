direct_answer_heading: "How long does repatriation from the Philippines to the UK take?"
direct_answer_intro: "Repatriation from the Philippines to the UK typically takes three to six weeks. The main reason for this timeline is the two-stage death certificate authentication process: the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) must authenticate the certificate, and the Department of Foreign Affairs (DFA) must stamp it for international use. Each stage takes time and cannot be rushed."
direct_answer_points:
  - "The PSA and DFA dual authentication alone can add two to four weeks to the process before airline cargo can be booked."
  - "Deaths outside Metro Manila or Cebu require internal transport to Manila first, adding further time and cost."
direct_answer_note: "Typhoon season from June to November can disrupt both internal and international flights. Cases started during this period should allow additional time."
