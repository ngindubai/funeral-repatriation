## Repatriation from Guinea-Bissau to the UK

Repatriating a loved one from Guinea-Bissau is a process that requires careful handling of Portuguese-language civil documentation, coordination with the non-resident British Embassy in Dakar, and a clear logistics plan for routing the remains to the UK. The absence of a resident UK diplomat in Bissau means families rely entirely on specialist repatriation firms to manage all ground coordination.

The FCDO advises British nationals travelling to Guinea-Bissau to contact the British Embassy in Dakar, Senegal for any consular assistance. The embassy's emergency number is +221 33 823 7392. For urgent consular assistance from anywhere, contact the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (24 hours).

### The Legal Framework

Guinea-Bissau's civil registration system follows Portuguese law, inherited from colonial administration. Death registration is handled by the **Conservatória do Registo Civil** in the relevant district. The export of human remains requires an export permit issued by the **Ministério da Administração Territorial**.

For deaths in rural areas or border regions, obtaining documentation may require travel to the nearest district court or registry office. This adds time and logistical cost to the process.

### Required Documents

To repatriate a body from Guinea-Bissau, the following documents are typically required:

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate (certidão de óbito) | Conservatória do Registo Civil |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Ministério da Administração Territorial |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, Dakar |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

All Portuguese-language documents require certified English translation before presentation to UK authorities.

### Routing and Transport

Guinea-Bissau's Osvaldo Vieira International Airport (OXB) in Bissau handles international cargo, but frequency is limited. Most repatriations use one of two routing options:

- **Via Dakar, Senegal**: Air Senegal operates scheduled services from Bissau to Dakar, where connections to Europe are available through multiple carriers.
- **Via Lisbon, Portugal**: TAP Air Portugal operates direct service between Bissau and Lisbon, with onward connections to the UK.

The choice of route depends on carrier availability, cargo scheduling, and the specific UK destination airport.

### Complexity and Risk Factors

Guinea-Bissau is rated **high complexity** for repatriation. The key risk factors are:

- **Political instability**: The country has experienced multiple coups and attempted coups since independence in 1974. The government structure can change with little warning.
- **Limited institutional capacity**: Outside Bissau, civil registry offices, mortuaries, and health authorities have limited capacity. Documentation processes that take days in a capital city may take significantly longer in provincial areas.
- **Drug trafficking**: Guinea-Bissau is a major transit point for cocaine trafficking from South America to Europe. This affects law enforcement priorities and, at times, border and airport operations.
- **Border area risks**: FCDO advises heightened vigilance in border areas near Senegal's Casamance region (north) and Guinea (south-east). These areas have restricted access and sporadic security incidents.

### Islamic Burial Timeline Pressure

Approximately 45% of the population is Muslim. Where the deceased is Muslim, family pressure for swift burial within Islamic timelines may arise. UK-based families should communicate this clearly to the specialist repatriation firm at the earliest opportunity so that documentation can be prioritised accordingly.

### Cremation in Guinea-Bissau

Cremation facilities do not exist in Guinea-Bissau. If cremation is required, the body must be repatriated to the UK in its entirety. Cremation can then proceed at a UK crematorium once all necessary releases have been obtained.
