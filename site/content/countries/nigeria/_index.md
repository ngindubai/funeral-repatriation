Nigeria has the largest diaspora community of any African country in the UK. Repatriation from Nigeria to the UK is a routine service for specialist funeral directors, but the Nigerian bureaucratic process has consistent friction points that families should understand from the outset.

Two documents cause the most delay: the National Population Commission death certificate, and the State Ministry of Health export permit. These are separate processes and must both be completed before a body can leave Nigeria.

## The National Population Commission death certificate

When someone dies in Nigeria, the attending physician or hospital issues a medical certificate of cause of death. This is not the legally recognised death certificate for international purposes.

The official death certificate is issued by the National Population Commission (NPC). Obtaining the NPC certificate requires submitting the medical certificate and proof of identity to the NPC office. Processing typically takes 5 to 14 days. In states with understaffed NPC offices, delays beyond this are common.

## British High Commission Abuja

The British High Commission in Abuja is the main consular contact. A Deputy High Commission operates in Lagos. Consular staff will register the death with the UK authorities, advise on Nigerian documentation, and can assist if there are administrative obstacles.

## Post-mortem

A post-mortem is required for violent, sudden, or suspicious deaths, conducted by a government pathologist. Forensic pathology capacity is concentrated in Lagos and Abuja. Deaths in other states may face delays of 10 to 21 days if the body must be transferred to a facility with the appropriate capacity. This is a significant factor for deaths in rural or northern states.

## Export permit

The State Ministry of Health (Lagos or Abuja) issues an export permit. This requires the NPC death certificate, embalming certificate, and freedom from infectious disease clearance. Allow 7 to 14 days. The Ministry does not accept the hospital medical certificate as a substitute for the NPC document.

## Air freight from Lagos

British Airways operates direct Lagos Murtala Muhammed International Airport (LOS) to London Heathrow flights with cargo capacity. Virgin Atlantic also serves this route. Abuja (ABV) has fewer direct cargo options; most repatriations from outside Lagos route through Lagos cargo terminal.
