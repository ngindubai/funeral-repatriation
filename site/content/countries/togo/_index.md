## Repatriation from Togo to the UK

Repatriating a loved one from Togo is managed under French civil law, with the Tribunal de Première Instance responsible for death certification and Parquet clearance required before export. Togo has no resident UK diplomatic presence; consular support is provided by the British High Commission in Accra, Ghana.

For urgent consular assistance, contact the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (24 hours). The British High Commission Accra can be reached on +233 302 213 250.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration in Togo takes place at the **Tribunal de Première Instance** in the relevant district. The Parquet must issue clearance before remains can be exported. The **Ministère de l'Intérieur** issues the formal export permit.

In Lomé and major towns, this process is straightforward. In rural areas, particularly in the Savanes and Kara regions, documentation infrastructure is weaker, and obtaining certificates may require travel to district administrative centres.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Acte de décès (death certificate) | Tribunal de Première Instance |
| Parquet clearance for export | Parquet du Tribunal |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Ministère de l'Intérieur |
| Consular death registration | British High Commission, Accra |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing and Transport

Lomé's Gnassingbé Eyadéma International Airport (LFW) is the sole international gateway. Standard routing options:

- **Via Paris**: Air France operates direct Lomé to Paris CDG service.
- **Via Casablanca**: Royal Air Maroc provides connections through Mohammed V Airport.
- **Via Brussels**: Brussels Airlines serves Lomé with onward UK connections.

### Security and Access

Togo is rated **moderate complexity** overall. The security picture divides by region:

- **Lomé and southern Togo**: Functioning infrastructure, civil registration works, moderate process complexity.
- **Kara region (central)**: Some civil registration gaps. Documentation may require additional time.
- **Savanes region (far north)**: FCDO advises against all but essential travel. Jihadist groups from Burkina Faso have operated in this area since 2021. Deaths here require specialist logistics, potential military coordination, and significantly extended timelines.

### Religious and Cultural Factors

Togo's religious composition is approximately Christianity (43%), indigenous beliefs (35%), and Islam (14%). Traditional funerary customs vary significantly by ethnic group, particularly among the Kabye in the north and the Ewe in the south. Some communities have specific practices around the handling and timing of burial that a specialist firm needs to manage sensitively.

### Cremation in Togo

Cremation facilities do not exist in Togo. Families requiring cremation must arrange for full repatriation of the body to the UK, where cremation can proceed once all UK legal requirements have been met.
