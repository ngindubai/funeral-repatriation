Hong Kong is home to a significant British expat community and is a major destination for UK business travellers and tourists. The city operates under a separate legal system from mainland China under the One Country Two Systems arrangement. For repatriation purposes, Hong Kong is handled as a distinct jurisdiction.

## Legal framework

Hong Kong law governs repatriation procedures, not mainland Chinese law. The key legislation is the Public Health and Municipal Services Ordinance (Cap. 132), which governs the movement of human remains. The Coroner's Ordinance (Cap. 504) governs inquests into unnatural, sudden, and unexplained deaths.

## Death registration

Deaths are registered with the Births Deaths and Marriages Registry (BDMR). The death certificate is issued in English and Chinese (Hong Kong uses both official languages). Registration of a hospital death is typically handled by the hospital; sudden deaths require police involvement before registration.

## British Consulate-General Hong Kong

The British Consulate-General handles consular cases in Hong Kong. Address: 1 Supreme Court Road, Admiralty, Hong Kong. Phone: +852 2901 3000. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 1908 516666.

Notify the Consulate-General on the day of death. Staff are experienced with repatriation cases given the size of the British community in Hong Kong.

## Coroner's Court and inquest

For any sudden, unnatural, or unexplained death, the Coroner takes jurisdiction. The Coroner may order a post-mortem carried out by the Government Forensic Pathologist. If an inquest is held, the body cannot be removed from Hong Kong until the Coroner grants permission to do so. Inquest timelines vary: in straightforward cases, permission is granted within 7 to 14 days of the post-mortem. Complex cases take longer.

The Coroner must formally issue removal permission before any export documentation can be applied for.

## Department of Health export permit

The export permit for human remains is issued by the Department of Health under Cap. 132. Required documents: death certificate, Coroner's removal permission, embalming certificate, freedom from contagious disease clearance. Processing takes 2 to 5 days.

## Embalming

Embalming is standard practice for international repatriation from Hong Kong. Licensed funeral directors in Hong Kong are experienced with the international cargo process.

## Flights from Hong Kong

Hong Kong International Airport (HKG) has direct daily flights to London Heathrow with Cathay Pacific and British Airways. Among the most frequent direct routes from Asia to the UK. Cargo booking is straightforward once all documentation is in order.
