Barbados is one of the most visited Caribbean islands by British tourists, with a large resident British expat community. The combination of English language, Commonwealth legal traditions, and direct flights to London makes this one of the more manageable Caribbean repatriation origins.

That said, Barbadian law has its own requirements. The process is not automatic simply because Barbados is a Commonwealth member.

## Death registration and the Coroner

Deaths must be registered with the Barbados Registration Department. For natural deaths with a clear medical cause, this is straightforward. For sudden, unexplained, or violent deaths, the Royal Barbados Police Force is notified and the Coroner's court may be convened.

The Queen Elizabeth Hospital in Bridgetown has pathology services and conducts post-mortems when ordered by the Coroner. Results are typically available within 5 to 10 days.

## British High Commission Bridgetown

The British High Commission in Bridgetown serves Barbados and several other Eastern Caribbean islands. Consular staff will issue relevant UK documentation and advise on Barbadian requirements.

The FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 1908 516666.

## Embalming

Embalming is required for international repatriation under the Barbados Funeral Directors and Embalmers Act. The island has experienced funeral directors with a strong track record on UK repatriations. This is not the bottleneck it can be in some other Caribbean jurisdictions.

## Export documentation

The Chief Medical Officer issues the export certificate, which includes embalming confirmation and a freedom from infectious disease clearance. This is submitted alongside the Barbados death certificate to complete the documentation package.

## Air freight to the UK

British Airways operates direct Bridgetown (BGI) to London Gatwick and London Heathrow flights. Virgin Atlantic also serves this route. Human remains travel as air cargo, and the UK funeral director coordinates collection from the cargo terminal on arrival.
