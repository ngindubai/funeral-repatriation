## Repatriation from Afghanistan to the UK

Afghanistan is the most complex repatriation destination for UK families. The UK Embassy in Kabul suspended operations in August 2021 following the Taliban's return to power. Civil administration is controlled by a government the UK does not formally recognise. Active conflict persists in multiple areas. Very few specialist repatriation firms maintain the active in-country capability required.

**Call the FCDO immediately: 020 7008 5000 (24 hours).** This is not optional — it is the essential first step for any death in Afghanistan. The British Embassy in Islamabad, Pakistan (+92 51 201 2000) provides what assistance is possible in these circumstances.

### The Political and Legal Context

When the Taliban took power on 15 August 2021, the UK suspended its Embassy in Kabul. The UK does not formally recognise the Taliban as the legitimate government of Afghanistan. This creates a specific complication for repatriation: documentation issued by Taliban-controlled civil authorities exists in a legal grey area for UK consular purposes.

In practice, a specialist repatriation firm with established Taliban-era contacts will manage the documentation process through the civil authority that controls the relevant area. The Taliban-controlled civil administration has issued death certificates and export permits for foreign national repatriations. The British Embassy in Islamabad facilitates consular registration where possible.

### Islamic Burial Requirements

Afghanistan is almost entirely Muslim. Islamic burial within 24 hours of death is universally practised. For repatriation to the UK, immediate and clear communication that repatriation is intended is essential. Families must act within hours, not days.

### What May Not Be Achievable

In areas of active conflict — including parts of Kandahar, Helmand, Kunar, Nuristan, and border regions — access to the body may be impossible or unsafe for ground operators. The FCDO will give honest guidance on what is currently achievable in specific locations. Do not assume repatriation is possible before receiving this guidance.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Taliban-controlled civil authority |
| Documentation on cause of death | Local authority |
| Embalming certificate (where available) | Mortuary (Kabul or major city) |
| Export permit | Taliban Ministry of Interior |
| Consular registration | British Embassy, Islamabad |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

All Dari and Pashto documents require certified English translation.

### Routing and Transport

Commercial air access to Afghanistan is limited. Kabul is served by Flydubai, Ariana Afghan Airlines, and Kam Air. All routes are subject to frequent schedule changes. Dubai is the primary connection hub for UK-bound cargo. Cargo acceptance requirements are subject to change.

### Cremation

Cremation is not practised in Afghanistan and no facilities exist. All remains must be repatriated in full.
