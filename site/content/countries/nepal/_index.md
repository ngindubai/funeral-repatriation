## Repatriation from Nepal

Nepal presents some of the most logistically demanding repatriations in the world. For UK families with a loved one who has died in Kathmandu, the process is manageable. For deaths on trekking routes in the Himalayas, the challenges multiply before any official process can begin.

The immediate priority for a Himalayan death is not documentation. It is physical recovery.

### Himalayan death recovery

Deaths above 5,000 metres require helicopter evacuation. Nepal Army Aviation operates rescue missions, as do private operators. Cost depends on altitude, weather windows, and location. Deaths at Everest Base Camp (5,364m), on Annapurna Circuit, or in the Langtang Valley require helicopter recovery before the body can enter the official Nepali system.

Weather in the Himalayas is the dominant variable. A clear day in October is very different from a pre-monsoon window in May. Your specialist coordinates with on-ground agents who have direct relationships with helicopter operators and know the current conditions.

### Official process in Kathmandu

Once the body reaches Kathmandu, the process involves: death registration at the Ward Office or District Administration Office (DAO); police report for unnatural deaths; post-mortem at Tribhuvan University Teaching Hospital (TUTH) if ordered; Ministry of Home Affairs or DAO export clearance; and embalming at a licensed Kathmandu mortuary.

Nepali documentation is in Nepali (Devanagari script). Certified English translations are required for UK-bound documents.

### Routing to the UK

No direct UK flights from Kathmandu. Qatar Airways via Doha to Heathrow is the standard cargo route. Air India via Delhi is an alternative.
