## Repatriation from North Korea to the UK

North Korea (the Democratic People's Republic of Korea) is the world's most closed state. Foreign nationals cannot enter or move freely without state permission and assigned minders. The UK has no resident embassy in Pyongyang. British nationals who encounter difficulties — including death — must rely on the **Swedish Embassy in Pyongyang**, which provides limited consular services to UK nationals by prior arrangement with North Korean authorities.

**Call the FCDO immediately on 020 7008 5000 (24 hours).** There is no other first step. The FCDO will liaise with the Swedish Embassy in Pyongyang and the British Embassy in Seoul (+82 2 3210 5500), which handles UK-DPRK consular matters.

### Who Is in North Korea

The vast majority of foreign nationals in North Korea are:
- Diplomatic staff at one of the small number of resident embassies
- Accredited journalists on supervised visits
- Tourists on highly controlled, officially sanctioned group tours
- Business contacts operating under strict DPRK supervision

All are accompanied by state-assigned minders at all times. Any death is immediately known to North Korean authorities and handled under their complete control.

### The Documentation Process

Documentation is issued by North Korean state authorities — the **Ministry of People's Security (Bowisong)** oversees civil registration. There is no independent verification available. The Swedish Embassy facilitates whatever communication and consular registration North Korean authorities permit.

Export of remains requires North Korean state authorisation. No specialist repatriation firm operates inside North Korea independently.

### Routing

**Pyongyang Sunan International Airport (FNJ)** is the only viable exit point. Air Koryo operates the Pyongyang–Beijing route. All cargo moves via Beijing, with onward connections to the UK.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Ministry of People's Security / civil registry |
| State authorisation for repatriation | North Korean state |
| Documentation via Swedish Embassy | Swedish Embassy, Pyongyang |
| Consular registration | British Embassy, Seoul |

### Realistic Expectations

North Korea repatriation is not a standard case that a repatriation firm can manage through normal channels. The outcome depends almost entirely on the decision of North Korean state authorities. The FCDO, working through the Swedish Embassy, will do what is possible. Families must be prepared for a lengthy process with no guaranteed outcome.
