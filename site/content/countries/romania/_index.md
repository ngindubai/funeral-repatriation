Romania is an EU member and one of the most connected Eastern European countries to the UK by air. The volume of Romanian nationals living and working in the UK, and the corresponding frequency of visits back to Romania, means this route is well-established for UK repatriation specialists.

## Legal framework

Romania is an EU member and signatory to the Council of Europe Agreement on the Transfer of Corpses (Strasbourg, 1973 — ETS No. 080). The agreement requires embalming and a laissez-passer for international transfer of human remains. Romanian domestic law, including the Civil Status Law and the Code of Criminal Procedure, also applies.

## Death registration

Deaths are registered at the local Starea Civila (Civil Status Office) in the district where the death occurred. The Certificat de deces (death certificate) is issued in Romanian. For unnatural, sudden, or violent deaths, the police are notified and the case is referred to the Prosecutor's Office.

## British Embassy Bucharest

## Prosecutor's post-mortem

When the Prosecutor orders a post-mortem, the Institut de Medicina Legala (Institute of Forensic Medicine) conducts the examination. Bucharest's institute handles the highest volume. Regional institutes in Cluj-Napoca, Iasi, Timisoara, and other cities cover their respective areas. Results typically available within 5 to 10 days. The Prosecutor issues a formal release order before embalming can begin.

## Embalming and laissez-passer

Embalming is mandatory for international transfer under the Strasbourg Agreement. The laissez-passer is issued by the County Council (Consiliul Judetean) or the local authority. Required documents: Certificat de deces, Prosecutor's release (if applicable), embalming certificate, freedom from contagious disease certificate. Processing takes 2 to 5 days once all documents are submitted.

## Flights from Romania

Bucharest Henri Coanda Airport (OTP) has direct flights to London Luton, Stansted, Heathrow, and other UK airports with Wizz Air, Ryanair, TAROM, and British Airways. Cluj-Napoca (CLJ) also has direct UK routes. High frequency of departures means cargo bookings are rarely delayed by flight availability.
