South Sudan is the world's youngest country, having gained independence from Sudan in July 2011. Civil war broke out in December 2013 and continued until the September 2018 ceasefire agreement. Armed conflict continues in multiple states. The British Embassy in Juba is a resident mission, but its ability to assist in conflict-affected areas is limited.

For UK families, the core challenges are: a very underdeveloped civil registration system, almost no mortuary infrastructure outside Juba, and ongoing armed conflict in large parts of the country.

## Who to contact first

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: **020 7008 5000** (available 24 hours). The British Embassy Juba: +211 912 179 100.

FCDO advises against all travel to Unity State, Upper Nile State, Western Equatoria, and Central Equatoria outside Juba. For deaths in these areas, call the Embassy to assess what is possible before making any other decisions.

## Civil registration

South Sudan's civil registration system dates from 2011. The Office of the Registrar General handles death registration. Capacity is limited and processes may be slow.

The attending physician certifies cause of death. For non-natural deaths, the South Sudan National Police Service (SSNPS) must attend and investigate before the certificate is issued. Police capacity outside Juba is very limited.

## Ministry of Health export permit

The Ministry of Health issues the export permit. A licensed funeral director must prepare and embalm the body. Embalming facilities exist in Juba but are not available elsewhere.

For deaths outside Juba, the first step is getting the body to the capital — this may require air charter depending on location and current security conditions.

## Routing from Juba

Juba International Airport (JUB):
- Ethiopian Airlines to Addis Ababa (ADD) — primary UK-bound route
- Kenya Airways to Nairobi (NBO) — connecting to London
- EgyptAir to Cairo (CAI) — connecting to London

Juba has reasonable air connections for a regional capital, and both Ethiopian Airlines and Kenya Airways offer consistent services.

## Conflict zones: not achievable

FCDO advises against all travel to Unity State (oil fields area), Upper Nile State, Western Equatoria, and Central Equatoria outside Juba. Civilian body recovery from these areas is not achievable by standard means. The British Embassy will advise on any specialist channels if applicable.

## What a specialist firm does

A specialist firm coordinates with the British Embassy Juba, SSNPS, and the Ministry of Health. For remote cases, they assess air charter feasibility for body transfer to Juba. All Juba-side management is handled directly with local contacts — Embassy liaison provides a critical backstop given the fragility of local systems.
