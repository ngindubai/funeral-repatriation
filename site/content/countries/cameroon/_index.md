## Repatriation from Cameroon

Cameroon is a Commonwealth country and the British High Commission in Yaoundé is the consular authority. There are no direct UK flights; cargo routes via Paris CDG (Air France) and Addis Ababa (Ethiopian Airlines) are the main options.

Cameroon has a dual administrative character that sets it apart from other African origins. The country is predominantly French-speaking, but the North West and South West regions are Anglophone: they were administered under British mandate after the First World War and retain English-language administration. This means the language of official documentation, and the administrative culture, differ depending on where in Cameroon the death occurred.

### Francophone versus Anglophone procedures

Deaths in the eight Francophone regions (including Yaoundé in the Centre region and Douala in the Littoral region) follow French civil law tradition. Documentation is in French; certified French-to-English translations are required for UK use.

Deaths in the Anglophone North West and South West regions involve English-language documentation. These regions have experienced significant security disruption since 2017; the FCDO advises against all travel to both regions. Deaths in these areas involve access constraints on top of the standard procedure.

### Official process

Deaths are registered at the local Centre d'Etat Civil (civil registrar). For unnatural deaths, the Judicial Police (Brigade de Recherches) investigates under the authority of the Tribunal de Grande Instance, which must order any post-mortem and authorise release of the body. Post-mortems in complex cases are referred to the Centre Hospitalier Universitaire (CHU) in Yaoundé or Douala.

The Ministere de la Sante Publique issues the export permit.
