Comoros is an archipelago of three main islands in the Indian Ocean, between the northern tip of Madagascar and the Mozambique coast. The three islands — Grande Comore (Ngazidja), Anjouan (Nzwani), and Mohéli (Mwali) — each have their own island administrations under the federal government of the Union of the Comoros. The capital Moroni is on Grande Comore.

The country is almost entirely Sunni Muslim. Islamic burial customs are observed, and community pressure for rapid local burial is strong. Early, decisive contact with local authorities is essential if repatriation is the goal.

## Who to contact first

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: **020 7008 5000** (available 24 hours). The British High Commission Port Louis, Mauritius provides non-resident consular coverage for Comoros: +230 202 9400.

The first practical question is: which island? The answer determines which local administration is involved and whether inter-island transfer is required before the international process begins.

## British High Commission Port Louis: non-resident coverage

There is no British Embassy in Comoros. All consular services — including death registration for UK purposes and liaison with Comorian authorities — come from the British High Commission in Mauritius. This is a well-established arrangement given the small size of Comoros and the regional role of Port Louis.

## Civil registration

Comoros operates French civil law for civil matters, supplemented by Islamic personal status law. Death registration runs through the état civil (civil status office) of the relevant island.

The attending physician certifies cause of death. The civil registrar issues the acte de décès (death certificate) in French and Arabic. For non-natural deaths, the Gendarmerie Nationale must attend and issue clearance.

## Three-island administration

If the death occurs on Anjouan or Mohéli, the body must be transferred to Grande Comore before international export can proceed — there are no cargo-capable international departures from Anjouan or Mohéli airports. Inter-island transfer is typically by small aircraft (charter) or boat. Allow 1 to 2 additional days for this step.

## Ministry of Health export permit

Once civil registration and Gendarmerie clearance are complete, the Ministry of Health issues the export permit. A licensed funeral director embalms and prepares the body. All French and Arabic documents require certified English translation.

## Routing from Grande Comore

Prince Said Ibrahim International Airport (HAH), Moroni, Grande Comore:
- Air Austral to Réunion (REU) — connecting to Paris CDG and London
- Ethiopian Airlines to Addis Ababa (ADD) — connecting to London
- Kenya Airways to Nairobi (NBO) — connecting to London

All routes to London require at least two connections. Cargo coordination takes advance planning.

## What a specialist firm does

A specialist firm with Indian Ocean or East African coverage manages Gendarmerie clearance, civil registration, Ministry of Health permit, and the inter-island logistics if required. Early engagement is particularly important given the Islamic burial pressure — every hour matters in the first day.
