## Repatriation from Israel: What Families Need to Know

Israel is a medium-complexity repatriation. Direct UK flights from Ben Gurion Airport make the logistics manageable once documentation is in place. But Israel is not a Strasbourg Convention signatory, so the simplified European pathway does not apply. The Ministry of Health export permit requires both police clearance and completed death registration. Hebrew documentation requires certified translation.

The British community in Israel is diverse: the British-Jewish diaspora visiting family and friends, Christian pilgrimage tourists, backpackers in Tel Aviv and Eilat, and business travellers. Each group presents different repatriation circumstances.

### Death Registration in Israel

For natural deaths in hospital, a physician issues the death certificate (Teudat Ptirah). The death is registered with the Ministry of Interior (Misrad HaPanim). This is the starting point for the export permit application.

For sudden, unexplained, or unnatural deaths, the Israeli police open an investigation. The National Forensic Medicine Centre at Abu Kabir in Tel Aviv conducts forensic post-mortems. Abu Kabir is the main forensic facility for central and southern Israel; the National Center for Forensic Medicine also receives cases from Tel Aviv district. Post-mortem reports are issued in Hebrew.

### The Documentation Chain

1. Teudat Ptirah (death certificate) from the Ministry of Interior.
2. Police investigation closure letter (for unnatural deaths).
3. Post-mortem report from Abu Kabir if required.
4. Ministry of Health export permit.
5. Certified English translations of all Hebrew documents.
6. Embalming certificate.

### Cremation in Israel

Israel does not operate state crematoria. Orthodox religious law historically prohibited cremation, and Israeli law reflected this until the establishment of the Or HaGanuz private crematorium near Haifa in 2008. Or HaGanuz is the only crematorium operating in Israel and is managed privately.

In practice, most British families repatriate the full body to the UK and arrange cremation there if required. It is simpler, faster, and more predictable than attempting to arrange Or HaGanuz at distance.

### Eilat Deaths

British tourists at the Red Sea resort of Eilat are served by Ramon International Airport (ETM), which opened in 2019 to replace the old Eilat Airport. ETM has limited direct UK connections. Cargo routing for Eilat deaths typically transfers to Ben Gurion Airport in Tel Aviv. Allow additional time for the transfer and re-processing.

### Routing

Ben Gurion International Airport in Tel Aviv (TLV) is the main departure point. El Al, British Airways, and easyJet operate direct flights to London. Cargo handling is established and efficient once documentation is in place.
