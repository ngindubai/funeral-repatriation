Tokelau is a non-self-governing territory of New Zealand comprising three low-lying coral atolls — Atafu, Nukunonu and Fakaofo — in the South Pacific, approximately 500 kilometres north of Samoa. The total land area is around 10 square kilometres; maximum elevation is approximately 5 metres above sea level. About 1500 people live across the three atolls.

There is no airport. The only regular access is by vessel from Apia, Samoa, on a voyage of approximately two days. The vessel operates on an approximately fortnightly schedule. This single fact governs everything about repatriation from Tokelau.

For UK families dealing with a death on Tokelau, the first call should be to the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (available 24 hours). New Zealand law applies, and the NZ Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade (MFAT) is the administering authority.

## Legal Framework and Authorities

Tokelau is a New Zealand territory. The Tokelau National Government (Taupulega) handles local governance; formal legal and administrative authority rests with the NZ Administrator of Tokelau, operating through MFAT in Wellington. Deaths are registered via the Tokelau National Government and reported to MFAT.

Non-natural deaths fall under New Zealand Coroner jurisdiction in practice, though the remote nature of the territory means that practical application is handled carefully through MFAT and the NZ Police.

UK consular assistance is provided by the British High Commission in Wellington. The BHC does not have a presence on Tokelau.

The UK travel advisory for Tokelau is at [gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice/tokelau](https://www.gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice/tokelau).

## The Repatriation Route

The standard route is:

1. Vessel from the relevant atoll to Apia, Samoa (approximately 2 days; fortnightly schedule)
2. Apia (APW) to Auckland (AKL) approximately 3 hours via Samoa Airways or Air New Zealand
3. Auckland (AKL) to London Heathrow (LHR) approximately 26 hours via Qantas or Air New Zealand

A death on Nukunonu, for example, when the vessel is due to call at Fakaofo first, may mean waiting for an inter-atoll leg before departing for Samoa. Every case requires individual assessment.

Samoa transit documentation must be in order before the vessel departure. A specialist firm should begin coordinating Samoan and New Zealand documentation simultaneously.

## Required Documents

| Document | Issuing Authority |

| New Zealand death certificate | Tokelau National Government / MFAT |
| New Zealand Coroner's release | NZ Coroner (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate (if applicable) | Licensed practitioner |
| Vessel cargo documentation | Tokelau vessel operator |
| Samoa transit documentation | Samoa authorities |
| Air cargo documentation (each leg) | Airline/cargo operator |
| UK receiving funeral director confirmation | UK funeral director |
| NZ export permit for human remains | New Zealand Government |

## Community Considerations

Atafu and Fakaofo are predominantly Congregationalist Christian communities. Nukunonu is predominantly Roman Catholic, reflecting different missionary histories on the three atolls. Village elders (Taupulega) hold significant authority in community decisions, including those around death and burial. A specialist firm should be prepared to engage respectfully with both family and community leadership.
