Niger entered a political crisis in July 2023 when the Presidential Guard overthrew President Mohamed Bazoum. The Conseil National pour la Sauvegarde de la Patrie (CNSP) military junta has governed since. ECOWAS imposed sanctions (since partially lifted) and several Western nations suspended or reduced their diplomatic presence. Air France suspended Niamey service in the immediate aftermath.

For UK repatriation, this is the operational reality: no resident British Embassy, a military authority controlling all administrative processes, reduced international airline access, and significant armed group activity across multiple regions.

## British consular support: Abuja, Nigeria

The British Embassy Abuja holds non-resident accreditation for Niger. All consular services are provided from Abuja.

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first: **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). British Embassy Abuja: +234 9 462 2100.

Do not travel to Niger. The FCDO advises against all but essential travel to the entire country.

## Who is affected by this route

UK deaths in Niger are rare. The primary affected groups:
- Aid workers and NGO staff (many pulled back post-coup, but some remain)
- Journalists and correspondents
- Dual nationals (UK-Nigerien) visiting family

There is very limited UK tourism to Niger. The Agadez region (historic trans-Saharan trade hub) previously attracted some independent travellers; this has effectively ceased since 2020.

## The post-coup administrative reality

The CNSP military government controls all civilian administrative institutions, including the Ministry of Public Health, the Police Nationale du Niger, and the civil status offices. There is no independent civilian pathway for administrative processing.

In practice, the civil registration process operates — death certificates are issued through civil status offices, police clearance is issued through Police Nationale. But the political context means that any complication, delay, or additional clearance requirement reflects military authority decisions that the British Embassy in Abuja cannot readily contest.

## Conflict-affected regions: no-go areas

FCDO advises against all travel to:
- The region of Tillabéri (including areas near the Mali and Burkina Faso borders — Sahel armed groups active)
- The region of Diffa (Lake Chad basin — Boko Haram/ISWAP activity)
- The region of Tahoua north of Tahoua city
- All areas within 100km of the Libyan and Algerian borders

For deaths in these areas, body recovery by civilian operators is not currently achievable. Specialist crisis firms with confirmed Sahel network contacts may be able to assess access, but families should be prepared for the possibility that repatriation is not currently achievable from active conflict areas.

## Documentation required

| Document | Language | Issuer |
|---|---|---|
| Death certificate (acte de décès) | French | Civil Status Office |
| Medical certificate | French | Ministry of Public Health physician |
| Police Nationale clearance | French | Police Nationale du Niger |
| Ministry of Public Health export permit | French | Ministry of Public Health |
| Certified translations | English | Certified translator |

## Routing to the UK

Diori Hamani International Airport (NIM) in Niamey:
- Addis Ababa via Ethiopian Airlines — primary current route for UK cargo
- Casablanca via Royal Air Maroc — connecting to London
- Air France status variable post-coup — check current service before planning

Routing via Addis Ababa (Ethiopian Airlines with London connections) is the most reliable current pathway.
