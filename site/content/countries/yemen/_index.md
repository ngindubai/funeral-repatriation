## Repatriation from Yemen to the UK

Yemen has been in civil war since 2014–15, when the Houthi movement (Ansar Allah) seized control of Sana'a and forced the internationally recognised government to Aden and, effectively, into exile in Riyadh. Saudi Arabia leads a coalition supporting the IRG. UN-brokered ceasefire arrangements have reduced but not ended hostilities. The humanitarian situation is among the worst in the world.

**Call the FCDO immediately: 020 7008 5000 (24 hours).** The British Embassy in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia (+966 11 488 0077) provides the limited consular assistance that is available.

### The Dual Administration Problem

Yemen's split creates a fundamental question for every repatriation case: which authority controls the location where death occurred?

- **IRG-controlled areas (Aden, Hadramawt, Marib, south and east)**: Documentation through the internationally recognised government's civil authorities. Aden Airport is the main exit point. More tractable for repatriation purposes.
- **Houthi-controlled areas (Sana'a, Hudaydah, Sa'dah, north-west Yemen)**: Documentation through Houthi-controlled authorities. Sana'a Airport has very limited, unpredictable scheduled service. Significantly more complex to achieve repatriation.
- **Active frontline areas**: Not accessible. Repatriation is not achievable in active combat zones.

### Islamic Burial Requirements

Yemen is almost entirely Muslim. Islamic burial within 24 hours is universally practised. Communicating the intention to repatriate immediately — within hours of learning of the death — is essential. Islamic scholars permit delay where the family is based abroad, but the family must act without delay.

### Routing and Transport

- **Aden International Airport (ADE)**: Main accessible gateway under IRG. Flydubai, Air Arabia, and Yemenia operate limited services via Dubai and Amman.
- **Sana'a International Airport (SAH)**: Houthi-controlled. Very limited and unpredictable scheduled service.
- **Seiyun Airport (GXF)**: Hadhramaut region. Limited access, used for some humanitarian/charter operations.

All routes are subject to frequent change. Cargo acceptance for human remains requires advance confirmation with carriers.

### Cremation

Cremation is not practised in Yemen. All remains must be repatriated in full to the UK.

### Realistic Expectations

Yemen is one of the two or three most difficult repatriation destinations in the world for UK families. British nationals are extremely rare in Yemen; any such case is a specialist emergency. Call the FCDO first. Then engage a specialist firm that can honestly assess what is achievable from the specific location.
