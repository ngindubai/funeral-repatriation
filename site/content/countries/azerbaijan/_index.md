## Repatriation from Azerbaijan: What Families Need to Know

Azerbaijan sits at the intersection of the South Caucasus and the Caspian Sea, and Baku has become a significant destination for British business travellers and tourists visiting the oil city's restored old town. The British Embassy Baku is one of the more active embassies in the region for consular services.

One practical advantage distinguishes Azerbaijan from most of its neighbours: Azerbaijan Airlines (AZAL) operates a direct service from Baku Heydar Aliyev International Airport to London Heathrow. This is the only direct Caucasus or Central Asian connection to a UK airport that also carries cargo, and it simplifies logistics compared to routing through Istanbul or Dubai.

### Documentation and Translation

Azerbaijani uses a Latin-script alphabet (since 1991, replacing Cyrillic). Certified English translation of all official documents is required — death certificates, police reports, post-mortem records, Ministry of Health export permits. UK-accredited Azerbaijani translators are more available than for Armenian or Georgian.

The documentation chain:

1. Death certificate from the State Registry of Civil Acts (ASAN Xidmet).
2. Police investigation closure for unnatural deaths.
3. Post-mortem report from the Republican Centre of Forensic Medical Expertise, Baku.
4. Ministry of Health export permit.
5. Certified English translations of all documents.
6. Embalming certificate.

### Mountain and Rural Deaths

The Greater Caucasus mountain range crosses northern Azerbaijan. The Sheki, Guba, and Qax regions attract British visitors for nature and cultural tourism. Deaths in these mountainous areas require evacuation and road transfer to Baku before documentation begins. Transfer times from the north to Baku run between 4 and 8 hours depending on road conditions.

### The Nagorno-Karabakh Warning

The Nagorno-Karabakh region and surrounding areas are a conflict zone. The FCDO advises against all travel to this region. Deaths occurring in or near this area involve additional Azerbaijani military or security authorities. Standard consular timelines do not apply. Notify the British Embassy Baku immediately — they have protocols for conflict-zone deaths that go beyond standard consular processing.
