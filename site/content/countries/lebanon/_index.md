## Repatriation from Lebanon: What Families Need to Know

Lebanon is a higher-complexity repatriation, shaped by its unique confessional legal structure, ongoing political instability, and infrastructure fragility. The British Embassy in Beirut handles all consular functions for British nationals. The Embassy and a specialist repatriation firm are the two contacts a family should establish immediately.

Lebanon has a large diaspora community in the UK. Many British nationals of Lebanese heritage travel frequently to visit family, making Lebanon one of the higher-volume repatriation origins from the Middle East, despite its small geographic size.

### The Confessional Legal System

Lebanon's civil law is structured around 18 recognised religious communities. For death registration and body release, the relevant personal status court depends on the deceased's registered religion. Sunni Muslim deaths are registered with Sunni courts; Shia with Shia courts; Maronite Christian with Maronite courts; Druze with Druze courts, and so on. Each community has its own registration system alongside the civil state.

In practice, this means the first step is establishing the deceased's registered religious community, which determines which authority issues the Wafat certificate (death certificate). The Ministry of Interior, Civil Status Department, ultimately registers the death in the state civil record.

### Unnatural Deaths: ISF and the Judge of Instruction

For unnatural or sudden deaths, the Internal Security Forces (ISF) manage the scene and open an investigation. Cases involving suspicious circumstances are referred to the Judge of Instruction (an examining magistrate), who must issue an order releasing the body before any transport can be arranged. This magistrate review adds time: in complex cases, it can take several weeks.

### Beirut Airport: A Single Point of Failure

Lebanon has only one international airport: Beirut-Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY). There is no alternative. The airport has experienced closure and reduced-capacity periods during political crises, military activity, and infrastructure events. Families and repatriation firms managing a case from Lebanon should always have a contingency plan for what happens if the airport is temporarily closed or operating at limited capacity.

Middle East Airlines (MEA) is the primary carrier operating from BEY. Air France operates via Paris CDG. Turkish Airlines operates via Istanbul.

### Cremation

Cremation is not available in Lebanon. It is prohibited under Islamic law for Muslims, and is not practised by the mainstream Christian communities (Maronite, Greek Orthodox, Armenian) that make up the remainder of the population. All repatriations from Lebanon involve full body transport.
