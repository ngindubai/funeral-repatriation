## Repatriation from Mozambique: What Families Need to Know

Mozambique presents a specific combination of challenges rarely encountered in other African repatriations. It is a Commonwealth member, which means the British High Commission Maputo provides consular support. But Mozambique is a former Portuguese colony, so every official document — the death certificate, the post-mortem report, the export permit — is issued in Portuguese. Certified English translations are not optional. They are a legal requirement before UK entry formalities can be completed.

This matters practically. Translation takes time. Finding certified translators for legal and medical Portuguese is not straightforward from the UK. A specialist repatriation firm with established in-country contacts is the most reliable way to manage this.

### The Documentation Chain

The Registrar of the Conservatória do Registo Civil issues the Atestado de Óbito (death certificate). For deaths that are sudden, unnatural, or circumstances-unknown, the Polícia de Investigaçăo Criminal (PIC) must open an investigation before the body can be released. The Government Pathologist at Hospital Central de Maputo performs the post-mortem. The Ministry of Health then issues the laissez-passer mortuaire — the international body transit certificate — which authorises the body to leave the country.

Each step requires the preceding step to be complete. There is no parallel processing.

### Routing and Flights

There are no direct flights between Mozambique and the UK. Maputo International Airport (MPM) connects to Johannesburg O.R. Tambo (JNB) via LAM Mozambique Airlines and South African Airways. From Johannesburg, cargo can route to London Heathrow or Gatwick on long-haul carriers. Beira Airport (BEW) connects to Johannesburg as an alternative for deaths in central Mozambique.

Transit through South Africa requires compliance with South African Civil Aviation Authority (SACAA) standards for human remains handling. This adds an additional documentation step.

### Coastal and Remote Deaths

Mozambique draws British visitors mainly to its Indian Ocean coastline: Tofo Beach, the Bazaruto Archipelago, Vilanculos, and the offshore islands. Deaths at these locations are handled initially by local provincial hospitals, which have variable documentation capacity. In some cases, the body must be transferred to Maputo before the full documentation chain can begin. This adds days to the timeline.

### The Cabo Delgado Security Situation

The FCDO advises against all travel to parts of Cabo Delgado province in northern Mozambique. Deaths in these areas involve additional constraints: consular access may be restricted, security forces rather than standard civil police may control the scene, and documentation timelines can be unpredictable. Families dealing with a death in Cabo Delgado should contact the British High Commission Maputo immediately and work with a repatriation specialist who has experience in conflict-adjacent areas.
