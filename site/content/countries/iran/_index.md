## Repatriation from Iran to the UK

Iran presents a very high complexity repatriation scenario not only because of the civil documentation process and Islamic burial timeline pressure, but because of the UK-Iran diplomatic relationship and the specific complications facing British-Iranian dual nationals. The British Embassy in Tehran is operational — it reopened in 2015 after being stormed by demonstrators in 2011 — but consular access to detained or deceased British nationals has historically been constrained.

For urgent consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British Embassy Tehran can be reached on +98 21 6405 2000.

### British-Iranian Dual Nationals: A Critical Distinction

Iran does not recognise dual nationality. A British national who is also an Iranian national is treated solely as an Iranian citizen by Iranian authorities. This means:

- The British Embassy cannot demand consular access as a right in these cases.
- Iranian authorities may refuse British Embassy involvement in documentation or release of remains.
- The family must engage a specialist firm with strong established contacts with local Iranian authorities.

This is not a bureaucratic technicality. It is a fundamental limitation that has directly affected the ability to assist British-Iranian families in previous cases.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration in Iran is handled by the **State Organisation for Civil Registration (SABTE AHVAL)**. All deaths must be registered, and a death certificate issued in Persian (Farsi). Where the cause of death is not from natural causes, the **Medical Legal Organisation (Pezeshki Qanuni)** must issue clearance. This process can add significant time, particularly where the cause of death is unclear or disputed.

The Ministry of Interior issues the export permit once all documents are in place.

All Persian documents require certified English translation.

### Islamic Burial Requirements

Iran is approximately 98% Muslim, predominantly Shia. Islamic tradition requires burial within 24 hours. For repatriation to the UK, the family must communicate the intention clearly and immediately. Islamic scholars permit delay where the family is abroad, but action must begin within hours of learning of the death.

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|

| Medical Legal Organisation clearance | Medical Legal Organisation (Pezeshki Qanuni) — non-natural deaths |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Ministry of Interior |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, Tehran |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing and Transport

| Airport | Code | Primary Routing |
|---|---|---|
| Imam Khomeini International Airport | IKA | Istanbul (Turkish Airlines), Dubai (Emirates/flydubai), Doha (Qatar Airways) |
| Mehrabad Airport | THR | Domestic and some regional services |

Direct flights between Iran and the UK have been suspended since 2012. All repatriation routing goes via a Middle East or Turkish hub.

### Sanctions Considerations

UK and international sanctions related to Iran's nuclear programme complicate financial transactions with Iranian entities. A specialist repatriation firm experienced in Iran will have the necessary compliance procedures in place. Families should not attempt to make direct payments to Iranian parties without specialist guidance.

### Cremation

Cremation is not practised in Iran. All remains must be repatriated in full to the UK.
