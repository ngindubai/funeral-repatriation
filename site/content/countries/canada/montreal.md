Montreal is the only major Canadian city where the legal framework for death registration and documentation is entirely different from the rest of the country. Quebec operates under the Code civil du Québec, a civil law system derived from the French legal tradition, rather than the common law that governs all other Canadian provinces. For British families, this means the paperwork looks different, some terminology differs, and the issuing authorities are distinct from those in Ontario or British Columbia.

## Quebec coronial and registration system

Deaths in Quebec are handled by the Bureau du coroner du Québec, based in Quebec City but with jurisdiction across the province including Montreal. The coroner investigates deaths that are violent, sudden, obscure, or occur in custody. This is equivalent in scope to the coroner's remit in other Canadian provinces.

Death certificates in Quebec are issued by the Directeur de l'état civil (DEC) — the Quebec civil registry — rather than a vital statistics agency as in other provinces. The DEC is the sole authority for death registration in Quebec. Certified copies are available from the DEC office in Quebec City, or through their online ordering system.

Processing times: 5 to 15 working days for straightforward certified deaths. Coronial cases: 3 to 6 weeks, sometimes longer where investigation is ordered. Montreal cases involving road traffic fatalities, workplace accidents, or deaths in hospital under unusual circumstances will typically involve the coroner.

Source: Bureau du coroner du Québec, *Informations pour les familles*, 2024; Directeur de l'état civil, *Certificats de décès*, 2024.

## French-language documentation

All official Quebec documentation is issued in French. Death certificates from the DEC are in French by default. A certified English translation is required for UK repatriation purposes. The translation must be completed by a certified translator — a bilingual family member's version is not legally acceptable.

Plan for an additional 3 to 5 working days for certified translation once the original certificate is issued.

## Montreal-Trudeau International Airport cargo

Montréal-Trudeau International Airport (YUL) operates direct flights to London Heathrow. Air Canada offers a direct YUL-LHR service; British Airways also operates this route. This makes the cargo leg straightforward by Canadian standards. The licensed Montreal funeral home coordinates the air cargo booking.

Documentation required for export from Montreal:
- Quebec death certificate (DEC-certified copy, translated to English)
- Embalming certificate
- Freedom from infection certificate
- Burial transit permit (Quebec)
- Airline cargo booking confirmation
- Passport of the deceased

## British consular contacts

The British Consulate General in Montreal (1000 De La Gauchetière Street West) handles consular registration and documentation assistance for British nationals in Quebec. For urgent cases, contact the FCDO 24-hour line: +44 (0)20 7008 5000.

## Typical timeline from Montreal

Straightforward cases with no coroner involvement: 7 to 14 days, plus 3 to 5 days if translation is needed. Coronial cases: 3 to 6 weeks plus translation time. Complex cases: allow 8 weeks.

## Key local considerations

Quebec's distinct legal system means some standard Canadian funeral industry procedures do not apply. Confirm that the Montreal funeral director has direct experience with Quebec-specific documentation for UK repatriation, not just general Canadian international experience.
