Ethiopia is East Africa's most historically and culturally significant destination for British visitors. Lalibela, Aksum, the Blue Nile falls, the Simien Mountains, and the Omo Valley are all established on the UK tourism circuit. The country's size and geography mean that deaths in the regions present significant transfer and logistics challenges.

## Legal framework

Ethiopia is not a signatory to the Strasbourg Agreement. Repatriation is governed by Ethiopian domestic law and bilateral consular conventions. The Federal Police, Ministry of Health, and Civil Registry are the primary authorities. Documentation is in Amharic, written in the Ge'ez script.

## Death registration

Deaths in Addis Ababa are registered with the Addis Ababa City Administration civil registry. In regional areas, registration occurs at local government level. For deaths in very remote areas (Danakil Depression, Omo Valley, high-altitude Simien Mountains), reaching a civil registry office may require several days of travel or coordination by local contacts.

## British Embassy Addis Ababa

The British Embassy in Addis Ababa must be notified immediately. Address: Comoros Street, PO Box 858, Addis Ababa. Phone: +251 11 617 0100. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 1908 516666. The Embassy also covers Djibouti; specify Ethiopia when calling.

## Federal Police and post-mortem

For violent, sudden, or unexplained deaths, the Federal Police take jurisdiction. A forensic post-mortem may be ordered at St. Paul's Hospital Millennium Medical College in Addis Ababa, the primary forensic facility. Regional forensic capacity is very limited. The body must be in Addis Ababa before a post-mortem can be conducted.

## Transfer from regions

Deaths in Lalibela, the Simien Mountains, the Omo Valley, or other regional areas require physical transfer to Addis Ababa. Options: charter aircraft from a regional airstrip (fastest, most expensive), scheduled Ethiopian Airlines domestic flight (slower, more complex), or road (slowest). Your repatriation specialist coordinates this with local agents.

## Ministry of Health export permit

The Ministry of Health issues the export permit for human remains. Required documents: death certificate, Federal Police release, post-mortem report (if applicable), embalming certificate, freedom from contagious disease clearance. Allow 5 to 10 days.

## Amharic translation

All documentation is in Amharic (Ge'ez script). Certified translation into English requires a specialist Amharic-English translator. This is not covered by general East African language services. Allow for translation time alongside other documentation steps.

## Routing to the UK

Ethiopian Airlines operates a major international hub at Addis Ababa Bole Airport (ADD). The airline has direct and one-stop services to London Heathrow. Cargo routing via ADD to Heathrow is efficient once export documentation is in place.
