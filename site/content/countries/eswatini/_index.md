Eswatini — formerly Swaziland, renamed in 2018 — is a small landlocked kingdom in Southern Africa, bordered by South Africa and Mozambique. For UK repatriation purposes it presents a relatively accessible case: English is an official language, the British High Commission maintains a resident presence in Mbabane, and documentation is straightforward. The practical constraint is routing, which goes entirely via Johannesburg.

The most common UK deaths in Eswatini involve tourists visiting Hlane Royal National Park and Mkhaya Game Reserve, business travellers, NGO and development sector workers, and members of the UK-Swazi diaspora community.

## British High Commission Mbabane

The British High Commission in Mbabane is a resident mission. High Commission staff are familiar with the small but consistent UK population in Eswatini and handle death cases through the standard Registrar General process.

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first: **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). British High Commission Mbabane: +268 2404 2581.

## Death registration

Death registration goes through the Registrar General's Office. The attending physician issues the certificate of cause of death. The Registrar General issues the death certificate in English.

For non-natural deaths, the Royal Eswatini Police Service attends the scene and must issue clearance before the death certificate is issued.

## Royal Eswatini Police Service: non-natural deaths

Violent, sudden, or unexplained deaths require Royal Eswatini Police Service attendance and investigation. For routine accident cases, police clearance typically takes 5 to 10 working days. Cases requiring criminal investigation take longer and timeline becomes unpredictable.

## Ministry of Health export permit

Once the death certificate is in hand, the Ministry of Health issues the export permit for human remains. This is a straightforward administrative process in Eswatini, typically completed in 2 to 3 working days.

## Eswatini's absolute monarchy

Eswatini is the last absolute monarchy in sub-Saharan Africa. King Mswati III holds executive, legislative, and judicial authority. Administrative decisions in Eswatini are not subject to independent judicial review in the way they would be in a constitutional democracy. This has a practical implication: there is no mechanism to challenge delays through a courts process. The specialist firm's relationship with local funeral directors and authorities is the most effective lever for managing cases efficiently.

## Routing to the UK

No direct Eswatini-UK flights exist. Standard routing:

| Route | Notes |
|---|---|
| King Mswati III Airport (SHO) → Johannesburg JNB → London Heathrow | Primary route — multiple daily JNB-LHR flights |
| Matsapha Airport (MTS) → JNB → London | Alternative if SHO congested |
| JNB → London via British Airways, South African Airways, Virgin Atlantic | All viable for cargo |

Firms with JNB cargo experience are essential. The JNB transit step is standard for all Southern Africa repatriations and well-established.

## Documentation required

| Document | Language | Issuer |
|---|---|---|
| Death certificate | English | Registrar General's Office |
| Medical certificate | English | Attending physician |
| Police clearance | English | Royal Eswatini Police Service |
| Ministry of Health export permit | English | Ministry of Health |
| Embalming certificate | English | Licensed funeral director |
