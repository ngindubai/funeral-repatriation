Svalbard is a Norwegian Arctic archipelago approximately 1,300 kilometres north of mainland Norway. Under the 1920 Svalbard Treaty, citizens of signatory nations (including the UK) may live and work there, but Norwegian law governs all civil matters. The Governor (Sysselmannen) in Longyearbyen is the sole civil, environmental, and emergency authority.

The overwhelming majority of Svalbard's population lives in Longyearbyen. Barentsburg (Russian) and Ny-Ålesund (research) are the only other significant settlements. Deaths anywhere on the vast archipelago — including at Pyramiden, on glaciers, or in the wilderness — require transfer to Longyearbyen before international arrangements can proceed.
