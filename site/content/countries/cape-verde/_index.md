Cape Verde is one of the most popular UK package holiday destinations in Africa. TUI, Jet2, and easyJet operate direct services from regional UK airports to Sal (SID) and Boa Vista (BVC). Most British deaths on the islands occur in the Senegambia resort strip on Sal, around Santa Maria Beach, or in Boa Vista's coastal hotels.

Bringing a loved one home involves Portuguese paperwork, an inter-island transport step for smaller islands, and routing via Lisbon or Las Palmas rather than a direct flight to the UK.

## The consular process in Cape Verde

There is no resident British Embassy in Cape Verde. The British Embassy in Dakar, Senegal, is accredited to Cape Verde and manages formal consular registration. In-country, an Honorary Consul in Praia (Santiago Island) provides initial assistance.

When a British national dies in Cape Verde:

1. The local civil registry issues the Certidao de Obito (death certificate)
2. In non-natural deaths, the Instituto Nacional de Medicina Legal (INML) provides forensic clearance
3. The Honorary Consul registers the death provisionally and notifies British Embassy Dakar
4. The Ministry of Health issues a laissez-passer mortuaire (export permit)
5. The body is prepared and routed to the UK

The Honorary Consul does not hold full consular authority. For anything beyond initial registration — repatriation authorisation, complex investigations, family liaison — British Embassy Dakar takes over.

## Inter-island transfers: the hidden complication

Cape Verde is an archipelago of ten islands spread across 400 kilometres of Atlantic Ocean. If the death occurs on a smaller island — Fogo, Santo Antăo, Săo Nicolau, Săo Vicente, Brava, or Maio — the remains must reach Sal or Santiago before a transatlantic cargo flight is possible.

Inter-island options:

| Route | Typical transfer time |
|---|---|
| Fogo to Santiago | 1–2 days (TACV Cabo Verde Airlines or charter) |
| Santo Antăo to Săo Vicente then Santiago | 2–3 days (ferry + air) |
| Săo Nicolau to Sal | 1–2 days (charter or TACV) |

This adds 3 to 7 days to the total cost. Factor this in from the start.

## No cremation in Cape Verde

There are no cremation facilities anywhere in the Cape Verde islands. This is not negotiable — families who want cremation must repatriate the body to the UK first, then arrange cremation through a UK funeral director on arrival.

If you are asking whether ashes can be brought to Cape Verde (for a memorial or scattering), the answer is yes. Ashes transported as hand luggage must comply with IATA Packing Instruction 650 and airline rules — typically a sealed, sift-proof container with a cremation certificate.

## Documentation and translation

All documents are in Portuguese. The UK authorities require certified English translations. Required document set:

| Document | Issuer |
|---|---|
| Certidao de Obito | Civil registry |
| INML forensic clearance (non-natural deaths) | INML |
| Embalming / Freedom from Infection certificate | Licensed funeral director |
| Laissez-passer mortuaire | Ministry of Health |
| British consular registration | Honorary Consul / British Embassy Dakar |

## Water sports deaths: what to know

Surfing, kiteboarding, and diving deaths are a consistent cause of British fatalities in Cape Verde. Conditions off the northern coast of Sal and around Boa Vista can exceed the capability of recreational participants without proper instruction.

In ocean drowning cases, recovery of remains may take time. INML forensic examination is required for all non-natural deaths. Timeline estimates above assume remains are recovered.

## What to do when someone dies in Cape Verde

1. Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: 020 7008 5000 (24 hours)
2. Contact the Honorary Consul in Praia: +238 260 3040
3. Notify the insurer immediately — most UK package holiday insurance covers repatriation
4. Do not authorise body movement between islands before documentation is in order
