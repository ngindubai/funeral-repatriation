## Repatriation from Tunisia

Tunisia receives significant numbers of British tourists, particularly to the resort areas of Hammamet, Sousse, Port El Kantaoui, and Djerba. The country has three international airports serving UK routes: Tunis-Carthage (TUN), Monastir Habib Bourguiba (MIR), and Djerba-Zarzis (DJE). This distribution of airport access simplifies cargo routing compared to countries with a single hub.

The Tunisian repatriation process follows French administrative tradition. All documentation is in Arabic; certified Arabic-to-English translations are required for UK use.

### Unnatural deaths: the Parquet process

For any unnatural, violent, or unexplained death, the Parquet (Public Prosecutor's Office) takes jurisdiction. The Parquet orders a post-mortem at the Institut National de Medecine Legale (INML) in Tunis if forensic examination is needed. Deaths in Djerba or Sousse requiring INML involvement must be transferred to Tunis, adding two to three days before forensic examination begins.

For straightforward resort deaths (swimming pool accidents, cardiac events), the Parquet typically releases the body faster than in cases with any criminal element.

### Cremation

Cremation is not available in Tunisia. Islamic law governs. Non-Muslim cremation facilities do not exist. Repatriation of the body is the only option for UK families.

### Translation

All official documents are in Arabic. Certified Arabic-to-English translations are required. Allow two to three working days for translation once all documents are available. This is a necessary step and cannot be shortened without using an unqualified translator.
