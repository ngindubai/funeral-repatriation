## Repatriation from Curaçao to the UK

Curaçao is a constituent country of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, located in the southern Caribbean Sea approximately 65 km north of the Venezuelan coast. The capital is Willemstad, whose colourful waterfront is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Curaçao is a significant tourist destination and financial centre.

A substantial number of UK nationals visit Curaçao for holidays each year.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British Embassy The Hague (+31 70 427 0427) handles consular matters for Curaçao on a non-resident basis.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration in Curaçao is managed by the **Registro Civil** (Civil Registry). Documentation is issued in Dutch and/or Papiamentu — the local creole language, which differs slightly from Aruba's Papiamento. Non-natural deaths require investigation and clearance by the **Korps Politie Curaçao**.

### Document Translation

Dutch and Papiamentu death certificates require certified translation into English for the UK Coroner. Your specialist repatriation firm will arrange this.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Registro Civil, Curaçao |
| Certified English translation | Sworn translator |
| Police clearance | Korps Politie Curaçao (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local funeral director |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Health authority |
| Export permit | Curaçaoan authorities |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, The Hague |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

**Curaçao Hato International Airport (CUR)** has direct **KLM** flights to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS). From Amsterdam, frequent connections to UK airports are available. Miami connections are an alternative via American Airlines.

### Cremation

Cremation is available in Curaçao. Ashes are returned to the UK in a sealed urn.
