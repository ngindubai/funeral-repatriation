## Repatriation from Republic of Congo to the UK

The Republic of Congo — often called Congo-Brazzaville to distinguish it from the neighbouring Democratic Republic of Congo — is a French civil law jurisdiction with a working civil registration system in its two main cities: Brazzaville and Pointe-Noire. UK consular support is non-resident, handled by the British Embassy in Kinshasa, DRC, which sits directly across the Congo River.

For urgent consular assistance, contact the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (24 hours). The British Embassy Kinshasa can be reached on +243 81 556 0200.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is handled at the **Tribunal de Grande Instance** in the relevant district. The Parquet must issue clearance before remains can be released for export. The **Ministère de l'Intérieur** issues the export permit.

Civil registration in Brazzaville and Pointe-Noire is functioning and established. In rural areas, particularly in the northern Likouala and Sangha departments, documentation infrastructure is sparse and obtaining certificates may require significant travel to provincial administrative centres.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Acte de décès (death certificate) | Tribunal de Grande Instance |
| Parquet clearance for export | Parquet du Tribunal |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Ministère de l'Intérieur |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, Kinshasa |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing and Transport

The Republic of Congo has two international gateways:

- **Brazzaville — Maya-Maya Airport (BZV)**: Air France operates direct service to Paris CDG. Turkish Airlines serves Brazzaville via Istanbul. Ethiopian Airlines provides connections via Addis Ababa.
- **Pointe-Noire — Pointe-Noire Airport (PNR)**: Air France also operates direct service to Paris from Pointe-Noire, which serves the oil industry hub on the Atlantic coast.

The choice between the two airports depends on where death occurred. Brazzaville is generally the primary hub for repatriation logistics.

### Security Considerations

The Republic of Congo is rated **high complexity**. Key concerns:

- **Pool department**: FCDO advises against all but essential travel to the Pool department, south of Brazzaville. The area has a history of armed militia activity, most recently the Ninja militia. Sporadic incidents have continued in recent years. Deaths in Pool require specialist logistics and may involve police or military coordination.
- **Northern jungle provinces (Likouala, Sangha)**: Extremely remote, with very limited road access. Light aircraft is often the only option for transport. Deaths here require significant lead time before documentation can begin.
- **Brazzaville and Pointe-Noire**: Elevated standard precautions apply, but the cities function normally and the documentation process is well-established.

### Distinguishing Republic of Congo from DRC

A frequent source of confusion: the **Republic of Congo** (capital: Brazzaville) is a separate country from the **Democratic Republic of Congo** (capital: Kinshasa). They share a border along the Congo River. The two countries have different governments, legal systems, and repatriation processes. The British Embassy in Kinshasa (DRC) provides consular coverage for the Republic of Congo as a non-resident function.

### Cremation

Cremation facilities are not available in the Republic of Congo. Families requiring cremation must arrange full repatriation of the body to the UK.
