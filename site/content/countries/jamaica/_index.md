Jamaica is a popular British Caribbean destination with direct flights from both Kingston and Montego Bay to London Gatwick. The Jamaican-British diaspora is large, and many UK families travel to visit relatives as well as for tourism.

Repatriation from Jamaica follows the English-speaking Caribbean legal model. The Coroner has jurisdiction over sudden, violent, and unexplained deaths. Death certificates are issued in English. The British High Commission in Kingston is experienced with repatriation cases.

The most significant complication specific to Jamaica is violent crime. Jamaica has one of the highest murder rates in the Caribbean. While the majority of British tourist deaths are from natural causes or accidents, crime-related deaths involving UK nationals do occur and automatically trigger a police investigation. This suspends the repatriation process until police and the Coroner have completed their work.

Direct flights from Kingston Norman Manley International and Montego Bay Sangster International to London Gatwick operate year-round. British Airways and Virgin Atlantic both serve this route. This direct connectivity keeps freight costs lower than destinations requiring transit hubs.

Contact the British High Commission Kingston at +1 876 936 0700. The High Commission will provide a list of Jamaican funeral directors with UK repatriation experience.
