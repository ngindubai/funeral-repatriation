The Faroe Islands are an archipelago of 18 islands in the North Atlantic, approximately 200 miles north of Scotland. They are an autonomous territory of the Kingdom of Denmark, with their own government (Løgting) and language. Danish law applies to civil matters including death registration.

Atlantic Airways operates direct flights to London Gatwick from Vágar Airport, making the Faroes one of the more accessible North Atlantic territories for UK families. English is widely spoken. The British Embassy Copenhagen handles consular matters on a non-resident basis.
