Namibia is one of the most visited countries in southern Africa, attracting British tourists for self-drive safaris, desert landscapes, and wildlife. The country is large — over twice the size of France — and much of it is extremely remote.

The British High Commission is resident in Windhoek. English is the sole official language. Namibia is a Commonwealth member. These factors all simplify the repatriation process relative to most African destinations.

## The self-drive safari risk

The most significant cause of British visitor deaths in Namibia is self-drive road accidents. British tourists commonly drive thousands of kilometres connecting Etosha National Park, Sossusvlei, Damaraland, and the Skeleton Coast. Much of this driving is on gravel roads, often far from any town or medical facility, without mobile coverage. A single-vehicle accident in the Kunene region or on the Skeleton Coast can take hours for emergency services to reach.

Any unnatural death, including road accidents, requires a State pathologist examination and Magistrate's Court coroner process before repatriation can proceed. State pathologist capacity in Windhoek is limited; complex post-mortems can be significantly delayed.

## Remote areas

**Kaokoveld and Kunene region** in the northwest. One of Africa's most remote inhabited areas. Accessible only by 4WD on rough tracks or by small charter aircraft. Transfers to Windhoek can take multiple days.

**Skeleton Coast Wilderness** in the far northwest. A restricted-access national park requiring a special permit from the Namibia Tourism Board. The nearest charter airstrip is Möwe Bay. Even by air, transfers to Windhoek take a full day.

**Caprivi Strip (Zambezi Region)** in the northeast. Borders four countries. Deaths require transfer to Katima Mulilo and then onward by road or air to Windhoek.

**Etosha National Park.** The most visited park. Deaths require transfer to Tsumeb or Ondangwa before Windhoek.

## Air routing

Hosea Kutako International Airport is 45 km east of Windhoek. All cargo connects via Johannesburg O.R. Tambo International (JNB) on British Airways, South African Airways cargo, or Ethiopian Airlines. There are no direct UK flights from Namibia.
