Estonia's most significant logistical constraint for repatriation is its forensic pathology system: there is exactly one accredited forensic pathology institute in the country. The Kohtuekspertiisi ja Kriminalistika Keskus (KKK — Centre of Forensic Science, formerly the Estonian Forensic Science Institute) in Tallinn handles all forensic post-mortems for the entire country. Deaths requiring forensic examination from Tartu, Pärnu, or anywhere else in Estonia are transferred here. In a small country, this concentration of forensic capacity is administratively efficient, but it means all cases flow through one point — and caseload at the centre directly determines how quickly cases are processed.

## What the British Embassy does — and does not do

The British Embassy Tallinn (Wismari 6, 10136 Tallinn) is the UK consular authority for all deaths in Estonia.

**The Embassy can:** Register the death in UK consular records. Advise on Estonian documentation for UK authorities. Provide a list of funeral homes with repatriation experience.

**The Embassy cannot:** Repatriate the body. Pay any costs. Override Estonian legal processes.

FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 (0)20 7008 5000.

## What Estonian law requires

**Under the Kriminaalmenetluse seadustik (Code of Criminal Procedure)**, sudden, violent, or unexplained deaths are reported to Estonian Police and Border Guard Board (Politsei- ja Piirivalveamet, PPA), who decide whether a forensic examination is required. Forensic work is referred to the KKK in Tallinn.

For deaths with a certified cause, the treating physician issues the surmateatis (death certificate) and the process does not involve police.

## The documentation chain

**1. Surmateatis (death certificate).** Issued by the treating physician or KKK forensic physician.

**2. Surmatõend (registered death certificate).** Registered with the Population Register (Rahvastikuregister) via the Health Board.

**3. International transport permit.** Issued by the Estonian Health Board (Terviseamet) for international repatriation. The Terviseamet is the competent authority for export documentation in Estonia.

**4. Certified Estonian-to-English translations.** Required for all documents submitted to UK authorities.

**5. Embalming certificate.**

**6. Freedom from infection certificate.**

**7. IATA cargo documentation.**

Source: Kriminaalmenetluse seadustik (Estonia); Terviseamet, *Surnukehade transport*, 2024.

## Airport and cargo routing

Tallinn Airport (TLL) has no direct LHR service. The standard cargo routing is TLL to Helsinki Vantaa (HEL, 1 hour by air), then HEL-LHR via British Airways. An alternative: TLL to Riga (RIX, 4 to 5 hours by road or 1 hour by air) and onward connections. The funeral director will advise on current routing based on cargo schedules. Build in at least one extra day for the connecting leg.

## Timeline from Tallinn

- Natural death with certified cause: 7 to 14 days (plus translation time)
- KKK forensic case: 14 to 28 days
- Complex investigation: 4 to 8 weeks

## Key local considerations

Tallinn Old Town is one of Europe's most popular British stag and hen party destinations. Deaths among young British tourists — typically alcohol and accident-related — occur each summer and are well-documented in Estonian police records. The PPA and KKK are experienced with British cases. The British Embassy Tallinn processes a significant number of death notifications for a small country.
