## Repatriation from Uzbekistan: What Families Need to Know

Uzbekistan has seen a sharp rise in British tourism over the last decade, driven by the Silk Road heritage cities of Samarkand, Bukhara, and Khiva. The infrastructure for repatriation from Tashkent is solid relative to other Central Asian destinations. The British Embassy Tashkent handles a growing number of consular death cases.

Tashkent has an advantage most Central Asian capitals lack: better transport connections and a more developed administrative system. This does not make the process simple, but it does mean timelines tend to be shorter than in Kazakhstan or Kyrgyzstan.

### Silk Road Tourist Cities

Most British visitors to Uzbekistan travel to Samarkand, Bukhara, or Khiva — all of which are several hours from Tashkent by rail or road. Deaths in these cities require transfer to Tashkent for post-mortem and documentation. Uzbekistan's main rail network connects these cities reasonably well, and the transfer process, while adding days, is not as complex as mountain evacuations in neighbouring countries.

Extreme summer temperatures in the Silk Road cities present a genuine risk. Bukhara's August temperatures regularly reach 42 to 44 degrees Celsius. British tourists unaccustomed to these conditions are vulnerable.

### Documentation and Translation

All official Uzbek documents — death certificates, police reports, post-mortem records, Ministry of Health permits — are issued in Uzbek, which uses a Latin-script alphabet. Certified English translation is required.

UK-accredited Uzbek translators exist, though the pool is smaller than for Russian or Arabic. Contact the British Embassy Tashkent promptly for their current translator recommendations.

The documentation chain:

1. Death certificate from the local Uzbek civil registry authority.
2. Police investigation closure for unnatural deaths.
3. Post-mortem report from the Republican Centre of Forensic Medical Expertise, Tashkent.
4. Ministry of Health export permit.
5. Certified English translations of all Uzbek documents.
6. Embalming certificate.

### Cargo Routing

Uzbekistan Airways operates a direct service from Tashkent International (TAS) to London Gatwick. This is one of the few direct Central Asian connections to the UK. Cargo capacity on this route is limited and schedules change seasonally — always confirm current availability with your repatriation provider before making logistics plans based on the direct service.

If the direct service is unavailable, cargo connects via Istanbul (Turkish Airlines) or Dubai (flydubai).
