## Repatriation from Bolivia

Bolivia presents two distinct repatriation challenges: geography and altitude. The country is landlocked, so all international cargo must route through a neighbouring hub. La Paz El Alto Airport (LPB) sits at 4,061 metres above sea level, the world's highest commercial airport, which limits aircraft cargo capacity and affects flight availability.

The more operationally significant airport for international cargo is Santa Cruz Viru Viru (VVI). Most repatriations route through Santa Cruz to Lima (LATAM) or São Paulo before connecting to Heathrow.

### Altitude deaths

Deaths from altitude sickness (soroche) are common among UK tourists visiting La Paz, Lake Titicaca, the Uyuni Salt Flat, and the Altiplano. Soroche can cause fatal pulmonary or cerebral oedema with little warning. Bolivian law treats these as unnatural deaths. The FELCC (Special Force Against Crime) and the Fiscalia (Prosecutor's Office) take jurisdiction. A post-mortem at the Instituto de Investigaciones Forenses (IDIF) is typically ordered.

Do not assume a soroche death will be processed quickly because the cause seems medically straightforward. The legal process runs its course regardless.

### Deaths in remote tourist areas

Deaths in the Salar de Uyuni (the salt flat area, accessed via Uyuni town) or at Tiwanaku archaeological site require transfer to La Paz before official processes begin. Deaths in the Amazon basin (Rurrenabaque area) require road or light aircraft evacuation.

### Documentation

The Acta de Defuncion from SERECI (Registro Civil) is the primary document. The Fiscalia release order, IDIF post-mortem report, Ministry of Health export permit, and embalming certificate complete the documentation package. All documents are in Spanish; certified English translations are required for UK use.
