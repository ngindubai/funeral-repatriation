Guatemala attracts British visitors for its colonial architecture, active volcanoes, and ancient Mayan ruins. Most come to Antigua Guatemala, Lake Atitlan, and Tikal. Each of these areas carries its own logistical profile for repatriation.

The British Embassy is resident in Guatemala City. All documentation is in Spanish. There are no direct flights to the UK; connections via Miami (American Airlines), Atlanta (Delta), or Mexico City (Aeromexico/Iberia) are standard for cargo routing.

## The forensic process: INACIF

All deaths classified as unnatural in Guatemala must pass through the Instituto Nacional de Ciencias Forenses (INACIF), the national forensic institute. INACIF is based in Guatemala City. Any death involving trauma, accident, or unclear cause requires INACIF involvement, a formal autopsy, and Ministerio Publico (prosecutor's office) sign-off before the body can be released.

For deaths outside Guatemala City, the body must first be transferred to Guatemala City before the INACIF process can begin. This transfer adds time.

## Risk areas by location

**Antigua Guatemala** is a UNESCO World Heritage colonial city 45 minutes from Guatemala City. Deaths here can typically be processed via INACIF Guatemala City faster than other provinces.

**Lake Atitlan** in the Western Highlands. The lake is surrounded by volcanoes; villages around it are accessible only by boat. Drownings and boat accidents are a documented risk. Any death requires transfer to Quetzaltenango or Guatemala City.

**Tikal National Park and Peten lowlands** in northern Guatemala. This remote jungle region requires road or air transfer to Guatemala City before repatriation begins (flight approximately 1 hour; road 8 to 10 hours).

**Active volcanoes.** Fuego, Pacaya, and Acatenango are active and visited by tourists. The June 2018 eruption of Fuego killed over 190 people. Any death on a volcano hike requires INACIF investigation and may involve difficult access conditions.

## Documentation

All documents are in Spanish. Death certificates, forensic reports, civil registry documentation, and the Ministry of Health export permit all require certified translation into English for UK reregistration. Your repatriation company handles this translation, but it adds time and cost.
