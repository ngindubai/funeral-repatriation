## Repatriation from Jordan

Jordan sits at the efficient end of Middle East repatriation complexity. The Civil Status and Passports Department processes death registrations with reasonable speed. Royal Jordanian operates direct flights between Amman and London Heathrow, which removes the multi-leg cargo routing that complicates other regional repatriations.

For UK nationals who die of natural causes in Amman hotels or hospitals, the process is relatively straightforward. Unnatural deaths introduce a Ministry of Interior referral and a post-mortem at Jordan University Hospital's forensic medicine unit. The Prosecutor's Office must authorise release before embalming can begin.

### Deaths in the southern desert and Petra

Petra and Wadi Rum attract significant British tourism. Deaths at these sites require transfer to Amman before any official process starts. Local police secure the scene; the body is then transferred by road to the capital. This adds one to three days to the overall timeline.

### Cremation

Cremation is not widely available in Jordan. Islamic law prohibits it for Muslims. Facilities for non-Muslims are extremely limited. Most UK families repatriate the body rather than pursue cremation in-country.

### Documentation

The core documents required are: death certificate from the Civil Status and Passports Department, Ministry of Interior release (for unnatural deaths), post-mortem report if ordered, Ministry of Health export permit, and embalming certificate. British Embassy Amman issues UK consular documentation.

### Working with the British Embassy Amman

Address: Abdoun, PO Box 87, Amman 11118. Phone: +962 6 590 9200. FCDO 24-hour emergency: +44 1908 516666.

The Embassy registers the death with UK authorities and provides guidance on Jordanian government requirements. Consular staff cannot arrange repatriation directly but can provide lists of local funeral directors experienced with international transfers.
