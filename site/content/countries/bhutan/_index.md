## Repatriation from Bhutan to the UK

Bhutan is a small Buddhist kingdom in the eastern Himalayas. It controls tourism strictly through a daily visitor tariff — all foreign visitors must be on an approved tour with a licensed Bhutanese guide. This system means that the death of a foreign national in Bhutan is always known to the tour operator immediately, and the tour operator becomes the first point of contact alongside the FCDO.

For urgent consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British High Commission New Delhi (+91 11 2419 2100) handles consular matters for Bhutan on a non-resident basis.

### The Tour Operator's Role

Because every foreign visitor is on a registered tour, the licensed Bhutanese tour operator has both a legal and practical role when a foreign national dies. The operator will liaise with local authorities and can facilitate access to documentation processes. Notify the tour operator immediately alongside the FCDO.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration in Bhutan is handled by the **Civil Registration and Census Authority (CRCA)**. The CRCA issues the official death certificate. Where death is not from natural causes, the **Royal Bhutan Police** must investigate and clear the case before the body can be released.

Documentation is primarily in Dzongkha with some English elements. All documents require certified English translation.

### Paro Airport: Limited Capacity

**Paro International Airport (PBH)** is one of the most challenging airports in the world — the approach requires special certification and only a small number of pilots are qualified to operate there. This limits the airlines serving Paro to Drukair (Royal Bhutan Airlines) and Bhutan Airlines, operating to Delhi, Kolkata, Bangkok, and Kathmandu.

Cargo capacity is limited. Advance coordination with carriers is essential for human remains transport.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Civil Registration and Census Authority (CRCA) |
| Royal Bhutan Police clearance | Royal Bhutan Police (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate (where available) | Local mortuary |
| Export authorisation | Relevant authority |
| Consular death registration | BHC New Delhi |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Cremation

There are no commercial cremation facilities in Bhutan suitable for foreign nationals. Full body repatriation to the UK is required in all cases.
