## Repatriation from Armenia: What Families Need to Know

Armenia is a small country in the South Caucasus with a growing British visitor profile. Yerevan has become a destination for cultural tourists and short-break travellers. The British Embassy Yerevan is experienced with consular death cases. The core challenge for most families is one that has nothing to do with consular relations: the Armenian script.

### The Translation Problem

Act on this immediately.

Armenian documentation — death certificates, police records, post-mortem reports, Ministry of Health export permits — is written in the Armenian script. This script is unique to Armenia and has no relation to Georgian, Cyrillic, Arabic, or any other alphabet. UK-accredited Armenian legal translators are rare. Fewer practitioners hold the required accreditation than for Georgian, and significantly fewer than for most European languages.

Contact the British Embassy Yerevan at the point of death notification. Ask for their current recommended translators before documents are issued. A translator engaged in advance can begin work the moment documents are ready. Starting the search after documents arrive adds days — sometimes a week or more — to the timeline.

### Forensic Processing

All deaths that are unnatural, unexplained, or involve any third-party element require post-mortem by the Republican Institute of Forensic Medical Expertise in Yerevan. This is the national forensic facility. Regional cases are transferred to Yerevan for post-mortem if forensic examination is required.

### Mountain and Regional Deaths

Mount Aragats (4,090m) is the highest peak in Armenia and attracts British trekkers. Deaths on or near Aragats require rescue, evacuation, and transfer to Yerevan before documentation can begin. Lake Sevan — the largest lake in the Caucasus, roughly 60 km east of Yerevan — is a popular tourist destination and site of drowning incidents.

Deaths in the Debed Canyon (northeast Armenia) and border regions require road transfer to Yerevan. The border with Turkey is largely closed. The border with Azerbaijan is conflict-sensitive.

### Cargo Routing

There are no direct cargo flights between Armenia and the UK. Repatriation cargo connects via Vienna (Austrian Airlines) or Frankfurt (Lufthansa). Wizz Air operates London Luton to Yerevan but, as a low-cost carrier, does not handle human remains cargo.
