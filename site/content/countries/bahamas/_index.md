## Repatriation from the Bahamas: What Families Need to Know

The Bahamas is a Commonwealth country with an English official language, a British High Commission in Nassau, and well-established consular procedures. For deaths on New Providence (Nassau) or Grand Bahama (Freeport), the administrative process is relatively clear. The complexity increases with distance from Nassau: the Out Islands (Family Islands) have no forensic or documentation capacity, and every case from the outer archipelago requires transfer to the main island first.

Drowning, snorkelling, and water sports deaths account for the majority of British tourist fatalities in the Bahamas.

### The British High Commission

The British High Commission Nassau handles consular cases with regularity. The Bahamas is a popular British holiday, cruise, and second-home destination. The High Commission team has established working relationships with Bahamian authorities.

Contact the High Commission emergency line as the first priority. They will issue a Certificate of No Impediment to Cremation if needed and advise on current documentation requirements.

### Forensic Process

For unnatural or unexplained deaths, the Department of Pathology at Princess Margaret Hospital (PMH) in Nassau conducts post-mortems. This covers New Providence and cases transferred from most Out Islands.

Grand Bahama is different. Deaths in Freeport and Grand Bahama are processed through Rand Memorial Hospital. Rand Memorial has its own pathology department. Do not assume Nassau-based contacts apply to Grand Bahama cases.

For complex or contested forensic cases, Bahamian authorities may involve forensic pathologists from Florida given the geographical proximity to Miami.

### Out Island Transfer

The Bahamas consists of around 700 islands and cays. British tourists visit the Exumas (Staniel Cay, Exuma Cays), Abacos (Marsh Harbour, Treasure Cay, Man-O-War Cay), Eleuthera, Long Island, Cat Island, and other Family Islands. None of these have local forensic or documentation capacity.

Deaths on Out Islands require:
1. Air or boat transfer to Nassau (or Freeport for northern Grand Bahama);
2. Post-mortem at PMH or Rand Memorial if required;
3. Documentation and export permit process in Nassau.

Inter-island charter flights serve most inhabited islands. Boat transfers apply on smaller cays.

### The Documentation Chain

1. Death certificate from the Registrar General's Department.
2. Police investigation report (for unnatural deaths).
3. Post-mortem report from PMH or Rand Memorial if required.
4. Ministry of Health export permit.
5. Embalming certificate.

All documentation is in English.

### Routing

Lynden Pindling International Airport (NAS) in Nassau is the main international cargo hub. There are no nonstop scheduled flights from the Bahamas to the UK. All cargo connects via Miami (American Airlines) or New York (British Airways or American Airlines connecting to London Heathrow). Pre-book cargo capacity before confirming dates.
