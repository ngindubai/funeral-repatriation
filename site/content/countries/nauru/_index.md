## Repatriation from Nauru to the UK

Nauru is the world's smallest island state by area — a raised coral island of 21 km² in the central Pacific Ocean. It sits roughly 300 km south of the Marshall Islands and 1,300 km east of Papua New Guinea. The entire island can be circumnavigated by road in around 20 minutes. Population is approximately 10,000.

Nauru has a complex modern history: a phosphate mining boom brought extraordinary wealth in the 1970s and 1980s, followed by economic collapse as deposits depleted. The island is also known internationally as the site of Australia's offshore immigration processing centres. British nationals visit in very small numbers, mostly for business connected to the offshore processing operation or for specialist diving.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British High Commission Suva, Fiji (+679 322 9100) handles consular matters for Nauru on a non-resident basis.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is handled by the **Nauru Vital Statistics Registry**. Non-natural deaths require **Nauru Police Force** investigation and clearance. Documentation is in English. Australian administrative and legal influence remains significant.

### Airline Access

**Nauru International Airport (INU)** is served exclusively by **Nauru Airlines**. Routes include Nauru to Brisbane and Nauru to Honiara/Tarawa. Frequency is limited — typically a few services per week. Cargo capacity is restricted.

All international repatriation routing goes via **Brisbane (BNE)**, where standard international air freight services can carry the repatriation to UK airports.

### Mortuary Infrastructure

Nauru has very limited medical and mortuary infrastructure. Complex medical cases have historically been evacuated to Australia or Fiji rather than managed locally. A specialist firm will need to coordinate in advance to establish what embalming and holding capacity is available at the time of death.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Nauru Vital Statistics Registry |
| Police clearance | Nauru Police Force (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Health authority |
| Export permit | Relevant Nauruan authority |
| Australian transit documentation | Australian Border Force (if via Brisbane) |
| Consular death registration | BHC Suva, Fiji |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Australian Transit

Because all routing goes via Brisbane, Australian transit documentation for human remains must be arranged. Australia has detailed requirements for the import and transit of human remains through its airports.

### Cremation

Cremation is not available in Nauru. Full body repatriation via Brisbane is required.
