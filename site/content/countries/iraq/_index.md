## Repatriation from Iraq to the UK

Iraq is a very high complexity repatriation destination due to active security threats across significant parts of the country, strong Islamic burial time requirements, and complex Arabic and Kurdish documentation processes. That said, the Kurdistan Region in the north is substantially more manageable, and the British Embassy in Baghdad and British Consulate in Erbil both provide resident consular support.

For urgent consular assistance, contact the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (24 hours). The British Embassy Baghdad can be reached on +964 790 192 6280. The British Consulate Erbil can be reached on +964 750 444 7101.

### The Regional Picture

Iraq is not a uniform environment. The repatriation process differs significantly by region:

- **Kurdistan Region (Erbil, Sulaymaniyah, Duhok)**: Significantly more stable. Civil administration functions well. The British Consulate in Erbil provides direct, resident consular support. Timelines at the shorter end of the range.
- **Baghdad and central Iraq**: Higher complexity. Militia presence in some suburban areas. Civil administration functions but can be slower. Use the British Embassy Baghdad.
- **Southern Iraq (Basra, Najaf, Karbala)**: Iranian-backed militia groups active in some areas. Documentation through Basra and provincial offices. Routing via Dubai.
- **Northern/western Iraq (Mosul, Anbar, Nineveh Plains)**: Highest complexity. Residual ISIL activity in some areas. Disputed territory between federal and KRG administrations. Specialist handling required.

### Islamic Burial Requirements

Approximately 97% of Iraq's population is Muslim. Islamic tradition requires burial within 24 hours of death. For repatriation to the UK, which takes considerably longer than 24 hours, the family must communicate the intention to repatriate clearly and early. Islamic scholars permit delay for repatriation purposes where the family is based abroad. A specialist repatriation firm experienced in Iraq will manage this sensitively.

### The Legal Framework

Death certification in Iraq is handled by the **Civil Status Directorate (Mudiriyyat al-Ahwal al-Madaniyyah)**. Where death is not from natural causes, the **Medico-Legal Directorate** must issue clearance before the body can be released. The Ministry of Interior issues the export permit.

Documentation is in Arabic in most of Iraq, and in Kurdish in the Kurdistan Region. Certified English translations are required for all documents presented to UK authorities.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate (shahada wafah) | Civil Status Directorate |
| Medico-Legal clearance (if applicable) | Medico-Legal Directorate |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Ministry of Interior |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy Baghdad / Consulate Erbil |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Cremation

Cremation is not practised in Iraq and no cremation facilities exist. All British nationals who die in Iraq must be repatriated in full for burial or cremation in the UK.
