## Repatriation from Georgia: What Families Need to Know

Georgia is a medium-complexity repatriation with one critical practical issue that separates it from most other destinations: the documentation is in Georgian Mkhedruli script, and very few UK-based translators are accredited in Georgian legal language. This is not a language problem that can be resolved with a general translation service. Act on it immediately.

Georgia is not an EU member and has not signed the Strasbourg Convention. The full consular processing pathway applies. The British Embassy in Tbilisi is experienced with British national deaths, particularly since Georgia's tourism footprint among British visitors has grown significantly since 2020.

### Georgian Language: The Specialist Translator Issue

Georgian is written in the Mkhedruli script, unique to Georgia. It is not related to any Cyrillic or Latin script. Most document translation agencies do not have accredited Georgian legal translators on staff.

For repatriation purposes, you need a translator who is:
- Accredited for Georgian legal and administrative language (not just conversational Georgian);
- Recognised by the British Embassy Tbilisi as appropriate for death registration documents;
- Available to work with the Georgian Civil Registry Agency document format.

Contact the British Embassy Tbilisi immediately and ask for their current list of approved interpreters and translators for Georgian legal documents. Do not wait until the documents are issued. A three to five day wait for a specialist translator, on top of the documentation processing time, is a real risk.

### The Levan Samkharauli National Forensics Bureau

The Levan Samkharauli National Forensics Bureau in Tbilisi is Georgia's national forensic medicine service. It conducts post-mortems for all unnatural, suspicious, or sudden unexplained deaths. The Bureau is a well-functioning institution by regional standards. Post-mortem turnaround for straightforward cases is typically five to ten working days.

### The Documentation Chain

1. Death certificate from the Civil Registry Agency (LEPL).
2. Police investigation closure (for unnatural deaths).
3. National Forensics Bureau post-mortem report if required.
4. Ministry of Labour, Health and Social Affairs export permit.
5. Certified English translations of all Georgian documents (Mkhedruli script).
6. Embalming certificate.

### Mountain Deaths in Kazbegi and Svaneti

Georgia's Caucasus Mountains are a growing destination for British trekkers and climbers. Two areas produce the most incidents.

Kazbegi (Stepantsminda) is the main trekking hub, about three hours from Tbilisi. The Kazbegi Mountain Rescue Service is active and well-organised. Recovery from the main trails around Mount Kazbek typically takes 24 to 48 hours in good conditions.

Svaneti is significantly more remote. The main town is Mestia, reached by a long mountain road from Zugdidi. Deaths in the high Svaneti backcountry — the trails toward Ushba, or toward the Russian border — can involve very extended recovery operations. Assume 72 hours minimum for remote Svaneti recovery; longer in winter.

Deaths at Gudauri ski resort (Georgia's main ski area, about two hours from Tbilisi) involve ski patrol response. Gudauri is well-developed by regional standards and has an established resort medical facility.

### Routing

Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) is the main hub. Wizz Air operates direct flights between London Gatwick (LGW) and TBS. Kutaisi International Airport (KUT) also receives Wizz Air services from the UK. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is an alternative. Human remains cargo must be pre-booked; confirm the carrier's specific requirements before finalising the repatriation plan.
