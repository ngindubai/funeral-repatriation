## Repatriation from Kuwait: What Families Need to Know

Kuwait is a medium-complexity repatriation. It has a direct UK flight link via Kuwait Airways to London Heathrow, which simplifies logistics compared with Gulf states that require connecting via Dubai or Doha. But the documentation process involves two separate authorities for unnatural deaths — the Criminal Investigation Department and the Public Prosecution — and all documents are in Arabic, requiring certified translation before UK entry formalities can be completed.

The British community in Kuwait is substantial and predominantly professional: oil sector, finance, construction, and diplomatic staff. This gives the British Embassy Kuwait City a practised familiarity with repatriation cases, which is a practical advantage.

### Who Manages Unnatural Deaths

The Ministry of Interior's Criminal Investigation Department (CID) manages the scene and opens an investigation for any death that is not confirmed natural by a Ministry of Health physician. Once the CID investigation is concluded, the case moves to the Public Prosecution (Al-Niyaba Al-Amma), which must issue a formal release order before the body can be released for preparation and transport.

This two-stage authorisation process — CID then Public Prosecution — adds time compared to jurisdictions where a single authority handles both functions.

### The Documentation Chain

1. Death certificate from the Ministry of Health, Kuwait.
2. CID clearance letter (for unnatural deaths).
3. Public Prosecution release order (for unnatural deaths).
4. Ministry of Health export permit (laissez-passer mortuaire).
5. Embalming certificate.
6. Certified English translations of all Arabic documents.

All of these must be in place before cargo booking can be confirmed.

### Routing

Kuwait International Airport (KWI) is the only international airport. Kuwait Airways operates direct flights to London Heathrow. This is a genuine advantage over Gulf states without direct UK routes. However, cargo capacity for human remains must be pre-booked through approved IATA cargo handlers. Availability is not guaranteed on every departure date.

### Cremation

Cremation is prohibited under Islamic law and is not available in Kuwait. All repatriations from Kuwait involve full body transport.
