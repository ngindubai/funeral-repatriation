## Repatriation from Ecuador

Ecuador has two main cargo hubs: Quito Mariscal Sucre International Airport (UIO) in the Andean highlands, and Guayaquil Jose Joaquin de Olmedo International Airport (GYE) on the Pacific coast. For repatriation purposes, the relevant airport depends on where the death occurred and where post-mortem facilities are used.

### The Fiscalia requirement

For any unnatural, violent, or suspicious death, the Fiscalia General del Estado (Prosecutor's Office) takes jurisdiction. The Fiscalia orders a post-mortem at the Instituto Nacional de Medicina Legal (INML) in Quito or Guayaquil. The body cannot be released for repatriation until the Fiscalia issues its release order. This is the single most important document in an Ecuadorian repatriation.

In straightforward accident cases, the Fiscalia can process within 10 to 14 days. In cases with criminal suspicion, timelines extend significantly.

### Galapagos Islands deaths

The Galapagos are 1,000 kilometres from the Ecuadorian mainland. Deaths on the islands require evacuation by commercial flight or charter to Guayaquil airport. Local police on the islands notify the Fiscalia on the mainland. The evacuation adds two to four days before any official mainland process can begin.

### Routing to the UK

No direct UK flights from Ecuador. American Airlines via Miami to Heathrow is the standard cargo route. Avianca via Bogota and Iberia via Madrid are alternatives. Allow one to two additional days for multi-leg cargo.
