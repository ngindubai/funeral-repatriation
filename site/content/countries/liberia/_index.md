Liberia is an English-speaking West African republic on the Atlantic coast. Founded by freed American and Caribbean slaves in the 1820s, it has an English-language legal and administrative system. The British Embassy in Monrovia is a resident mission.

For UK families, Liberia is among the more manageable West African repatriation destinations — English throughout, a resident Embassy, and a clear civil registration system. The key practical constraint is the country's limited infrastructure outside Monrovia.

## Who to contact first

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: **020 7008 5000** (available 24 hours). The British Embassy Monrovia: +231 886 483 113.

## British Embassy Monrovia

The British Embassy is a resident mission in Monrovia. It registers the death for UK purposes and provides liaison with Liberian authorities. English-language documentation means no translation is needed for UK administrative purposes.

## Civil registration

Liberia uses an American-influenced common law system with English-language civil registration. Death registration goes through the Vital Statistics Division. The attending physician certifies cause of death in English. The Vital Statistics Division issues the death certificate in English.

For non-natural deaths, the Liberia National Police (LNP) must attend and investigate before the death certificate is issued. For straightforward cases in Monrovia, police clearance typically takes 3 to 7 days.

## Ministry of Health export permit

Once death registration and police clearance are complete, the Ministry of Health issues the export permit. A licensed funeral director embalms and prepares the body.

## Outside Monrovia: road transfer to capital

Liberia's road network is limited, particularly in rural areas, rubber plantation counties (Margibi, Bong), and mining regions. For deaths outside Monrovia, road transfer to the capital is required before preparation and export processing can begin. Condition of roads varies by season — the rainy season (May to October) can make some routes impassable.

## Routing from Roberts International Airport

Roberts International Airport (ROB) is 60 kilometres east of Monrovia. Ground transfer between Monrovia and the airport takes 1 to 2 hours.

- Brussels Airlines to Brussels — primary UK cargo route, connecting London
- Ethiopian Airlines to Addis Ababa (ADD) — connecting to London
- Royal Air Maroc to Casablanca (CMN)

## What a specialist firm does

A specialist firm coordinates with the British Embassy, LNP, and Ministry of Health in Monrovia. For rural cases, they assess road transfer feasibility and timing. English documentation means the process is administratively simpler than Francophone West African destinations — but local logistics knowledge remains important.
