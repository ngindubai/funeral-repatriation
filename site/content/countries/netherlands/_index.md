The Netherlands is a major destination for British tourists, business travellers, and short-term expatriates. Amsterdam draws significant visitor numbers year-round. The British Embassy is in The Hague, and a consulate operates in Amsterdam.

Repatriation from the Netherlands is generally efficient. The Dutch bureaucratic system is organised, documentation is available in multiple languages, and Amsterdam Schiphol is one of Europe's principal cargo hubs. Direct KLM Cargo flights to Heathrow, Manchester, and Edinburgh mean freight times are short and costs are comparatively low.

The Dutch death certificate, the Akte van Overlijden, is issued by the civil registry (gemeente). It shows cause of death and is available with an English translation. The document is prepared by the local municipality where the death occurred, and processing takes one to three working days.

The main complication in the Netherlands is judicial oversight of unnatural deaths. Any death in a public place, a sudden death, or a death where the cause is unclear triggers automatic police notification and referral to the public prosecutor (officier van justitie). In Amsterdam, deaths involving drugs are automatically classified as requiring investigation. The public prosecutor decides whether a judicial post-mortem is needed.

Contact the British Embassy in The Hague at +31 70 427 0427. The consulate in Amsterdam can assist with cases arising in the city. The Embassy will provide a list of funeral directors familiar with UK repatriation requirements.
