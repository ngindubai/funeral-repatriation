Oman is one of the Gulf's most stable and visitor-friendly countries. British nationals travel for Muscat's historic quarter, the Wahiba Sands, the Hajar Mountains, and the diving at Musandam. There is also a long-established British expat community. Repatriation from Oman is manageable for experienced specialists, though the absence of direct UK flights adds a cargo routing step.

## Legal framework

Oman is not a signatory to the Strasbourg Agreement. Repatriation is governed by Omani domestic law and bilateral consular conventions. The Civil Status Authority, Royal Oman Police, and Ministry of Health are all involved.

## Death registration

Deaths are registered with the Civil Status Authority (Hayat al-Ahwal al-Madaniya). The death certificate is issued in Arabic. For hospital deaths, registration proceeds through the hospital. For unnatural or sudden deaths, the Royal Oman Police take jurisdiction at the scene.

## British Embassy Muscat

The British Embassy in Muscat must be notified immediately. Address: PO Box 185, Mina Al Fahal, Muscat 116. Phone: +968 2460 9000. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 1908 516666.

## Royal Oman Police and post-mortem

For violent, sudden, or unexplained deaths, the Royal Oman Police conduct an investigation. A forensic post-mortem may be ordered. The Police must formally release the body before any export process can begin. Police investigations are typically completed within 7 to 14 days for clear-cut cases.

## Ministry of Health No Objection Certificate

The Ministry of Health issues the No Objection Certificate (NOC) required for exporting human remains. The NOC requires: Arabic death certificate, Police release (if applicable), embalming certificate, and freedom from contagious disease clearance. Allow 3 to 7 days for the NOC.

## Embalming

Embalming is required for international repatriation. Licensed mortuaries in Muscat are experienced with international procedures. For deaths outside Muscat (Salalah, Nizwa, Sohar), the body is typically transferred to Muscat for embalming.

## Routing to the UK

No direct flights operate from Oman to the UK. Cargo routes via Dubai (Emirates) or Doha (Qatar Airways) to London Heathrow are standard. Both hubs have established human remains cargo handling procedures. Muscat International Airport (MCT) has good freight capacity.
