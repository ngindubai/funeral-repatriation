## Repatriation from Uganda

Uganda is a Commonwealth country and the British representative office is the High Commission in Kampala. Entebbe International Airport (EBB) has no direct UK flights; the main cargo routing is Kenya Airways via Nairobi Jomo Kenyatta, or Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa.

Uganda's main draw for UK tourists is wildlife: gorilla trekking in Bwindi Impenetrable National Park, safaris in Queen Elizabeth National Park and Murchison Falls, and white-water rafting on the Nile near Jinja. Deaths in these settings add specific procedural complexity.

### National park deaths

Deaths in any Ugandan national park require involvement from the Uganda Wildlife Authority (UWA) alongside the Uganda Police Force CID. The UWA must certify what occurred in the park before the body is released. This adds one to three days to the timeline in addition to the standard police investigation.

Gorilla trekking deaths in Bwindi are rare but do occur. Altitude (Bwindi sits at approximately 2,400 metres), exertion, and medical incidents during treks are the usual causes. The remoteness of Bwindi adds transfer time to Kampala.

### Official process in Kampala

Deaths are registered with the Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB). The Uganda Police Force CID investigates unnatural deaths. Post-mortems are conducted at Mulago National Referral Hospital's Department of Forensic Pathology in Kampala. The Ministry of Health issues the export permit once all documentation is complete.
