## Repatriation from Algeria: What Families Need to Know

Algeria is a high-complexity repatriation shaped by three main factors: Arabic and French documentation requiring certified English translations, a decentralised administration where 58 wilayas (provinces) each operate their own body transport permit process, and the absence of direct UK flights.

Algeria has one of the largest Algerian-heritage communities in Western Europe. Many British nationals of Algerian descent travel regularly to visit family in Algeria. This makes Algeria a higher-volume repatriation origin than its tourism figures would suggest.

### Who Investigates Unnatural Deaths

Jurisdiction depends on location. In cities, the Police Judiciaire (judicial police) investigates unnatural or suspicious deaths. In rural and regional areas, the Gendarmerie Nationale takes over. Both issue clearance letters once investigation is complete. The distinction matters because the Gendarmerie, covering most of Algeria's vast territory, varies more in processing speed than the urban Police Judiciaire.

Post-mortems for complex cases go to the Institut National de Médecine Légale at CHU Mustapha in Algiers. Regional hospitals in Oran and Constantine handle local forensic cases.

### The Wilaya System

Algeria's 58 wilayas each issue body transport permits independently. The laissez-passer mortuaire is issued at wilaya level, not by a central national authority. Algiers wilaya processes requests relatively quickly. Remote southern wilayas — Tamanrasset, Illizi, Adrar — move more slowly and have less experience with international repatriation cases. Where a death occurs outside Algiers, transferring the body to the Algiers wilaya before initiating the permit process is sometimes the most practical approach.

Ministry of Interior approval is required on top of the wilaya permit for international transport. This adds a step that can take three to seven days.

### Translation Requirements

Every document is in Arabic. Many are also in French. Certified English translations of both are required before UK entry formalities can be completed. Translators must hold recognised qualifications (CIOL or equivalent). A specialist repatriation firm with established Algeria contacts will have translator relationships in place. Building these from scratch in the UK takes time and risks producing translations that do not meet the required standard.

### Routing

Houari Boumediene Airport (ALG) in Algiers is the main international cargo hub. Air Algérie operates cargo services via Paris Charles de Gaulle and Frankfurt. Air France and Transavia provide additional options via Paris. Oran (ORN) and Constantine (CZL) airports offer regional alternatives for deaths in western or eastern Algeria.

### FCDO Restrictions

The FCDO advises against all travel to Algeria's southern border regions — areas bordering Mali, Mauritania, Niger, and Libya — due to the presence of armed groups. Deaths in these areas involve military or security force coordination and cannot be managed through standard civilian channels. Deaths in Kabylie (the Berber highland region east of Algiers) are also subject to periodic FCDO advisories.
