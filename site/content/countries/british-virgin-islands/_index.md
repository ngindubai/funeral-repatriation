## Repatriation from the British Virgin Islands to the UK

The British Virgin Islands (BVI) is a British Overseas Territory in the northeastern Caribbean, comprising the main island of Tortola along with Virgin Gorda, Jost Van Dyke, Anegada, and around 50 smaller cays and islets. Road Town on Tortola is the capital. BVI is one of the Caribbean's most popular sailing destinations.

As a British Overseas Territory, the Crown's interests are represented by the Governor. Consular functions are handled via the FCO in London. Contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours) immediately after a death.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is handled by the **Civil Registry and Passport Office** in Road Town, Tortola, under the BVI Government. English common law applies throughout. Non-natural deaths require investigation and clearance by the **Royal Virgin Islands Police Force** before funeral arrangements proceed.

### Outer Islands

Deaths on **Virgin Gorda**, **Jost Van Dyke**, **Anegada**, or remote cays require transfer by water taxi or light aircraft to Tortola for registration and mortuary services. This adds time and cost.

### No Cremation Facilities

No cremation facilities are available in the BVI. Full body repatriation to the UK is required.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Civil Registry and Passport Office, BVI Government |
| Police Force clearance | Royal Virgin Islands Police Force (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local funeral director |
| Freedom from infection certificate | BVI health authority |
| Export permit | BVI Government |
| UK Coroner notification | FCO / HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

**Terrance B. Lettsome International Airport (EIS)** on Beef Island near Tortola has connections to **Antigua (ANU)**, from which **British Airways** operates direct service to London Gatwick (LGW). Routing via Miami or New York is an alternative.
