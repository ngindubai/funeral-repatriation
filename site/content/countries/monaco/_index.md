## Repatriation from Monaco to the UK

Monaco is a city-state of just over 2 square kilometres on the French Riviera, bordered entirely by France. It has its own civil and legal system but draws heavily on French civil law. A significant number of British nationals live in Monaco as residents, and many more visit each year.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British Embassy Paris (+33 1 44 51 31 00) handles consular matters for Monaco on a non-resident basis.

### The Legal Framework

Civil registration in Monaco is handled by the **Direction de l'Etat Civil** (Civil Registry Office). The Direction issues the official death certificate. Where death is not from natural causes, the **Sûreté Publique** (Monaco police) investigates and must authorise release of the body.

Documentation is in French and requires certified English translation.

### Routing: Nice in 25 Minutes

Monaco has no commercial airport. **Nice Côte d'Azur Airport (NCE)** is approximately 25 minutes by road or by helicopter (Monaco has helipad facilities). Nice offers multiple direct daily flights to London and other UK airports. Several airlines operate this route including British Airways, easyJet, and Vueling.

### Cremation Options

Monaco has no cremation facilities. If cremation is required, transfer to Nice or Cannes in France is the standard option. Cremation in France follows French civil law procedures, after which the ashes are repatriated to the UK. This adds one step but is well-established and manageable.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Direction de l'Etat Civil (French) |
| Police clearance | Sûreté Publique (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Health authority |
| Export authorisation | Relevant authority |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, Paris |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |
