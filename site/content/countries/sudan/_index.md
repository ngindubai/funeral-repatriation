Sudan has been in civil war since April 2023, when fighting broke out between the Sudan Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF). The British Embassy in Khartoum suspended all operations and was evacuated. Khartoum International Airport has been non-operational since that point.

Repatriation from Sudan in current conditions is among the most operationally demanding situations any specialist firm faces. This page describes the process as it stands — but families must understand that standard procedures do not apply, and many cases will not be achievable through any pathway in the short term.

## The consular position

The British Embassy Khartoum suspended operations in April 2023. There is no British consular presence in Sudan. Consular assistance for British nationals is provided remotely through FCDO London.

When a British national dies in Sudan, the next step is to call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: **020 7008 5000**. This line operates 24 hours a day.

Do not travel to Sudan to assist with a repatriation. FCDO advises against all travel to the entire country.

## What has changed since April 2023

Before the civil war, Sudan was a functioning — if challenging — repatriation destination with a resident British Embassy and operational airports. The situation since April 2023 is categorically different:

- **Khartoum Airport**: Sustained damage during fighting. Non-operational. No flights.
- **British Embassy Khartoum**: Evacuated. No staff present. No on-the-ground consular support.
- **SAF and RSF territorial split**: Different authorities control different parts of the country. The applicable authority depends on who controls the area where the death occurred.
- **Port Sudan**: Currently under SAF control and has limited scheduled international flights. This is the only practical exit point at present.
- **Darfur**: Severe ongoing violence. Bodies in this region may be unrecoverable.

## What specialist firms can and cannot do

A small number of firms operate in active conflict zones. In Sudan, their capabilities are genuinely limited. What they may be able to do:
- Maintain contact with local agents in areas accessible to SAF or RSF
- Coordinate body preservation and holding while documentation is obtained
- Work with Egyptian, Qatari, or Saudi connections to establish routing via Port Sudan
- Coordinate with FCDO London and any available Sudanese civil registration offices

What is not possible in current conditions:
- Expediting official documentation in areas with no functioning government
- Extracting bodies from active combat zones
- Providing any timeline certainty

## If the death occurred before April 2023

Cases where a death occurred before the civil war and where remains are still in Sudan face the additional complication that documentation issued by Khartoum pre-war authorities may or may not be accepted by current controlling authorities. Specialist firms with Sudan-specific knowledge should be consulted.

## Document framework (where accessible)

| Document | Issuer |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Civil Registry (where functioning) |
| Medical certificate of cause of death | Physician/medical officer (where accessible) |
| Authority clearance | SAF or RSF authority depending on area |
| Export permit | Ministry of Health representative (where accessible) |
| Certified translations | Certified Arabic-to-English translator |

## What to do first

Call FCDO: **020 7008 5000**. Provide the full name and location of the deceased. The FCDO will confirm current consular capability and, where possible, refer to specialist firms known to operate in the region.

Do not contact Sudanese authorities directly without specialist guidance.
