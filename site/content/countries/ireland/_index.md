Ireland is the nearest international repatriation origin for UK families. The English language, similar legal traditions, frequent direct transport links, and short distances combine to make the repatriation process more accessible than any other international destination.

Road and ferry repatriation is possible in some cases, with routes from Dublin, Rosslare, or Cork to Holyhead, Fishguard, or Swansea. This removes air freight entirely and can be the most cost-effective option when the Coroner has released the body without delay. Your UK funeral director can advise on whether road and ferry repatriation is appropriate for your family's circumstances.

The Irish Coroner's Act 1962 (as amended) governs sudden and unnatural deaths. The Coroner must be satisfied with the cause of death before releasing the body. Road accidents and drownings are the most common triggers for Coroner involvement in British national deaths. Ireland's coastline and rural road network are frequent contexts for accidents involving UK visitors.

Since Brexit, GHIC (Global Health Insurance Card) no longer provides free healthcare in Ireland for UK nationals. Hospital costs before death can be significant. Comprehensive travel insurance is essential, including health cover and repatriation.

The British Embassy in Dublin operates at +353 1 205 3700. Ireland's English-speaking administration means families can often deal directly with Irish funeral directors and the Coroner's office without relying on Embassy intermediary.
