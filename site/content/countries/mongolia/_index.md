Mongolia has seen significant growth as a UK adventure travel destination. The Gobi desert, the central steppe, and the Altai mountain range attract independent travellers, organised tours, and overlanding groups. The British Embassy in Ulaanbaatar maintains a resident presence and has experience with UK traveller deaths.

The structural challenge specific to Mongolia is geography. Ulaanbaatar sits in the Tuul River valley in the north of the country. The Gobi desert begins approximately 500 kilometres south. The Altai range is in the west. There are very few paved roads outside the capital, and the country is the world's most sparsely populated. A death in a remote location means body recovery must happen before any standard repatriation process can begin.

## British Embassy Ulaanbaatar

The British Embassy Ulaanbaatar is a resident mission with experience of UK traveller deaths. The Embassy registers the death and provides the notifications required for UK purposes.

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first: **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). British Embassy Ulaanbaatar: +976 11 458133.

## Death registration

Registration goes through the Civil Registration and Information Department. For natural deaths in hospital or medical settings, the attending physician certifies cause of death and the Department issues the certificate typically within 1 to 3 working days.

## State Forensic Science Center: non-natural deaths

All violent, sudden, or unexplained deaths are referred to the State Forensic Science Center in Ulaanbaatar. The National Police Agency must attend the scene. The Center examines the body, certifies cause of death, and issues the forensic certificate before the death certificate can be issued.

This process typically takes 5 to 14 days. The body must be transported to Ulaanbaatar for examination if the death occurred outside the capital.

## Remote body recovery

A significant proportion of UK deaths in Mongolia occur in remote areas. The recovery sequence:

1. **Location confirmation** — GPS coordinate or nearest aimag (province) capital
2. **Local emergency contact** — Provincial governors have emergency services authority; the Mongolian National Emergency Management Agency (NEMA) coordinates helicopter and ground response
3. **Body transport to Ulaanbaatar** — helicopter for accessible locations; vehicle convoy for road-accessible locations; this adds 1 to 5 days
4. **State Forensic Science Center examination in Ulaanbaatar**
5. **Standard registration and export process**

Helicopter charter in Mongolia is limited. Weather in the Altai and central regions can close operations for 24 to 48 hours at a time, particularly in autumn and spring.

## Routing to the UK

Chinggis Khaan International Airport (UBN) opened in 2021, replacing the original Buyant-Ukhaa airport in central Ulaanbaatar. Primary cargo routes:
- Ulaanbaatar → Beijing Capital (PEK) via MIAT Mongolian Airlines or Air China → London
- Ulaanbaatar → Seoul Incheon (ICN) via Korean Air or MIAT → London

Both Beijing and Seoul offer multiple daily London connections. Beijing is the most common cargo transit point.

## Cremation

Cremation is available in Ulaanbaatar. Ashes can be transported to the UK as cremated remains — a simpler and faster process than body repatriation. For families open to this option, discuss it early with the specialist firm and British Embassy.
