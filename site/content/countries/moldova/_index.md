Moldova is a compact Eastern European country with a resident British Embassy in Chisinau and direct flights to London Luton. By Eastern European standards it is a manageable repatriation destination. The British national population in Moldova is small — primarily aid workers, EU programme staff, and a small number of business visitors — and the Embassy handles cases regularly.

The one significant complication unique to Moldova is Transnistria.

## The consular process

The British Embassy Chisinau is the primary consular contact. The Embassy registers the death and issues the notification needed for UK probate. For families in the UK, the FCDO Emergency Travel Line operates 24 hours: 020 7008 5000.

The British Embassy Chisinau can be contacted directly: +373 22 22 59 02.

## Death registration

Death registration in Moldova goes through the Civil Status Agency (Agenția Servicii Publice). The attending physician certifies cause of death. The Agency then issues the death certificate.

For natural deaths in medical settings, this moves quickly. For non-natural deaths, the State Medical Examiner Service becomes involved before the certificate can be issued.

## Non-natural deaths: State Medical Examiner

Violent, sudden, or unexplained deaths go to the State Medical Examiner Service. Moldova's forensic infrastructure is functional at the Chisinau level. For non-natural deaths:

1. Police attend and secure the scene
2. State Medical Examiner examines and certifies cause of death
3. Police confirm no criminal prosecution pending
4. Civil Status Agency issues death certificate
5. Ministry of Health issues export permit
6. Funeral director prepares body
7. Cargo routed via Chisinau Airport

Routine accident cases at the Medical Examiner typically take 5 to 10 days.

## Transnistria: the separatist region

Transnistria (officially Pridnestrovian Moldavian Republic) is a de facto independent separatist territory on Moldova's eastern border, controlled by Russian-aligned authorities since 1992. It is not recognised by the UK, the EU, or the United Nations.

Deaths in Transnistria involve Transnistrian authorities, not the Moldovan government. The British Embassy in Chisinau has limited ability to assist in Transnistria — Transnistrian authorities do not accept the Embassy's authority over their territory. FCDO advises against travel to Transnistria.

For families dealing with a death in Transnistria:
- Contact FCDO: 020 7008 5000 immediately
- Do not rely on Moldovan civil registration procedures — Transnistrian authorities issue their own documents
- A specialist firm with post-Soviet separatist region experience is required
- The Russian-language FCDO travel page for Moldova details current consular limitations

## Routing from Chisinau

Chisinau International Airport (KIV) has direct flights to London Luton (Wizz Air). Additional connections to UK airports via:
- Bucharest (TAROM, Air Moldova)
- Vienna (Austrian Airlines)
- Istanbul (Turkish Airlines)

Direct London connections make the routing relatively simple for standard cases.

## Documentation

Documents are in Romanian (Moldovan dialect). Russian is widely spoken and some documents may be in Russian. Certified English translations of all documents are required by UK authorities.

Core document set:

| Document | Language | Issuer |
|---|---|---|
| Death certificate | Romanian | Civil Status Agency |
| Medical Examiner certificate | Romanian | State Medical Examiner |
| Police clearance | Romanian | National Police |
| Export permit | Romanian | Ministry of Health |
| Embalming certificate | — | Licensed funeral director |

FCDO: 020 7008 5000. British Embassy Chisinau: +373 22 22 59 02.

For standard Moldova cases a specialist international firm with Eastern European experience is recommended. For Transnistria cases, only firms with confirmed post-Soviet separatist region experience should be engaged.
