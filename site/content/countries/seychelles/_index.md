## Repatriation from Seychelles: What Families Need to Know

Seychelles is a small island nation with a premium tourism reputation and a British High Commission with genuine consular experience. For straightforward deaths on Mahe, the process follows a clear path and English-language documentation removes one of the most common administrative delays.

Two factors complicate Seychelles repatriations more than most families anticipate: limited forensic capacity, and island dispersal.

### Limited Forensic Capacity

Seychelles Hospital in Victoria, Mahe is the only location in Seychelles capable of conducting forensic post-mortems. The facility can manage routine cases. But in complex or forensically ambiguous cases — disputed drowning, deaths with possible third-party involvement, cases where cause of death is unclear — the local forensic pathologist may recommend transferring the body to Reunion (about 1,600 km south-west) or South Africa for specialist analysis.

That transfer, if required, adds 10 to 20 days to the process. It is not a decision that can be rushed. The local authorities must make the call.

This is the key risk factor for Seychelles repatriations. Any death involving water — drowning, diving, snorkelling accident, current fatality — carries the risk of triggering a complex forensic assessment.

### Island Dispersal

Seychelles consists of 115 islands. The vast majority of British tourists stay on the three main islands: Mahe, Praslin, and La Digue. Deaths on Praslin can reach Mahe by air (PRI airport, short flight). Deaths on La Digue are more complicated.

La Digue has no airport. The only access is by boat from Praslin. In poor weather, sea crossings are delayed. A death on La Digue means at minimum a boat transfer to Praslin, then a flight to Mahe, before documentation can begin.

Deaths on private islands (Denis, Bird, Silhouette, Fregate) require charter boat or helicopter evacuation to Mahe, which adds both time and cost.

### The Documentation Chain

1. Death certificate from the Civil Status Office.
2. Seychelles Police Force report (for unnatural deaths).
3. Post-mortem report from Seychelles Hospital (or Reunion/South Africa if transferred).
4. Ministry of Health export permit.
5. Embalming certificate.

Documents are in English.

### The British High Commission

The British High Commission in Victoria handles consular cases. Seychelles is a Commonwealth country. The High Commission has experience with British national deaths given the premium tourist volumes — drowning, diving, and heat-related illness are the most commonly encountered cases.

### Routing

Seychelles International Airport (SEZ) is on Mahe. British Airways operates seasonal direct London Heathrow services. Emirates and Air Seychelles provide alternatives via connections. Pre-book cargo capacity before confirming departure dates.
