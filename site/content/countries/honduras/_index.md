Honduras draws large numbers of British travellers for one reason above most others: the Bay Islands. Roatan and Utila sit on the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef, the second largest in the world, and both islands have built their economies around scuba diving. Utila is particularly popular with budget divers seeking PADI certifications. Roatan attracts wealthier visitors and cruise ship passengers.

Most British deaths in Honduras occur in the Bay Islands. Scuba diving fatalities, both decompression illness and drowning, are the most common presentation for specialist repatriation companies.

## The Consular Position

There is no resident British Embassy in Honduras. The Honorary British Consul in Tegucigalpa handles initial consular contact. The British Embassy in Guatemala City has formal accreditation for Honduras and takes over serious cases.

Your first call should be to the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: 020 7008 5000.

## Bay Islands Deaths: The Island Transfer Problem

Deaths on Roatan, Utila, or Guanaja cannot be processed on the island for repatriation. The body must be transferred to the mainland first.

From Roatan, this means a flight or ferry to La Ceiba, then onward to Tegucigalpa or San Pedro Sula. Roatan Juan Manuel Galvez International Airport (RTB) connects to mainland Honduras via regional carriers. The transfer adds 2 to 4 days to the process before the Ministerio Publico or civil registration can begin.

## Documentation and the Forensic Process

All documentation is in Spanish. Death certificates, police reports, and any Ministerio Publico orders require certified translation into English for UK death registration, probate, and insurance purposes.

Natural deaths are registered at the Registro Civil. Unnatural deaths, including all diving fatalities, road accidents, and drownings, go to the Ministerio Publico and require a post-mortem at Hospital Escuela Universitario in Tegucigalpa or the regional hospital in San Pedro Sula.

The Ministerio Publico forensic process in Honduras can be slow. Allow 5 to 8 weeks for any unnatural death.

## Safety in Tegucigalpa and San Pedro Sula

Both cities have high violent crime rates. The FCDO issues specific warnings for Tegucigalpa's Comayaguela district and parts of San Pedro Sula. Deaths involving firearms or gang violence require full Ministerio Publico investigations that can take many months to complete.

## Cargo Routing

Honduras has no direct flights to the UK. All cargo routes via US hub airports — primarily Miami (MIA) and Houston (IAH) — on American Airlines or United Airlines cargo. Toncontin Airport (TGU) in Tegucigalpa has a difficult approach; some cargo operators prefer San Pedro Sula (SAP) routing.
