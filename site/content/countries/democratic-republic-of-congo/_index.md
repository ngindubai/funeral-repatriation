The Democratic Republic of Congo is a country of extremes for UK repatriation. Kinshasa, the capital, has a functioning British Embassy, an international airport with multiple UK connections, and an established legal process. Eastern DRC — home to one of the world's longest-running armed conflicts — is a different proposition entirely, where standard repatriation procedures may simply not apply.

This split defines everything about DRC repatriation. The first question for any family is where the death occurred. The answer determines whether repatriation is straightforward, very difficult, or not currently achievable.

## British Embassy Kinshasa

The British Embassy is resident in Kinshasa and operational. The Embassy registers the death and issues the notifications required for UK purposes.

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first: **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). British Embassy Kinshasa: +243 81 555 8900.

For deaths in eastern DRC, the Embassy will advise on current consular capacity in specific areas. Do not travel to eastern DRC.

## The Kinshasa process

For deaths in Kinshasa and accessible western DRC:

1. **Police Nationale Congolaise** attendance for non-natural deaths
2. **Institut National Médico-Légal (INML)** forensic certification — required for non-natural deaths. INML capacity in Kinshasa is limited; expect delays of 7 to 21 days
3. **Parquet** (state prosecutor) clearance confirming no criminal prosecution pending
4. **Civil Registry** death certificate issued
5. **Ministry of Health** export permit
6. Funeral director preparation and cargo booking via Ndjili Airport (FIH)

The INML step is the most consistent source of delay. The institution has limited capacity relative to the caseload in a city of 15 million. Priority processing is not reliably available.

## Eastern DRC: the conflict reality

The armed conflict in eastern DRC has continued in various forms since the 1990s. As of 2024-2025, North Kivu and South Kivu face active fighting involving the M23 movement and the Armed Forces of the DRC (FARDC), alongside numerous other armed groups. Ituri province faces similar conditions.

FCDO travel advisories:
- **Against all travel**: North Kivu (including Goma), South Kivu, Ituri province, areas within 50km of the DRC-Rwanda border, areas within 50km of the DRC-Uganda border in North Kivu

For deaths in these areas, the realistic position is:
- Goma (GOM airport) has limited scheduled service that suspends during active conflict
- Body recovery from combat areas requires specialist crisis recovery firms — a very small number operate in DRC
- Timelines are unpredictable and may extend to months
- In some cases, repatriation is not currently achievable

| Death certificate (acte de décès) | French | Civil Registry |
| Medical certificate | French | Attending physician |
| INML forensic certificate | French | Institut National Médico-Légal |
| Parquet clearance | French | Parquet (state prosecutor) |
| Police Nationale clearance | French | Police Nationale Congolaise |
| Ministry of Health export permit | French | Ministry of Health |
| Certified translations | English | Certified translator |

## Routing to the UK

From Kinshasa Ndjili International Airport (FIH):
- Brussels via Brussels Airlines (direct)
- Addis Ababa via Ethiopian Airlines
- Nairobi via Kenya Airways

Brussels Airlines is the primary and most reliable route for DRC-UK cargo.
