Ukraine is an active conflict zone. This is not a standard repatriation case. If a British national has died in Ukraine, call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line immediately: **020 7008 5000**. Do not wait.

Since Russia's full-scale invasion on 24 February 2022, FCDO has advised against all travel to Ukraine. Boryspil International Airport has been closed to civilian traffic since that date. Every repatriation from Ukraine now involves overland transit through Poland before air cargo can connect to the UK.

## Who dies in Ukraine?

The British nationals who die in Ukraine now are predominantly: long-term residents who were already living there before the invasion, journalists covering the war, humanitarian aid workers and volunteers, and NGO staff supporting the Ukrainian civilian population. The death toll from the conflict itself includes a small number of British volunteers who have joined Ukrainian defence efforts.

Each of these categories involves different legal and documentation frameworks. Your repatriation specialist must liaise with FCDO from the outset.

## The airport closure and overland routing

Boryspil International Airport (KBP) near Kyiv served as Ukraine's main international hub. It has been closed since 24 February 2022. There is no civilian air cargo leaving Ukraine directly.

The established exit route runs by road or rail to Poland. The most used crossing is Medyka on the Polish side (Ukrainian side: Shehyni), connecting to Przemysl and then Rzeszow. Rzeszow-Jasionka Airport in southeastern Poland handles cargo connections. Warsaw Chopin Airport and Krakow John Paul II Airport offer onward services to UK destinations.

Journey time from Kyiv to the Polish border: typically 8 to 12 hours by road depending on conditions and crossing wait times.

## British consular support

The British Embassy Kyiv suspended normal consular operations from its Kyiv premises following the invasion. Consular support for Ukraine cases is now coordinated via the FCDO Emergency Travel Line and a British presence maintained in Lviv.

For any death in Ukraine, the FCDO Emergency Travel Line (020 7008 5000) is your first call. Specialist repatriation companies must work directly with FCDO to navigate the atypical process.

## Documentation

All Ukrainian official documentation is in Ukrainian (Cyrillic script). Death certificates, forensic reports, and export permits all require certified translation into English. This adds time and cost to every case.

In government-held areas, administrative processes continue. In areas affected by active fighting or occupation, access to bodies and official documentation may be impossible until conditions change.
