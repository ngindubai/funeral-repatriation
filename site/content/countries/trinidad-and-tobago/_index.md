## Repatriation from Trinidad and Tobago: What Families Need to Know

Trinidad and Tobago is one of the most straightforward Caribbean repatriations for UK families. A resident British High Commission, direct British Airways flights, English documentation throughout, and a Commonwealth legal system all reduce both cost and complexity compared to most other Caribbean and Pacific destinations.

The key geography point: most British tourists visit Tobago, not Trinidad. The two islands are different in character — Trinidad is the larger, more industrial island and home to the capital Port of Spain; Tobago is the beach and dive island. Deaths on Tobago require inter-island transfer to Trinidad for major forensic examination, but this transfer is quick and routine.

### British High Commission Port of Spain

The British High Commission is on St Clair Avenue in Port of Spain, Trinidad. They are the first official point of contact for deaths of British nationals anywhere in Trinidad and Tobago. They have a well-established working relationship with the Eric Williams Medical Sciences Complex (EWMSC), which handles national forensic pathology.

### Tobago Deaths

Deaths on Tobago are first assessed at Tobago Regional Hospital in Scarborough. For any death requiring full forensic post-mortem — all unnatural deaths, and some natural-cause deaths where the certifying doctor requires it — the body must be transferred to EWMSC in Mount Hope, Trinidad. Caribbean Airlines operates a scheduled inter-island service multiple times daily; the flight is 20 minutes. This transfer is standard practice and adds one to two days to the overall timeline.

Speyside on Tobago is internationally rated for underwater photography and shark diving. Diving-related deaths at Speyside require specialist forensic pathological examination at EWMSC.

### The Coroner Process

All unnatural deaths in Trinidad and Tobago trigger a coroner inquest. This is a formal legal requirement and is not bypassed for foreign nationals. The process is well-understood by local funeral directors and by the British High Commission. It typically adds several days to the documentation phase but is not an unusual complication.

### Cargo Routing

British Airways operates London Gatwick to Port of Spain (POS) direct. This is a significant advantage: direct cargo routing to the UK without inter-island or third-country transit. Virgin Atlantic also serves Tobago (TAB) from Gatwick during peak season, though Tobago cargo typically goes through Trinidad regardless.
