Russia is one of the most operationally demanding repatriation destinations in the world for UK families. The combination of bureaucratic complexity, diplomatic strain since 2022, suspended direct flights, and the involvement of multiple government ministries means these cases require specialist handling from the outset.

This is not a destination where a standard funeral director should attempt to manage the case. Only firms with an established Russian contact network and experience navigating Ministry of Foreign Affairs procedures should be engaged.

## The consular position

The British Embassy in Moscow remains technically open but has operated with significantly reduced staff since 2022. Consular services continue, but the Embassy's capacity to intervene in complex cases is limited compared to previous years.

When a British national dies in Russia, the next of kin should contact the FCDO Emergency Travel Line (020 7008 5000) as the first call. The Embassy in Moscow can register the death and issue a notification for UK purposes, but it cannot expedite the Russian administrative process.

## The Russian documentation process

Death registration in Russia goes through the ZAGS Civil Registry Office (ЗАГС). This issues the primary death certificate — the Svidetelstvo o smerti. The process:

1. Attending physician certifies cause of death
2. ZAGS Civil Registry issues the Svidetelstvo o smerti
3. Ministry of Foreign Affairs apostille is applied to all documents for international use
4. For non-natural deaths, the Investigative Committee must issue clearance
5. Health authority export permit obtained
6. Body embalmed and prepared by licensed Russian mortuary
7. Cargo arranged via Istanbul, Dubai, or another non-direct routing hub

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs apostille stage alone typically adds 10 to 20 working days.

## Non-natural deaths: Investigative Committee involvement

All violent, sudden, or unexplained deaths in Russia are investigated by the Investigative Committee of Russia (ICR). The ICR must issue formal clearance — confirming no prosecution is pending — before the body can be released.

In straightforward accident cases, this is nominally a formality. In practice, the timeline is variable. Cases involving dual nationals, individuals connected to sensitive industry sectors, or deaths in restricted areas of Russia can face extended investigation periods.

Families should not assume that ICR clearance will follow a predictable schedule.

## Routing since 2022

Direct flights between the UK and Russia are suspended. Air cargo from Russia to the UK must route via third countries. The primary options used by specialist firms:

| Route | Carrier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Moscow → Istanbul → London | Turkish Airlines | Most reliable current option |
| Moscow → Dubai → London | Emirates | Emirates still operates Moscow routes |
| Moscow → Helsinki or Riga → London | Varies | Limited availability, subject to change |

Routing is subject to rapid change based on the political and operational situation. A specialist firm with active Russia contacts will know current options; this should be confirmed at time of engagement.

## Moscow versus other Russian cities

The bureaucratic infrastructure in Moscow and St Petersburg is well-established. Cases arising in other Russian cities — particularly in Siberia, the Russian Far East, or the North Caucasus — are substantially more complex. Local authorities in remote regions have less experience with international repatriation. Local transfer to Moscow before international export is often required, adding time and cost.

Deaths in the North Caucasus (Chechnya, Dagestan, Ingushetia) involve regions with active FCDO travel advisories and can involve security service interest.

| Document | Language | Who issues |

| Svidetelstvo o smerti (death certificate) | Russian | ZAGS Civil Registry |
| Cause of death certificate | Russian | Attending physician/pathologist |
| Investigative Committee clearance | Russian | ICR (non-natural deaths only) |
| Ministry of Foreign Affairs apostille | — | Russian MFA |
| Export permit | Russian | Health authority |
| Embalming certificate | Russian | Licensed mortuary |
| Certified translations | English | Certified translator |

## What to do first

Contact the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: 020 7008 5000. This line operates 24 hours a day. Then engage a specialist international repatriation firm with verified Russia experience before contacting any local services in Russia directly.

Do not assume the British Embassy in Moscow can actively manage the Russian bureaucratic process on your behalf. The Embassy can register the death and provide a notification document, but the administrative process is managed by Russian authorities.
