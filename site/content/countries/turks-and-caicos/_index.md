## Repatriation from the Turks and Caicos Islands to the UK

The Turks and Caicos Islands (TCI) is a British Overseas Territory in the northern Caribbean, comprising two island groups: the Caicos Islands and the Turks Islands. The capital is Cockburn Town on Grand Turk, though the tourism hub is Providenciales (Provo). TCI is a significant British holiday destination known for Grace Bay Beach.

As a British Overseas Territory, the Crown's interests are represented by the Governor. Consular functions are handled via the FCO in London. Contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours) to register a death and obtain guidance.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is managed by the **Registrar General's Office** under the TCI Government. English common law applies. Non-natural deaths require investigation and clearance by the **Royal Turks and Caicos Islands Police Force**.

### Multiple Islands

TCI covers several inhabited islands. Deaths on **Grand Turk** (the capital island), **South Caicos**, **North Caicos**, or **Middle Caicos** require internal transfer to Providenciales for international routing — adding time and cost. Providenciales has the territory's main international airport.

### No Direct UK Flights

Providenciales International Airport (PLS) does not have direct UK service. All repatriation routes via the United States — primarily Miami (MIA). This triggers US USDA/CBP import documentation requirements for human remains transiting the USA.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Registrar General's Office, TCI Government |
| Police Force clearance | Royal TCI Police Force (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local funeral director |
| Freedom from infection certificate | TCI Health Authority |
| Export permit | TCI Government |
| US transit documentation | USDA/CBP (US transit) |
| UK Coroner notification | FCO / HM Coroner, UK |

### Cremation

No cremation facilities are available in TCI. Full body repatriation to the UK is required.
