Somalia presents the most fragmented administrative environment of any country covered on this site. The Federal Government of Somalia (FGS) in Mogadishu controls some urban centres, but Al-Shabaab retains control of large rural areas across south and central Somalia. Puntland operates semi-autonomously in the northeast. Somaliland, in the northwest, has functioned as a self-governing entity since 1991 with its own police, courts, and civil registration system.

FCDO advises against all travel to Somalia. The British Embassy in Mogadishu, re-opened in 2013, operates under severe security restrictions and cannot provide standard consular assistance outside a limited operating perimeter.

## Who to contact first

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: **020 7008 5000** (available 24 hours). The British Embassy Mogadishu operates under strict security protocols — the FCDO will advise on current consular capacity and the appropriate contact channel.

The first and most urgent question is: where exactly did the death occur? The answer determines which administrative track applies and what is achievable.

## Three administrative tracks

**FGS-controlled areas (including Mogadishu):** Civil registration through FGS civil status offices. Somalia Police Force attendance for non-natural deaths. Ministry of Health export permit. Turkish Airlines (Mogadishu-Istanbul) is the primary UK route.

**Puntland (Garowe, Bosaso):** Semi-autonomous administration with its own civil registration. Bosaso Airport (BSA) connects via Addis Ababa. Process follows Puntland authority procedures.

**Somaliland (Hargeisa, Berbera):** Somaliland has a more stable and functional civil administration. Deaths are registered through Somaliland civil status offices. Hargeisa Egal International Airport (HGA) connects via Addis Ababa on Ethiopian Airlines. Timelines are generally shorter than for FGS-controlled areas.

## Islamic burial pressure

Somalia is almost entirely Sunni Muslim. Local custom and community expectation strongly favour rapid local burial. Families must communicate clearly and promptly — through the Embassy and through local contacts — that repatriation is the intent. Delay in stating this can result in pressure for immediate burial that is very difficult to reverse.

## Al-Shabaab-controlled areas: not achievable

Cases in Al-Shabaab-controlled rural areas — including most of Lower Shabelle, parts of Middle and Lower Jubba, and Bay and Bakool regions — are not achievable by civilian repatriation means. The British Embassy will advise on what, if anything, is possible via specialist crisis channels.

## Routing

- Mogadishu (MGQ): Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST); Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD)
- Hargeisa (HGA): Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD)
- Bosaso (BSA): Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD)

Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the most reliable route for UK-bound cargo from Mogadishu.
