## Repatriation from Equatorial Guinea to the UK

Equatorial Guinea is the only Spanish-speaking country in sub-Saharan Africa. It is split geographically between **Bioko Island** (where the capital Malabo is located) and **Río Muni**, the mainland enclave between Cameroon and Gabon. All international repatriation flights depart from Malabo Airport on Bioko Island.

The country has been governed by President Teodoro Obiang Nguema Mbasogo since a coup in 1979, making his government one of the longest-serving in the world. FCDO advises caution regarding dealings with local authorities. For urgent consular assistance, contact the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (24 hours). The British High Commission Yaoundé can be reached on +237 222 220 796.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is handled at the **Registro Civil** in the relevant district. The **Ministerio del Interior y Corporaciones Locales** issues the export permit once death certification and health documentation are complete.

Documentation is in Spanish. Certified English translations are required before UK authorities will process any documentation.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Certificado de defunción (death certificate) | Registro Civil |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Ministerio del Interior |
| Consular death registration | British High Commission, Yaoundé |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Island and Mainland Logistics

Equatorial Guinea's geography creates a two-stage logistics challenge for mainland deaths:

- **Bioko Island (Malabo)**: The capital and main hub. International repatriation exits directly from Malabo Airport (SSG).
- **Río Muni (mainland)**: Deaths in the mainland region require overland or internal flight transfer to Malabo before international repatriation can proceed. The mainland has limited road infrastructure in some areas.

### Routing and Transport

- **Via Paris**: Air France operates direct Malabo to Paris CDG service, the most reliable repatriation route to the UK.
- **Via Addis Ababa**: Ethiopian Airlines serves Malabo with connections to London Heathrow.

### Political and Bureaucratic Risk

Equatorial Guinea's government maintains tight controls on civil society and the movement of foreign nationals. Processing times at government ministries can vary significantly. A specialist repatriation firm with current in-country contacts is essential to avoid documentation delays that can arise from bureaucratic unpredictability.

### Cremation

Cremation facilities are not available in Equatorial Guinea. All UK nationals must be repatriated in full for cremation or burial in the UK.
