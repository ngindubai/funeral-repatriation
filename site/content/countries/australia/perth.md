Perth has one practical advantage over the east coast Australian cities: its geographic position. At UTC+8, Perth sits closer in time zone to the UK than Sydney or Brisbane, making real-time communication with British families more manageable. It is also better positioned for routing via Singapore and the Middle East.

## Western Australia coronial process

The Western Australia State Coroner operates out of Perth and has jurisdiction over all deaths that are unexpected, unexplained, or occur in circumstances requiring investigation. The Coroner's Court of Western Australia is located on Hay Street, East Perth.

For deaths certified without coronial involvement, the WA Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages processes death certificates. Timeline: 5 to 15 working days. Where the coroner becomes involved, expect 3 to 8 weeks for certification. Western Australia's vast size means deaths in mining regions, remote areas, or the north-west of the state (Broome, Port Hedland, Karratha) may require additional transfer arrangements before documentation can be finalised in Perth.

Source: Coroner's Court of Western Australia, *Information for Families*, 2024.

## Perth Airport cargo

Perth Airport (PER) has direct passenger flights to London Heathrow operated by Qantas (the only non-stop Perth-London service, approximately 17 hours). This makes Perth one of very few Australian cities with a direct UK cargo routing option. British Airways does not operate direct Perth-London services.

Human remains can be shipped via Qantas Freight on the Perth-London Heathrow route, subject to cargo space availability. Alternatively, cases are routed via Singapore Changi (SIN) or Dubai (DXB). The licensed Perth funeral director handles the cargo booking.

Documentation required for export from Perth:
- Western Australia death certificate (certified copy)
- Embalming certificate
- Freedom from infection certificate
- WA authority for disposal
- Airline cargo booking confirmation (PER-LHR or connecting via SIN/DXB)
- Passport of the deceased

## British consular contacts

The British Consulate in Perth (Level 26, 108 St Georges Terrace) handles consular registration and documentation assistance for British nationals in Western Australia. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 (0)20 7008 5000.

## Typical timeline from Perth

Direct route (PER-LHR via Qantas): 10 to 18 days in straightforward cases. Connecting routes (via SIN or DXB): 14 to 21 days. Coronial involvement: 4 to 8 weeks. Remote area cases (north-west WA): add 3 to 7 days for the in-country transfer.

## Key local considerations

Deaths in Western Australia's mining regions often involve workplace fatalities, which require WorkSafe WA investigation before the coroner can certify the death. Families in this situation should expect a significantly extended timeline and should not make UK funeral arrangements until the coronial release is confirmed.

Perth funeral homes with international repatriation experience tend to be concentrated in the inner suburbs. For deaths in the Pilbara, Kimberley, or remote Goldfields region, the local funeral director will often need to transfer to a Perth firm for international processing.
