## Repatriation from Anguilla to the UK

Anguilla is a British Overseas Territory in the northeastern Caribbean, located approximately 8 km north of Sint Maarten. The Valley is the capital. Anguilla is known as a luxury destination, with some of the finest beaches in the Caribbean and a small permanent population of around 18,000.

As a British Overseas Territory, the Crown's interests are represented by the Governor. Consular functions are handled via the FCO in London. Contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours) immediately after a death.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is handled by the **Registrar's Office** in The Valley, under the Government of Anguilla. English common law applies throughout. Non-natural deaths require investigation and clearance by the **Royal Anguilla Police Force** before funeral arrangements proceed.

### No Cremation Facilities

No cremation facilities are available in Anguilla. Full body repatriation to the UK is required.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Registrar's Office, Government of Anguilla |
| Police Force clearance | Royal Anguilla Police Force (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local funeral director |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Anguilla health authority |
| Export permit | Government of Anguilla |
| UK Coroner notification | FCO / HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

**Clayton J. Lloyd International Airport (AXA)** has limited capacity and regional connections. The most practical route is via **Princess Juliana International Airport, Sint Maarten (SXM)** — reached by a 10-minute air shuttle or short ferry. From Sint Maarten, **KLM** operates direct service to Amsterdam (AMS) for UK connections.

Alternatively, connections to **Antigua (ANU)** provide access to **British Airways** direct service to London Gatwick (LGW).
