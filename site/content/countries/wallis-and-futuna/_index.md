Wallis and Futuna is a French collectivity in the South Pacific comprising two island groups approximately 2,000 kilometres apart: the Wallis Islands and the Futuna/Alofi group. Combined population is approximately 11,000. It is among the most isolated French territories on earth.

French civil law applies. The Administrator-Superior (Administrateur supérieur), appointed by the French state, governs the territory alongside three traditional kingdoms. There is no direct link to mainland France — all international routing goes through Nouméa, New Caledonia.
