## Repatriation from Samoa: What Families Need to Know

Samoa sits in the central South Pacific and is increasingly visited by British tourists drawn by its beaches, culture, and relatively straightforward connection through Auckland. The country has a settled consular arrangement for British nationals — the New Zealand High Commission in Apia handles emergency consular assistance under the UK-New Zealand Consular Sharing Agreement.

One important clarification before anything else: Samoa and American Samoa are two entirely different countries with entirely different processes. The Independent State of Samoa (this page) uses its own documentation and the NZ High Commission. American Samoa is US territory and falls under US consular services.

### Who to Call

1. New Zealand High Commission Apia — for immediate consular assistance.
2. FCDO Emergency Travel Line in London — 020 7008 5000 (24 hours).
3. A specialist UK repatriation company.

The British High Commission in Wellington, New Zealand, holds formal accreditation to Samoa and should also be notified.

### Documentation

English and Samoan are both official languages. Death certificates, police reports, and most official documentation are issued in English. This is a material advantage — no certified translation is required in most cases, which reduces both cost and delay.

The documentation chain:

1. Death certificate from the Samoa Births, Deaths and Marriages Registration.
2. Police investigation closure for unnatural deaths.
3. Post-mortem report from TTM Hospital, Apia.
4. Ministry of Health export permit.
5. Embalming to IATA standards, with certificate.

### Savai'i Island

Savai'i is Samoa's larger island, roughly an hour from Apia by ferry. Deaths on Savai'i require transfer to Apia for post-mortem and documentation. The ferry service is regular under normal conditions, so this transfer typically adds one to two days rather than the longer delays seen in archipelago nations like Vanuatu or Tonga.

### Sunday Observance

Samoa observes Sunday strictly. Government offices and many businesses close, church attendance is widespread, and official documentation processes do not progress on Sundays. For repatriation purposes, this effectively removes one day per week from the processing timeline. A case that arrives at the Ministry of Health on a Saturday afternoon may not progress until Monday.

### Cargo Routing

All cargo connections operate via Auckland (Air New Zealand) or Sydney (Qantas) from Faleolo International Airport (APW). There are no direct UK connections. Auckland is the more common hub due to proximity.
