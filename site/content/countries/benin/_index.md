## Repatriation from Benin to the UK

Repatriating a loved one from Benin is a structured but time-sensitive process governed by French civil law. The Tribunal de Première Instance handles legal certification, and Parquet clearance is required before remains can be exported. The British High Commission in Accra provides non-resident consular coverage for British nationals who die in Benin.

For urgent consular assistance, families should contact the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (24 hours). The British High Commission Accra can be reached on +233 302 213 250.

### The Legal Framework

Benin's civil registration system follows the French civil law model. Death registration is handled at the **Tribunal de Première Instance** in the relevant commune. Before export of remains is authorised, the **Parquet** (public prosecutor's office) must issue a clearance confirming no criminal investigation is pending.

Export permits are issued by the **Ministère de l'Intérieur**. All documentation must be complete before the remains can be released to a repatriation firm for transport.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Acte de décès (death certificate) | Tribunal de Première Instance |
| Parquet clearance for export | Parquet du Tribunal |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Ministère de l'Intérieur |
| Consular death registration | British High Commission, Accra |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing and Transport

Cotonou's Cadjehoun Airport (COO) is the main international gateway. The primary routing options are:

- **Via Paris**: Air France operates Cotonou to Paris CDG, with straightforward UK connections.
- **Via Casablanca**: Royal Air Maroc serves Cotonou with connections through Mohammed V Airport.
- **Via Addis Ababa**: Ethiopian Airlines serves Cotonou with connections to London Heathrow.

### Security and Access Considerations

Benin is rated **moderate complexity** for repatriation from the main urban areas. The picture changes sharply in the north:

- **Northern border departments (Alibori, Atacora)**: FCDO advises against all travel within 5km of the Burkina Faso border. Jihadist groups operating in the Sahel have extended operations into northern Benin since 2022. Deaths in these areas require specialist logistics and may involve military or police coordination for access.
- **Border areas with Niger**: The Malanville area near the Niger border has elevated risk.
- **Cotonou and the south**: Moderate risk. Infrastructure is functional and the documentation process is well-established.

### Religious and Cultural Factors

Benin has a notably diverse religious landscape: Christianity (c.48%), Vodoun and indigenous traditions (c.28%), and Islam (c.24%). Vodoun funerary customs vary by family and community. Some traditional practices involve specific timing requirements or restrictions on how remains are handled. A specialist repatriation firm with experience in the region will understand how to navigate these sensitivities respectfully.

### Cremation in Benin

Cremation is not practised in Benin and no cremation facilities exist. Families requiring cremation must repatriate the body to the UK, where cremation can take place once all necessary UK releases have been obtained.
