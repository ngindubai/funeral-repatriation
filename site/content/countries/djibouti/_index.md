Djibouti is a small nation at the southern entrance to the Red Sea, bordered by Eritrea, Ethiopia, and Somalia. Its strategic position has made it home to both French and American military bases and a commercial hub for Horn of Africa trade. For UK repatriation purposes, Djibouti presents a workable process with two important features: there is no resident British Embassy in the country, and all civil documentation is in French and Arabic.

The most common UK deaths in Djibouti involve merchant seafarers, military personnel connected to allied forces, aid workers, and business travellers.

## British consular support: Addis Ababa

The British Embassy Addis Ababa holds non-resident accreditation for Djibouti. All consular services — including death registration for UK purposes — are provided remotely from Addis Ababa.

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first: **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). British Embassy Addis Ababa: +251 11 617 0100.

The Embassy will advise on the current process and provide the notification required for UK administrative purposes. A specialist firm with French-speaking East African contacts is required to manage the in-country documentation.

## Civil registration

Death registration goes through Djibouti's civil registry system (état civil) — a French colonial inheritance. The attending physician issues the medical certificate of cause of death. The registrar issues the acte de décès (death certificate) in French and Arabic.

## Tribunal de Première Instance: non-natural deaths

For violent, sudden, or unexplained deaths, the Tribunal de Première Instance (court of first instance) must issue an authorisation before the body can be released. The Police Nationale attend the scene. The examining magistrate reviews the case and issues the necessary order. For straightforward accident cases this typically takes 10 to 21 days.

## Ministry of Health export permit

Once civil registration is complete and any Tribunal clearance is in hand, the Ministry of Health issues the export permit. This is a straightforward administrative step in Djibouti, typically completed in 3 to 5 working days.

## Documentation required

| Document | Language | Issuer |
|---|---|---|
| Death certificate (acte de décès) | French | Civil Registry |
| Medical certificate | French | Attending physician |
| Tribunal de Première Instance order | French | Examining magistrate |
| Police Nationale clearance | French | Police Nationale du Djibouti |
| Ministry of Health export permit | French/Arabic | Ministry of Health |
| Certified translations | English | Certified translator |

## Routing to the UK

Djibouti-Ambouli International Airport (JIB) connects to:
- Addis Ababa (Ethiopian Airlines) — primary UK cargo connection
- Dubai (flydubai, Air Arabia) — alternative with Emirates/BA connections
- Istanbul (Turkish Airlines) — connecting to London

The Addis Ababa route via Ethiopian Airlines is the most reliable and established for UK-bound cargo.
