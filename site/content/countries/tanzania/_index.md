Tanzania attracts British visitors for two primary reasons: safari holidays in the northern national parks (Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater, Tarangire) and beach holidays on Zanzibar Island. These two geographies create very different repatriation challenges. A death in Dar es Salaam is significantly more straightforward than a death in a remote tented camp in the Serengeti.

## Remote safari deaths: what families need to know

If someone dies in a remote national park, the body must first be transported to the nearest Government Chemist Laboratory Agency (GCLA) facility before any formal post-mortem can be conducted. In practice, this means reaching a town with appropriate facilities, which for the northern park circuit often means Arusha. Internal transport may involve light aircraft from a bush airstrip.

This internal logistics phase can take 3 to 5 days before the formal documentation process even begins. Tanzania National Parks Authority (TANAPA) may need to issue clearance for removal from the park. Wildlife-related deaths (animal attacks) add a further investigation layer.

## Zanzibar deaths

Zanzibar is a semi-autonomous region of Tanzania with its own administration. Deaths on the island require involvement of the Zanzibar Registration and Identification Department as well as the mainland GCLA. The body must be transferred from Zanzibar to Dar es Salaam by domestic flight or ferry before international repatriation documentation can be fully processed. Allow an additional 2 to 4 days.

## British High Commission Dar es Salaam

The British High Commission in Dar es Salaam is the primary consular contact. For Zanzibar deaths, there is a British Consular contact on the island. Consular staff will advise on documentation and liaise with Tanzanian authorities.

## Post-mortem and export documentation

The GCLA conducts post-mortems for violent, sudden, or suspicious deaths. The Ministry of Health issues the export permit, freedom from infectious disease certificate, and embalming documentation. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs issues the laissez-passer.

## Air freight to the UK

Julius Nyerere International Airport (DAR) in Dar es Salaam is the main cargo hub. Kenya Airways via Nairobi and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa are the primary routing options. British Airways operates via Nairobi on some routings. No direct Dar es Salaam-UK cargo flights exist.
