Ivory Coast (officially Côte d'Ivoire) is the world's largest cocoa producer and a significant hub for British commodity traders, agricultural business, and NGO workers. Abidjan is the economic capital, a large and sophisticated West African city with an international airport, multiple teaching hospitals, and a substantial British diplomatic presence.

Yamoussoukro is the official political capital but all practical functions, including the airport, the main hospitals, and the British Embassy, are in Abidjan.

Most British deaths occur in Abidjan or in the western tourist zone around Man and the Taď National Park. Business travellers make up a significant share of cases. NGO workers and humanitarian staff operating in the north and west represent the most logistically complex cases.

## The Consular Position

The British Embassy is resident in Abidjan. Direct consular support is available.

Your first call should be to the British Embassy Abidjan and the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: 020 7008 5000.

## The Forensic Process

Natural deaths are registered at the local mairie (town hall). Unnatural deaths go to the Institut National de Médecine Légale (INML). Forensic pathology capacity in Abidjan is concentrated at CHU de Treichville, CHU de Cocody, and CHU de Yopougon. Outside Abidjan, forensic capacity is very limited. Deaths in the interior require road transfer to Abidjan before post-mortem can take place.

All documentation is in French throughout. Certified French-to-English translation is required for UK death registration, probate, and insurance purposes. This is a significant cost and time addition compared to English-speaking destinations.

## Northern and Western Regions

The northern regions near the Mali and Burkina Faso borders, including Korhogo and Odienne, have elevated security risk and very limited medical infrastructure. Deaths here require road transfer to Abidjan, which can take 8 to 12 hours on roads that are poor in wet season. The FCDO travel advisories for northern Ivory Coast should be read carefully before travel.

## No Direct UK Flights

Felix Houphouet-Boigny International Airport (ABJ) has no direct flights to the UK. Air France via Paris CDG is the standard routing. Brussels Airlines via Brussels and Royal Air Maroc via Casablanca are alternatives. Cargo capacity on these routes is limited, and booking should be confirmed early.
