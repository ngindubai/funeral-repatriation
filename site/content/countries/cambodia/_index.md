direct_answer_intro: "Repatriation from Cambodia to the UK takes 14 to 28 days in a straightforward case. The Cambodian documentation process involves multiple government departments and is one of the more administratively demanding in Southeast Asia. Phnom Penh cases move faster than those originating in Siem Reap or coastal areas."
direct_answer_points:
  - "Cambodian documentation involves the Ministry of Interior, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and local authorities. Translation, legalisation, and multi-department approvals make this process longer than most European cases."
  - "Documentation alone takes 10 to 21 days. This is the primary reason Cambodia repatriations do not complete quickly even in circumstances with no investigation or post-mortem."
direct_answer_note: "The British Embassy in Cambodia can provide a list of local contacts and advise on the process, but cannot accelerate Cambodian government approval timelines."
