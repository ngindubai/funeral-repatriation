Phnom Penh is the capital city and administrative centre of Cambodia. All national authorities relevant to repatriation are headquartered here: the Ministry of Interior, the Ministry of Health, the National Committee for Disaster Management, and the Cambodian National Police. A death in Phnom Penh is administratively better-positioned than a death in a provincial town, because the relevant government offices are accessible without long-distance domestic travel.

Most repatriation cases from Phnom Penh involve British tourists, NGO workers, or business travellers. The British Embassy is located in the city.

## What the British Embassy does

The British Embassy Phnom Penh (27-29 Street 75, Sangkat Sras Chak, Khan Daun Penh, Phnom Penh) is the primary UK consular authority for Cambodia.

**The Embassy can:** Register the death in UK consular records. Advise on Cambodian documentation requirements. Provide a local funeral director referral list.

**The Embassy cannot:** Repatriate the body. Pay any costs. Instruct Cambodian state authorities.

FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 (0)20 7008 5000.

## What Cambodian law requires

Under Cambodian law, sudden, violent, or unexplained deaths are reported to the Cambodian National Police, which determines whether a judicial investigation is required. For hospital deaths with a certified cause, the treating physician issues a death confirmation document.

Death certificates are issued by the Ministry of Interior through the commune or district administration. The Ministry of Health issues the international exit permit (authorising the body to leave Cambodia by air).

## The documentation chain

**1. Death confirmation** from treating physician or police attendance record.

**2. Death certificate** from commune or district administration (Ministry of Interior).

**3. Police clearance** (for sudden, unexplained, or violent deaths).

**4. Ministry of Health exit permit** (ការអនុញ្ញាតឱ្យដឹកជញ្ជូន) — required before the body can leave Cambodia.

**5. Embalming certificate** — Cambodian law and IATA requirements both apply.

**6. Freedom from infection certificate.**

**7. IATA cargo documentation.**

Source: Cambodian Ministry of Health; FCDO Cambodia travel advice, 2024.

## Airport and cargo routing

Phnom Penh International Airport (PNH, Pochentong International Airport, 10km from the city centre) serves the capital. Direct UK cargo services are not available from PNH. The standard routing is:

1. PNH to Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK), approximately 1 hour
2. BKK to London Heathrow (LHR) via Thai Airways or British Airways, approximately 11 hours

Alternatively: PNH to Singapore Changi (SIN) then SIN-LHR. The licensed Cambodian funeral director arranges the international cargo booking from PNH and the connecting leg.

## British consular contacts

The British Embassy Phnom Penh is located at 27-29 Street 75, Khan Daun Penh. There is no British consular presence outside Phnom Penh in Cambodia. For deaths in provincial areas (Siem Reap, Sihanoukville, Battambang), the Phnom Penh Embassy is still the competent authority.

## Timeline from Phnom Penh

- Hospital-certified natural death: 10 to 21 days
- Police investigation, no judicial referral: 14 to 28 days
- Judicial investigation: 4 to 8 weeks or more

## Key local considerations

Mortuary infrastructure in Phnom Penh is more developed than in provincial Cambodia but varies significantly between facilities. A licensed private funeral home with experience in international cases is strongly recommended over hospital mortuary storage. The Ministry of Health exit permit process is the most common source of delay: ensure the appointed funeral director has an established relationship with the relevant Ministry office.
