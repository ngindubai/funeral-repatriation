Siem Reap is Cambodia's second city and home to Angkor Wat, the country's primary tourist destination. It receives a large volume of international visitors, including British nationals on temple tours, adventure travel itineraries, and longer-stay visits. Deaths in Siem Reap — whether from medical emergencies, road accidents, or other causes — follow Cambodian national law but are handled through provincial and regional authorities rather than the central Phnom Penh ministries.

The British Embassy is in Phnom Penh. There is no British consular presence in Siem Reap.

## What the British Embassy does

The British Embassy Phnom Penh (27-29 Street 75, Sangkat Sras Chak, Khan Daun Penh) handles all consular work for deaths in Cambodia, including Siem Reap.

**The Embassy can:** Register the death in UK consular records. Advise on Cambodian documentation requirements. Provide a local funeral director referral list.

**The Embassy cannot:** Repatriate the body. Pay any costs. Instruct Cambodian state authorities. Send staff to Siem Reap in most circumstances.

FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 (0)20 7008 5000.

## What Cambodian law requires

The same documentation chain applies as for all Cambodian deaths: death certification through the commune or district administration (Ministry of Interior), police clearance for sudden deaths, and the Ministry of Health international exit permit. In Siem Reap, these processes go through Siem Reap Provincial authorities. The Ministry of Health exit permit is a national-level document but the initial stages are handled provincially.

Certified English translations of all Khmer-language documents are required for UK authorities.

## The documentation chain

**1. Death confirmation** from treating physician (Siem Reap Provincial Hospital or a private clinic) or police attendance record.

**2. Death certificate** from Siem Reap Provincial or district commune administration.

**3. Police clearance** (for sudden, violent, or unexplained deaths via Siem Reap Provincial Police).

**4. Ministry of Health exit permit** — requires submission through the Siem Reap Provincial Health Department, which forwards to Phnom Penh for central approval.

**5. Embalming certificate.**

**6. Freedom from infection certificate.**

**7. IATA cargo documentation.**

Source: Cambodian Ministry of Health; FCDO Cambodia travel advice, 2024.

## Airport and cargo routing

Angkor International Airport (REP) serves Siem Reap. Cargo capacity from REP is limited. The standard repatriation routing is:

1. Domestic transfer from Siem Reap to Phnom Penh (PNH) — approximately 45 minutes by air, or 5 to 6 hours by road
2. PNH to Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK), approximately 1 hour
3. BKK to London Heathrow (LHR), approximately 11 hours

Most licensed Cambodian funeral directors with international experience operate from Phnom Penh. The Siem Reap-based funeral director handles initial preparation; the Phnom Penh firm coordinates the international cargo leg.

## Timeline from Siem Reap

- Hospital-certified natural death: 12 to 25 days
- Police investigation: 14 to 35 days
- Cases requiring transfer to Phnom Penh for processing: add 2 to 5 days

## Key local considerations

Siem Reap's mortuary infrastructure is less developed than Phnom Penh. Cold storage in provincial hospitals is limited, and the humidity is extreme. Private funeral homes in Siem Reap with proper cold storage are the recommended option. Road accidents on Highway 6 (the main Phnom Penh to Siem Reap route) are a documented cause of death for tourists, and these cases invariably involve police and possible judicial investigation.
