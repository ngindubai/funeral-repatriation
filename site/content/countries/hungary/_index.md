Budapest is one of Europe's most visited city break destinations, and Hungary's thermal spas, Danube crossings, and lake resorts (Balaton) attract a steady flow of British visitors. Most deaths involving British nationals occur in Budapest.

## Legal framework

Hungary is an EU member and signatory to the Council of Europe Agreement on the Transfer of Corpses (Strasbourg, 1973 — ETS No. 080). The agreement requires embalming and a laissez-passer for international transfer. Hungarian domestic law also applies.

## Death registration

Deaths are registered at the local anyakonyvi hivatal (civil registry office). A halotti anyakonyvi kivonat (death certificate extract) is issued. For unnatural deaths, the police and Public Prosecutor are notified.

All documentation is in Hungarian. Certified translation into English is required before the documents can be used in a UK context. Your UK repatriation specialist will arrange this.

## British Embassy Budapest

The British Embassy in Budapest must be notified. Address: Harmincad Utca 6, Budapest 1051. Phone: +36 1 266 2888. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 1908 516666.

## Public Prosecutor and post-mortem

For violent, sudden, or suspicious deaths, the Public Prosecutor orders a forensic post-mortem conducted by the National Institute of Forensic Medicine (ORVOSI) in Budapest. The Budapest institute is well-resourced. Regional capacity is adequate. Results typically available within 5 to 10 days.

## Embalming and laissez-passer

Embalming is mandatory under the Strasbourg Agreement. The laissez-passer is issued by the Government Office (Kormanyablak) or district authority. Once the death certificate, embalming certificate, freedom from contagious disease clearance, and laissez-passer are all in order, the body can be booked as air cargo.

## Flights from Budapest

Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) has direct flights to London Heathrow, Gatwick, Luton, and Stansted with British Airways, easyJet, Ryanair, and Wizz Air. Multiple daily departures mean cargo bookings are not delayed by flight availability.
