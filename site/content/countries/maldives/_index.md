The Maldives is an exclusively resort destination. Every death on a resort island begins with the same first step: transfer to Male, the capital island, where all official authorities and documentation infrastructure are located. There is no death registration, no pathology, and no coffin preparation available on resort islands.

This geography is the defining factor in Maldives repatriation. The speedboat or seaplane transfer to Male adds time and cost before anything else begins. Families should be aware of this from the moment they receive the news.

The Maldives is a 100% Islamic nation. Cremation is forbidden under Islamic law and is not available anywhere in the Maldives. Full-body repatriation is the only option for British families. This makes the Maldives one of the few global destinations where the choice between cremation and repatriation does not exist.

Embalming services in Male are limited. Zinc-lined coffins are not a standard local item and must be sourced through specialist repatriation agents. These factors, combined with limited pathology capacity for post-mortems, mean the Maldives requires specialist handling from the outset.

The British High Commission in Colombo covers the Maldives. There is no British consular presence on the Maldivian islands. Call +94 11 539 0639 or the FCDO 24-hour line at +44 (0)20 7008 5000.

Comprehensive travel insurance including full repatriation cover is essential. The FCDO travel advice for the Maldives specifically notes that medical facilities are extremely limited outside Male and that evacuation or repatriation costs can be very high.
