## Repatriation from Aruba to the UK

Aruba is a constituent country of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, situated in the southern Caribbean Sea approximately 29 km north of the Venezuelan coast. It is a major tourist destination known for its beaches, water sports, and stable climate. The capital is Oranjestad. Despite being part of the Dutch kingdom, Aruba has full autonomy over internal affairs.

A significant number of UK nationals visit Aruba each year for holidays and cruises.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British Embassy The Hague (+31 70 427 0427) handles consular matters for Aruba on a non-resident basis.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration in Aruba is managed by the **Departamento di Registro Civil** (Civil Registry of the Government of Aruba). Documentation is issued in Dutch and/or Papiamento — Aruba's widely spoken creole language. Non-natural deaths require investigation and clearance by the **Korps Politie Aruba** (Aruban Police Corps).

### Document Translation

Both Dutch and Papiamento death certificates require certified translation into English for the UK Coroner. Your specialist repatriation firm will arrange this.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Departamento di Registro Civil |
| Certified English translation | Sworn translator |
| Police clearance | Korps Politie Aruba (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local funeral director |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Health authority |
| Export permit | Aruban authorities |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, The Hague |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

**Queen Beatrix International Airport (AUA)** near Oranjestad has direct **KLM** flights to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS), with daily service. From Amsterdam, frequent connections to UK airports are available.

### Cremation

Cremation is available in Aruba. Ashes are returned to the UK in a sealed urn.
