Cancun is Mexico's busiest resort destination and the single largest origin point for UK tourist deaths in Mexico. The Caribbean resort corridor -- Cancun, Playa del Carmen, Tulum, and the Riviera Maya -- attracts around 500,000 British visitors each year. Deaths here span water sports accidents, road incidents (particularly on Highway 307), sudden cardiac events in the heat, and alcohol-related causes. The Quintana Roo state authorities and the local British Consulate handle a consistent volume of tourist death cases and are more experienced than the equivalent offices in most Mexican states.

## British Consulate Cancun

The British Consulate Cancun (Royal Windsor Centre, 3rd Floor, Blvd Kukulcan Km 13, Hotel Zone) is the UK consular post covering Quintana Roo and the Yucatan Peninsula. This is the primary point of contact for UK families dealing with a death in Cancun or the wider Riviera Maya.

**The Consulate can:** Register the death in UK consular records. Advise on Quintana Roo documentation requirements. Provide a funeral director referral list for Cancun.

**The Consulate cannot:** Repatriate the body. Pay any costs. Instruct Mexican state authorities.

FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 (0)20 7008 5000.

## What Quintana Roo law requires

**Ministerio Publico (MP):** All sudden, violent, unexplained, or unattended deaths in Quintana Roo must be reported to the local Ministerio Publico. In Cancun and Playa del Carmen, the MP offices handle tourist deaths regularly and are reasonably efficient at processing straightforward cases. The MP must issue a clearance before the licensed funeral director can take custody of the body.

**Registro Civil:** The death certificate (acta de defuncion) is issued by the Registro Civil of the municipality of death -- Benito Juarez for most Cancun cases, Solidaridad for Playa del Carmen and Tulum.

**State health export permit:** The Secretaria de Salud Quintana Roo issues the international transport permit authorising the body to leave Mexico.

## The documentation chain

**1. Acta de defuncion** from local Registro Civil.

**2. Ministerio Publico clearance** and, where applicable, autopsy report from SEMEFO (Servicio Medico Forense).

**3. International transport permit** from Secretaria de Salud Quintana Roo.

**4. Embalming certificate** from the licensed Cancun funeral director.

**5. Freedom from infection certificate.**

**6. Certified Spanish-to-English translations** of all documents for UK authorities.

**7. IATA cargo documentation from CUN.**

Source: Reglamento de la Ley General de Salud en Materia de Control Sanitario; Secretaria de Salud Mexico, 2024.

## Airport and cargo routing

Cancun International Airport (CUN) is one of Mexico's busiest airports, but its direct UK cargo connectivity is limited. Options:

1. CUN to Mexico City (MEX, approximately 2 hours) then MEX-LHR direct via British Airways -- most reliable route to London
2. CUN to Miami (MIA, approximately 2 hours) then MIA-LHR -- alternative if MEX routing is unavailable
3. Direct charter routes from CUN to UK airports exist in summer season but cargo capacity on charters is not guaranteed

The licensed Cancun funeral director arranges the cargo booking and domestic leg to MEX or international leg to MIA.

## Timeline from Cancun

- Hospital-certified natural death: 10 to 18 days
- MP involvement, clear circumstances: 14 to 21 days
- Forensic autopsy (SEMEFO): 21 to 35 days
- Deaths in remote resort areas (Tulum, Bacalar): add 3 to 7 days for logistics

## Key local considerations

Deaths in the hotel zone (Zona Hotelera) typically involve the hotel's own procedures before the MP is called. Families should request the hotel to preserve all documentation and surveillance footage from the outset, particularly in cases where the cause of death is unclear. The hotel has a commercial interest in resolving the situation quickly; the family's interest is in accurate documentation.

Highway 307 running south from Cancun through the Riviera Maya has a documented record of serious road accidents. Deaths on this road involve the Agencia Estatal de Investigacion (state investigative agency) and typically require more documentation time than hotel-based deaths.
