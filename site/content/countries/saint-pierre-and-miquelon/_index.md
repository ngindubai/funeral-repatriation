Saint Pierre and Miquelon is a French overseas collectivity of eight small islands off the south coast of Newfoundland, Canada — the last remnant of France's once-vast North American territories. French civil law applies in full. Population is approximately 6,000.

The unusual geography means repatriation routing goes through Canadian airports — Halifax or St John's — rather than directly to France. Air Saint-Pierre operates the limited schedule. Dense North Atlantic fog and severe winter weather frequently cause disruption.
