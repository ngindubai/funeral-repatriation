Eritrea is one of the world's most closed states. The government of the People's Front for Democracy and Justice (PFDJ) controls all administrative, legal, and civil processes. There is no independent judiciary, no free press, and limited international accountability. This context defines everything about repatriation from Eritrea.

The British Embassy in Asmara maintains a resident presence, but its capacity to intervene in domestic Eritrean administrative decisions is genuinely limited. Families should understand from the outset that the timeline for repatriation from Eritrea is not within the control of the Embassy, a specialist firm, or the family.

The most common repatriation scenario is UK nationals of Eritrean heritage visiting family in Eritrea, or Eritreans with British residency. The UK-Eritrean diaspora is substantial, primarily in London.

## British Embassy Asmara

The British Embassy Asmara is a small resident mission. Embassy staff register the death and provide the notifications required for UK administrative purposes.

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first: **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). British Embassy Asmara: +291 1 120004.

## The administrative process

Death registration goes through the Regional Administration. The Ministry of Health issues the export permit. For non-natural deaths, additional clearances are required from the Ministry of Health and potentially National Security.

The sequence:
1. Physician certifies cause of death
2. Regional Administration registers the death and issues the death certificate
3. Ministry of Health reviews and issues export permit
4. National Security clearance (may be required — not always predictable when this applies)
5. Funeral director preparation
6. Cargo via Asmara International Airport

There is no mechanism to expedite any of these steps. Communication from Eritrean authorities about the status of a case is limited and irregular.

## National Security involvement

Eritrea's national security apparatus — operating under PFDJ control — may require separate clearance before a body is released for export. This is not routinely required for natural deaths, but has been a factor in cases involving:
- Dual nationals (Eritrean and British)
- Persons who had previously left Eritrea without authorisation
- Deaths that occurred in sensitive locations
- Cases where the deceased had political associations

Families should disclose the full background of the case to both the FCDO and their specialist firm to allow an accurate assessment.

## Limited airline access

Asmara International Airport (ASM) has very limited scheduled international service. Current routes: Cairo (EgyptAir), Jeddah (Eritrean Airlines), Dubai (connections). Ethiopian Airlines previously served Asmara but service has been inconsistent since the 2018-2022 Eritrea-Ethiopia conflict period.

Cargo routing is typically: Asmara → Cairo or Dubai → London. The limited frequency of departures means cargo holding in Asmara may add days.

## Documentation required

| Document | Language | Issuer |
|---|---|---|
| Death certificate | Tigrinya/Arabic | Regional Administration |
| Medical certificate | Tigrinya | Ministry of Health |
| Ministry of Health export permit | Tigrinya | Ministry of Health |
| Regional Administration clearance | Tigrinya | Regional Administration |
| National Security clearance | Tigrinya | National Security (if required) |
| Certified translations | English | Certified translator |

## What families should know

Eritrea is not a destination where a specialist firm can accelerate outcomes through established relationships. The firm's value is in navigating the documentation requirements accurately and maintaining communication with the Embassy and Eritrean authorities through the process.

Families should not make arrangements for UK funeral services until the export permit is confirmed in hand. Delays at any stage can push the timeline beyond initial estimates.
