Pakistan is among the most frequent origins for repatriation to the UK. The scale of the Pakistani diaspora in Britain means specialist funeral directors in cities including Bradford, Birmingham, Manchester, and London handle Pakistan repatriations routinely. However, the process still has bureaucratic steps that must be completed in sequence, and delays outside Pakistan's major cities are common.

## The two death certificates

Understanding the Pakistani death certificate system is the single most useful thing a family can know before beginning the process.

When someone dies in Pakistan, the hospital or attending physician issues a medical certificate of cause of death. This is the first document. It is necessary but not sufficient for international repatriation.

The legally recognised death certificate for international purposes is issued by the Union Council (local government body). The Union Council certificate requires the medical certificate and proof of identity. Processing takes 3 to 7 days in Lahore, Karachi, and Islamabad. In smaller towns and rural areas, it takes longer. Without the Union Council certificate, the District Health Authority will not issue the export No Objection Certificate. Source: Pakistani Registration of Births, Deaths and Marriages Act.

## British High Commission Islamabad

The British High Commission in Islamabad is the primary consular contact. Address: Diplomatic Enclave, Ramna 5, Islamabad. Phone: +92 51 201 2000. Deputy High Commissions operate in Karachi and Lahore.

## Medico-Legal Officer examination

For violent, sudden, or suspicious deaths, the body is referred to the Medico-Legal Officer (MLO). The MLO system is the Pakistani equivalent of a coroner-ordered post-mortem. Capacity is concentrated in Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad. Deaths in smaller cities or rural areas may require transfer to the nearest MLO-equipped facility, which adds time.

## Embalming and Islamic considerations

Embalming is required under international repatriation regulations. Islamic tradition generally opposes embalming, as it is seen as contrary to the principle of returning the body to the earth in its natural state. This creates a tension that many Pakistani families navigate with their local religious authority (imam).

A number of Pakistani funeral directors operating in the UK are experienced in meeting legal requirements while minimising the extent of embalming and conducting the process with appropriate respect. Families should discuss their requirements at the outset. Source: Islamic Medical Association guidelines; international repatriation regulations.

## Air freight to London

Pakistan International Airlines (PIA) and British Airways both operate direct flights from Lahore (LHE), Karachi (KHI), and Islamabad (ISB) to London Heathrow. This is a genuine logistical advantage: no transit routing is required, which reduces both cost and risk. Human remains travel as air cargo.
