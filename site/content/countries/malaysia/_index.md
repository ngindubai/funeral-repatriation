Malaysia is home to a significant British expatriate community centred in Kuala Lumpur and Penang, alongside steady British tourist traffic to the peninsula and to East Malaysia (Sabah and Sarawak). The British High Commission operates from Kuala Lumpur.

Repatriation from Malaysia operates within two distinct legal frameworks depending on the deceased's religion. For non-Muslim deceased, the civil court and National Registration Department have jurisdiction. For Muslim deceased, the Syariah court holds jurisdiction and Islamic law governs preparation and timelines, including the requirement for burial within 24 hours where possible.

Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA) has direct Malaysia Airlines and British Airways flights to London Heathrow, which keeps freight times and costs manageable. Cases originating in East Malaysia require domestic transfer to KL before international freight can proceed.

The Sijil Kematian (Malaysian death certificate) is bilingual in Malay and English. Processing takes two to five working days after the medical officer's certification and any police clearance.

Contact the British High Commission in Kuala Lumpur at +60 3 2170 2200 immediately. The High Commission will provide a list of funeral directors experienced with UK repatriation and can certify the death certificate for UK registration.
