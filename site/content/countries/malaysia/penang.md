Penang operates under the same national Malaysian legal framework as Kuala Lumpur, but the administrative geography differs. Documentation is processed through Penang state authorities rather than Federal Territory offices, and the cargo route involves a domestic connection to Kuala Lumpur before the international leg. For British families, the practical difference is a 1 to 2 day extension to the baseline timeline.

## What Malaysian law requires in Penang

The Inquests Act 1960 (Malaysia) applies throughout the country. In Penang, sudden, violent, or unexplained deaths are referred to the Magistrate's Court in its Coroner capacity. Penang jurisdiction covers both Penang Island (Pulau Pinang) and Seberang Perai on the mainland.

The Polis Diraja Malaysia (PDRM) North District contingent handles tourist and resident deaths. Hospital Pulau Pinang (Penang General Hospital) is the principal government hospital and operates the Forensic Medicine Unit for official post-mortems in Penang.

There is no British consular office in Penang. All consular matters are handled by the British High Commission in Kuala Lumpur.

## The documentation chain

**1. Laporan Polis.** Filed by PDRM at the scene or hospital.

**2. Post-mortem report or Coroner's certificate.** Conducted at Hospital Pulau Pinang's Forensic Medicine Unit, if ordered.

**3. Sijil Kematian.** Issued by the Penang state office of the National Registration Department (JPN Pulau Pinang). Processing: 3 to 7 working days.

**4. Permit to remove body abroad.** Issued by the Penang State Health Department (Jabatan Kesihatan Negeri Pulau Pinang).

**5. Embalming certificate.** Prepared by the licensed Penang funeral director.

**6. Freedom from infection certificate.**

**7. IATA cargo documentation.**

Source: Inquests Act 1960 (Malaysia), Laws of Malaysia; National Registration Department Malaysia, *Death Registration Procedures*, 2024.

## Airport and cargo routing

Penang International Airport (PEN) operates scheduled flights to Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KUL), approximately 1 hour. International cargo — including human remains — routes through KUL. British Airways and Malaysia Airlines operate KUL-LHR. The Penang-to-KUL domestic leg typically adds 1 working day.

Alternatively, for families arranging repatriation, the licensed funeral director in Penang manages the road or air transfer to KUL for cargo handover.

## British consular contacts

British High Commission Kuala Lumpur (185 Jalan Ampang). No BHC presence in Penang. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 (0)20 7008 5000.

## Timeline from Penang

- Natural death with certifiable cause: 7 to 14 days (plus 1 to 2 days for KUL cargo transfer)
- Coroner case: 14 to 28 days
- Full Coroner's inquest: 6 to 12 weeks

## Key local considerations

Penang has a disproportionately large British presence: George Town is one of the most popular long-stay destinations for British expats in Southeast Asia, driven by the Malaysia My Second Home (MM2H) programme. Deaths among older long-term residents, with complex estate and documentation situations, are a feature of Penang repatriation cases.

Penang's medical tourism industry is strong. British visitors receiving medical treatment in Penang private hospitals who die during treatment are a documented category. In these cases, the treating physician can often certify the cause of death directly, avoiding the Coroner referral and shortening the timeline.
