Timor-Leste became an independent state in 2002 after more than two decades of Indonesian occupation following Portuguese colonial withdrawal. It is one of the world's youngest nations. The civil registration system, while functioning, reflects the country's recent institutional history — processes can be slower and less consistent than in more established states.

For UK families, the most important fact is this: there is no British Embassy in Timor-Leste. British nationals receive consular assistance from the Australian Embassy in Dili under the standing UK-Australia consular sharing arrangement. The Australian Embassy is experienced with this role.

## Australian Embassy Dili: consular support for British nationals

The Australian Embassy in Dili provides consular assistance for British nationals in Timor-Leste by arrangement between the UK and Australian governments. This includes death registration for UK purposes and liaison with local authorities.

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first: **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). Australian Embassy Dili: +670 332 2111.

Be clear when calling that you are a British national — you will be directed to the UK consular services channel.

## Civil registration

Death registration goes through the Civil Registration Service. The attending physician certifies cause of death. The Civil Registry issues the death certificate in Portuguese and Tetum.

For non-natural deaths, the National Police of Timor-Leste (PNTL) must attend before the body is moved.

## Outside Dili: limited infrastructure

Timor-Leste is a small country but has very limited mortuary and medical infrastructure outside the capital. If the death occurred in a district outside Dili — Baucau, Liquiçá, Manufahi, or the remote Oecusse enclave — the body will need to be transported to Dili before the standard documentation and preparation process can begin. Road conditions vary and transport may take 2 to 8 hours.

The Oecusse enclave, separated from the rest of Timor-Leste by Indonesian territory, is the most logistically complex location. Road access goes through West Timor (Indonesia). Air access is via the Oecusse airstrip — charter only.

## Documentation required

| Document | Language | Issuer |
|---|---|---|
| Death certificate | Portuguese / Tetum | Civil Registration Service |
| Medical certificate | Portuguese | Ministry of Health physician |
| PNTL police clearance | Portuguese | National Police of Timor-Leste |
| Ministry of Health export permit | Portuguese | Ministry of Health |
| Certified translations | English | Certified translator |

## Routing to the UK

Presidente Nicolau Lobato International Airport (DIL) in Dili connects to:
- Bali, Indonesia (TransNusa, Garuda) — Singapore connections to London
- Darwin, Australia (Airnorth) — Sydney connections to London

The Bali route via Singapore Airlines is the most commonly used for UK-bound cargo. Darwin via Sydney is the alternative. Both involve at least two connections before London.

## What to expect

Given Timor-Leste's institutional development stage, families should expect some variability in processing times. The Australian Embassy provides a practical buffer — their staff have direct working relationships with Timorese authorities and can identify and resolve administrative complications. A specialist firm with South-East Asian or Pacific experience is recommended.
