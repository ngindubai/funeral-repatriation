## Repatriation from Liechtenstein to the UK

Liechtenstein is a doubly landlocked microstate in central Europe, bordered by Switzerland and Austria. With a population of around 40,000, it is one of the world's smallest nations. It uses the Swiss franc, operates under a German-influenced civil law system, and has excellent road access to Zürich. British nationals visiting Liechtenstein typically do so as part of a wider European trip.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British Embassy Bern (+41 31 359 7700) handles consular matters for Liechtenstein on a non-resident basis.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration in Liechtenstein is handled by the **Amt für Bevölkerung und Zivilregister** (Population and Civil Registry Office). All deaths must be registered and a death certificate issued before any repatriation steps can proceed.

Where death is not from natural causes, the **Landgericht** (Regional Court) oversees the investigation and must authorise release.

Documentation is in German throughout and requires certified English translation.

### Routing: Zürich

Liechtenstein has no commercial airport. **Zürich Kloten (ZRH)** is approximately 1.5 hours by road and is the primary hub for repatriation. Multiple direct daily flights to UK airports. **Basel-Mulhouse (BSL)** is approximately 2 hours by road and provides an alternative.

A specialist repatriation firm will arrange the hearse transfer as part of the overall repatriation.

### Cremation

Cremation is available in Liechtenstein. Repatriation of ashes to the UK is a practical alternative to full body repatriation.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate (in German) | Amt für Bevölkerung und Zivilregister |
| Court clearance (non-natural deaths) | Landgericht (Regional Court) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Health authority |
| Export authorisation | Relevant authority |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, Bern |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### The Simplest Cases in Europe

Liechtenstein's combination of stable governance, functioning civil law, proximity to a major international hub, and a small, accessible territory makes it among the least complex repatriation destinations a UK family will encounter. The documentation requirement for German translation is the main practical step beyond the standard process.
