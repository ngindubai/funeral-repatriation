## Repatriation from Austria: What Families Need to Know

Austria is a low-complexity European repatriation by administrative standards. It is an EU member, a Strasbourg Convention signatory, has direct UK flights from Vienna, and operates established forensic pathology through the Institut für Gerichtliche Medizin at the Medical University of Vienna. The process is well-understood and, in city deaths, fast.

But Austria has a significant Alpine dimension. Austria's ski resorts — in Tyrol, Salzburg, Carinthia, and Vorarlberg — draw hundreds of thousands of British tourists each winter. Skiing is by far the most common context for British tourist deaths in Austria. Off-piste skiing, avalanche exposure, and collision accidents on groomed slopes all occur. Mountain rescue recovery must happen before any death documentation can begin, and in avalanche conditions or remote terrain, that recovery is not always immediate.

### Austria's Unique First Step: The Totenbeschau

Austria has a distinctive feature not found in most European countries: the Totenbeschau. Before an official death certificate (Sterbeurkunde) can be issued, a local Totenbeschauer (authorised death examiner — often a local GP with forensic certification) must examine the body and complete a Totenbeschau document. This is a mandatory first step, even in clear natural deaths.

Only after the Totenbeschau is complete does the local Standesamt (registry office) issue the Sterbeurkunde.

### Unnatural Deaths: Staatsanwaltschaft and Gerichtsmedizin

For unnatural, suspicious, or sudden deaths, the Staatsanwaltschaft (Public Prosecution) authorises the case and may order a post-mortem. The post-mortem is carried out by the Gerichtsmedizin — the forensic medicine institute attached to a university (Vienna, Graz, or Innsbruck depending on location). Once the Staatsanwaltschaft confirms the investigation is complete, the district authority (Bezirksverwaltungsbehörde) issues the Überführungsgenehmigung (international transport permit).

### Alpine Resort Cases

Deaths at major ski resorts (St. Anton, Kitzbühel, Mayrhofen in Tyrol; Zell am See, Saalbach in Salzburg; Lech, Zürs in Vorarlberg) are handled by the local Bergrettung (Alpine Rescue Service) and local police. The Bergrettung recovers the body and hands it over to the local police district. The Totenbeschau and Sterbeurkunde are then issued in the nearest town with a Standesamt.

For deaths in this region, Innsbruck Airport (INN) provides useful direct or connecting access. For all other Austrian cases, Vienna International Airport (VIE) is the main cargo hub with established SAS, Austrian Airlines, and British Airways connections to London.

### Strasbourg Convention

The Überführungsgenehmigung issued by the Bezirksverwaltungsbehörde is accepted directly by UK authorities under the Strasbourg Convention. Additional consular legalisation is not required.
