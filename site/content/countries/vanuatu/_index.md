## Repatriation from Vanuatu: What Families Need to Know

Vanuatu presents a specific challenge that families should understand before any other detail: there is no British Embassy or High Commission resident in Vanuatu. Under the UK-Australia Consular Sharing Agreement, the Australian High Commission in Port Vila provides emergency consular assistance to British nationals. The FCDO in London should also be notified. The British High Commission in Port Moresby holds formal diplomatic accreditation to Vanuatu but is not the operational first contact.

This arrangement works — it is established and Australian consular staff are trained for these situations — but it is a different process from countries with a resident British diplomatic presence.

### Who to Call

1. Australian High Commission Port Vila — for immediate consular assistance in-country.
2. FCDO Emergency Travel Line in London — 020 7008 5000 (24 hours).
3. A specialist UK repatriation company.

### The 80-Island Challenge

Vanuatu is an archipelago of 80 islands spread over 1,300 km of the South Pacific. Most British visitors stay in Port Vila on Efate, or travel to Luganville on Espiritu Santo (Santo). Deaths in these locations connect relatively quickly to Vila Central Hospital.

Deaths on Tanna (where Mount Yasur is located), Malekula, Ambrym, or any of the outer islands require inter-island charter or boat transfer to Port Vila before documentation begins. For remote outer islands, this transfer phase can take two to five days depending on available transport and weather.

### Forensic Capacity

Vila Central Hospital in Port Vila is the main medical and forensic facility. Its forensic pathology capacity is limited. For complex deaths — disputed cause, trauma requiring specialist examination, possible criminal involvement — the Vanuatu government may request specialist forensic assistance from Australia. This assistance takes time to arrange and adds significantly to the overall timeline.

### Mount Yasur and Volcanic Risk

Mount Yasur on Tanna Island is one of the world's most accessible active volcanoes and attracts British tourists. Deaths from volcanic activity, toxic gas inhalation, or falls near the crater rim do occur. Deaths in this context involve additional investigation and require inter-island transfer to Port Vila before documentation can proceed.

### Cyclone Season

Cyclone season runs from November to April. Tropical cyclones regularly disrupt inter-island boat and charter aircraft services. A death on an outer island during cyclone season may face extended transfer delays before the body can reach Port Vila.
