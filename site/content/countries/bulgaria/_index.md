Bulgaria is a popular summer holiday destination for British travellers, particularly the Black Sea resorts of Sunny Beach, Golden Sands, and Albena. Deaths at these coastal resorts, some distance from Sofia, add an internal transfer step before international documentation can be processed.

Bulgaria is a member of the European Union and a signatory to the Council of Europe Agreement on the Transfer of Corpses (Strasbourg, 1973). This provides a clear legal framework for repatriation, but it does not eliminate Bulgarian domestic requirements. Every step must be completed in the correct sequence before a body can be released.

## What happens immediately after a death in Bulgaria

The death must be registered with the local municipality (obshtina). For deaths in resort areas, this is typically handled by the local administration rather than a city office. A Bulgarian death certificate (Akta za smartta) is issued.

For any death that is sudden, unexplained, or where the cause is unclear, the Regional Prosecutor's Office is notified. The Prosecutor authorises the release of the body to the forensic institute. This step alone can take 3-7 days. There is no way to bypass it.

## British Embassy Sofia

The British Embassy in Sofia must be notified as early as possible. Consular staff will issue UK-facing documentation, advise on what Bulgarian paperwork is required, and can help if there are administrative difficulties. They cannot override Bulgarian legal requirements or accelerate a post-mortem.

Contact the Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office (FCDO) emergency line: +44 1908 516666 (24 hours, 7 days).

## Embalming and the laissez-passer

Bulgarian law requires embalming for all international repatriations. The embalming must be carried out by a licensed Bulgarian funeral director. A certificate of embalming is then submitted alongside the application for the laissez-passer, which is the official transit permit issued by the Bulgarian Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

The laissez-passer, combined with the Akta za smartta and the certificate of freedom from contagious disease (issued by the Regional Health Inspectorate), forms the documentation package required for air freight clearance.

## Air freight from Sofia

Sofia Airport (SOF) has direct routes to London Heathrow and Gatwick with several carriers. Human remains must be transported in a hermetically sealed zinc-lined coffin, as required under the Council of Europe Agreement. The UK funeral director coordinates collection from the cargo facility on arrival.
