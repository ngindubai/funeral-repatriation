Brunei presents a manageable repatriation case for UK families. The British High Commission maintains a full resident presence in Bandar Seri Begawan. Direct Royal Brunei Airlines flights connect London Heathrow to Brunei International Airport (BWN). The administrative system is well-organised by regional standards.

The distinguishing factor is religious classification. Islam is Brunei's state religion and Malay Islamic Monarchy (Melayu Islam Beraja — MIB) is the constitutional framework. Muslim deaths in Brunei fall under the jurisdiction of the Department of Religious Affairs, which must issue clearance before any export permit is granted. For UK families dealing with a non-Muslim death, this adds no complication. For Muslim deaths, the Religious Affairs process must run in parallel with civil registration.

## British High Commission Bandar Seri Begawan

The British High Commission in Bandar Seri Begawan provides full consular services. The High Commission registers the death and issues the notifications required for UK probate and administrative purposes. Staff are experienced with the British expat community in Brunei, which includes oil and gas sector workers, educators, and families connected to British Garrison Brunei.

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line first: **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). British High Commission Bandar Seri Begawan: +673 222 2231.

## British Garrison Brunei

British Garrison Brunei is one of the UK's longest-standing overseas military deployments. It houses the 1st Battalion, Royal Gurkha Rifles and associated British Army units. Deaths among Garrison personnel or dependants follow a distinct process coordinated between the British High Commission, the Garrison command, and the Ministry of Defence's Defence Inquiries. Families in this situation will be assigned a casualty notification officer. The civilian repatriation process described here does not apply.

## Civil registration: standard deaths

For non-Muslim deaths, or where the deceased is not subject to Islamic personal law, registration goes through the Brunei civil registry. The attending physician certifies cause of death. The civil registry issues the death certificate in Malay and English.

For non-natural deaths — accidents, sudden illness without prior medical history — the Royal Brunei Police Force attends, and the Attorney General's Chambers may require an inquest before the death certificate is issued. This adds 7 to 14 days in most cases.

## Department of Religious Affairs: Muslim deaths

Brunei's Department of Religious Affairs (Jabatan Hal Ehwal Ugama Islam, JHEAS) must clear Muslim deaths before an export permit can be issued. The process involves:

1. Certification that Islamic funeral rites have been considered
2. Confirmation that repatriation to the UK is permissible under the circumstances
3. Issue of religious clearance document

The Department typically processes these in 2 to 5 working days for routine cases. The British High Commission can advise on the specific requirements for each case.

## Documentation required

| Document | Language | Issuer |
|---|---|---|
| Death certificate | Malay / English | Civil Registry |
| Medical certificate | English | Attending physician |
| Police clearance | English | Royal Brunei Police Force |
| Attorney General clearance | English | Attorney General's Chambers |
| Religious Affairs clearance | Malay | Department of Religious Affairs (Muslim deaths) |
| Ministry of Health export permit | English | Ministry of Health |
| Embalming certificate | English | Licensed funeral director |

## Routing to the UK

Royal Brunei Airlines operates direct flights from Brunei International Airport (BWN) to London Heathrow. This is the standard and most reliable cargo route. Connecting options via Singapore Changi (SIN) are available through Singapore Airlines and other carriers.

Cargo transit: 2 to 3 days from Brunei to the UK under standard cargo conditions once documentation has cleared.
