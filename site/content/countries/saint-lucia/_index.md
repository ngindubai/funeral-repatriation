## Repatriation from Saint Lucia: What Families Need to Know

Saint Lucia is one of the UK's most popular honeymoon and luxury resort destinations. The Pitons, the drive-in sulphur springs at Soufriere, and the resort hotels along the west coast draw significant British visitor volumes year-round.

There is no resident British diplomatic post in Saint Lucia. An Honorary British Consul operates in Castries and handles day-to-day consular contact. The British High Commission in Bridgetown, Barbados, has formal accreditation to Saint Lucia and coordinates on more complex cases.

### Who to Call

1. Honorary British Consul Castries — first consular contact.
2. FCDO Emergency Travel Line: 020 7008 5000 (24 hours).
3. A specialist UK repatriation company.

### The Pitons and Southern Resort Area

The majority of British visitors stay in the resort corridor between Rodney Bay in the north and Soufriere in the south. The Pitons — Gros Piton and Petit Piton — are UNESCO-listed volcanic peaks. Deaths near the Pitons or in the Soufriere volcanic park area require road transfer north to Victoria Hospital in Castries. The road is winding and takes approximately 2 hours from Soufriere to Castries in normal traffic.

### Victoria Hospital

Victoria Hospital in Castries is the main national hospital and the site for forensic post-mortem examinations. Forensic pathology capacity is limited by UK standards. The coroner inquest process for unnatural deaths is standard throughout the Eastern Caribbean.

### Cargo Routing

Saint Lucia cargo connects to the UK via Hewanorra International Airport (UVF) in Vieux Fort, the southern end of the island. Cargo transits to Barbados (BGI) and then connects with British Airways or Virgin Atlantic direct services to London Gatwick. Some routing uses Antigua as an alternative transit point.

Note: George F. L. Charles Airport (SLU) near Castries handles inter-island and short-runway aircraft only — it is not usable for cargo repatriation purposes.
