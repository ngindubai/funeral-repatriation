## Repatriation from Montserrat to the UK

Montserrat is a British Overseas Territory in the Leeward Islands of the eastern Caribbean. A catastrophic series of eruptions from the **Soufrière Hills volcano** in 1995–1997 destroyed the former capital Plymouth and forced evacuation of most of the southern half of the island. Today, the island's de facto capital is **Brades** in the north, with a permanent population of approximately 5,000.

As a British Overseas Territory, the Crown's interests are represented by the Governor. Contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours) immediately after a death.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is handled by the **Registrar General's Office** in Brades, Government of Montserrat. English common law applies. Non-natural deaths require clearance by the **Royal Montserrat Police Force** before funeral arrangements proceed.

### Soufrière Hills Exclusion Zone

The southern zone remains off-limits due to ongoing volcanic activity. The zone boundary is set by the **Montserrat Volcano Observatory (MVO)** and can change with volcanic activity. Any death occurring in or near the zone may involve additional complications.

### No Cremation Facilities

No cremation facilities are available in Montserrat. Full body repatriation to the UK is required.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Registrar General's Office, Government of Montserrat |
| Police Force clearance | Royal Montserrat Police Force (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local funeral director |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Montserrat health authority |
| Export permit | Government of Montserrat |
| UK Coroner notification | FCO / HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

**John A. Osborne Airport (MNI)** near Brades handles turboprop aircraft only — no jet service. Regional airlines fly to **Antigua (ANU)**, from which **British Airways** operates direct service to London Gatwick (LGW).
