## Repatriation from San Marino to the UK

San Marino is one of the world's smallest countries — 61 square kilometres entirely surrounded by the Emilia-Romagna and Marche regions of Italy. It claims to be the world's oldest republic, dating from 301 AD. British nationals visiting typically do so as a day trip from the Italian Adriatic coast. Repatriation, when required, follows Italian civil law practice closely.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British Embassy Rome (+39 06 4220 0001) handles consular matters for San Marino on a non-resident basis.

### The Legal Framework

Civil registration in San Marino is handled by the **Ufficio di Stato Civile** (Civil Registry Office). The Ufficio issues the official death certificate. Where death is not from natural causes, the **Corpo della Gendarmeria** (San Marino police/gendarmerie) investigates and must authorise release.

Documentation is in Italian throughout, requiring certified English translation.

### Routing: Rimini or Bologna

San Marino has no commercial airport. **Federico Fellini International Airport in Rimini (RMI)** is approximately 30 minutes by road and is the standard choice for repatriations. **Bologna Guglielmo Marconi Airport (BLQ)** is approximately 1 hour by road and offers more frequent UK connections.

### Cremation Options

San Marino has no cremation facilities. Transfer to Italy for cremation follows Italian civil law procedures. After cremation, the ashes are repatriated to the UK. This is a well-established route.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate (Italian) | Ufficio di Stato Civile |
| Gendarmerie clearance | Corpo della Gendarmeria (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Health authority |
| Export authorisation | Relevant authority |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, Rome |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |
