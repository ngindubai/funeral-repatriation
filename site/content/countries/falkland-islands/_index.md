The Falkland Islands are a British Overseas Territory in the South Atlantic Ocean, approximately 8,000 miles from the UK and around 300 miles east of the Argentine coast. The islands are made up of two main islands, East Falkland and West Falkland, and around 778 smaller islands. Stanley, on East Falkland, is the capital.

English common law applies throughout. All official documentation is in English. Death registration and death investigation follow procedures closely modelled on those of England and Wales, administered by the Falkland Islands Government.

## Who to call first

Contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). Because the Falkland Islands are a British Overseas Territory, the Governor's Office in Stanley also has a responsibility to assist British nationals. Both lines should be used.

## Legal framework

Death registration in the Falkland Islands is handled by the Registrar of Births, Deaths and Marriages, based in Stanley. Non-natural deaths are reported to the Royal Falkland Islands Police Force, which investigates and, where required, notifies the Falkland Islands Coroner. The Coroner must issue a release before any transfer can proceed.

For hospital deaths at King Edward VII Memorial Hospital (the islands' sole hospital, in Stanley), the treating physician issues the death certificate. Sudden or unexplained deaths go through the police and Coroner's process.

## Routing to the UK

Mount Pleasant Airport (MPN), on East Falkland, is the main international airport. There are two realistic routing options:

**Option 1 — RAF rotation.** The RAF operates a regular Mount Pleasant to RAF Brize Norton (BZZ) rotation as part of the South Atlantic Air Bridge. Human remains can be carried on this service with advance coordination. This is generally the most direct route to the UK, but scheduling is controlled by the RAF and not by civilian repatriation firms. Timing depends on the rotation schedule.

**Option 2 — Commercial via Chile.** LATAM Airlines operates the commercial service between MPN and Punta Arenas (PUQ) in Chilean Patagonia. From Punta Arenas, connections go to Santiago (SCL), from which flights to London Heathrow are available. This routing involves Chilean transit, which adds documentation requirements. Total transit time for this route is typically 2 to 4 days.

## Required documents

| Document | Issuing authority |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Registrar of Births, Deaths and Marriages, FIG |
| Coroner's order | Falkland Islands Coroner |
| Police clearance | Royal Falkland Islands Police Force (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local funeral director |
| Freedom from infection certificate | FIG Health Department |
| Export permit | Falkland Islands Government |
| Chilean transit documentation | Where applicable via LATAM route |

## Cremation

There are no cremation facilities on the Falkland Islands. Full body repatriation to the UK is the only option unless families arrange transfer to Chile for cremation before return.

## Practical considerations

The population of the Falkland Islands is approximately 3,500. Most permanent British residents are involved in farming, fisheries, or the oil and gas sector. Military personnel and their families are based at Mount Pleasant. Tourist arrivals are primarily cruise ship passengers on Antarctic itineraries.

Cold storage infrastructure is limited outside Stanley and King Edward VII Memorial Hospital. Specialist repatriation firms should be appointed as early as possible to ensure body preparation is handled correctly while documentation is being processed.
