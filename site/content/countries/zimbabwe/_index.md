## Repatriation from Zimbabwe

Zimbabwe rejoined the Commonwealth in December 2018, but the representative office is the British Embassy in Harare, not a High Commission. British Airways operates direct services from Harare Robert Gabriel Mugabe International Airport (HRE) to London Heathrow, giving direct cargo access.

Zimbabwe attracts British visitors primarily for Victoria Falls, Hwange National Park, and Lake Kariba. Deaths in national parks and at Victoria Falls require involvement from the Zimbabwe Parks and Wildlife Management Authority alongside the Zimbabwe Republic Police.

### Victoria Falls deaths

Victoria Falls is the most common site for British tourist deaths in Zimbabwe. Activities including bungee jumping, white-water rafting, and gorge swings carry inherent risk. Deaths at the Falls or within the national park area require Zimbabwe Parks and Wildlife Management Authority certification before the Zimbabwe Republic Police can proceed with their investigation. This adds one to three days to the standard timeline.

The Zambian side of Victoria Falls is administered separately; deaths there follow Zambian rather than Zimbabwean procedures. Confirm which side of the border the death occurred.

### Official process

Deaths are registered at the Registrar General's Office under the Civil Registry Act. For unnatural deaths, the Zimbabwe Republic Police investigate. Post-mortems are conducted at Harare Central Hospital's Department of Forensic Medicine. The Attorney General's Office must authorise both the post-mortem and the release of the body; this step is the primary variable in the overall timeline.

The Ministry of Health and Child Care issues the export permit once all documentation is complete.

### Administrative considerations

Zimbabwean government department capacity is variable. Queues and procedural delays occur at a higher rate than in comparable Southern African countries. A specialist with established local agents in Harare is particularly valuable here.
