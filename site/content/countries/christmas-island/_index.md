Christmas Island is an Australian external territory in the Indian Ocean, approximately 1550 kilometres south of Java and 2600 kilometres northwest of Perth. It is home to around 2000 residents, predominantly of Chinese and Malay descent, with a smaller Australian-background population. The island is best known for its red crab migration and its immigration detention facility.

Australian law applies in full. UK families dealing with a death on Christmas Island should call the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (available 24 hours) and expect consular assistance to be provided by the British High Commission in Canberra.

## Legal Framework and Authorities

Christmas Island is administered by the Australian Government through the Administrator of Christmas Island. The Australian Federal Police operates on the island. Non-natural deaths fall under the Western Australia Coroner's jurisdiction.

UK consular assistance is handled remotely through the British High Commission in Canberra, with the Honorary Consul in Perth providing closer liaison.

The UK travel advisory for Christmas Island is at [gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice/christmas-island](https://www.gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice/christmas-island).

## The Repatriation Route

The standard route is:

1. Christmas Island Airport (XCH) to Perth (PER) approximately 3.5 hours via Virgin Australia (approximately twice weekly)
2. Perth (PER) to London Heathrow (LHR) approximately 17 hours via Qantas or partner airlines

Twice-weekly departures from XCH mean that timing is important. A death occurring the day after a departure could mean waiting up to a week for the next available flight. A specialist firm will monitor schedules and manage cargo bookings accordingly.

## Community Considerations

Christmas Island has a significant Muslim Malay community and a substantial Buddhist and non-religious Chinese community. Cultural and religious expectations around death and burial differ between these groups. A specialist firm experienced in multicultural repatriation will be essential to ensure the family's wishes are respected.

Deaths involving the immigration detention facility may involve additional administrative layers, particularly where the deceased is not an Australian or British national.
