## Repatriation from Slovenia: What Families Need to Know

Slovenia is one of the more straightforward central European repatriations. It is an EU member, a Strasbourg Convention signatory, and has a functioning forensic institute at the University of Ljubljana. The Strasbourg laissez-passer mortuaire replaces the standard national export permit, which reduces bureaucratic steps compared with non-Strasbourg countries.

Slovenia has grown rapidly as a British activity tourism destination: Lake Bled, the Julian Alps, the Soca Valley, and winter skiing at Kranjska Gora all draw significant British visitor numbers.

### The Strasbourg Convention

Slovenia signed the Strasbourg Convention on the Transfer of Bodies (Council of Europe, 1973). For repatriation to the UK, this means a laissez-passer mortuaire is issued rather than a country-specific export permit. The laissez-passer is the primary transport document. Requirements under the Convention are standardised, which makes the process more predictable than in non-signatory countries.

### The Institute of Forensic Medicine

Post-mortems for unnatural, suspicious, or sudden unexplained deaths are conducted by the Institut za sodno medicino (Institute of Forensic Medicine) at the Faculty of Medicine, University of Ljubljana. This is the sole national forensic pathology centre. Cases from anywhere in Slovenia transfer to Ljubljana for full forensic examination.

### Mountain Deaths: Triglav and the Julian Alps

Triglav National Park is Slovenia's only national park and covers the Julian Alps in the northwest. Mount Triglav (2864m) is the highest peak and a major objective for British climbers and trekkers. Deaths on Triglav or in the wider Julian Alps are handled by the Gorska Reševalna Služba (GRS — Mountain Rescue Association of Slovenia), which operates rescue posts throughout the range.

GRS recovery from Triglav typically takes 4 to 24 hours depending on terrain and weather. Recovery is required before the forensic and documentation chain can begin.

The Soca Valley — popular for kayaking, rafting, and swimming in the glacial Soca River — generates drowning and water sports deaths. These are handled by local police and transferred to Ljubljana for post-mortem.

### Documentation Chain

1. Smrtovni list (death certificate) from the Upravna Enota (administrative unit).
2. Police investigation closure for unnatural deaths.
3. Forensic post-mortem report from Institute of Forensic Medicine, Ljubljana.
4. Strasbourg Convention laissez-passer mortuaire.
5. Certified English translations of Slovenian documents.
6. Embalming certificate confirming preparation to Strasbourg standards.

### Routing

Ljubljana Jože Pucnik Airport (LJU) has no nonstop services to the UK since Adria Airways ceased operations in 2019. Connections run via Vienna (Austrian Airlines), Frankfurt or Munich (Lufthansa), or Amsterdam (KLM). Human remains cargo routes via Vienna or Frankfurt are most common.

easyJet operates seasonal passenger services from London Gatwick to Ljubljana but these are passenger aircraft, not cargo. Cargo must be pre-booked on a full-service carrier.
