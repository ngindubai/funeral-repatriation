## Repatriation from Palau to the UK

Palau is a Pacific island nation comprising approximately 340 islands in the western Pacific Ocean, situated roughly 500 km east of the Philippines. It is internationally known as a world-class diving and marine tourism destination, and a small but consistent number of UK nationals visit each year.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British Embassy Manila (+63 2 8858 2200) handles consular matters for Palau on a non-resident basis.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is handled through the **Bureau of Vital Statistics**. Where death is not from natural causes, the **Palau Police Department** must investigate and issue clearance before the body can be released. Palau's legal framework has significant US influence from the Trust Territory era.

### Mortuary Infrastructure

Palau has very limited mortuary infrastructure compared to most countries that receive international tourists. Embalming capacity exists in Koror, but is limited in volume and technical capability. A specialist firm will need to coordinate closely with local contacts to manage timelines.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Bureau of Vital Statistics |
| Police clearance | Palau Police Department (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Relevant authority |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, Manila |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing and Transport

**Roman Tmetuchl International Airport (ROR)** near Koror is Palau's main airport. International connections go via:

- **Manila (MNL)** — Philippines Airlines or United Airlines, then to London Heathrow
- **Guam (GUM)** — United Airlines, then connecting to the US and onwards to UK

There are no direct flights to the UK or Europe from Palau.

### Cremation

Cremation is not available in Palau. Full body repatriation to the UK is required.
