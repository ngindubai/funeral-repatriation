The Central African Republic is a landlocked country in the heart of Africa, bordered by Chad, Sudan, South Sudan, the Democratic Republic of Congo, the Republic of Congo, and Cameroon. Despite its vast territory and natural resources, it is consistently ranked among the world's most fragile states.

Armed groups — including former Séléka factions, anti-Balaka militias, and others — control large swaths of the country outside Bangui. Russian Wagner Group (Africa Corps) has been deployed since 2018 and plays a significant role in government military operations.

## Who to contact first

Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: **020 7008 5000** (available 24 hours). The British Embassy Yaoundé, Cameroon provides non-resident consular coverage for CAR: +237 222 220 545.

Do not travel to CAR outside Bangui. For deaths in Bangui, do not travel without FCDO guidance — the all-but-essential advisory applies there too.

## British Embassy Yaoundé: non-resident coverage

All consular services for British nationals in CAR come from the British Embassy in Yaoundé. The Embassy registers the death for UK purposes and advises on current conditions and the feasibility of any given case.

## Civil registration in Bangui

CAR operates French civil law. Death registration in Bangui runs through the état civil. Documentation is in French. The attending physician certifies cause of death. The civil registrar issues the acte de décès (death certificate).

For non-natural deaths, the Forces Armées Centrafricaines (FACA) or police must attend and issue clearance. Given the security environment, this can involve both national and international (MINUSCA UN peacekeeping) chains of authority.

## Ministry of Health export permit

Once civil registration and security clearance are complete, the Ministry of Health issues the export permit. A licensed funeral director embalms and prepares the body in Bangui. All documents require certified English translation.

## Routing from Bangui

Bangui M'Poko International Airport (BGF):
- Ethiopian Airlines to Addis Ababa (ADD) — most reliable UK cargo route
- Air France direct Bangui-Paris CDG — connecting London
- Kenya Airways to Nairobi (NBO)

Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is the most reliable current option for UK-bound cargo.

## Outside Bangui: not achievable

FCDO advises against all travel to all areas of CAR outside Bangui. Armed groups control most of the country. Body recovery from these areas is not achievable by civilian repatriation means. Even Bangui remains under an all-but-essential advisory.

## What a specialist firm does

A specialist firm with Central African Francophone experience manages FACA/police clearance, civil registration, and Ministry of Health in Bangui. Non-resident Embassy coordination via Yaoundé is essential. Given the security environment, all management is done remotely — do not travel.
