The Pitcairn Islands sit in the South Pacific, roughly halfway between New Zealand and Peru. The group consists of four islands: Pitcairn itself, where approximately 45 people live, and three uninhabited outliers — Henderson, Ducie and Oeno. There is no airport. The only regular access is the supply vessel, which calls approximately every three months.

For UK families dealing with a death on Pitcairn, the situation is without parallel in British repatriation experience. Contact the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 immediately — available 24 hours a day — because the Governor of the Pitcairn Islands, based in Auckland, will need to be engaged from the outset.

## Why the Pitcairn Islands Present Unique Challenges

Pitcairn is a British Overseas Territory. The Governor is appointed by the UK and based in Auckland; the Island Magistrate's Court handles local judicial matters on the island itself. Death registration falls to the Island Registrar.

With a resident population of approximately 45, there is no funeral home, no embalming facility, and no crematorium. Any repatriation requires coordinating across the Island Council, the Governor's Office in Auckland, French Polynesian transit authorities (for the onward flight from Mangareva), and UK authorities.

The FCDO travel advisory should be reviewed before and during any case at [gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice/pitcairn-island](https://www.gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice/pitcairn-island).

## The Repatriation Route

The standard route is:

1. Sea passage on the supply vessel from Pitcairn to Mangareva, French Polynesia (approximately 36 hours)
2. Air Tahiti flight from Mangareva to Papeete (PPT)
3. Air France PPT–CDG (approximately 22 hours)
4. UK connection from Paris Charles de Gaulle

The supply vessel schedule is the single biggest constraint. It operates approximately quarterly. Where timelines are critical, specialist firms may explore charter vessel options, but costs are exceptional and scheduling still depends on weather and sea conditions in the South Pacific.

French Polynesia is an overseas collectivity of France. Transit documentation must comply with both French Polynesian and French metropolitan requirements, including certified English translations of all French-language documentation.

## Required Documents

| Document | Issuing Authority |
|---|---|
| Pitcairn Islands death certificate | Island Registrar, Pitcairn |
| UK consular death registration | Governor's Office, Auckland |
| Repatriation authority | Governor's Office, Auckland |
| Embalming certificate (if applicable) | Licensed practitioner |
| French Polynesia transit documentation | French Polynesian civil authorities |
| Cargo documentation (each leg) | Airline/shipping operator |
| UK receiving funeral director confirmation | UK funeral director |

## Costs and Timelines

There are no standard cost benchmarks for Pitcairn repatriation. The logistical variables — charter vessel, multi-leg routing, remote documentation — make every case unique. Families should contact a specialist international repatriation firm before making any assumptions. For current pricing, [contact our team directly](/contact/).

A realistic timeline is 30 to 90 days. This is not a reflection of administrative delay; it is a direct consequence of geography and vessel frequency.

## Practical Considerations for Families

Notify the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 at the earliest opportunity. The Governor's Office in Auckland will be the primary point of official contact. Travel insurance policies should be reviewed for coverage in British Overseas Territories — Pitcairn's exceptional isolation means many standard policies have exclusions.
