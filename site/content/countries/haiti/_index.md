## Repatriation from Haiti to the UK

Haiti is one of the most challenging repatriation destinations in the world for UK families. Sustained gang violence, a largely dysfunctional state apparatus, and a collapsed civil registration system in many urban areas create a repatriation environment unlike almost anywhere else in the Western Hemisphere.

FCDO advises against all but essential travel to Haiti. This guidance has been in place for an extended period due to the security situation. For urgent consular assistance, contact the FCDO on 020 7008 5000 (24 hours). The British High Commission Kingston can be reached on +1 876 936 0700.

### The Security Situation

Since the assassination of President Jovenel Moïse in July 2021, and through 2024–2025, criminal gangs — particularly the G9 alliance and Viv Ansanm coalition — have seized control of a significant proportion of Port-au-Prince and the surrounding area. The Multinational Security Support Mission (MSS), led by Kenya and authorised by UN Security Council Resolution 2699, began deploying in 2024, but the security situation in large parts of the capital and the Artibonite department remains severe as of May 2026.

The practical consequence for repatriation: in gang-controlled areas, access to the body and to civil registration offices may not be possible for days, weeks, or at all. A specialist repatriation firm will advise honestly on what is achievable in specific circumstances.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration in Haiti is handled by the **Office National d'Identification (ONI)**. Before remains can be exported, the **Parquet Civil** must confirm that no criminal investigation is pending and issue clearance.

In areas where civil registration has functionally collapsed, the process of obtaining a formal death certificate becomes a specialist task requiring on-the-ground contacts and, in some cases, significant waiting time.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Acte de décès (death certificate) | Office National d'Identification (ONI) |
| Parquet Civil clearance | Parquet Civil |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Ministry of Health |
| Export permit | Ministry of Interior |
| Consular death registration | British High Commission, Kingston |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

All French-language documents require certified English translation before UK authorities will process them.

### Routing and Transport

Toussaint Louverture International Airport (PAP) in Port-au-Prince is Haiti's main international gateway. Security incidents have periodically disrupted airport operations. Standard routing options:

- **Via Miami**: American Airlines operates Port-au-Prince to Miami, with straightforward UK connections.
- **Via New York (JFK)**: American Airlines and other carriers serve the route.

Schedule reliability is affected by the security situation and can change with limited notice.

### Vodou and Religious Considerations

Haiti's religious landscape is a unique blend of Catholicism, Protestantism, and Vodou traditions. Vodou funerary practices can involve specific rituals relating to the handling and timing of burial. A specialist repatriation firm experienced in Haiti will understand how to manage these sensitivities with respect.

### Cremation

Cremation facilities are not available in Haiti. Families requiring cremation must arrange full repatriation of the body to the UK.
