Greenland is the world's largest island and an autonomous territory of the Kingdom of Denmark. The vast majority of its 56,000 residents live in coastal settlements separated by uninhabited wilderness. Danish law governs civil matters including death registration.

Most international air connections route through Nuuk or Kangerlussuaq to Copenhagen. Air Greenland is the sole operator on most routes, with limited weekly frequencies. The British Embassy in Copenhagen handles consular matters on a non-resident basis.
