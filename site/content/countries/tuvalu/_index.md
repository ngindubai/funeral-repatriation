## Repatriation from Tuvalu to the UK

Tuvalu is a Polynesian island nation of nine low-lying coral atolls and reef islands in the central Pacific Ocean, with a total land area of just 26 km² spread across 756,000 km² of ocean. The population is approximately 11,000. The capital, Funafuti, sits on an atoll of the same name. Tuvalu is one of the world's least-visited countries, and very few British nationals travel here.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The British High Commission Suva, Fiji (+679 322 9100) handles consular matters for Tuvalu on a non-resident basis.

### The Legal Framework

Death registration is handled by the **Tuvalu Births, Deaths and Marriages Registry**. Non-natural deaths require **Tuvalu Police Service** investigation and clearance. Documentation is in English throughout.

### Outer Atoll Transport

Only Funafuti has an international airport. The eight remaining atolls are accessible only by boat — the government vessel MV Nivaga III provides inter-island transport, but schedules are infrequent and weather-dependent. A death on an outer atoll may require days simply to move the body to Funafuti.

### Mortuary Infrastructure

Tuvalu has very limited mortuary and embalming capacity. A specialist repatriation firm must confirm local capability before committing to any timeline.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | Tuvalu Births, Deaths and Marriages Registry |
| Police clearance | Tuvalu Police Service (non-natural deaths) |
| Embalming certificate | Licensed local mortuary |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Health authority |
| Export permit | Relevant authority |
| Consular death registration | BHC Suva, Fiji |
| UK Coroner permission (if applicable) | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

All international repatriation routes via **Nadi (NAN)** or **Suva (SUV)** in Fiji on Fiji Airways, then connecting to Sydney, Singapore, or Hong Kong for UK flights.

### Cremation

Cremation is not available in Tuvalu. Full body repatriation is required.
