Bringing a loved one home from Angola is more complex than most UK families expect. The paperwork is in Portuguese. Funeral infrastructure outside Luanda is limited. If your relative worked in the oil sector, their employer's HR and insurance teams will already be involved — but the process still requires specialist coordination.

This page sets out exactly what happens and what you need to do.

## What triggers the repatriation process in Angola

When a British national dies in Angola, the process starts with the Criminal Investigation Service (Servico de Investigacao Criminal, SIC) if the death is sudden, unexplained, or violent. The SIC must authorise release of the body before a funeral director can proceed with embalming or preparation.

For natural deaths in hospital, the attending doctor issues the death certificate (Certidao de Obito) and the civil registry records the death. This is usually faster than an SIC case.

The British Embassy in Luanda then registers the death consularly. If you are in the UK, you can appoint a repatriation specialist with power of attorney to handle everything on the ground.

## Documentation: what is needed and why

Every document produced in Angola is in Portuguese. The UK authorities require certified English translations before import. That means engaging a sworn translator — and that adds days to the process.

The core document set:

| Document | Who issues it |
|---|---|
| Certidao de Obito (death certificate) | Civil registry |
| SIC forensic release | SIC — for non-natural deaths |
| Freedom from Infection / embalming certificate | Licensed funeral director |
| Laissez-passer mortuaire | Angolan Ministry of Health |
| British consular death registration | British Embassy Luanda |

The UK Coroner is notified on arrival. A post-mortem in the UK may be required if the cause of death is unclear or the SIC report is incomplete.

## Oil industry deaths: specific considerations

Angola has a large British expatriate population working in the oil and gas sector — BP, TotalEnergies, and numerous service companies operate in Luanda and offshore in the Kwanza Basin. When a company employee dies:

- The employer HR team typically activates corporate travel or group life insurance immediately
- Some policies include repatriation in their terms; others require separate coordination
- Employer HR holding power of attorney means the family may not need to manage any Angolan paperwork directly
- Offshore deaths require coordination with INAMAR and may involve a recovery vessel before any aviation transfer is possible

Always ask the employer what their duty-of-care protocol covers before engaging separately.

## Landmine risk and remote province deaths

Certain provinces — particularly Cuando Cubango and Moxico in southeastern Angola — retain landmine contamination from the 1975–2002 civil war. HALO Trust mapping is the definitive reference. Deaths in these areas may involve complex recovery operations before any repatriation step begins.

In all remote province deaths, the first priority is getting a local death certificate from the nearest municipal civil registry. Without it, no subsequent step can proceed.

## What to do when someone dies in Angola

1. Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: 020 7008 5000 (24 hours)
2. Contact the British Embassy Luanda: +244 222 334 582
3. Notify the insurer or employer HR immediately — establish who holds authority
4. Do not authorise embalming or body movement before SIC clearance in non-natural deaths
5. Contact a UK-based repatriation specialist with Angola experience
