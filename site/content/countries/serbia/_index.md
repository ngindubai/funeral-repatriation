## Repatriation from Serbia

Serbia is a Strasbourg Convention signatory and Council of Europe member. The Strasbourg Convention (signed 1973) standardises multilingual death certificate extracts, which reduces translation complexity compared to non-signatory countries. For UK families, this means the Izvod iz maticne knjige umrlih (death certificate extract) is issued in a standardised format.

Air Serbia operates direct flights from Nikola Tesla Airport (BEG) to London Heathrow. This makes Serbia one of the most accessible non-EU European countries for cargo repatriation.

### Unnatural deaths: MUP and the Prosecutor's Office

For violent, sudden, or unexplained deaths, the MUP (Ministry of Interior Police) investigates the scene. The Javno Tuzilastvo (Public Prosecutor's Office) then takes jurisdiction and may order a post-mortem at the Institute of Forensic Medicine, University of Belgrade.

The Prosecutor must formally authorise release before embalming can proceed. For straightforward accidents, this process moves within 7 to 14 days. For deaths with criminal elements, timelines extend.

### Deaths outside Belgrade

Serbia has popular ski resorts at Kopaonik and Zlatibor and river tourism along the Danube. Deaths at these locations are handled by local police. For complex cases requiring Institute of Forensic Medicine involvement, the body is transferred to Belgrade.

### Documentation and language

Serbian documentation is in Serbian (Cyrillic script). Certified English translations are required for UK use. Allow two to three days for translation once all documents are complete.
