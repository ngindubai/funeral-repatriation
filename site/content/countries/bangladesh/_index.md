## Repatriation from Bangladesh

Bangladesh has a substantial UK diaspora, which means the repatriation route between Dhaka and the UK is one of the most frequently used in South Asia. Biman Bangladesh Airlines operates direct flights from Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport (DAC) to London Heathrow. Local funeral directors in Dhaka are well-experienced with UK documentation requirements.

Bangladesh is a Commonwealth country. The consular representative is the British High Commission, not an Embassy.

### Official process

Deaths are registered at the local Union Parishad (rural) or City Corporation (urban). The certified death certificate is issued under the Births and Deaths Registration Act 2004.

For unnatural deaths, Bangladesh Police investigate. The magistrate court may order a post-mortem at Dhaka Medical College Hospital (DMCH) Forensic Department or an approved divisional hospital. The Directorate General of Health Services (DGHS) issues the export permit.

### Deaths outside Dhaka

Chittagong, Sylhet, and Rajshahi all have divisional hospitals, but forensic capacity varies. For complex cases, transfer to Dhaka Medical College Hospital may be required. Sylhet is of particular note: it has a large population with UK connections and deaths there are a relatively frequent consular matter.

### Monsoon season

The monsoon (June to October) affects road access in low-lying areas of Bangladesh, particularly in Sylhet Division which is prone to flooding. If a death occurs in a rural area during monsoon, transfer logistics may be delayed. This is a planning consideration, not a reason to delay notification.
