## Repatriation from Ghana

Ghana is a Commonwealth country with established consular links to the UK. The British High Commission (not Embassy) in Accra processes UK consular documentation. British Airways operates direct flights from Kotoka International Airport to Heathrow, which removes the routing complexity common across West Africa.

Documentation is in English throughout, which eliminates translation costs and delays. Ghana Health Service processing can still extend timelines, particularly for deaths requiring investigation.

### Deaths outside Accra

Ghana is a large country. Deaths in Kumasi (Ashanti Region), Cape Coast (Central Region), or the northern regions require transfer to Accra for post-mortem at Korle Bu Teaching Hospital and for Ghana Health Service processing. Cold storage outside Accra is limited; transfer should be arranged within 24 to 48 hours of death.

### Post-mortem and documentation

The Department of Pathology and Forensic Medicine at Korle Bu Teaching Hospital handles post-mortems for complex or investigated cases. The Ghana Police Service issues a police report for unnatural deaths. The Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) Registry issues the certified death certificate. Ghana Health Service issues the export permit.

### British High Commission Accra

Address: Julius Nyerere Link, Accra. Phone: +233 30 221 3250. FCDO 24-hour emergency: +44 1908 516666.

As Ghana is a Commonwealth country, the representative office is a High Commission. Consular services are broadly equivalent to an Embassy. Staff can register the death with UK authorities and advise on current Ghanaian requirements.

### Cremation

Cremation is available in Accra but not widely practiced in Ghanaian culture. Ashes repatriation from Ghana is less common than full body repatriation. If cremation is preferred, confirm availability with your specialist before proceeding.
