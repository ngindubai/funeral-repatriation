Cuba presents a unique set of challenges. The Cuban state controls every step of the process, from the forensic examination to the funeral services to the export permit. Families have limited ability to influence timelines or make independent choices. Understanding what is fixed by Cuban law, and what your UK repatriation specialist can manage, is the most important first step.

## The state autopsy requirement

Cuba's forensic authority, Medicina Legal, conducts autopsies on all foreign nationals who die in Cuba. This is not limited to suspicious or violent deaths. It applies universally. The autopsy cannot be refused. Results typically take 7 to 14 days. The body cannot be released to a funeral service until the Medicina Legal examination is complete and signed off.

This requirement is the single largest source of delay in Cuban repatriations. It must be built into any timeline estimate from day one.

## State funeral services

Cuba operates a state monopoly on funeral services through the Empresa de Servicios Funerarios. Private funeral homes have a very limited presence. Embalming must be carried out by state-approved personnel. This is non-negotiable. Families cannot arrange for an independent funeral director to prepare the body.

## British Embassy Havana

The British Embassy in Havana is the primary consular contact. Embassy staff will register the death, issue relevant UK documentation, and assist where possible with the Cuban bureaucracy. They cannot waive the Medicina Legal requirement or accelerate the MINREX export documentation process.

In an emergency, call the FCDO 24-hour line: +44 1908 516666.

## Export documentation from MINREX

The Cuban Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MINREX) issues the formal export documentation package: the laissez-passer, embalming certificate, and sanitary clearance. This process runs parallel to (but after) the Medicina Legal process and typically takes a further 10 to 20 days once the forensic report is received.

In total, from death to departure, 21 to 35 days is the realistic minimum for a straightforward case.

## Routing to the UK

There are no direct flights from Cuba to the UK. Most repatriations route via Havana (HAV) to Madrid (MAD) with Iberia, then onward to a UK airport. Some route via Cancun (CUN) or Toronto (YYZ). Each transit country has its own documentation requirements. The UK specialist must coordinate transit handling at each point.
