## Repatriation from Martinique to the UK

Martinique is an island in the eastern Caribbean Sea and an overseas region and department (région et département d'outre-mer) of the French Republic. As an integral part of France, it is also an outermost region of the European Union. The capital is **Fort-de-France**. Martinique is a popular destination for French and UK tourists.

For consular assistance, contact the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours). The **British Embassy Paris** (+33 1 44 51 31 00) handles consular matters for Martinique on a non-resident basis.

### The Legal Framework

Because Martinique is a French department, the same civil law that applies in Paris applies in Fort-de-France. Death registration is handled by the **mairie** (town hall) of the commune where the death occurred — the same process as anywhere in metropolitan France.

Non-natural deaths require investigation by the **Gendarmerie nationale** or **Police nationale** depending on location. All documentation is in French.

### Required Documents

| Document | Issued By |
|---|---|
| Acte de décès (death certificate) | Mairie of commune of death |
| Certified English translation | Sworn translator |
| Gendarmerie/Police nationale clearance | (Non-natural deaths) |
| Certificat d'embaumement | Licensed local funeral director |
| Certificat de non-contagion | Health authority |
| Autorisation de sortie du territoire | French authorities |
| Consular death registration | British Embassy, Paris |
| UK Coroner permission | HM Coroner, UK |

### Routing to the UK

**Martinique Aimé Césaire International Airport (FDF)** near Fort-de-France has direct **Air France** service to Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG), with a flight time of approximately 9 hours. UK connections from CDG are frequent.

### Cremation

Cremation is available in Martinique. Ashes may be returned to the UK in a sealed urn.
