The Czech Republic is a compact country with well-developed infrastructure. Prague is the dominant destination for British visitors, with the Giant Mountains (Krkonose) and Bohemian spa towns also attracting UK travellers. Most deaths involving British nationals occur in Prague or its immediate surroundings.

## Legal framework

The Czech Republic is an EU member and signatory to the Council of Europe Agreement on the Transfer of Corpses (Strasbourg, 1973 — ETS No. 080). The agreement requires embalming and a laissez-passer for international transfer of human remains. Czech domestic law, including the Act on Civil Registry and the Code of Criminal Procedure, also applies.

## Death registration

Deaths are registered at the local Matrika (registry office) in the district where the death occurred. A death certificate (Umrtni list) is issued in Czech. For unnatural deaths, the police and State Prosecutor are notified.

## British Embassy Prague

The British Embassy in Prague must be notified. Address: Thunovska 14, 118 00 Prague 1. Phone: +420 257 402 111. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 1908 516666. Consular staff will issue UK documentation and advise on Czech procedures.

## State Prosecutor and post-mortem

For violent, sudden, or suspicious deaths, the State Prosecutor orders a forensic post-mortem conducted by the Institute of Forensic Medicine. Prague's institute is well-staffed. Regional centres across the country have adequate capacity, though turnaround can be slower outside the capital. Results typically arrive within 5 to 10 days of the examination.

## Embalming and laissez-passer

Embalming is mandatory under the Strasbourg Agreement. The laissez-passer is issued by the Ministry of Interior or delegated regional authority. Once all four documents are in order (Umrtni list, embalming certificate, freedom from contagious disease clearance, laissez-passer), the body is booked as air cargo.

## Flights from Prague

Vaclav Havel Airport Prague (PRG) has direct flights to London Heathrow, Gatwick, Stansted, and other UK airports. British Airways, easyJet, and Ryanair serve this route. The frequency of direct flights is an advantage: there is rarely a significant wait for available cargo capacity.
