China is home to a significant British expat community, and British nationals also visit for tourism, business, and study. Deaths in China are among the most documentation-intensive repatriations undertaken by UK specialists.

## Legal framework

China is not a signatory to the Strasbourg Agreement. All procedures follow Chinese domestic law and bilateral consular conventions. The primary legislation governing human remains export is administered by the Ministry of Civil Affairs (Minzheng Bu).

## Death registration

The Si Wang Yi Zheng Shu (death certificate) is issued by the local Civil Affairs Bureau (Minzheng Ju). Hospital deaths are registered directly. Deaths outside hospital require a police report first. For unnatural deaths, the Public Security Bureau (PSB) takes jurisdiction immediately.

## British Embassy and Consulates-General

The British Embassy in Beijing covers the whole country. Consulates-General operate in Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, and Wuhan. For deaths in their respective regions, the relevant Consulate-General handles the case. British Embassy Beijing address: 11 Guang Hua Lu, Chaoyang District, Beijing 100600. Phone: +86 10 5192 4000. FCDO 24-hour emergency line: +44 1908 516666.

Contact the nearest British diplomatic post on the day of death. Do not make any arrangements with a local mortuary until Embassy staff have advised which facilities are approved for international repatriation.

## PSB investigation and forensic post-mortem

For violent, sudden, or suspicious deaths, the PSB conducts its investigation before any post-mortem is ordered. The forensic post-mortem is carried out by PSB forensic departments. The PSB must formally close its investigation and release the body before any other steps can proceed. In complex cases, PSB investigations take 2 to 4 weeks.

## Approved mortuary and embalming

Embalming must be carried out at a mortuary approved by the local Civil Affairs Bureau. Not all provinces have facilities meeting international standards. The British Embassy or Consulate-General will confirm the approved facility in your region. Do not proceed with any embalming arrangement without this confirmation.

## Chinese Customs clearance

The Jian Yi Zheng Shu (health clearance certificate) is issued by Chinese Customs. It confirms the remains are free from contagious disease and are cleared for international export. This step cannot be skipped or expedited.

## Exit permit and documentation authentication

The exit permit for human remains is issued by the local Civil Affairs Bureau. All Chinese-language documents require certified translation into English. Depending on the destination, authentication via the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs may also be required. Your repatriation specialist manages this authentication chain.

## Flights from China

British Airways and Virgin Atlantic operate direct daily flights from Beijing Capital (PEK) and Shanghai Pudong (PVG) to London Heathrow. Air China also serves this route. Direct flights are an advantage once documentation is complete: no multi-leg delays.
