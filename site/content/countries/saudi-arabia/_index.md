## Repatriation from Saudi Arabia: What Families Need to Know

Saudi Arabia is one of the most complex Gulf repatriations, and one of the most important. The British expat community in Saudi Arabia is large and predominantly employed in the oil and gas sector, defence and military advisory roles, and major infrastructure projects. British national deaths in Saudi Arabia are a significant consular workload for the British Embassy Riyadh and Consulate-General Jeddah.

There is no Strasbourg Convention. There is no EU framework. Every document is in Arabic and requires multi-stage authentication before UK authorities will accept it. The Bureau of Investigation and Public Prosecution must close its investigation before the Ministry of Health will issue an export permit. These are sequential steps. You cannot run them in parallel.

### The Two Consular Offices

Saudi Arabia is large enough to have two British posts. The British Embassy Riyadh covers the central and eastern regions, including Riyadh and the Eastern Province (where much of the oil sector is concentrated). The British Consulate-General Jeddah covers the western region, including Jeddah, Mecca province, and Medina province.

Contact the office relevant to the region where the death occurred.

### Step One: BIPP Investigation

For any death that is not confirmed natural by a hospital physician, the Bureau of Investigation and Public Prosecution (BIPP) opens an investigation. This investigation must formally close before the body can be released.

Natural hospital deaths can bypass the BIPP process if the treating physician confirms the cause of death on the death certificate without referral.

### Step Two: Death Certificate and Municipal Registration

The death certificate is issued by the municipality's Civil Affairs department (baladiya). This is a separate step from the BIPP process. In employer-managed cases, the employer's HR or local legal representative typically handles this registration.

### Step Three: Ministry of Foreign Affairs Authentication

All Saudi-issued documents must be authenticated by the Saudi Ministry of Foreign Affairs before the British Embassy can formally accept and act on them. This authentication step is not required in European repatriations and is one of the reasons Saudi Arabia takes longer.

### Step Four: Ministry of Health Export Permit

With BIPP clearance and authenticated documents, the Ministry of Health issues the export permit. The British Embassy can make formal representations to the Ministry of Health if there is unexplained delay after BIPP clearance.

### Step Five: Certified Translation

Every Arabic document requires certified English translation by a translator accredited by the British Embassy or recognised by UK courts. Unofficial translations are not accepted.

### Employer-Managed Cases

Deaths among British nationals employed by Saudi Aramco, BAE Systems, the oil sector, or major construction projects typically involve employer-managed repatriation processes. Employers with established in-country legal and HR infrastructure can handle BIPP liaison and document authentication more quickly than a family acting alone. Contact the employer's HR department as one of your first calls.

### Cremation

Cremation is prohibited under Islamic law and is not available in Saudi Arabia. All repatriations involve full body transport.

### Routing

Riyadh King Khalid International Airport (RUH) and Jeddah King Abdulaziz International Airport (JED) both operate direct UK flights. Saudia and British Airways fly the Riyadh and Jeddah to London Heathrow routes. Dammam King Fahd International Airport (DMM) connects via Gulf hubs.
