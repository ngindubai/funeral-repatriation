El Salvador is Central America's smallest country and, until recently, one of its most dangerous. Government security crackdowns from 2022 have transformed the homicide rate, and tourism — surfing, coffee farms, colonial Suchitoto — is growing. But British deaths still occur, and the repatriation process is more layered than the country's small size suggests.

The Honorary Consul covers initial paperwork. Everything that matters goes through British Embassy Guatemala City, 350 kilometres away.

## The consular structure

El Salvador does not have a resident British Embassy. The Honorary Consul in San Salvador provides initial consular support: death registration, family liaison, and document witnessing. Full consular authority — formal death registration, repatriation authorisation — rests with British Embassy Guatemala City, which is accredited to El Salvador.

This means timelines depend partly on Guatemala City embassy workload and communication speed. A UK-based specialist with an established relationship with both offices makes a measurable difference.

## Non-natural deaths and the IML

The Instituto de Medicina Legal (IML) handles forensic pathology for non-natural deaths. The IML operates under the Fiscalia General de la Republica (FGR), which is the attorney general's office. Any death classified as suspicious, violent, or unexplained triggers IML investigation and FGR oversight.

The IML in San Salvador operates efficiently by regional standards, but case backlogs during busy periods can add 5 to 10 days. Until the IML issues its forensic release certificate and the FGR confirms no criminal proceedings are pending, the body cannot be moved.

For deaths that generate criminal investigation — which could include a tourist drowning if foul play cannot be ruled out — timelines extend further.

## Documentation required

All documentation is in Spanish. The UK requires certified English translations.

| Document | Issuer |
|---|---|
| Partida de Defuncion (death certificate) | RNPN civil registry |
| IML forensic release certificate | Instituto de Medicina Legal |
| FGR clearance (if criminal investigation opened) | Fiscalia General de la Republica |
| Embalming and preservation certificate | Licensed funeral home |
| Ministry of Health export permit | Ministry of Health |
| British consular registration | British Embassy Guatemala City |

## Death causes among British visitors

The growth in surf tourism has made Pacific coast water deaths more common. El Zonte (Bitcoin Beach) and Punta Roca attract international surfers; rip currents on the open Pacific coast are strong. Volcano hiking — Santa Ana, Izalco — also generates incidents.

Road traffic accidents on the CA-1 Pan-American Highway and in San Salvador are the most consistent cause. Urban violent crime is much reduced from peak years but FCDO recommends vigilance in specific neighbourhoods in San Salvador and Santa Ana.

## What to do when someone dies in El Salvador

1. Call the FCDO Emergency Travel Line: 020 7008 5000 (24 hours)
2. Contact the Honorary Consul in San Salvador
3. Notify the insurer immediately
4. Do not authorise body movement before IML clearance in non-natural deaths
5. Contact a UK-based repatriation specialist familiar with the Guatemala City embassy process
