## Repatriation from Myanmar: What Families Need to Know

Myanmar is among the most complex repatriations currently available to British families. The February 2021 military coup removed the civilian administration and replaced it with military control at every level of government. What was already a challenging bureaucratic environment has become unpredictable. Timelines that were previously 14 to 21 days now run to 35 to 60 days in standard cases, and much longer when deaths occur near conflict zones.

Families dealing with a death in Myanmar should contact the British Embassy Yangon immediately and engage a specialist repatriation firm with direct experience of post-coup Myanmar procedures. General repatriation firms without Myanmar experience should be avoided.

### The Military Administration Structure

Since February 2021, the State Administration Council (SAC) controls all government functions. At the local level, this means the Township Medical Officer and Township Administrator — both now operating under military oversight — must authorise all death-related documentation. The Township Police Station issues crime scene or investigation reports. All of these officials report upward through military-aligned chains of command.

There is no single national authority to approach. Processes vary by township and region. Yangon cases are generally faster than regional or border cases.

### Documentation in Burmese

Every official document is issued in Burmese script. This includes the death certificate, the police report, the certificate of no objection from the Township Administrator, and the Ministry of Health export permit. Certified English translations are required by UK authorities before entry formalities can be completed. Finding qualified translators for legal and medical Burmese in the UK is specialist work.

### The British Embassy

The British Embassy is located in Yangon, not in Naypyidaw (the official capital since 2006). All consular assistance for British nationals in Myanmar is coordinated from the Yangon Embassy. The Embassy operates an emergency line 24 hours a day.

The Embassy's ability to assist in areas with active conflict or in regions the FCDO advises against entering is constrained. They cannot compel the military administration to release a body or expedite documentation.

### Routing

Yangon International Airport (RGN) handles all international cargo. No direct flights to the UK operate. Standard routing is via Bangkok Suvarnabhumi with Thai Airways or Bangkok Airways, via Singapore Changi with Singapore Airlines, or via Kuala Lumpur with Malaysia Airlines. All three hubs have established cargo handling procedures for human remains.

### FCDO Restrictions

The FCDO advises against all but essential travel to Myanmar and against all travel to Rakhine State, Chin State, Kachin State, Kayah/Karenni State, Shan State (East and South), and the border areas with Thailand, India, Bangladesh, and China. Deaths in these areas involve military-controlled scenes, restricted consular access, and documentation timelines that cannot be reliably estimated.
