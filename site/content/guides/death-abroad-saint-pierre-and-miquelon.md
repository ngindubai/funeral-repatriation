## What to Do When Someone Dies in Saint Pierre and Miquelon

Saint Pierre and Miquelon is a French overseas collectivity of small islands 25 kilometres off the south coast of Newfoundland, Canada. French civil law applies in full — all documentation is in French. The unusual geography means repatriation routing goes through Canadian airports rather than directly to France.

Call the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** immediately. The British Embassy Paris (+33 1 44 51 31 00) handles Saint Pierre and Miquelon on a non-resident basis.

### Step 1: Notify the FCDO and Local Authorities

The attending physician or hospital certifies the death. Deaths are registered with the **mairie** (town hall). Non-natural deaths require clearance from the **Gendarmerie nationale** before funeral arrangements proceed.

Register the death with the **British Embassy, Paris**.

### Step 2: Obtain the Death Certificate and Translation

The mairie issues an **acte de décès** in French. A certified English translation is required for the UK Coroner.

### Step 3: Prepare the Documentation

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Acte de décès + certified English translation | UK Coroner and border requirements |
| Gendarmerie clearance | Required for non-natural deaths |
| Certificat d'embaumement | International air transport requirement |
| Certificat de non-contagion | Customs and quarantine |
| Autorisation de sortie du territoire | Export permit |
| Canadian transit documentation | May be required depending on routing |
| Consular registration | British Embassy, Paris |
| UK Coroner import notification | Required before the body enters the UK |

No cremation is available on the islands.

### Step 4: Arrange the Flight

**Air Saint-Pierre** flights from Saint-Pierre Airport (FSP) to Halifax Stanfield International Airport (YHZ) in Nova Scotia, or to St John's International Airport (YYT) in Newfoundland. Then **Air Canada** or **Air Transat** to London. North Atlantic fog and winter weather frequently disrupt the schedule — build significant contingency into planning.

### Step 5: Repatriation to the UK

On arrival in the UK, the body is received by a registered UK funeral director after UK Coroner confirmation.
