## When Someone Dies in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines: The First Steps

Call the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours) or contact the British High Commission Bridgetown, Barbados (+1 246 430 7800). The BHC Bridgetown handles consular matters for Saint Vincent and the Grenadines on a non-resident basis.

If the death occurred on one of the smaller Grenadines islands — Mustique, Bequia, Canouan, or Union Island — an internal transfer to Saint Vincent must happen before international repatriation can begin. Communicate the island location to the FCDO and any specialist firm from the start.

## The Legal Process

Death registration is handled by the **Registrar General's Office**. The Registrar General issues the official death certificate. Where death was not from natural causes, the **Coroner's Court** must issue a certificate before the body can be released. All documentation is in English — no translation required.

## Document Checklist

| Document | Issued By | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Death certificate | Registrar General's Office | English — no translation needed |

## Grenadines Island Transfers

Deaths on outlying islands require internal transfer — by inter-island ferry or light aircraft — to Saint Vincent's Argyle International Airport before the international repatriation can proceed. A specialist firm will arrange this as part of the overall process.

## Routing to the UK

Most UK-bound repatriation routes via **Barbados (BGI — Grantley Adams International)**, with direct connections to UK airports. Timelines typically run 7–21 days.

## What to Do in the UK

Notify your travel insurer immediately. Gather travel documents, passport copies, and any documentation about the circumstances of death.
