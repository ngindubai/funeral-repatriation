## When Someone Dies in Monaco: The First Steps

Call the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours) or contact the British Embassy Paris (+33 1 44 51 31 00). The British Embassy in Paris handles consular matters for Monaco on a non-resident basis.

Monaco is a low-complexity destination. The process is straightforward and timelines are short. The main practical steps are the French-language documentation and the road transfer to Nice airport.

## The Legal Process

Death registration is handled by Monaco's **Direction de l'Etat Civil** (Civil Registry Office). The Direction issues the official death certificate in French. Where death was not from natural causes, the **Sûreté Publique** (Monaco police) must investigate and authorise release.

Certified English translation is required for all French documents.

## Transfer to Nice Airport

There is no airport in Monaco. **Nice Côte d'Azur Airport (NCE)** is approximately 25 minutes by road or helicopter. Multiple direct daily flights serve UK airports. A specialist firm will manage the transfer and cargo booking.

If cremation is required, transfer to Nice or Cannes in France for cremation, then ashes repatriation to the UK. Timelines typically run 3–10 days.
