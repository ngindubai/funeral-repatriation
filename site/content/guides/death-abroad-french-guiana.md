## What to Do When Someone Dies in French Guiana

French Guiana is an overseas department and region of France on the north-east coast of South America. It is legally part of France — French civil law applies in full and all official documentation is in French. For UK families, the process mirrors Martinique, Guadeloupe, and Réunion, with one significant addition: deaths in the vast interior jungle require getting the body to Cayenne before international arrangements can begin.

Call the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** immediately. This 24-hour line connects you to consular staff. The **British Embassy Paris** (+33 1 44 51 31 00) handles French Guiana on a non-resident basis.

### Step 1: Notify the FCDO and Local Authorities

The attending physician or hospital certifies the death. Deaths are registered with the **mairie** (town hall) of the commune where the death occurred. Non-natural deaths require clearance from the **Gendarmerie nationale** or **Police nationale** before funeral arrangements proceed.

Register the death with the **British Embassy, Paris**.

### Step 2: Interior Deaths — Getting the Body to Cayenne

Deaths in the interior jungle, along the Maroni or Oyapock river systems, or near the Suriname or Brazil borders require transport to Cayenne before international repatriation is possible. This may involve small aircraft, river boat, or road transfer depending on location. Allow additional days. Contact a specialist firm immediately — interior logistics require local knowledge.

### Step 3: Obtain the Death Certificate and Translation

The mairie issues an **acte de décès** in French. A certified English translation is required for the UK Coroner.

### Step 4: Prepare the Documentation

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Acte de décès + certified English translation | UK Coroner and border requirements |
| Gendarmerie/Police clearance | Required for non-natural deaths |
| Certificat d'embaumement | International air transport requirement |
| Certificat de non-contagion | Customs and quarantine |
| Autorisation de sortie du territoire | Export permit |
| Consular registration | British Embassy, Paris |
| UK Coroner import notification | Required before the body enters the UK |

### Step 5: Arrange the Flight

Direct **Air France** flights from Cayenne-Félix Éboué Airport (CAY) to Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG), approximately 9 hours. UK connections from CDG are frequent.

### Step 6: Repatriation to the UK

On arrival in the UK, the body is received by a registered UK funeral director after UK Coroner confirmation.
