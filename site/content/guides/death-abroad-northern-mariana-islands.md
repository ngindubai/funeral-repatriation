## What to Do When Someone Dies in the Northern Mariana Islands

The Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands (CNMI) is a US commonwealth territory in the western Pacific. US federal law governs body export from the CNMI — this runs alongside the UK Coroner import requirements. There are no direct UK flights from Saipan.

Call the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** immediately. The British Embassy Tokyo (+81 3 5211 1100) handles the CNMI on a non-resident basis.

### Step 1: Notify the Authorities

The attending physician or hospital certifies the death. Deaths are registered with **CNMI Vital Statistics**. For non-natural deaths, the **CNMI Medical Examiner** has jurisdiction and must issue clearance before the body can leave the territory.

### Step 2: Outer Island Transfer

Deaths on **Tinian** or **Rota** require inter-island transfer to Saipan before international repatriation can proceed. Arrange this through a local funeral director or your specialist firm.

### Step 3: US Federal Export Requirements

All international body transfers from the CNMI must comply with US federal requirements:

- Embalming to US federal standard
- US federal air transport certificate
- CNMI Vital Statistics death certificate

### Step 4: Prepare the Documentation

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| CNMI death certificate | US federal and UK Coroner requirements |
| Medical Examiner clearance | Required for non-natural deaths |
| US federal embalming and air transport certificate | International air transport |
| UK Coroner import notification | Required before entering the UK |

### Step 5: Arrange the Flight

From Saipan International Airport (SPN):

- **United Airlines** or **Japan Airlines** SPN to Tokyo Narita (NRT) or Haneda (HND), then UK connections
- Via **Guam (GUM)** then onward to Tokyo or direct to Honolulu

Allow 24 or more hours of transit time.

### Step 6: Repatriation to the UK

On arrival in the UK, the body is received by a registered UK funeral director after UK Coroner confirmation.
