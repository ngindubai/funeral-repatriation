## What to Do When Someone Dies in Puerto Rico

Puerto Rico is a US unincorporated territory in the Caribbean. US federal law governs body export — this applies in full alongside the UK Coroner import requirements. UK families must satisfy both systems.

Call the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** immediately. The Honorary British Consul in San Juan is the nearest on-the-ground consular contact; the British Embassy Washington DC (+1 202 588 7800) handles formal matters.

### Step 1: Notify the Authorities

The attending physician or hospital certifies the death. Deaths are registered at the **Puerto Rico Vital Statistics Registry**. For non-natural deaths, the **Puerto Rico Institute of Forensic Sciences** (Instituto de Ciencias Forenses) has jurisdiction — its clearance is mandatory before the body can leave the territory.

### Step 2: US Federal Export Requirements

All international body transfers from Puerto Rico must comply with US federal requirements:

- Embalming to US federal standard
- US federal air transport certificate
- Death certificate issued by Puerto Rico Vital Statistics Registry

Your UK repatriation specialist coordinates with a licensed Puerto Rico funeral director to meet these requirements.

### Step 3: Prepare the Documentation

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Puerto Rico death certificate | US federal and UK Coroner requirements |
| Institute of Forensic Sciences clearance | Required for non-natural deaths |
| US federal embalming and air transport certificate | International air transport |
| UK Coroner import notification | Required before entering the UK |

### Step 4: Arrange the Flight

**British Airways** operates direct flights from Luis Muñoz Marín International Airport (SJU), San Juan, to London Heathrow (LHR), approximately 8.5 hours. American Airlines connections via Miami (MIA), Chicago (ORD), or New York (JFK) are alternatives.

### Step 5: Repatriation to the UK

On arrival in the UK, the body is received by a registered UK funeral director after UK Coroner confirmation.
