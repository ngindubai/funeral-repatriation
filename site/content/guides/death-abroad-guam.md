## What to Do When Someone Dies in Guam

Guam is a US organised territory in the western Pacific. US federal law governs body export — this runs alongside the UK Coroner import requirements. There are no direct UK flights; routing is via Tokyo or Honolulu, adding significant transit time.

Call the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** immediately. The British Embassy Tokyo (+81 3 5211 1100) handles consular matters for Guam on a non-resident basis.

### Step 1: Notify the Authorities

The attending physician or hospital certifies the death. Deaths are registered with **Guam Vital Statistics**. For non-natural deaths, the **Guam Medical Examiner** has jurisdiction and must issue clearance before the body can leave the territory.

### Step 2: US Federal Export Requirements

All international body transfers from Guam must comply with US federal requirements:

- Embalming to US federal standard
- US federal air transport certificate
- Guam Vital Statistics death certificate

Your specialist firm coordinates with a licensed Guam funeral director to meet these requirements.

### Step 3: Prepare the Documentation

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Guam death certificate | US federal and UK Coroner requirements |
| Medical Examiner clearance | Required for non-natural deaths |
| US federal embalming and air transport certificate | International air transport |
| UK Coroner import notification | Required before entering the UK |

### Step 4: Arrange the Flight

No direct UK flights from Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport (GUM). Main routing options:

- **United Airlines** or **Japan Airlines** GUM to Tokyo Narita (NRT) or Haneda (HND), then onward to London
- Via **Honolulu (HNL)** then UK connections from US gateway airports

Allow 24 or more hours of transit time in routing calculations.

### Step 5: Repatriation to the UK

On arrival in the UK, the body is received by a registered UK funeral director after UK Coroner confirmation.
