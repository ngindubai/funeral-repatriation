## What to Do When Someone Dies in Anguilla

Anguilla is a British Overseas Territory in the northeastern Caribbean, just north of Sint Maarten. English common law applies and all documentation is in English. There are no direct UK flights — repatriation routes via Sint Maarten or Antigua. No cremation facilities are available on the island.

Call the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** immediately — this 24-hour line connects you to consular staff. The Governor's Office in The Valley also assists UK nationals.

### Step 1: Notify the FCDO and Local Authorities

The attending physician or hospital certifies the death. All deaths are registered with the **Registrar's Office** in The Valley. Non-natural deaths require clearance from the **Royal Anguilla Police Force** before funeral arrangements proceed.

### Step 2: No Cremation Available

No cremation facilities exist in Anguilla. Full body repatriation to the UK is required.

### Step 3: Engage a UK Repatriation Specialist

Anguilla's limited local mortuary capacity and multi-leg routing to the UK require coordinated specialist management. A firm handles the Registrar's Office, Police Force clearance (where needed), the local funeral director, and the airline connections.

### Step 4: Prepare the Documentation

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Death certificate | UK Coroner and border requirements |
| Police Force clearance | Required for non-natural deaths |
| Embalming certificate | International air transport requirement |
| Freedom from infection certificate | Customs and quarantine |
| Export permit | Government of Anguilla |
| UK Coroner notification | Required before the body enters the UK |

### Step 5: Arrange the Flight

The most practical first leg is to **Sint Maarten's Princess Juliana Airport (SXM)** via 10-minute air shuttle or short ferry from Anguilla. From SXM, **KLM** operates direct service to Amsterdam (AMS) for UK connections.

Alternatively, connect to **Antigua (ANU)** for **British Airways** direct service to London Gatwick (LGW).

### Step 6: Repatriation to the UK

On arrival in the UK, the body is received by a registered UK funeral director after UK Coroner confirmation.
