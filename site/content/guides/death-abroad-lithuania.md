## What to Do When Someone Dies in Lithuania

Lithuania is an EU member with a resident British Embassy in Vilnius and direct UK flights. The process is manageable, with Lithuanian documentation requiring certified translation and non-natural deaths involving Prokuratūra clearance.

### Step 1 — Contact the FCDO or the Embassy

Call the FCDO on **020 7008 5000** (24 hours), or the British Embassy Vilnius directly at +370 5 246 2900. Full consular support is available in Lithuania.

### Step 2 — Appoint a UK Repatriation Specialist

A specialist firm manages the Registrų centras process, certified translation, and coordination with Lithuanian funeral directors.

### Step 3 — Death Registration at the Registrų centras

The **Registrų centras** (Register Centre — Civil Metrical Records division) issues the official death certificate (Mirties liudijimas) in Lithuanian. Your specialist firm coordinates this locally.

### Step 4 — Prokuratūra Clearance (Non-Natural Deaths)

Where death was not from natural causes, the **Prokuratūra** must authorise release. Establish early whether this applies.

### Step 5 — Certified Translation

The Lithuanian death certificate requires certified English translation for the UK Coroner.

### Step 6 — Routing

Direct flights from Vilnius (VNO) or Kaunas (KUN) to London. Frequent Ryanair and Wizz Air services.

### Cremation

Cremation is available — crematoria in Vilnius and Kaunas.

### Realistic Timeline

Expect **5–14 days** for natural deaths. Prokuratūra cases extend this.
